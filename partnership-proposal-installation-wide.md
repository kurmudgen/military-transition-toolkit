## Current Situation

### TAP Program Challenges (GAO Findings 2024)

**Documented Gaps:**
- 25% of servicemembers required to attend TAP classes don't show up
- 70% don't start TAP >1 year in advance as required
- DOD not effectively using performance data to improve outcomes
- Limited evidence that federal transition programs are effective

**Servicemember Feedback:**
- Only 15% felt TAP adequately prepared them for civilian employment
- 47% of post-9/11 veterans describe transition as difficult or very difficult
- 40-75% overall describe transition challenges

**Employment Outcomes:**
- 43% leave first civilian job within one year
- 60%+ underemployed relative to skills
- 12.3% unemployment for veterans ages 18-24

**Program Limitations:**
- Narrow focus on employment/education/benefits
- One-size-fits-all approach
- Limited personalization by military background
- Classroom-based, not available 24/7
- No ongoing post-separation support

---

## Proposed Solution

### Platform Integration with TAP

**Before TAP:**
- Servicemembers receive access 12+ months before separation
- Begin exploring careers and building knowledge
- Arrive to TAP better prepared with specific questions

**During TAP:**
- Supplements classroom instruction with deep-dive resources
- Provides rating/MOS-specific examples and guidance
- Take-home reference materials

**After TAP:**
- Continues support through separation and beyond
- Refreshes knowledge when needed
- Tracks progress toward career goals

### Platform Capabilities

**1. Personalized Career Roadmaps** (500+ guides covering every Navy rating, Marine Corps MOS, Coast Guard rating)
**2. Military Skills Translation** (Automated MOS/Rating to civilian jobs)
**3. Benefits Navigation** (GI Bill calculator, 78 state benefits guides)
**4. Family Support** (Spouse employment, licensing reciprocity)
**5. Resource Hub** (Resumes, interview prep, salary negotiation)
