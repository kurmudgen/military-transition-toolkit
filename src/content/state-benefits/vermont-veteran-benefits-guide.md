# Vermont Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Vermont—the Green Mountain State—offers veterans full military retirement tax exemption, property tax exemptions, and exceptional quality of life in New England's most rural state. With small-town charm, beautiful landscapes, and strong community values, Vermont attracts veterans seeking peaceful living despite limited job market.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 40,000 veterans (7.8% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, property tax exemptions, veteran preference
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. Outstanding natural beauty and outdoor recreation
  3. High quality of life and safety
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Limited job market
  2. Cold, snowy winters
  3. Higher cost of living
- **Best cities for veterans**: Burlington, Rutland, South Burlington, Montpelier, Barre

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans: Property tax exemption based on disability
- 100% disabled: Significant exemptions
- Surviving spouses: May continue exemptions

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Vermont income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- VT income tax: 3.35% to 8.75%

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates

### Education Benefits

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- UVM: Veteran services
- Vermont State University: Veteran support

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- Veterans: Preference in state hiring
- Disabled veterans: Additional preference

### Healthcare Benefits

**Vermont Veterans Home (Bennington)**
- 150 beds
- Skilled nursing and residential care

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Vermont tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Vermont income tax.

**Q: Is the job market limited?**
A: Yes. Vermont is largely rural with limited major employers. Many veterans telecommute.

**Q: How cold is Vermont?**
A: Cold. January averages in teens. Significant snowfall. Cold winters are part of life.
