# Connecticut Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Connecticut—the Constitution State—may be small in size but offers substantial benefits for veterans. With complete exemption of military retirement pay, strong property tax benefits, and one of the nation's best education benefits for veterans' dependents, Connecticut punches above its weight for veteran support. The state's high cost of living is offset by higher salaries and excellent access to New York and Boston job markets.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 175,000 veterans (6.2% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, 100% disabled veteran property tax exemption, free tuition for dependents of disabled veterans, strong VA healthcare access
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. Full military retirement pay tax exemption
  2. Excellent education benefits for dependents
  3. Access to NYC and Boston job markets
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Very high cost of living (top 5 nationally)
  2. High property taxes (even with exemptions)
  3. Limited military installations (smaller veteran community)
- **Best cities for veterans**: Hartford, New Haven, Stamford, Waterbury, New London

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- All veterans: $1,500 off assessed value
- Disabled veterans: Additional exemptions based on disability percentage
- 100% disabled veterans: Full property tax exemption in many towns
- Surviving spouses: Continue exemption
- War-time bonus: Additional exemptions for specific conflicts
- Note: Local implementation varies by municipality

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Connecticut income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- Social Security: Partially exempt depending on income
- Active duty pay: Exempt while on active duty outside CT

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- Purple Heart recipients: Free plates
- POW: Free registration and plates
- Medal of Honor: Free plates and registration
- Various military specialty plates available

**Hunting and Fishing Licenses**
- Disabled veterans: Free inland and marine fishing license
- Service-connected disabled: Reduced-cost combination license
- Resident veterans: Discounted licenses

**State Park Benefits**
- Connecticut residents 65+: Free admission
- Disabled veterans: Discounted camping
- Free Veterans Day admission at state parks

### Education Benefits

**Veterans Tuition Waiver**
- Connecticut National Guard: Tuition waiver at state schools
- Dependents of disabled veterans: Free tuition at state schools
- 100% disabled or KIA dependents: Full tuition and fees waived

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Active duty and families: In-state rates
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- University of Connecticut: Veterans Affairs & Military Programs
- Connecticut State University System (CSCU): Veteran services at all campuses
- Community colleges: 12 campuses with veteran coordinators

**Vocational Training**
- Connecticut Technical Education and Career System

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Connecticut tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Connecticut state income tax.

**Q: What property tax exemption can I get?**
A: All veterans get $1,500 off assessed value. Disabled veterans get additional exemptions. 100% disabled veterans may get full exemption in many towns.

**Q: Can my children get free tuition?**
A: Yes. Dependents of 100% disabled veterans or those killed in action can receive free tuition at Connecticut state colleges and universities.

**Q: How do I get the veteran designation on my license?**
A: Bring DD-214 to any DMV office. "VETERAN" is printed on your license.

**Q: Is Connecticut expensive?**
A: Yes, one of the highest cost-of-living states. However, salaries are also higher, and military retirement tax exemption helps significantly.

**Q: What are the best veteran employers?**
A: Electric Boat (submarines), Pratt & Whitney (engines), and Sikorsky (helicopters) are the premier defense employers with strong veteran hiring programs.

**Q: Is there a VA hospital in Connecticut?**
A: Yes. VA Connecticut Healthcare System has campuses in West Haven and Newington, plus clinics throughout the state.

**Q: How does Connecticut compare to other New England states?**
A: Connecticut exempts military retirement (Massachusetts doesn't). Connecticut has more defense jobs. Higher cost than some but access to NYC market.

**Q: Where should I live for best value?**
A: Hartford area and Waterbury offer better value than Fairfield County. New London good for Navy careers.

**Q: Can I use military experience for licensing?**
A: Yes. Connecticut credits military training toward occupational licensing requirements.

**Q: What's the veterans home like?**
A: Rocky Hill Veterans Home is the largest state-operated veterans home in New England with over 520 beds, including skilled nursing, memory care, and residential living.

**Q: Is commuting to NYC realistic?**
A: Yes from Fairfield County. Metro-North trains run frequently to Grand Central. Stamford to NYC is about 1 hour.

**Q: What about the submarine base?**
A: Naval Submarine Base New London is the primary submarine base on the East Coast. Electric Boat (General Dynamics) builds submarines nearby—major employer.

**Q: Does Connecticut have veterans preference?**
A: Yes, 10 points for veterans and 15 points for disabled veterans in state employment.

**Q: What outdoor activities are available?**
A: Beaches, hiking (Appalachian Trail), fishing, boating on Long Island Sound, state parks, limited skiing.

## Comparison to Other States

**vs. Massachusetts**: Both expensive. Connecticut exempts military retirement; Massachusetts doesn't. Connecticut has more defense manufacturing. Massachusetts has larger economy overall.

**vs. New York**: New York exempts first $20,000 of military retirement (Connecticut exempts all). NYC has more jobs but much higher cost. Connecticut offers NYC access at lower cost.

**vs. Rhode Island**: Similar size and character. Rhode Island slightly lower cost. Connecticut has more defense jobs. Both have submarine community.

**vs. New Jersey**: Both expensive, both near NYC. New Jersey exempts military retirement. Connecticut has more defense manufacturing. New Jersey has no state sales tax on clothing.

**vs. Pennsylvania**: Pennsylvania much lower cost of living. Similar tax treatment of military retirement. Pennsylvania has more land. Connecticut has NYC/Boston access.
