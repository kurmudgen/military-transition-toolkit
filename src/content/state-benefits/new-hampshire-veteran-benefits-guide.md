# New Hampshire Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

New Hampshire—the Granite State—offers veterans no state income tax, no sales tax, property tax credits, and strong veteran benefits. With "Live Free or Die" as its motto, New Hampshire attracts veterans seeking tax advantages and New England quality of life.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 100,000 veterans (9.2% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: No state income tax, no sales tax, property tax credits, veterans preference, All Veterans Tax Credit
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state income tax on any income
  2. No sales tax
  3. High quality of life and safety
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. High property taxes
  2. Higher housing costs
  3. Cold winters
- **Best cities for veterans**: Manchester, Nashua, Concord, Portsmouth, Dover

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Credits**
- All Veterans Tax Credit: $500 credit for all veterans
- Service-Connected Total Disability: Up to $2,000 credit
- Optional Veterans' Tax Credit: Additional local credits
- Surviving spouses: May continue credits

**Income Tax Benefits**
- New Hampshire has NO state income tax on earned income
- Military retirement: Not taxed
- VA disability: Not taxed
- Interest and dividends: 3% tax (phasing out by 2027)
- NO sales tax on purchases

**Vehicle Registration**
- Disabled veterans: Free plates
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates

### Education Benefits

**Tuition Waiver**
- Children of veterans who died on active duty: Free tuition
- 100% disabled veteran dependents: Tuition waiver

**In-State Tuition**
- Veterans: Immediate in-state tuition
- Choice Act compliant

### Healthcare Benefits

**New Hampshire Veterans Home (Tilton)**
- 200 beds
- Skilled nursing and residential care

## Common Questions

**Q: Does New Hampshire tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. New Hampshire has no state income tax on earned income including military retirement.

**Q: Is there really no sales tax?**
A: Correct. New Hampshire is one of five states with no sales tax.

**Q: Why are property taxes high?**
A: With no income or sales tax, local property taxes fund most government services.

**Q: What about Portsmouth Naval Shipyard?**
A: Major employer for veterans. Located on Maine side but serves NH area.
