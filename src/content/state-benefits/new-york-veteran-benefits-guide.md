# New York Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

New York—the Empire State—offers veterans strong benefits including partial military retirement tax exclusion, property tax exemptions, one of the best state veteran education benefits in the nation, and access to the largest job market in America. Despite high costs, New York provides veterans extensive benefits and unmatched career opportunities.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 650,000 veterans (4.3% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Military retirement exclusion (up to $20,000), Cold War Veterans Exemption, Veterans Alternative Tax Exemption, Military Enhanced Recognition Incentive and Tribute (MERIT) scholarship
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. Access to NYC job market with highest salaries
  2. Military Enhanced Recognition Incentive and Tribute (MERIT) scholarships
  3. Strong property tax exemptions
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Very high cost of living (especially NYC area)
  2. High state taxes
  3. Only partial military retirement exclusion
- **Best cities for veterans**: Buffalo, Syracuse, Albany, Rochester, Long Island

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Veterans Exemption: 15% of assessed value
- Combat Zone Service: Additional 10%
- Cold War Veterans: 15% exemption
- Disabled Veterans: Based on disability (up to full exemption)

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: Up to $20,000 excluded from NY income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- NY income tax: 4% to 10.9% (high rates)

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates

### Education Benefits

**MERIT Scholarship (Military Enhanced Recognition Incentive and Tribute)**
- Children of deceased/disabled veterans: Full SUNY/CUNY tuition
- Room and board included
- One of nation's best state education benefits

**Veterans Tuition Awards**
- Full-time/part-time awards for eligible veterans
- Covers SUNY, CUNY, and private schools

**In-State Tuition**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Choice Act compliant

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- Veterans: Preference in civil service
- Disabled veterans: Additional preference
- Strong protections in state law

### Healthcare Benefits

**New York State Veterans Homes (5 Facilities)**
- Oxford, Montrose, Batavia, St. Albans, Queens

## Common Questions

**Q: Does New York fully exempt military retirement?**
A: No. New York excludes up to $20,000 of military retirement pay from state income tax.

**Q: Is the MERIT scholarship valuable?**
A: Yes. It covers full SUNY/CUNY tuition plus room and board—one of the best state veteran education benefits.

**Q: Where is Fort Drum?**
A: Northern NY near Watertown. Home of 10th Mountain Division.
