# Utah Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Utah—the Beehive State—offers veterans partial military retirement tax exclusion, property tax exemptions, and outstanding outdoor recreation. With a booming economy, low unemployment, and Hill AFB, Utah attracts veterans seeking quality of life, family-friendly communities, and strong job market despite not fully exempting military retirement.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 130,000 veterans (5.8% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Partial military retirement exclusion, property tax exemptions, Purple Heart tax credit, veteran preference
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. Excellent job market and economy
  2. Outstanding outdoor recreation
  3. Family-friendly communities
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Military retirement not fully exempt
  2. Growing cost of living
  3. Air quality issues in winter
- **Best cities for veterans**: Salt Lake City, Ogden, Provo, St. George, Logan

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans: Property tax exemption based on disability
- Surviving spouses: May continue exemptions

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: Partial exemption (tax credit up to $1,590)
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- Utah income tax: 4.65% flat rate

**Purple Heart Tax Credit**
- Purple Heart recipients: Additional tax credit

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates

### Education Benefits

**Utah National Guard Tuition Waiver**
- Guard members: Tuition waiver at state schools

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- University of Utah: Veteran services
- Utah State: Military-friendly
- BYU: Veteran support (private)

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- Veterans: Preference in state hiring
- Disabled veterans: Additional preference

### Healthcare Benefits

**Utah State Veterans Home (Ogden)**
- 108 beds
- William E. Christoffersen Salt Lake Veterans Home

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Utah fully exempt military retirement pay?**
A: No. Utah provides a partial exclusion through a tax credit (up to about $1,590).

**Q: What is Hill AFB?**
A: Major Air Force installation near Ogden. Largest employer in Utah. Depot maintenance and logistics.

**Q: Is Utah good for families?**
A: Excellent. Utah consistently ranks top for families with strong schools and safe communities.
