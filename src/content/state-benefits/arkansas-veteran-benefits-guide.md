# Arkansas Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Arkansas—"The Natural State"—offers veterans a blend of Southern hospitality, extremely affordable living, and meaningful state benefits. While not as well-known as Texas or Florida for veterans, Arkansas provides significant tax advantages, including full exemption of military retirement pay, making it an increasingly attractive option for those seeking low cost of living and rural beauty.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 195,000 veterans (8.3% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement pay exemption, property tax relief for disabled veterans, free hunting/fishing licenses, strong veterans preference
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. Very low cost of living (15% below national average)
  2. No state tax on military retirement pay
  3. Affordable housing and land
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Limited major metropolitan job markets
  2. Fewer VA healthcare facilities than neighboring states
  3. Lower average salaries compared to national rates
- **Best cities for veterans**: Little Rock, Fayetteville/Rogers (NW Arkansas), Fort Smith, Hot Springs, Jonesboro

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans: Homestead tax credit based on disability percentage
- 100% disabled veterans: Complete property tax exemption on homestead
- Surviving spouses: Exemption continues if not remarried
- Must apply through county assessor's office

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Arkansas state income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed at state level
- National Guard/Reserve retirement: Also exempt

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans (50%+): Reduced registration fees
- 100% disabled veterans: Free registration
- Purple Heart recipients: Free specialty plates
- POW: Free registration and plates
- Various military specialty plates available

**Hunting and Fishing Licenses**
- Disabled veterans (100%): Free lifetime hunting and fishing license
- Disabled veterans (60%+): Free combination license
- All resident veterans: Discounted licenses available

**State Park Benefits**
- Arkansas State Parks offer veteran discounts on camping
- Free day-use for disabled veterans at some parks
- Special veteran camping weekends

### Education Benefits

**State-Funded Tuition Programs**
- Arkansas National Guard Tuition Incentive Program
- Dependents of killed or disabled veterans: Free tuition at state schools
- Military Dependents Scholarship for children of deceased veterans

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Active duty and dependents: In-state rates
- Choice Act compliant for GI Bill users

**Public University Programs**
- University of Arkansas (Fayetteville): Veterans Resource and Information Center
- Arkansas State University: Military and Veteran Services
- University of Central Arkansas: Veterans Affairs office
- UA Little Rock: Veteran Student Services

**Vocational Training**
- Arkansas Career Training Institute
- Two-year colleges throughout state
- Apprenticeship programs accept GI Bill
- Workforce development programs

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference in State Jobs**
- 5 points for honorably discharged veterans
- 10 points for disabled veterans
- Preference applies to all state employment
- Must claim at time of application

**State Hiring Programs**
- Arkansas Workforce Centers: Veteran priority of service
- Jobs for Veterans State Grant program
- Helmets to Hardhats partnerships

**Licensing Fee Waivers**
- Military experience recognized toward licensing
- Expedited licensing for transitioning service members
- Some professions offer fee waivers

**Business Benefits**
- Small Business Development Centers assistance
- Arkansas Capital Corporation veteran programs
- State contracting preferences being developed

### Healthcare Benefits

**Additional Healthcare Benefits**

### Other Benefits

- Military specialty plates for all branches

## How to Access State Benefits

### Step 1: Establish Residency

- Obtain Arkansas driver's license
- Register to vote in Arkansas
- Register vehicles
- Establish domicile intent
- File Arkansas tax return

### Step 2: Register with State DVA

- Visit veterans.arkansas.gov
- Contact county veterans service officer
- Bring DD-214 and VA disability letter
- Apply for veteran driver's license designation

### Step 3: Apply for Benefits

- Property tax exemption: County assessor before May 31
- Vehicle registration: Arkansas DFA office
- Hunting/fishing license: Arkansas Game and Fish Commission
- Education benefits: Through school and DVA

### Step 4: Get Connected

- Join local VFW or American Legion post
- Register with Arkansas Division of Workforce Services

- Connect with base transition services

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Arkansas tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Arkansas state income tax.

**Q: What property tax exemption can I get?**
A: 100% disabled veterans receive complete property tax exemption on their homestead. Other disabled veterans receive partial credits based on disability percentage.

**Q: How do I get the veteran designation on my license?**
A: Bring DD-214 to any Arkansas DFA revenue office. No additional fee.

**Q: Is there free hunting and fishing for veterans?**
A: Yes. 100% disabled veterans get free lifetime licenses. 60%+ disabled veterans get free combination licenses.

**Q: Which Arkansas city has the best job market?**
A: Northwest Arkansas (Bentonville/Fayetteville area) has the strongest growth. Little Rock has the most government jobs.

**Q: How does Arkansas compare to Texas for veterans?**
A: Arkansas has lower cost of living and full military retirement tax exemption. Texas has no state income tax at all and more job opportunities. Both are veteran-friendly.

**Q: Are there state veterans cemeteries?**
A: Yes, Arkansas State Veterans Cemetery in North Little Rock. Fort Smith and Fayetteville National Cemeteries also serve veterans.

**Q: Can my dependent children get free tuition?**
A: Yes, if you were killed or disabled in service. The dependent scholarship covers tuition at state schools.

**Q: What VA facilities are in Arkansas?**
A: Two VA medical centers (Little Rock area and Fayetteville) plus numerous clinics statewide.

**Q: Is Arkansas good for retirement?**
A: Yes for affordability. Low cost of living, no military retirement tax, and outdoor recreation. Healthcare access in rural areas can be limited.

**Q: How do I find my county VSO?**
A: Call Arkansas DVA at (501) 370-3820 or visit veterans.arkansas.gov.

**Q: Can I use military experience for licensing?**
A: Yes, Arkansas recognizes military training for various occupational licenses.

**Q: What's the job market like in Arkansas?**
A: Growing in NW Arkansas (Walmart effect). Healthcare, logistics, and manufacturing are strong statewide. Salaries lower but so is cost of living.

**Q: Does Arkansas have veterans preference?**
A: Yes, 5 points for veterans and 10 points for disabled veterans in state employment.

**Q: What outdoor activities are available?**
A: World-class fishing, excellent hunting, hiking in Ozarks and Ouachitas, Buffalo River canoeing, state parks throughout.

## Comparison to Other States

**vs. Texas**: Texas has larger economy and no state income tax. Arkansas has lower cost of living and full military retirement exemption (same effective benefit for military retirees). Texas has more VA facilities.

**vs. Oklahoma**: Very similar cost of living and benefits. Oklahoma has no state tax on military retirement (like Arkansas). Arkansas has better outdoor recreation. Oklahoma has more defense jobs.

**vs. Tennessee**: Tennessee has no state income tax. Similar cost of living. Tennessee has Nashville job market; Arkansas has NW Arkansas growth. Both veteran-friendly.

**vs. Missouri**: Similar states in many ways. Arkansas slightly lower cost of living. Missouri has Kansas City and St. Louis job markets. Both have Ozark recreation.

**vs. Mississippi**: Both very affordable. Arkansas has stronger economy (NW Arkansas). Similar veteran benefits. Mississippi has Gulf Coast; Arkansas has mountains.
