# Missouri Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Missouri—the Show-Me State—offers veterans complete exemption of military retirement pay, substantial property tax relief, and strong veteran support in America's heartland. With affordable cost of living, Fort Leonard Wood, and major metro areas in Kansas City and St. Louis, Missouri provides veterans diverse opportunities and solid benefits.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 390,000 veterans (8.0% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, property tax exemptions, Missouri Veterans' Survivor Grant, Motor Vehicle Sales Tax Exemption
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. Affordable cost of living
  3. Central location with two major metros
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Hot, humid summers
  2. Tornado risk in spring
  3. Lower salaries in some areas
- **Best cities for veterans**: Kansas City, St. Louis, Springfield, Columbia, Jefferson City

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans (100%): Full property tax exemption
- Disabled veterans: Partial exemptions based on disability
- Surviving spouses: Continue exemptions

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Missouri income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- Missouri income tax: 1.5% to 4.8%

**Motor Vehicle Sales Tax Exemption**
- Disabled veterans: Exempt from sales tax on vehicle purchases
- Significant benefit on major purchases

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates

### Education Benefits

**Missouri Veterans' Survivor Grant**
- Children/spouses of deceased veterans: Financial assistance
- Covers Missouri public colleges

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- University of Missouri System: Veteran services
- Missouri State University: Veterans Center
- All state universities have veteran services

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- 5 points for veterans
- 10 points for disabled veterans
- Applies to state positions

**State Hiring Programs**
- Missouri Division of Workforce Development
- Missouri Job Centers: Priority of Service

### Healthcare Benefits

**Missouri Veterans Homes (7 Facilities)**
- Cape Girardeau, Cameron, Mexico, Mt. Vernon, St. James, St. Louis, Warrensburg
- Over 1,300 total beds statewide

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Missouri tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Missouri income tax.

**Q: What property tax exemption can I get?**
A: 100% disabled veterans get full exemption. Other disabled veterans receive partial exemptions.

**Q: What military installations are in Missouri?**
A: Fort Leonard Wood (Army training), Whiteman AFB (B-2 bombers), and National Guard facilities.

**Q: Is Missouri affordable?**
A: Yes. Both Kansas City and St. Louis metros offer affordable living compared to similar-sized cities.

**Q: How does Missouri compare to Kansas?**
A: Both exempt military retirement. Similar cost of living. Missouri has larger cities (KC and StL).
