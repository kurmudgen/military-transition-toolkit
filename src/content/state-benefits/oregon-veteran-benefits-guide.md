# Oregon Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Oregon—the Beaver State—offers veterans full military retirement tax exemption, no sales tax, property tax exemptions, and outstanding natural beauty. With a growing tech sector, no sales tax on purchases, and quality of life, Oregon attracts veterans despite higher housing costs in Portland area.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 270,000 veterans (8.0% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, no sales tax, property tax exemptions, veteran preference
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. No sales tax
  3. Outstanding natural beauty and outdoor recreation
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. High housing costs in Portland area
  2. Rainy winters (western Oregon)
  3. Limited major military installations
- **Best cities for veterans**: Portland metro, Salem, Eugene, Bend, Medford

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans: Property tax exemption based on disability
- 100% disabled: Significant exemption
- Surviving spouses: May continue exemptions

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Oregon income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- Oregon has NO sales tax

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates

**Hunting and Fishing Licenses**
- Disabled veterans: Reduced-fee licenses
- Pioneer License for 65+ veterans

### Education Benefits

**Oregon National Guard Tuition Assistance**
- Guard members: Tuition assistance

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- Oregon State: Veteran services
- University of Oregon: Veteran services
- All state schools have veteran support

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- 5 points for veterans
- 10 points for disabled veterans
- Applies to state and many local positions

### Healthcare Benefits

**Oregon Veterans Homes (2 Facilities)**
- The Dalles: 151 beds
- Lebanon: 154 beds

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Oregon tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Oregon income tax.

**Q: Does Oregon have sales tax?**
A: No. Oregon is one of five states with no sales tax.

**Q: Is Portland expensive?**
A: Yes. Portland metro housing costs are high. Consider Salem, Eugene, or southern Oregon for lower costs.

**Q: What military installations are in Oregon?**
A: Limited. Primarily National Guard facilities. Many veterans commute to Joint Base Lewis-McChord in WA.
