# Iowa Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Iowa—the Hawkeye State—offers veterans heartland hospitality combined with solid benefits and one of the most affordable costs of living in America. With complete exemption of military retirement pay, property tax credits, and the War Orphans Educational Assistance program, Iowa provides veterans a stable, family-friendly environment. The state's strong agricultural economy and growing technology sector offer diverse employment opportunities.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 185,000 veterans (7.6% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, homestead tax credit, War Orphans Educational Assistance, motor vehicle tax exemption for disabled veterans
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. Very affordable cost of living
  3. Strong family-friendly communities
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Harsh winters with significant snow
  2. Limited major metropolitan job markets
  3. Fewer VA healthcare facilities than larger states
- **Best cities for veterans**: Des Moines, Cedar Rapids, Iowa City, Davenport (Quad Cities), Sioux City

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans: Homestead tax credit
- 100% disabled veterans: Full property tax exemption on homestead
- War veterans: Military service tax exemption (property)
- Surviving spouses: Continue exemptions
- Business property: Disabled veteran-owned business exemption

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Iowa income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- Iowa income tax rates: 4.4% to 5.7% (does not apply to military retirement)

**Motor Vehicle Tax**
- Disabled veterans (100%): Exempt from motor vehicle fees
- Other disabled veterans: Reduced fees

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates
- Medal of Honor: Free
- Multiple veteran plates available

**Hunting and Fishing Licenses**
- Disabled veterans (permanently): Free lifetime license
- Resident veterans: Discounted licenses

### Education Benefits

**War Orphans Educational Assistance**
- Children of deceased veterans: Financial assistance for college
- Must be Iowa resident
- Applies to Iowa public and private schools

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Active duty and families: In-state rates
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- University of Iowa: Veterans Center
- Iowa State University: Veterans Center
- University of Northern Iowa: Military and Veteran Student Services
- All Regent universities have veteran services

**Iowa National Guard Tuition Aid**
- Guard members: Up to 100% tuition at state schools

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- 5 points for veterans
- 10 points for disabled veterans
- Applies to state merit positions
- Preference in hiring and retention

**State Hiring Programs**
- Iowa Workforce Development veteran services
- IowaWORKS centers: Priority of Service
- Veterans Employment Representatives

**Licensing Fee Waivers**
- Military training credited toward licensing
- Expedited licensing for veterans and spouses

**Business Benefits**
- Iowa SBDC programs
- Targeted Small Business certification
- State contracting opportunities

### Healthcare Benefits

**Additional Healthcare Benefits**

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Iowa tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Iowa state income tax.

**Q: What property tax exemption can I get?**
A: 100% disabled veterans get full property tax exemption. Other disabled veterans receive credits based on disability rating.

**Q: How do I get the veteran designation on my license?**
A: Bring DD-214 to any DOT office. Veteran designation added at no charge.

**Q: What is the War Orphans Educational Assistance?**
A: Financial assistance for college education for children of deceased veterans. Must be Iowa resident.

**Q: What VA facilities are in Iowa?**
A: Two VA Medical Centers (Des Moines and Iowa City) plus numerous clinics throughout the state.

**Q: Is Iowa really that affordable?**
A: Yes. Iowa consistently ranks among the lowest cost-of-living states, especially for housing.

**Q: How cold are the winters?**
A: Cold with significant snow. January averages in the teens to low 20s. Wind chill can be brutal.

**Q: What about job opportunities?**
A: Strong in insurance (Des Moines), agriculture/food processing, and manufacturing. Lower salaries offset by low cost of living.

**Q: Is the Iowa Veterans Home good?**
A: Yes. The Iowa Veterans Home in Marshalltown is one of the largest and most respected state veterans homes in the nation.

**Q: Can I use military experience for licensing?**
A: Yes. Iowa credits military training toward occupational licensing requirements.

**Q: How does Iowa compare to Nebraska?**
A: Very similar in many ways. Iowa slightly larger economy. Nebraska has no tax on military retirement (like Iowa). Similar cost of living.

**Q: What outdoor activities are available?**
A: Excellent pheasant hunting, fishing, state parks, rivers for boating, and extensive bike trail system.

**Q: Is the veteran community active?**
A: Very active. Iowa has veterans affairs offices in all 99 counties—one of the most comprehensive state systems.

**Q: What about healthcare access?**
A: Good access to VA facilities in Des Moines and Iowa City. Rural areas may require travel for specialty care.

**Q: Are there good schools?**
A: Iowa has consistently strong public education. University of Iowa and Iowa State are well-regarded.

## Comparison to Other States

**vs. Nebraska**: Very similar benefits and cost of living. Both exempt military retirement. Nebraska slightly smaller economy. Similar culture.

**vs. Minnesota**: Minnesota has higher cost of living but larger economy. Both exempt military retirement. Minnesota has Minneapolis-St. Paul metro; Iowa has Des Moines.

**vs. Illinois**: Illinois has larger economy and Chicago, but higher taxes and state fiscal issues. Both exempt military retirement. Iowa more affordable.

**vs. Missouri**: Missouri slightly lower cost of living. Both exempt military retirement. Missouri has Kansas City and St. Louis. Similar overall.

**vs. South Dakota**: South Dakota has no state income tax at all. Iowa has more diverse economy. South Dakota lower population. Similar cost of living.
