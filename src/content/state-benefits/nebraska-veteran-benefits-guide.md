# Nebraska Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Nebraska—the Cornhusker State—offers veterans full military retirement tax exemption, affordable living, and strong Midwestern values. With Offutt Air Force Base (home to US Strategic Command) and a diverse economy, Nebraska provides veterans stability and opportunity in America's heartland.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 120,000 veterans (8.0% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, homestead exemptions, waived tuition for dependents, strong veterans preference
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. Affordable cost of living
  3. Strong defense presence (Offutt AFB/STRATCOM)
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Cold winters
  2. Limited metro areas
  3. Lower salaries than coastal states
- **Best cities for veterans**: Omaha, Lincoln, Bellevue, Grand Island, Kearney

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans (100%): Full homestead exemption
- Disabled veterans: Partial exemptions based on disability
- Surviving spouses: Continue exemptions

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Nebraska income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- Nebraska income tax: 2.46% to 5.84%

**Vehicle Registration**
- Disabled veterans: Free plates
- POW, Purple Heart, Medal of Honor: Free plates

### Education Benefits

**Waiver of Tuition for Dependents**
- Children of deceased/disabled veterans: Free tuition at state schools
- Nebraska National Guard: Tuition assistance

### Healthcare Benefits

**Nebraska Veterans' Homes**
- Grand Island: 226 beds
- Norfolk: 222 beds
- Bellevue: 202 beds
- Scottsbluff: 43 beds
- Kearney: 122 beds
