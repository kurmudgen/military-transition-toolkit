# Washington Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Washington—the Evergreen State—offers veterans no state income tax, property tax exemptions, and a booming economy. With Joint Base Lewis-McChord, tech giants like Amazon and Microsoft, and stunning natural beauty, Washington attracts veterans seeking strong career opportunities despite higher costs in the Seattle area.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 520,000 veterans (8.5% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: No state income tax, property tax exemptions, veteran preference, hunting/fishing benefits
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state income tax on any income
  2. Joint Base Lewis-McChord
  3. Tech industry opportunities
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. High cost of living (Seattle area)
  2. Rainy weather (western WA)
  3. Growing traffic
- **Best cities for veterans**: Tacoma, Seattle, Spokane, Olympia, Vancouver

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans: Property tax exemption based on income/disability
- Surviving spouses: May continue exemptions

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Washington has NO state income tax
- Military retirement: Not taxed
- VA disability: Not taxed
- All income: Not taxed

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates

**Hunting and Fishing Licenses**
- Disabled veterans (30%+): Reduced-fee license
- 100% disabled: Free license

### Education Benefits

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- University of Washington: Veteran services
- Washington State: Military-friendly
- All state colleges have veteran services

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- 5% of total score for veterans
- 10% for disabled veterans
- Applies to state positions

### Healthcare Benefits

**Washington State Veterans Homes (4 Facilities)**
- Orting, Port Orchard, Retsil, Spokane

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Washington tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Washington has no state income tax on any income.

**Q: Is living in Vancouver WA worth it?**
A: Popular choice. No WA income tax, access to Portland OR (no sales tax shopping). Best of both worlds.

**Q: What is JBLM?**
A: Joint Base Lewis-McChord. Combined Army/Air Force base near Tacoma. One of largest bases in US.

**Q: Is eastern Washington different?**
A: Yes. Eastern WA (Spokane) has lower costs, drier climate, and different culture than Seattle area.
