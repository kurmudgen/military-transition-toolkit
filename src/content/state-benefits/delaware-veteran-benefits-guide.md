# Delaware Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Delaware—"The First State"—may be the second-smallest state geographically, but it offers veterans significant benefits that make it worth considering. With no sales tax, complete exemption of military retirement pay, and proximity to major East Coast cities, Delaware provides a unique combination of tax advantages and strategic location. The state has a proud military heritage and an active veteran support network.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 70,000 veterans (9.1% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, no state sales tax, property tax exemptions for disabled veterans, free tuition for dependents
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No sales tax on any purchases
  2. Full military retirement pay tax exemption
  3. Strategic location between DC, Philadelphia, and Baltimore
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Limited VA healthcare facilities (no VA medical center)
  2. Small job market compared to neighboring states
  3. High property taxes in some areas
- **Best cities for veterans**: Wilmington, Newark, Dover, Middletown, Rehoboth Beach area

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans: Exemption based on disability rating and income
- Senior property tax credit: Available to veterans 65+
- Paraplegic/quadriplegic veterans: Full exemption

- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Delaware income tax

## VA Facilities in Delaware

### VA Medical Centers

Delaware does not have a VA Medical Center. Veterans are served by:

**Wilmington VA Medical Center** (Philadelphia, PA - 30 miles from Wilmington)
- Address: 1601 Kirkwood Highway, Wilmington, DE 19805 (VA Clinic)
- Medical Center: Coatesville, PA or Philadelphia VA

### VA Clinics (Community-Based Outpatient Clinics)

- **Wilmington CBOC**: 1601 Kirkwood Highway, Wilmington
- **Dover CBOC**: 1198 South Governors Avenue
- **Georgetown CBOC**: 21748 Liberty Lane

### Vet Centers

- **Wilmington Vet Center**: 2710 Centerville Road, Suite 103

### VA Regional Office

Delaware veterans are served by the Philadelphia VA Regional Office:
- Address: 5000 Wissahickon Avenue, Philadelphia, PA 19144
- Phone: 1-800-827-1000

### Important Note

Due to no in-state VA medical center, Delaware veterans may need to travel to:
- Philadelphia VA Medical Center
- Wilmington, PA VA Medical Center
- Baltimore VA Medical Center
- Perry Point VA Medical Center (MD)

The VA provides travel reimbursement for appointments.

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Delaware tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Delaware state income tax.

**Q: Is there a sales tax in Delaware?**
A: No. Delaware has no sales tax on any purchases, which is a significant benefit.

**Q: What property tax exemption can I get?**
A: Disabled veterans receive exemptions based on disability rating and income. Paraplegic/quadriplegic veterans may receive full exemption.

**Q: Is there a VA hospital in Delaware?**
A: No. Delaware has VA clinics but no VA medical center. Veterans typically use Philadelphia or Wilmington (PA) VA facilities.

**Q: How do I get the veteran designation on my license?**
A: Bring DD-214 to any DMV office. "VETERAN" is printed on your license.

**Q: Can my children get free tuition?**
A: Yes. Dependents of deceased or 100% disabled veterans can receive free tuition at Delaware state schools.

**Q: How does Delaware compare to Maryland?**
A: Delaware has no sales tax and exempts all military retirement. Maryland exempts first $15,000 of military retirement. Delaware is smaller with fewer VA facilities.

**Q: Is Delaware expensive?**
A: Moderately. Northern Delaware (near Wilmington) is more expensive. Central and southern Delaware are more affordable. No sales tax helps with overall costs.

**Q: What's the job market like?**
A: Strong in financial services and healthcare. Dover AFB provides federal jobs. Many commute to Philadelphia or Wilmington for work.

**Q: Where should I live for Dover AFB?**
A: Dover city or surrounding areas (Camden, Smyrna, Milford). Middletown is about 30 minutes north.

**Q: Is Delaware good for retirement?**
A: Yes for tax purposes—no sales tax, no military retirement tax, no Social Security tax. Beach communities popular for retirement.

**Q: Can I use military experience for licensing?**
A: Yes. Delaware credits military training toward occupational licensing and expedites the process.

**Q: What about VA travel reimbursement?**
A: Since there's no VA medical center in state, VA provides travel reimbursement for appointments at out-of-state facilities.

**Q: How do I find veterans preference jobs?**
A: State jobs through Delaware.gov list veteran preference. Federal jobs through USAJobs.gov.

**Q: What's the veteran community like?**
A: Active but smaller than military hub states. Dover AFB area has strongest military community. VSOs active statewide.

## Comparison to Other States

**vs. Maryland**: Delaware has no sales tax and full military retirement exemption. Maryland has more VA facilities and larger job market. Delaware significantly smaller but close to same markets.

**vs. Pennsylvania**: Pennsylvania has no tax on military retirement. Delaware has no sales tax. Similar proximity to Philadelphia. Pennsylvania has more VA facilities.

**vs. New Jersey**: New Jersey also exempts military retirement. Delaware has no sales tax (NJ has high sales tax except on clothes). Delaware lower cost of living.

**vs. Virginia**: Virginia has more military installations and VA facilities. Virginia taxes military retirement (partial exemption). Delaware closer to northeast markets.

**vs. North Carolina**: North Carolina has no military retirement tax. Lower cost of living. More military bases. Delaware has no sales tax advantage.
