# Illinois Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Illinois—the Land of Lincoln—is home to over 560,000 veterans and offers substantial state benefits including complete exemption of military retirement pay and one of the nation's strongest veterans preference systems. With a diverse economy anchored by Chicago, affordable living downstate, and a comprehensive VA healthcare network, Illinois provides veterans with solid opportunities despite challenges like high property taxes and harsh winters.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 560,000 veterans (5.6% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, property tax exemptions, free tuition for veterans, strong veterans preference (among the strongest in nation)
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. Strong veterans preference in state/local employment
  3. Illinois Veterans' Grant (free tuition at state schools)
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. High property taxes (among highest in nation)
  2. Cold winters with lake effect snow
  3. State fiscal challenges and outmigration concerns
- **Best cities for veterans**: Chicago suburbs, Springfield, Champaign-Urbana, Peoria, Rockford

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Standard veteran exemption: $5,000 off assessed value
- Returning veterans: Additional one-time $5,000 exemption
- Disabled veterans: Up to $100,000 off assessed value (based on rating)
- 70%+ disabled: Complete exemption available
- Surviving spouses: Continue exemptions

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Illinois income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- Illinois flat tax rate: 4.95% (does not apply to military retirement)

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- Standard veteran plates: $30 fee
- Medal of Honor, POW, Purple Heart: Free
- Various military specialty plates

**Hunting and Fishing Licenses**
- Disabled veterans: Free sport fishing license
- Military on leave: Resident rates
- Discounted licenses available

**State Park Benefits**
- Illinois residents: Free camping nights program
- Disabled veterans: Reduced fees

### Education Benefits

**Illinois Veterans' Grant (IVG)**
- Free tuition and fees at Illinois public colleges
- Available to veterans who served 1+ year active duty
- Must be Illinois resident
- Covers 120 credit hours equivalent
- One of best state education benefits in nation

**MIA/POW Scholarship**
- Dependents of MIA/POW: Free tuition at state schools

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Active duty and families: In-state rates
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- University of Illinois: Veterans Services (all campuses)
- Illinois State University: Military Student Services
- Southern Illinois University: Veteran Services

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference (Among Strongest in US)**
- 10 points for veterans

- Points added to passing scores
- Applies to state AND all local governments
- Promotion preference
- Retention preference during layoffs

**State Hiring Programs**

**Licensing Fee Waivers**
- Initial license fee waived for veterans

**Business Benefits**

- State contracting opportunities

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Illinois tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Illinois state income tax.

**Q: What is the Illinois Veterans' Grant?**
A: Free tuition and fees at all Illinois public colleges and universities for veterans who served 1+ year active duty and are Illinois residents. One of the best state education benefits in the nation.

**Q: What property tax exemption can I get?**
A: Standard veteran: $5,000 off assessed value. Disabled veterans: Up to $100,000 based on rating. 70%+ disability may receive complete exemption.

**Q: How do I get the veteran designation on my license?**
A: Bring DD-214 to any Secretary of State facility. "VETERAN" or military emblem added at no charge.

**Q: Is Chicago expensive?**
A: Yes, especially property taxes. Chicago suburbs vary widely. Downstate Illinois is very affordable.

**Q: How strong is Illinois veterans preference?**
A: Among the strongest in the nation. 10-15 points added to scores, plus applies to ALL local governments, not just state.

**Q: What VA facilities are in Illinois?**
A: Five VA medical centers plus numerous clinics. Excellent coverage, especially in Chicago metro.

**Q: Are property taxes really that high?**
A: Yes, among highest in nation. This partially offsets other benefits. Varies significantly by county and municipality.

**Q: Is Illinois losing population?**
A: Illinois has experienced outmigration, but remains a major state with significant opportunities, especially for those with veteran benefits.

**Q: Can I combine GI Bill with Illinois Veterans' Grant?**
A: Yes. IVG may cover what GI Bill doesn't (or vice versa), making education very affordable.

**Q: What about Scott AFB?**
A: Located near St. Louis in southern Illinois. Major installation with good employment opportunities.

**Q: How do I find a county veteran assistance commission?**
A: Contact IDVA or search veterans.illinois.gov for local resources.

**Q: What's the veteran community like?**
A: Very active. Illinois has some of the largest VFW and American Legion networks in the country.

**Q: Is the job market good?**
A: Varied. Chicago metro has diverse opportunities. Manufacturing remains important downstate. Healthcare growing statewide.

**Q: Can I use military experience for licensing?**
A: Yes. Illinois credits military training and waives initial licensing fees for veterans.
