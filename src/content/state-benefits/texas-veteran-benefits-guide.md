# Texas Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Texas—the Lone Star State—offers veterans no state income tax, substantial property tax exemptions, the Hazelwood Act education benefit, and access to the most military installations of any state. With a booming economy, affordable living, and strong military culture, Texas is the top destination for military retirees in America.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 1.5 million veterans (highest in nation)
- **Key state benefits**: No state income tax, property tax exemptions, Hazelwood Act (free college), Texas Veterans Land Board benefits
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state income tax on any income
  2. More military bases than any state
  3. Strong job market and affordable living
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Very hot summers
  2. Growing traffic in major metros
  3. High property taxes
- **Best cities for veterans**: San Antonio, Dallas-Fort Worth, Houston, Austin, El Paso, Killeen

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans (100%): Complete property tax exemption
- Disabled veterans: Partial exemptions (10%-90% rating)
- Surviving spouses: May continue exemptions

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Texas has NO state income tax
- Military retirement: Not taxed
- VA disability: Not taxed
- All income: Not taxed

**Texas Veterans Land Board**
- Land loans for veterans
- Home loans with competitive rates
- Home improvement loans

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates
- Medal of Honor: Free

### Education Benefits

**Hazelwood Act**
- Up to 150 hours free tuition at state schools
- One of best state education benefits in nation
- Legacy transfer to children if unused

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Choice Act compliant

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- Veterans: Preference in state hiring
- Disabled veterans: Additional preference

### Healthcare Benefits

**Texas State Veterans Homes (9 Facilities)**
- Amarillo, Big Spring, Bonham, El Paso, Floresville, Houston, McAllen, Temple, Tyler

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Texas tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Texas has no state income tax on any income.

**Q: What is the Hazelwood Act?**
A: Free tuition (up to 150 hours) at Texas public colleges for veterans. Unused hours can transfer to children.

**Q: What military bases are in Texas?**
A: Fort Cavazos (Hood), Fort Sam Houston, Fort Bliss, Lackland AFB, Randolph AFB, Sheppard AFB, Dyess AFB, NAS Corpus Christi, and more.

**Q: Is San Antonio good for veterans?**
A: San Antonio is "Military City USA" with the highest veteran concentration. Very military-friendly.
