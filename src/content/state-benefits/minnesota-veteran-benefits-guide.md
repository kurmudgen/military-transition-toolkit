# Minnesota Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Minnesota—the North Star State—offers veterans exceptional benefits including full military retirement tax exemption, property tax exemptions, the Minnesota GI Bill, and a strong veterans preference system. With a diverse economy, excellent schools, and high quality of life, Minnesota attracts veterans who value education, healthcare, and community despite its famously cold winters.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 310,000 veterans (7.1% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, Minnesota GI Bill (state-funded education benefit), property tax exclusions, strong veterans preference
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. Minnesota GI Bill program
  3. Excellent schools and healthcare
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Very cold winters
  2. Higher taxes on other income
  3. Higher cost of living in Twin Cities
- **Best cities for veterans**: Minneapolis/St. Paul, Rochester, Duluth, St. Cloud, Mankato

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exclusions**
- Disabled veterans (70%+): Up to $300,000 market value exclusion
- 100% disabled: Complete property tax exclusion
- Surviving spouses: May continue benefits
- Market value exclusion program

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Minnesota income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- Minnesota has high tax rates on other income (5.35% to 9.85%)

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Reduced or free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates
- Medal of Honor: Free

**Hunting and Fishing Licenses**
- Disabled veterans (100%): Free fishing license
- Resident veterans: Discounted licenses
- Military appreciation hunting opportunities

### Education Benefits

**Minnesota GI Bill**
- Up to $10,000 for eligible Minnesota veterans
- Service-connected disability: Higher amounts
- Can supplement federal GI Bill
- Covers tuition, fees, books

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Active duty and families: In-state rates
- Choice Act compliant

**Dependent Education**
- Surviving Dependents Scholarships
- Covers children of deceased veterans

**Public University Programs**
- University of Minnesota: Veterans Transition Center
- Minnesota State system: 37 institutions with veteran services
- Strong Yellow Ribbon participation

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- 10 points for veterans
- 15 points for disabled veterans
- One of strongest preference systems
- Applies to state and many local positions

**State Hiring Programs**
- Minnesota Department of Employment and Economic Development
- CareerForce centers: Priority of Service
- Veterans Employment Services

**Licensing Fee Waivers**
- Military training credited toward licensing
- Expedited licensing for veterans and spouses

### Healthcare Benefits

**Minnesota Veterans Homes (5 Facilities)**
- Minneapolis: 301 beds
- Hastings: 195 beds
- Silver Bay: 90 beds
- Fergus Falls: 93 beds
- Luverne: 83 beds

### Other Benefits

**License Plates**
- Multiple military specialty plates
- Disabled veteran plates (free)
- Gold Star Family plates

**Burial Benefits**
- Fort Snelling National Cemetery
- Minnesota State Veterans Cemetery (Preston)
- No cost for eligible veterans

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Minnesota tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Minnesota income tax.

**Q: What is the Minnesota GI Bill?**
A: State-funded education benefit providing up to $10,000 for eligible veterans, supplementing federal benefits.

**Q: What property tax exclusion can I get?**
A: Disabled veterans (70%+) can exclude up to $300,000 of market value. 100% disabled get complete exclusion.

**Q: How cold is Minnesota?**
A: Very cold. January averages around 10-15°F in Twin Cities, colder in northern areas.

**Q: Is Minnesota good for healthcare careers?**
A: Exceptional. Mayo Clinic, UnitedHealth, and numerous healthcare systems offer excellent opportunities.

**Q: How does Minnesota compare to Wisconsin?**
A: Both exempt military retirement. Minnesota has more Fortune 500s. Wisconsin slightly lower cost of living. Similar climate.
