# Hawaii Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Hawaii—the Aloha State—is home to one of the highest concentrations of veterans per capita in the nation due to its strategic military importance in the Pacific. With major installations spanning all service branches and unique state benefits, Hawaii attracts veterans seeking paradise living and continued connection to military life. However, the extremely high cost of living presents significant challenges that must be carefully weighed.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 110,000 veterans (9.4% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Military retirement tax exemption for those 65+, property tax exemptions, free tuition for dependents of disabled veterans, strong veterans preference
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. Paradise location with year-round beautiful weather
  2. Strong military presence and veteran community
  3. Excellent VA healthcare facilities
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Extremely high cost of living (highest in US)
  2. Military retirement is taxed (until age 65)
  3. Geographic isolation from mainland
- **Best cities for veterans**: Honolulu/Oahu, Kapolei, Mililani, Kailua, Maui (Kahului)

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans: $141,548 exemption on property value
- Totally disabled veterans (100%): Complete exemption
- Surviving spouses: Exemptions continue
- Must apply through county real property tax office

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay:
  - Under 65: Fully taxed
  - 65+: Exempt from Hawaii income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- Hawaii income tax rates: 1.4% to 11% (progressive)

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Reduced or waived fees
- Special plates for disabled veterans
- Military plates available

**Hunting and Fishing Licenses**
- Disabled veterans: Reduced-cost licenses
- Free saltwater fishing (no license required for shore fishing)

**State Park Benefits**
- Hawaii Kamaaina rates for residents
- Camping discounts available

### Education Benefits

**State-Funded Tuition Programs**
- Dependents of 100% disabled/deceased veterans: Free tuition at UH system
- Hawaii National Guard: Tuition assistance available

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Active duty and families: In-state rates
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- University of Hawaii System: Veterans services at all campuses
- UH Manoa: Veterans Student Services
- Community colleges: Veteran coordinators

**Vocational Training**
- Hawaii Community College System
- Apprenticeship programs
- GI Bill approved programs

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- 5 points for veterans

- Applies to state and county positions
- Additional preference for combat veterans

**State Hiring Programs**
- Hawaii Workforce Development Division veteran services

**Licensing Fee Waivers**
- Military experience credited toward licensing

**Business Benefits**

### Healthcare Benefits

### Other Benefits

**Burial Benefits**

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Hawaii tax military retirement pay?**
A: Under 65: Yes, fully taxed. Age 65+: Exempt from Hawaii income tax.

**Q: Can I afford Hawaii on military retirement?**
A: Challenging. Full retirement from senior enlisted or officer ranks may be manageable in modest housing. Many retirees need additional income.

**Q: What property tax exemption can I get?**
A: Disabled veterans get $141,548 exemption. 100% disabled veterans may receive full exemption.

**Q: How do I get the veteran designation on my license?**
A: Bring DD-214 to any DMV office. Veteran designation added to license.

**Q: Is there a VA hospital in Hawaii?**
A: Yes. Spark M. Matsunaga VA Medical Center on Oahu, plus clinics on all major islands.

**Q: Can my children get free tuition?**
A: Yes. Dependents of 100% disabled or deceased veterans receive free tuition at UH system schools.

**Q: How bad is traffic on Oahu?**
A: Very bad. Honolulu has some of the worst traffic in the US. Plan commutes carefully.

**Q: Should I ship my car to Hawaii?**
A: Often yes for reliability and cost savings long-term. Shipping costs $1,000-$2,000 from mainland.

**Q: Is housing really that expensive?**
A: Yes. Median home price on Oahu is $950,000+. Many veterans rent or live in military housing longer.

**Q: Can I live on other islands?**
A: Yes, but fewer job opportunities and must travel to Oahu for some VA services. Neighbor islands have VA clinics.

**Q: What about the COLA in Hawaii?**
A: Active duty receive Cost of Living Allowance (COLA) in Hawaii. Retirees do not receive COLA.

**Q: Is it worth living in Hawaii despite the cost?**
A: Personal decision. Quality of life is exceptional for those who love outdoor lifestyle, military community, and island living. Financial planning essential.

**Q: Can I use military experience for licensing?**
A: Yes. Hawaii credits military training toward occupational licensing.

**Q: What outdoor activities are available?**
A: World-class beaches, surfing, hiking, diving, fishing, golf, paddling—year-round outdoor paradise.

**Q: How do I find housing?**
A: Start early. Consider military housing if eligible. Work with local realtors familiar with veteran needs. Be prepared for sticker shock.

## Comparison to Other States

**vs. California**: California has more VA facilities and larger job market. Hawaii has better weather and smaller community. Both very expensive. California taxes military retirement; Hawaii exempts at 65.

**vs. Florida**: Florida has no income tax. Much lower cost of living. Larger job market. Hawaii has better year-round weather and no hurricanes.

**vs. Washington**: Washington has no income tax. Lower cost of living (still high). More mainland access. Hawaii has better weather.

**vs. Texas**: Texas has no income tax and much lower cost of living. More job opportunities. Hawaii has ocean, climate, and island lifestyle.

**vs. Virginia**: Virginia closer to DC job market. Lower cost of living. More seasonal weather. Hawaii has year-round outdoor lifestyle and strong Pacific presence.
