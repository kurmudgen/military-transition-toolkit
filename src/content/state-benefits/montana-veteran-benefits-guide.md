# Montana Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Montana—Big Sky Country—offers veterans complete exemption of military retirement pay, no sales tax, and unmatched outdoor recreation. With stunning natural beauty, strong veteran appreciation, and a slower pace of life, Montana attracts veterans seeking quality of life over urban amenities. The state has one of the highest veteran percentages in the nation.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 90,000 veterans (10.3% of adult population—among highest in nation)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, no sales tax, property tax exemptions, strong veterans preference
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. No sales tax
  3. Outstanding outdoor recreation and natural beauty
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Limited VA healthcare facilities
  2. Limited job market
  3. Cold winters, especially eastern Montana
- **Best cities for veterans**: Billings, Missoula, Great Falls, Helena, Bozeman

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans (100%): Property tax exemption
- Veterans: Additional homestead exemption
- Surviving spouses: Continue exemptions

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Montana income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- Montana has NO sales tax

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates
- Medal of Honor: Free

**Hunting and Fishing Licenses**
- Disabled veterans (100%): Free licenses
- Resident veterans: Discounted licenses

### Education Benefits

**Montana Veterans' Tuition Waiver**
- Waiver for war orphans and certain dependents
- In-state tuition for veterans

**Public University Programs**
- Montana State University: Veteran services
- University of Montana: Veteran services

### Healthcare Benefits

**Montana Veterans' Home (Columbia Falls)**
- Long-term care facility
- Skilled nursing and domiciliary

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Montana tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Montana income tax.

**Q: Does Montana have sales tax?**
A: No. Montana is one of five states with no sales tax.

**Q: What VA facilities are in Montana?**
A: VA Montana Health Care System at Fort Harrison plus clinics statewide. Limited specialty care may require travel.

**Q: What military installations are in Montana?**
A: Malmstrom AFB (Great Falls) operates Minuteman III ICBMs.

**Q: How cold is Montana?**
A: Cold, especially eastern Montana. Winters can reach -20°F or colder. Western valleys somewhat milder.
