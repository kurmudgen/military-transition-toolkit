# North Carolina Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

North Carolina—the Tar Heel State—is one of America's most military-friendly states, home to Fort Liberty (Bragg), Camp Lejeune, Cherry Point, and Seymour Johnson AFB. With full military retirement tax exemption, excellent property tax benefits, strong job market, and moderate cost of living, North Carolina offers veterans an outstanding combination of benefits and opportunities.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 720,000 veterans (8.9% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, property tax exclusions, veteran preference, scholarship programs
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. Major military installations and defense jobs
  3. Affordable cost of living with strong economy
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Hurricane risk on coast
  2. Growing traffic in Triangle and Charlotte
  3. Some areas have limited VA access
- **Best cities for veterans**: Fayetteville, Raleigh-Durham, Charlotte, Jacksonville, Wilmington

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exclusions**
- Disabled veterans (100%): First $45,000 assessed value exempt
- Surviving spouses: May continue exemptions

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from NC income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- NC income tax: 4.75% flat rate

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates
- Military specialty plates available

### Education Benefits

**NC Scholarships for Children of War Veterans**
- Children of wartime veterans: Free tuition at NC public schools
- Must meet specific criteria

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Active duty and families: In-state rates
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- UNC System: Strong veteran services
- NC State: Military-friendly
- All community colleges have veteran services

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- 5 points for veterans
- 10 points for disabled veterans
- Applies to state positions

**NC4Vets Program**
- State employment initiative for veterans
- Career transition assistance

### Healthcare Benefits

**NC State Veterans Homes (4 Facilities)**
- Fayetteville, Salisbury, Black Mountain, Kinston

## Common Questions

**Q: Does North Carolina tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from NC income tax.

**Q: What's the largest military installation?**
A: Fort Liberty (formerly Fort Bragg) is the largest Army installation by population, home to XVIII Airborne Corps and Special Operations.

**Q: Is the Research Triangle good for veterans?**
A: Excellent. The Raleigh-Durham area offers tech jobs, universities, and the Durham VAMC.
