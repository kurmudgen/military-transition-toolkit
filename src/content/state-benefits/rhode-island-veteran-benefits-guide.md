# Rhode Island Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Rhode Island—the Ocean State—offers veterans full military retirement tax exemption, property tax exemptions, and access to excellent education opportunities. Despite its small size, Rhode Island provides strong veteran benefits and access to the Boston and Providence job markets. Naval Station Newport makes Rhode Island significant to the military community.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 60,000 veterans (6.9% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, property tax exemptions, tuition waivers, veteran preference
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. Naval Station Newport (Navy)
  3. Access to Boston job market
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. High cost of living
  2. High property taxes
  3. Small state with limited job diversity
- **Best cities for veterans**: Providence, Warwick, Cranston, Newport, Pawtucket

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans (100%): Full property tax exemption
- Veterans: Various local exemptions
- Gold Star Parents: Property tax exemption

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from RI income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- RI income tax: 3.75% to 5.99%

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free registration
- POW: Free plates
- Purple Heart: Free plates

### Education Benefits

**Tuition Waivers**
- Children of deceased veterans: Free tuition at state schools
- Children of POW/MIA: Tuition waiver

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- URI: Veteran services
- CCRI: Military-friendly
- RIC: Veteran support

### Employment Benefits

**Veterans Preference**
- 5 points for veterans
- 10 points for disabled veterans
- Applies to state positions

### Healthcare Benefits

**Rhode Island Veterans Home (Bristol)**
- 240 beds
- Skilled nursing and domiciliary

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Rhode Island tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Rhode Island income tax.

**Q: What is Naval Station Newport?**
A: Home of Naval War College, Officer Candidate School, and Navy training. Major employer in Newport area.

**Q: Can I commute to Boston?**
A: Yes. Providence is about 50 minutes from Boston by car or commuter rail.
