# Idaho Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Idaho—the Gem State—has emerged as one of America's fastest-growing states and increasingly popular destination for veterans. With complete exemption of military retirement pay, affordable cost of living (though rising in popular areas), stunning natural beauty, and a friendly community atmosphere, Idaho offers veterans a high quality of life. The state's conservative values and outdoor recreation opportunities particularly appeal to many transitioning service members.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 120,000 veterans (8.2% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, property tax reduction for disabled veterans, strong veterans preference, excellent outdoor recreation
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. Outstanding outdoor recreation and quality of life
  3. Affordable cost of living (outside Boise metro)
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. Limited VA healthcare facilities (especially rural areas)
  2. Boise area housing costs rising rapidly
  3. Fewer job opportunities in some sectors
- **Best cities for veterans**: Boise, Meridian, Nampa, Idaho Falls, Coeur d'Alene

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans: Property tax reduction program (income-based)
- 100% disabled veterans: Up to $1,500 property tax reduction
- Circuit breaker program: Assists with property tax relief
- Surviving spouses: May continue benefits
- Must apply through county assessor

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Idaho income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed
- Idaho income tax rate: 5.8% flat rate

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Free or reduced registration
- Purple Heart: Free specialty plates
- POW: Free registration and plates
- Medal of Honor: Free
- Various military plates available

**Hunting and Fishing Licenses**
- Disabled veterans (40%+): Free combination license
- Idaho resident veterans: Discounted licenses
- Disabled American Veteran license: $1.75

**State Park Benefits**
- Idaho State Parks annual pass discount for veterans
- Camping discounts for disabled veterans

### Education Benefits

**Idaho War Veteran Scholarship**
- For Idaho residents who served in combat zones
- Awards up to $500/semester at Idaho colleges
- Must apply through Division of Veterans Services

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition upon enrollment
- Active duty and families: In-state rates
- Choice Act compliant

**Public University Programs**
- Boise State University: Veterans Services
- University of Idaho: Student Veterans Center
- Idaho State University: Veterans Services

**Vocational Training**
- College of Western Idaho
- Idaho State University technical programs
- Apprenticeship programs

### Employment Benefits

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Idaho tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Idaho state income tax.

**Q: What property tax relief can I get?**
A: Disabled veterans may qualify for property tax reduction up to $1,500 based on income and disability status.

**Q: How do I get the veteran designation on my license?**
A: Bring DD-214 to any DMV office. "VETERAN" printed on license at no charge.

**Q: Is Boise expensive?**
A: Increasingly yes. Housing costs have risen significantly. Canyon County (Nampa/Caldwell) is more affordable.

**Q: What VA facilities are in Idaho?**
A: Boise VA Medical Center plus clinics throughout state. Some specialty care may require travel to Salt Lake City or Spokane.

**Q: Is Idaho good for outdoor recreation?**
A: Exceptional. World-class skiing, fishing, hunting, hiking, and whitewater. One of the best states for outdoor enthusiasts.

**Q: How cold are the winters?**
A: Varies by location. Boise moderate (20s-30s). Northern Idaho and mountains much colder with more snow.

**Q: Can my military experience count for licensing?**
A: Yes. Idaho credits military training toward occupational licensing requirements.

**Q: What about the job market?**
A: Growing, especially in Boise metro. Technology, healthcare, and energy sectors strongest.

**Q: Is Idaho growing too fast?**
A: Boise area has grown rapidly, causing housing cost increases and some infrastructure strain. Other areas still affordable and less crowded.

**Q: How does Idaho compare to Montana?**
A: Similar outdoor lifestyle. Idaho slightly lower cost of living. Both exempt military retirement. Montana has no sales tax; Idaho does (6%).

**Q: Are there state veterans homes?**
A: Yes, four facilities: Boise, Pocatello, Lewiston, and Post Falls (newest).

**Q: What's the veteran community like?**
A: Active and supportive. Strong VSO presence. Mountain Home AFB creates local military community.

**Q: Can I get cheap hunting/fishing licenses?**
A: Yes. Disabled veterans (40%+) get free combination license. All veterans get discounted licenses.

**Q: Is Idaho good for families?**
A: Yes, generally. Good schools in many areas, low crime, outdoor activities, and family-friendly communities.

## Comparison to Other States

**vs. Montana**: Similar outdoor lifestyle and values. Idaho slightly lower cost of living. Montana has no sales tax. Idaho has more diverse economy (technology in Boise).

**vs. Utah**: Both growing rapidly. Utah has more religious influence (LDS). Idaho slightly lower cost of living. Both exempt military retirement.

**vs. Wyoming**: Wyoming has no state income tax. Idaho has more job opportunities. Similar outdoor recreation. Wyoming more rural.

**vs. Oregon**: Oregon has no sales tax. Idaho exempts military retirement (Oregon doesn't fully). Oregon has coast. Idaho more affordable.

**vs. Washington**: Washington has no income tax. Higher cost of living. More urban. Idaho more rural and conservative. Both have great outdoor recreation.
