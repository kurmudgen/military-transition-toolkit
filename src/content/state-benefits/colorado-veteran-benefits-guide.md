# Colorado Veteran Benefits: Complete Guide

Colorado has become one of America's premier destinations for veterans, offering a perfect blend of outdoor adventure, strong job market, and meaningful veteran benefits. The Centennial State exempts military retirement pay from state taxes and hosts major military installations including NORAD, Space Command, and multiple Army and Air Force bases. While cost of living has risen sharply in recent years, Colorado remains highly attractive for veterans seeking quality of life.

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total veteran population**: Approximately 380,000 veterans (8.5% of adult population)
- **Key state benefits**: Full military retirement tax exemption, property tax exemptions for disabled veterans, robust veterans preference, outdoor recreation passes
- **Top 3 reasons veterans move here**:
  1. No state tax on military retirement pay
  2. Outdoor recreation and quality of life
  3. Strong defense and aerospace job market
- **Top 3 challenges for veterans here**:
  1. High and rising cost of living (especially housing)
  2. Competitive job market
  3. Altitude adjustment can be challenging
- **Best cities for veterans**: Colorado Springs, Aurora/Denver Metro, Fort Collins, Pueblo, Grand Junction

## State Veteran Benefits Overview

### Financial Benefits

**Property Tax Exemptions**
- Disabled veterans (100%): 50% of first $200,000 exempt
- Surviving spouses of disabled veterans: Same exemption
- Veterans 65+: Additional homestead exemption available
- Must apply through county assessor

**Income Tax Benefits**
- Military retirement pay: 100% exempt from Colorado income tax
- VA disability compensation: Not taxed
- SBP/DIC payments: Not taxed at state level
- Social Security: Not taxed for most

**Vehicle Registration Benefits**
- Disabled veterans: Reduced fees
- POW: Free registration
- Purple Heart: Free plates
- Medal of Honor: Free plates and registration
- Various military plates available

**Hunting and Fishing Licenses**
- Disabled veterans (60%+): Free small game, fishing license
- 100% disabled veterans: Additional hunting permits discounted
- Military appreciation licenses and events

**State Park Benefits**
- Veterans with VA rating: Free Centennial Pass for state parks
- Disabled veterans: Additional camping discounts
- Free Centennial Pass for military family members during deployments

### Education Benefits

**State-Funded Tuition Programs**
- No state-specific veteran tuition waiver (use federal GI Bill)
- National Guard tuition assistance
- Dependents of deceased/disabled veterans may qualify for assistance

**In-State Tuition Rules**
- Veterans eligible for in-state tuition immediately

**Public University Programs**

## How to Access State Benefits

### Step 1: Establish Residency

- Obtain Colorado driver's license (within 90 days)
- Register to vote
- Register vehicles
- Establish domicile intent
- Keep residency documentation

### Step 2: Register with State DVA

- Visit colorado.gov/dmva
- Contact county veterans service officer
- Bring DD-214 and VA disability documentation
- Request veteran driver's license designation

### Step 3: Apply for Benefits

- Property tax exemption: County assessor before July 15
- State parks pass: At park offices with VA documentation
- Vehicle registration: DMV with DD-214
- Veterans Trust Fund: Through state DVA

### Step 4: Get Connected

- Join local VFW or American Legion post
- Register with Colorado Workforce Center
- Connect with base transition services
- Attend veteran job fairs and events

## Common Questions

**Q: Does Colorado tax military retirement pay?**
A: No. Military retirement pay is 100% exempt from Colorado state income tax.

**Q: What property tax exemption can I get?**
A: 100% disabled veterans can exempt 50% of the first $200,000 of property value. Other programs exist for seniors.

**Q: How do I get a free state parks pass?**
A: Veterans with any VA disability rating can get a free Centennial Pass. Apply at any state park with VA documentation.

**Q: How do I get the veteran designation on my license?**
A: Bring DD-214 to any DMV office. "VETERAN" is printed on your license at no extra charge.

**Q: Which Colorado city is best for defense jobs?**
A: Colorado Springs by far. It's home to NORAD, Space Command, Fort Carson, and multiple Space Force installations.

**Q: Is Colorado Springs expensive?**
A: Less expensive than Denver but still above national average. More affordable than other Colorado cities while having the most defense jobs.

**Q: What about altitude sickness?**
A: Common concern. Denver is 5,280 feet; Colorado Springs 6,035 feet. Most people adjust in days to weeks. Stay hydrated and take it easy at first.

**Q: Are there state veterans cemeteries?**
A: Veterans Memorial Cemetery of Western Colorado (Grand Junction) is state-operated. Fort Logan and Fort Lyon are national cemeteries.

**Q: How does Colorado compare to Texas for veterans?**
A: Both exempt military retirement from taxes. Colorado has better outdoor recreation but higher cost of living. Texas has more defense jobs overall but Colorado Springs rivals any Texas city for military/space jobs.

**Q: Is Denver good for veterans?**
A: Yes for job market and urban amenities. Higher cost of living than Colorado Springs. Less military-focused but strong aerospace/tech sector.

**Q: Can I use military experience for licensing?**
A: Yes, Colorado credits military training toward occupational licensing and expedites the process for veterans.

**Q: What VA facilities are in Colorado?**
A: Rocky Mountain Regional VA Medical Center (Aurora) is the primary facilityâ€”state-of-the-art opened in 2018. Plus numerous clinics statewide.

**Q: Is Colorado good for families?**
A: Yes, excellent schools in many areas, strong outdoor culture, good communities. Housing costs are the main challenge.

**Q: What's the veteran community like?**
A: Very strong, especially in Colorado Springs. Large military and veteran population creates excellent networks.

**Q: Are there veterans preference points for state jobs?**
A: Yes, 5 points for veterans and 10 points for disabled veterans on state classified positions.

## Comparison to Other States

**vs. Texas**: Both exempt military retirement pay. Texas has lower cost of living and no state income tax at all. Colorado has better outdoor recreation. Similar defense job opportunities.

**vs. Arizona**: Arizona has similar climate (drier, hotter). Colorado has mountains, Arizona has desert. Both have strong veteran benefits. Arizona lower cost of living.

**vs. Utah**: Similar outdoor recreation and conservative culture. Utah slightly lower cost of living. Colorado has larger defense sector. Both have strong veteran communities.

**vs. Washington**: Washington has no state income tax. Similar mountainous terrain. Washington has more rain. Colorado has more sunshine. Both have aerospace industry.

**vs. California**: California has more VA facilities but much higher cost of living. California taxes military retirement; Colorado doesn't. California has beaches; Colorado has mountains.
