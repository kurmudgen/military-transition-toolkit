- **Job outlook:** 32% growth through 2032 (much faster than average)

## What Is CompTIA Security+ Certification?

CompTIA Security+ is the most widely recognized entry-level cybersecurity certification in the world. It validates foundational skills in threat assessment, risk management, incident response, cryptography, and network security—everything needed to secure an organization's systems and data.

Most importantly for veterans: **Security+ is DoD 8570/8140 approved** for Information Assurance Technical (IAT) Level II and Information Assurance Management (IAM) Level I positions. This means it's required or preferred for most government cybersecurity jobs.

## Why Veterans Excel in Cybersecurity

Military training creates natural cybersecurity professionals:

**Security clearance:** You likely already have one. Cleared cybersecurity professionals are in massive demand and short supply.

**Operational security (OPSEC):** You've lived security protocols. Understanding why security matters is half the battle.

**Following procedures:** Cybersecurity requires strict adherence to policies and procedures—exactly what military training instills.

**Handling classified information:** You know how to protect sensitive data. The principles translate directly.

**Mission-critical mindset:** When a system is compromised, the response requires the same urgency and discipline you developed in the military.

**Adversarial thinking:** Understanding how the enemy operates helps you defend against cyber threats.

## Certification Requirements

### Education Prerequisites
- **Minimum education:** None required
- **Recommended:** A+ and Network+ or 2 years IT/security experience
- **GI Bill eligible:** Yes, for training programs

### Experience Requirements
- **Official recommendation:** 2 years IT administration with security focus
- **Reality:** Many pass with dedicated study and military background
- **Military equivalent:** Any security-related MOS, or IT MOS with security duties

### Exam Requirements
- **Number of exams:** 1 (SY0-701)
- **Questions:** Maximum 90 questions
- **Time limit:** 90 minutes
- **Passing score:** 750/900 (83%)
- **Question types:** Multiple choice, drag-and-drop, performance-based
- **Renewal:** Every 3 years

## Common Mistakes Veterans Make

1. **Underestimating the exam:** Security+ has an 83% passing score requirement—higher than A+ or Network+
2. **Skipping cryptography study:** It's confusing but essential—don't avoid it
3. **Memorizing without understanding:** The exam tests concepts, not just facts
4. **Ignoring performance-based questions:** These require hands-on security skills

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Security+ worth it in 2025?**
A: Absolutely. It's the most requested entry-level security certification and is required for most government security jobs (DoD 8570/8140).

**Q: Should I get Security+ or CEH first?**
A: Security+ first. It covers broader fundamentals and is more recognized for entry-level positions. CEH is more specialized (ethical hacking).

**Q: Can I pass Security+ with no IT experience?**
A: Yes, but it's harder. Plan for 3-4 months of dedicated study. Military technical training helps even if it wasn't IT-specific.

**Q: Does my clearance matter for Security+ jobs?**
A: Enormously. Cleared Security+ holders can earn $20,000-$50,000 more than non-cleared equivalents. Don't let your clearance lapse!

**Q: Security+ vs CISSP—which is better?**
A: Different levels. Security+ is entry-level; CISSP requires 5 years experience and is for senior roles. Start with Security+.

**Q: Is Security+ enough to get a cybersecurity job?**
A: For entry-level SOC analyst and similar roles, yes—especially with military background and clearance. Combine with hands-on experience (TryHackMe, home lab) for best results.

**Q: How hard is the Security+ exam?**
A: Harder than A+ and Network+. The 83% passing score is challenging. Performance-based questions require real understanding.

**Q: What's the difference between SY0-601 and SY0-701?**
A: SY0-701 is the current version (released November 2023). It covers newer topics like zero trust, cloud security, and updated threats. Take the current version.

**Q: Can I use GI Bill for Security+?**
A: GI Bill covers training programs, not standalone exams. Enroll in an approved cybersecurity program that includes Security+.

**Q: Should I get Security+ before or after leaving the military?**
A: Before if possible. Use SkillBridge, Onward to Opportunity, or TAP resources while still active duty.

**Q: What's DoD 8570/8140?**
A: DoD directive requiring specific certifications for information assurance jobs. Security+ qualifies you for IAT Level II positions—the most common entry point.

**Q: Is Security+ recognized outside the US?**
A: Yes, Security+ is globally recognized. It's particularly valued in countries with US military presence or defense partnerships.

**Q: How long does Security+ stay valid?**
A: 3 years. Renew through CEUs, higher-level certs, or other approved activities.

**Q: What home lab should I build for Security+?**
A: Start with virtual machines: Kali Linux, Windows 10, and a vulnerable target (DVWA, Metasploitable). TryHackMe is easier for beginners.

**Q: Are there Security+ jobs with no degree?**
A: Many. Government contractor positions especially care more about clearance and certification than degrees. Entry-level SOC roles often don't require degrees.

## Continuing Education

**Next certifications after Security+:**
1. **CompTIA CySA+** - Security analysis focus, natural progression
2. **CompTIA PenTest+** - If interested in offensive security
3. **CASP+** - Advanced Security+ for those not ready for CISSP
4. **CISSP** - After 5 years experience, the gold standard
5. **Cloud security certs (AWS Security, AZ-500)** - High demand area

**Typical career progression:**
Security+ → CySA+/PenTest+ → CISSP or specialized certs → CISO/Security Architect
