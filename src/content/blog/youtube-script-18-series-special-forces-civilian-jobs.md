**Context:** Your SF experience, language skills, and clearance make you one of the most employable veterans in high-paying sectors.

---

[MAIN CONTENT]

**Section 1: Market Value (60 seconds)**

**Entry contractor:** $95,000-$130,000/year
**Experienced contractor:** $130,000-$170,000/year
**Top-tier positions:** $170,000-$197,000+/year

Average SF contractor: $112,746/year. Top 10%: $197,385/year. With clearance and deployment experience, you're in high demand.

**Your advantages:** Combat experience, language skills, cultural expertise, leadership, TS/SCI clearance, weapons expertise, medical training (18D), communications (18E), engineering (18C).

**Section 2: Defense Contractors (Highest Pay) (120 seconds)**

**Overseas Security Contracting:** Personal security detail (PSD), site security, training missions. $120,000-$200,000/year depending on location/risk.

**Triple Canopy, Academi, Constellis:** PSD team members $140K-$180K. Team leaders $160K-$200K+. Requires deployments.

**Training Contractor:** Train foreign military (FID missions). $110,000-$160,000/year. Leverages your SF training background.

**Intelligence Support:** Contractor supporting CIA, DIA, JSOC. $130,000-$180,000/year. Often overseas rotations. TS/SCI required.

**Program Management:** Senior roles managing contracts. $150,000-$220,000/year. Requires 10+ years experience.

**Top companies:** CACI, Booz Allen Hamilton, Leidos, SAIC, L3Harris, DynCorp, SOC.

**Reality check:** High pay = deployments, danger, family separation. But if you're single or can handle separation, money is excellent.

**Section 3: Federal Law Enforcement (Stability Path) (90 seconds)**

**FBI Hostage Rescue Team (HRT):** Elite tactical. $73,000-$130,000/year. Must start as FBI special agent first (2-3 years).

**US Marshals SOG:** Special Operations Group. $80,000-$120,000/year. Fugitive apprehension, tactical operations.

**DEA Foreign-deployed Advisory & Support Team (FAST):** Counter-narcotics abroad. $85,000-$130,000/year.

**Secret Service CAT (Counter Assault Team):** Presidential protection. $75,000-$120,000/year.

**Diplomatic Security (State Dept):** Protect ambassadors overseas. $75,000-$120,000/year + overseas pay.

**Air Marshals:** Federal flight security. $50,000-$95,000/year. Extensive travel.

**Why federal:** Lower pay than contracting BUT pension after 20-25 years, stability, less risky, strong benefits.

**Section 4: Executive Protection (Corporate) (90 seconds)**

**High-Net-Worth Executive Protection:** Protect CEOs, celebrities, dignitaries. $85,000-$150,000/year salaried. $500-$2,000/day contract.

**Top EP companies:** Gavin de Becker & Associates, AS Solution, Global Guardian, Pinkerton. Require SF/SMU background for high-threat details.

**Corporate Security Director:** Fortune 500 security leadership. $120,000-$180,000/year. Strategic role, less operational.

**Residential Security Manager:** Ultra-high-net-worth families. $90,000-$140,000/year + housing sometimes. Live on estate.

**Travel Security Specialist:** Accompany clients worldwide. $80,000-$130,000/year + travel covered. See the world.

**Requirements:** Professional appearance, discretion, driving skills, medical training, clean record. Your SF background = top-tier candidate.

**Section 5: Training & Consulting (60 seconds)**

**Tactical Training Instructor:** Teach civilians, LEOs, security professionals. $70,000-$120,000/year. Companies like Asymmetric Solutions, Trident Concepts.

**Firearms Instructor:** Shooting schools, executive protection training. $60,000-$100,000/year. Some self-employed make more.

**Security Consulting:** Advise corporations, governments on security. $100,000-$200,000+/year. Requires reputation + network.

**Leadership Consulting:** Corporate leadership training leveraging SF experience. $90,000-$180,000/year. Growing market.

**Section 6: Intelligence Community (90 seconds)**

**CIA Paramilitary Operations Officer (SAC/SOG):** Covert operations. Salary classified but estimated $90,000-$150,000+ base. SF backgrounds heavily recruited.

**DIA Defense Clandestine Service:** Similar to CIA. $85,000-$140,000/year.

**NSA/CSS:** Signals intelligence operations. $80,000-$130,000/year.

**Contracting for IC:** Support CIA/DIA as contractor. $120,000-$180,000/year. Overseas rotations common.

**All-Source Analyst:** Leverage regional expertise. $90,000-$140,000/year. Your cultural knowledge valuable.

**Requirements:** TS/SCI (you likely have), poly required (new for most), extensive vetting, relocation often required.

**Section 7: Alternative Paths (60 seconds)**

**Foreign Internal Defense (FID) Contractor:** Train partner nations. $110,000-$160,000/year. Your SF bread-and-butter mission.

**Maritime Security:** Anti-piracy, vessel protection. $90,000-$140,000/year. Unique environment.

**Private Intelligence:** Corporate intel firms (Stratfor, etc). $85,000-$140,000/year. Research, analysis, forecasting.

**Non-Profit/NGO Security:** Protect humanitarian workers in conflict zones. $60,000-$95,000/year. Lower pay but mission-driven.

**Academia:** Teaching special operations at military schools. $75,000-$110,000/year. More stable lifestyle.

[CONCLUSION - 30 seconds]

**Summary:** SF has highest civilian earning potential of any MOS.

**Highest pay:** Overseas security contracting, $150K-$200K+, but deployments required
**Best stability:** Federal LEO (FBI HRT, Marshals SOG), $75K-$130K + pension
**Best lifestyle:** Executive protection, $85K-$150K, stay CONUS

**Critical:** Maintain TS/SCI clearance. Language skills = $10K-$30K premium. Deployable = higher pay.

**Next step:** Decide deployment tolerance. If yes → contractor route. If no → federal LEO or executive protection. Apply to multiple paths.
