[INTRO]
Staff Sergeant Marcus Williams, former Infantry Squad Leader with 3 combat deployments, became a police detective earning $98K within 5 years of leaving the Army. Military leadership, tactical skills, and decision-making under pressure made him exceptional law enforcement candidate.

---

[MAIN CONTENT]

**Military:** 11B Infantry Squad Leader, 8 years, 3 deployments Iraq/Afghanistan, led 9-person squad in combat operations, Bronze Star.

**Transition:** Used SkillBridge for 6-month police academy internship. Hired as patrol officer immediately after separation ($65K starting). Promoted to detective after 4 years ($98K).

**Path:** Patrol officer → Training officer → Detective (investigations unit). Specializes in violent crimes, robbery, homicide assist.

**Keys:** Military leadership highly valued, tactical training directly applicable, veterans fast-tracked through academy, strong performance led to rapid promotion.

**Advice:** Target large departments with veteran hiring programs. Use SkillBridge for academy. Military leadership experience qualifies you for accelerated promotion track. Consider federal law enforcement (FBI, DEA, ATF) for higher pay.

[CONCLUSION]
Marcus went from Infantry to $98K detective in 5 years. Military experience gave him massive advantage in law enforcement career progression.
