# VA Burial Benefits: Complete Guide to Veteran Death Benefits

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **National cemetery burial:** Free for eligible veterans (grave, opening/closing, headstone, perpetual care)
- **Burial allowance:** Up to $948 (service-connected) or $231 (non-service-connected) for burial/funeral costs
- **Headstones/markers:** Free for any eligible veteran, any cemetery
- **Presidential Memorial Certificate:** Free commemorative certificate
- **Insider tip:** Pre-plan and pre-register at a national cemetery to ease family burden

## VA Burial Benefits Overview

VA provides several death and burial benefits for veterans:
1. Burial in a VA national cemetery
2. Burial allowances (reimbursement for expenses)
3. Headstones, markers, and medallions
4. Burial flags
5. Presidential Memorial Certificates
6. Military funeral honors

**These benefits recognize service and help families during difficult times.**

## National Cemetery Burial

### What's Provided Free

**At no cost to family:**
- Gravesite in a national cemetery
- Opening and closing of the grave
- Government headstone or marker
- U.S. burial flag
- Perpetual care of the gravesite

### Who's Eligible

**Veterans:**
- Discharged under conditions other than dishonorable
- Minimum active duty service (24 months after 9/7/1980)
- Guard/Reserve with 20+ years creditable service

**Family members (in same gravesite):**
- Spouse (including surviving spouse who remarried after 57)
- Minor children
- Unmarried adult children with disabilities

### VA National Cemeteries

**155 national cemeteries across the U.S.:**
- Most states have at least one
- Arlington (special eligibility requirements)
- Various regional locations

**To find nearest:**
- cem.va.gov
- Search by state or zip code

### How to Arrange

**At time of death:**
1. Contact funeral home
2. Request burial at national cemetery
3. Provide DD-214 and death certificate
4. Funeral home coordinates with cemetery

**Pre-planning:**
1. Contact cemetery directly
2. Verify eligibility
3. Complete pre-registration
4. Keep records with important documents

## Burial Allowances

### Service-Connected Death

**If veteran died from service-connected disability:**
- Burial allowance: Up to $2,000
- No date limit for burial expense claims

### Non-Service-Connected Death

**If veteran died from non-service-connected cause:**
- Burial allowance: $948 (if hospitalized by VA at death)
- Burial allowance: $231 (all other non-service-connected)
- Plot allowance: $948 (if not buried in national cemetery)

### Who Receives the Allowance

**Payment goes to:**
- Person who paid burial expenses
- Usually surviving spouse or family
- Can be funeral home (if authorized)

### How to Apply

**VA Form 21P-530EZ:**
- Application for Burial Benefits
- Submit with:
  - Death certificate
  - DD-214
  - Receipts for burial expenses
  - Proof of payment

**Deadline:**
- Generally 2 years from burial
- No deadline for service-connected deaths

## Headstones, Markers, and Medallions

### Government Headstones/Markers

**Available free for:**
- Veterans buried in any cemetery (national, state, private)
- Unmarked graves of eligible veterans

**Types available:**
- Upright marble or granite headstone
- Flat bronze, granite, or marble marker
- Niche covers for columbaria

**Inscription includes:**
- Name
- Branch of service
- Birth and death dates
- War period
- Optional emblems of belief

### Memorial Markers

**For veterans whose remains are:**
- Not recovered
- Buried at sea
- Donated to science
- Cremated and scattered

**Memorial marker placed in cemetery to honor service.**

### Medallions

**For veterans buried in private cemeteries:**
- If family already has private headstone
- Medallion affixed to existing marker
- Shows veteran status

### How to Order

**VA Form 40-1330:**
- Application for headstone/marker
- Submit with DD-214
- No cost for headstone/marker itself

**Setting fee:**
- VA doesn't cover setting costs for private cemeteries
- National cemeteries include setting
- Some states cover setting costs

## Burial Flags

### What's Provided

**Free U.S. burial flag:**
- Same size displayed on casket
- Presented to next of kin
- Can be requested at funeral

### Who's Eligible

**Veterans who served during:**
- Wartime
- After January 31, 1955
- In peacetime and completed required service

**Also eligible:**
- Certain Guard/Reserve members
- Those who died on active duty

### How to Obtain

**Request through:**
- Funeral home (most common)
- VA regional office
- U.S. Post Office (with DD-214)

**VA Form 27-2008:**
- Application for Flag for Burial Purposes

## Presidential Memorial Certificates

### What It Is

**Engraved certificate:**
- Signed by current President
- Expresses nation's grateful recognition
- Suitable for framing

### Who Can Request

**Available to:**
- Surviving family members
- Friends of the veteran
- Veterans' organizations

**Can request multiple copies.**

### How to Request

**Apply online:**
- VA.gov/burials-memorials/memorial-items/presidential-memorial-certificates

**Or by mail:**
- Include DD-214
- Veteran's full name
- Dates of service
- Requestor's information

## Military Funeral Honors

### What's Required by Law

**Minimum requirements (all eligible veterans):**
- Folding and presentation of U.S. flag
- Playing of Taps (live or recorded)
- At least two uniformed military members

### Enhanced Honors

**May include (based on availability):**
- Rifle salute (3 volleys)
- Bugler playing Taps
- Color guard
- Additional military personnel
- Flyover (rare, special circumstances)

### How to Arrange

**Request through:**
- Funeral home
- Military installation casualty assistance
- Reserve/Guard unit
- Veterans' service organizations

**DOD contact:**
- 1-877-MILHONN (645-4666)

## State Veterans Cemeteries

### What They Offer

**Similar to national cemeteries:**
- Free or low-cost burial for veterans
- Often closer to home
- May have additional benefits

### Eligibility

**Usually:**
- State residence requirement
- Same veteran eligibility as national
- Family member burial allowed

### Finding State Cemeteries

**Contact:**
- State veterans affairs department
- Many states operate multiple cemeteries
- Some offer cremation niches

## Private Cemetery Burial

### VA Benefits Still Available

**Even with private burial:**
- Headstone/marker (free)
- Medallion (free)
- Burial flag (free)
- Burial allowance (if eligible)
- Presidential Memorial Certificate

### What's NOT Covered

**Family pays for:**
- Gravesite purchase
- Opening/closing of grave
- Setting headstone/marker
- Perpetual care

## Pre-Planning Benefits

### Why Pre-Plan

**Advantages:**
- Ensures wishes are known
- Reduces family burden
- Verifies eligibility in advance
- Locks in cemetery choice

### How to Pre-Register

**National cemetery pre-registration:**
1. Contact chosen cemetery
2. Provide DD-214
3. Complete eligibility verification
4. Receive confirmation
5. Inform family of arrangements

### Documents to Organize

**Keep together:**
- DD-214 (multiple certified copies)
- VA eligibility letter
- Cemetery pre-registration
- Funeral wishes
- Insurance information

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is burial at a national cemetery really free?**
A: Yesâ€”gravesite, opening/closing, headstone, and perpetual care at no cost.

**Q: Can my spouse be buried with me?**
A: Yes. Eligible dependents can be buried in the same gravesite.

**Q: What if I have a dishonorable discharge?**
A: Generally not eligible. Some exceptions exist; consult VA.

**Q: Can I be buried at Arlington?**
A: Special eligibility requirements apply. Most veterans qualify for other national cemeteries.

**Q: Do I need a certain length of service?**
A: Generally 24 months minimum for those entering after 9/7/1980, with exceptions.

**Q: What about cremation?**
A: Cremated remains can be buried or placed in columbarium at national cemeteries.

**Q: Can I choose my headstone inscription?**
A: Yes, within VA guidelines. Emblems of belief, optional text.

**Q: How do I prove my veteran status?**
A: DD-214 is primary document. VA can sometimes verify without it.

**Q: Can veterans' organizations help?**
A: Yes. VSOs often assist families with burial arrangements and benefits.

**Q: Is there a waiting period?**
A: No. Benefits available immediately upon veteran's death.

## Timeline for Survivors

### Immediately After Death

**Day of/Next day:**
- Contact funeral home
- Request military honors
- Notify VA (if receiving benefits)

**Within first week:**
- Obtain death certificates (10+ copies)
- Locate DD-214
- Apply for burial allowance
- Request headstone/marker

### First Month

**Complete:**
- Burial benefit applications
- DIC application (if applicable)
- Survivor benefit notifications
- Cancel VA healthcare (if applicable)

### Ongoing

**Later tasks:**
- Request Presidential Memorial Certificate
- Update records
- Ensure grave marker placed
- Apply for survivor benefits

## Survivor Benefits (Beyond Burial)

### DIC (Dependency and Indemnity Compensation)

**If death was service-connected:**
- Monthly payment to surviving spouse
- $1,612.75/month (2024)
- See DIC guide for details

### Survivor's Pension

**For wartime veterans' survivors:**
- Income-based benefit
- For low-income surviving spouses
- Different from DIC

### ChampVA

**Healthcare for survivors:**
- Surviving spouse/dependents
- If veteran was 100% P&T
- Or if receiving DIC

## Real Examples

### Example 1: National Cemetery Burial

**Situation:** Army veteran, 20 years service, died of natural causes

**Benefits received:**
- Burial at nearest national cemetery (free)
- Government headstone (free)
- Burial flag (free)
- Military honors (flag folding, Taps)
- Non-service-connected burial allowance: $948
- Presidential Memorial Certificate

**Family cost:** $0 for burial, allowance covered some funeral expenses

### Example 2: Private Cemetery with VA Benefits

**Situation:** Navy veteran, wife wanted local cemetery burial

**Benefits received:**
- VA headstone (free, set at family expense)
- Burial flag
- Burial allowance: $231 + $948 plot allowance
- Presidential Memorial Certificate

**Family cost:** Gravesite, opening/closing, setting headstone, funeral

### Example 3: Service-Connected Death

**Situation:** Marine veteran died from service-connected cancer

**Benefits received:**
- National cemetery burial (free)
- Burial allowance: $2,000
- Surviving spouse DIC: $1,612.75/month
- ChampVA for spouse

**Result:** Significant ongoing support for survivor
