# VA Adapted Housing Grants (SAH/SHA/HISA): Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **What it is:** Grants to modify or build accessible homes for severely disabled veterans
- **SAH Grant:** Up to $117,014 (2024) for major modifications or new construction
- **SHA Grant:** Up to $23,444 (2024) for smaller modifications
- **HISA Grant:** Up to $8,605 (2024) for home improvements for any disability
- **Key benefit:** You don't repay theseâ€”they're GRANTS, not loans
- **Insider tip:** Grants can be used multiple times up to maximum lifetime amount

## Understanding the Three Grant Programs

### Specially Adapted Housing (SAH) Grant

**For veterans with severe disabilities:**
- Loss or loss of use of both legs
- Loss or loss of use of both arms
- Blindness plus loss/loss of use of one leg
- Certain severe burns
- Loss or loss of use of one lower extremity + organic disease/injury
- ALS (Lou Gehrig's disease)

**Maximum grant (2024): $117,014**

**Uses:**
- Build new adapted home
- Modify existing home
- Purchase adapted home
- Pay down mortgage on adapted home

### Special Housing Adaptation (SHA) Grant

**For veterans with specific disabilities:**
- Blindness in both eyes (5/200 or less)
- Loss or loss of use of both hands
- Certain severe burns
- Respiratory injury requiring daily breathing assistance

**Maximum grant (2024): $23,444**

**Uses:**
- Adapt existing home
- Purchase adapted home
- Help with home modifications

### Home Improvements and Structural Alterations (HISA)

**For veterans enrolled in VA healthcare:**
- Any disability that requires home modifications
- Doesn't require the same severity as SAH/SHA

**Maximum grant:**
- Service-connected disability: $8,605 (lifetime)
- Non-service-connected disability: $2,000 (lifetime)

**Uses:**
- Grab bars
- Ramps
- Widened doorways
- Accessible bathroom modifications
- Other structural changes

## Eligibility Requirements

### SAH Grant Eligibility

**You qualify with service-connected:**
1. Loss or loss of use of BOTH lower extremities (requiring wheelchair, braces, crutches)
2. Loss or loss of use of BOTH upper extremities at or above the elbow
3. Blindness in both eyes (5/200 or less) PLUS loss or loss of use of one lower extremity
4. Loss or loss of use of one lower extremity + residuals of organic disease affecting balance/movement requiring wheelchair/braces
5. Severe burn injuries (deep partial-thickness or worse) on:
   - Both feet OR
   - One or both feet and one or both hands OR
   - One or both lower extremities or one or both hands which impairs mobility
6. ALS diagnosis (regardless of rating)

### SHA Grant Eligibility

**You qualify with service-connected:**
1. Blindness in both eyes (5/200 or less visual acuity)
2. Anatomical loss or loss of use of both hands
3. Severe burn injuries (as specified in regulations)
4. Respiratory injury requiring mechanical device for breathing

### HISA Eligibility

**You qualify if:**
- Enrolled in VA healthcare
- Have a disability (service-connected or not)
- Need home modifications to function
- Doctor certifies medical necessity

## Grant Amounts and Limits

### 2024 Grant Maximums

| Grant Type | Maximum Amount |
|------------|----------------|
| SAH | $117,014 |
| SHA | $23,444 |
| HISA (service-connected) | $8,605 |
| HISA (non-service-connected) | $2,000 |

### Lifetime Maximums

**SAH:**
- Can use up to 3 times total
- Combined cannot exceed maximum
- Unused portion available for future use

**SHA:**
- Can use up to 3 times total
- Combined cannot exceed maximum

**HISA:**
- One-time lifetime benefit
- Once used, no additional HISA funds available

### Annual Adjustments

Grant amounts adjust annually based on cost-of-construction index. Check VA.gov for current year amounts.

## How to Apply

### SAH/SHA Application Process

#### Step 1: Verify Eligibility

**Confirm you have:**
- Service-connected disability qualifying for SAH or SHA
- VA disability rating documentation
- Intent to occupy the home

#### Step 2: Apply Online or by Mail

**VA Form 26-4555:**
- Application for Specially Adapted Housing
- Available at VA.gov or from SAH agent

**Submit to:**
- VA Regional Loan Center
- Or your nearest VA SAH agent

#### Step 3: Work with SAH Agent

**VA assigns SAH agent who:**
- Reviews your application
- Determines eligibility
- Helps plan modifications
- Reviews construction plans
- Inspects completed work

#### Step 4: Submit Plans

**For new construction or major modifications:**
- Architectural plans required
- Must meet accessibility standards
- VA reviews and approves

#### Step 5: Construction/Modification

**During construction:**
- VA inspects at various stages
- Contractor paid in installments
- Must meet VA standards

#### Step 6: Final Inspection

**VA verifies:**
- Work completed as planned
- Meets accessibility standards
- Final payment released

### HISA Application Process

**Simpler process through VA healthcare:**

1. **Talk to VA provider** about needed modifications
2. **Get medical documentation** of necessity
3. **Apply through VA healthcare facility** prosthetics/sensory aids service
4. **VA arranges for work** or reimburses you

## Using Grants with VA Home Loan

### Combination Strategies

**SAH/SHA + VA loan:**
- Use grant for adaptations
- VA loan for home purchase
- No double-dipping (grant reduces purchase costs)

**Example:**
- Home purchase: $400,000
- SAH grant for modifications: $50,000
- VA loan needed: $400,000 + modifications built in
- Grant covers adaptation costs

### New Construction

**Build adapted home:**
- VA construction loan
- SAH grant covers accessibility features
- Results in wheelchair-accessible home

### Existing Home

**Modify current home:**
- Already own home (VA loan or other)
- SAH/SHA grant covers modifications
- Don't need to refinance

## Temporary Residence Adaptation (TRA) Grants

### What It Is

**For veterans living in family member's home:**
- Same disabilities as SAH/SHA
- Family member owns the home
- Veteran plans to live there temporarily

### TRA Amounts

- TRA (SAH-eligible): Up to $47,130
- TRA (SHA-eligible): Up to $8,415

### TRA Requirements

- Veteran meets SAH/SHA disability criteria
- Living in family member's home
- Modifications needed for accessibility
- Can use up to twice

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use SAH to buy an already-adapted home?**
A: Yes. Grant can help purchase a home that's already modified for accessibility.

**Q: Can I get SAH and SHA?**
A: No. You qualify for one or the other based on your specific disability.

**Q: Is the grant taxable?**
A: No. VA grants are not taxable income.

**Q: Can I use the grant on a rental property?**
A: No. Must be your primary residence that you own (or family member's home for TRA).

**Q: What if modifications cost more than the grant?**
A: You pay the difference. Grant is capped at maximum amount.

**Q: Can I do the work myself?**
A: Generally no. Must use licensed contractor. Labor not covered if you do it yourself.

**Q: How often can I use the grant?**
A: Up to 3 times for SAH/SHA, until maximum is reached. HISA is one-time.

**Q: What if I move?**
A: Can use remaining grant amount at new home (up to 3 uses total for SAH/SHA).

**Q: Can active duty members apply?**
A: Yes, if they have qualifying service-connected disability.

**Q: Does this affect my VA disability rating?**
A: No. Grant is separate from disability compensation.

**Tips:**

### VA Oversight

**VA involvement:**
- Reviews contractor qualifications
- Approves construction plans

**How contractors are paid:**
- Initial payment after contract signed
- Progress payments during construction
- Final payment after VA inspection
- VA sends payments directly to contractor (usually)

## If You're Denied

### Common Denial Reasons

1. **Disability doesn't meet criteria** - Not severe enough or wrong type
2. **Not service-connected** - Disability from other cause
3. **Property issues** - Not your residence, not adaptable
4. **Documentation missing** - Need medical evidence

### How to Appeal

1. **Request explanation** in writing
2. **Gather additional evidence:**
   - Medical documentation
   - Specialist opinions
   - Functional limitations
3. **File appeal** through VA
4. **Consider VSO assistance**

- Disability: Service-connected bilateral above-knee amputation
- Needs: Wheelchair-accessible home

- Disability: Service-connected paraplegia
- Owns: 2-story home, inaccessible
- Grant: SAH ($85,000 used)
- Modifications: Elevator installed, bathroom converted, kitchen modified, ramps added
- Remaining: $32,014 for future use
- **Result:** Can now live independently in own home**

**Profile:** Navy veteran, bilateral blindness

- Disability: Service-connected total blindness
- Needs: Home adaptations for visual impairment
- Grant: SHA ($23,444)

- **Result:** Safer, more navigable home environment**

**Profile:** Air Force veteran, service-connected back injury

- Disability: 60% service-connected back
- Needs: Bathroom safety modifications
- Grant: HISA ($8,605)

- **Result:** Reduced fall risk, maintained independence**

**Profile:** Army veteran, ALS diagnosis, living with parents

- Disability: Service-connected ALS
- Living: Parents' home
- Grant: TRA ($47,130)
