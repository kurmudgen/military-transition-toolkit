# VA Travel Pay Reimbursement: Get Paid for Medical Appointments Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **What it is:** Reimbursement for mileage and travel costs to VA medical appointments
- **Rate:** $0.67/mile (2024) for qualifying veterans
- **Who qualifies:** 30%+ disability, low income, travel for compensation exams, and others
- **How to claim:** File via BTSSS online, VA kiosk, or travel office
- **Insider tip:** File within 30 days of appointment—claims can be denied after that

## What Is VA Travel Pay?

VA Beneficiary Travel Pay reimburses eligible veterans for costs of traveling to and from VA healthcare appointments. This includes:
- Mileage reimbursement for personal vehicle
- Public transportation costs
- Special mode transportation (when medically necessary)
- Lodging and meals (in some cases)

**For many veterans, this adds up to hundreds or thousands of dollars annually.**

## Who Qualifies?

### Automatic Eligibility

**You automatically qualify if:**
- 30%+ service-connected disability rating
- Traveling for treatment of a service-connected condition (any rating)
- Receiving VA pension
- Income below VA threshold (updated annually)
- Traveling for a Compensation & Pension (C&P) exam

### Travel for Service-Connected Conditions

**Any veteran traveling for service-connected treatment:**
- Even 0% rating qualifies for that condition
- Must be traveling specifically for SC condition treatment
- Includes specialist appointments for SC conditions

### Income-Based Eligibility

**If income is below threshold:**
- VA national income threshold (updated yearly)
- Qualify regardless of disability rating
- Based on household income

**2024 threshold:** Approximately $35,000 (single) / $40,000 (with dependents) - verify current amounts

### Special Circumstances

**Additional qualifying situations:**
- Unable to defray travel costs
- Travel by special transportation (medically necessary)
- Beneficiary traveling with veteran as attendant
- Veterans traveling for dental care (if VA-authorized)

## How Much Will You Get?

### Mileage Rate

**2024 rate: $0.67 per mile**
- Round-trip distance from home to VA facility
- Calculated using shortest route
- Deductible may apply

### Deductible

**Standard deductible:**
- $3.00 per one-way trip
- $6.00 per round trip
- $18.00 monthly maximum

**Deductible waived for:**
- C&P exams
- 30%+ service-connected conditions
- Special mode transportation

### Example Calculations

**Example 1: 50 miles round trip, waived deductible**
- 50 miles × $0.67 = **$33.50**

**Example 2: 50 miles round trip, $6 deductible**
- 50 miles × $0.67 = $33.50
- Minus $6.00 deductible
- **$27.50**

**Example 3: Monthly appointments, 40 miles each**
- 4 appointments × 40 miles × $0.67 = $107.20
- Minus $18 max deductible
- **$89.20**

### Special Mode Transportation

**If medically necessary:**
- Ambulance
- Wheelchair van
- Special modified vehicle
- Actual cost reimbursed (with prior authorization)

### Lodging and Meals

**For qualifying overnight stays:**
- Lodging: Up to $77/night
- Meals: Up to $55/day
- Must be pre-authorized for most situations
- Only for medical necessity

## How to File Claims

### Method 1: BTSSS (Online)

**Beneficiary Travel Self-Service System:**
1. Go to va.gov/health-care/get-reimbursed-for-travel-pay
2. Sign in with ID.me, Login.gov, or DS Logon
3. Select "File new claim"
4. Enter appointment details
5. Submit claim

**Advantages:**
- Available 24/7
- Track claim status
- Submit anytime within 30 days

### Method 2: VA Kiosk

**At VA facility:**
1. Find travel kiosk in lobby/waiting area
2. Scan your VA ID or enter last 4 of SSN
3. Select eligible appointments
4. Confirm and submit

**Best for:**
- Filing same day as appointment
- Quick submission

### Method 3: Travel Office

**In person:**
1. Visit beneficiary travel office at VA facility
2. Provide appointment information
3. Staff processes claim

**Best for:**
- Complex claims
- Special transportation needs
- Questions about eligibility

### Method 4: Phone/Mail

**If needed:**
- Call travel office to submit claims
- Mail VA Form 10-3542 with receipts

## Filing Deadlines

### 30-Day Rule

**Claims should be filed within 30 days:**
- After this, claims may be denied
- Best practice: File immediately after appointment
- Use BTSSS same day if possible

### Late Claims

**If past 30 days:**
- May request late claim submission
- Needs valid reason
- Not guaranteed approval

## What You'll Need

### For Standard Claims

- Appointment date and time
- VA facility location
- Your address (starting point)
- Type of appointment

### For Special Transportation

- Prior authorization number
- Receipts for actual costs
- Medical documentation showing necessity

### For Community Care

**If seen at non-VA facility:**
- Same eligibility rules apply
- May need referral documentation
- Submit via BTSSS or travel office

## Tracking Your Claims

### Online Status Check

**Through BTSSS:**
1. Sign in
2. Select "Check claim status"
3. View pending, approved, denied claims

### Payment Method

**How you receive payment:**
- Direct deposit (fastest)
- Paper check
- Set up direct deposit in VA.gov profile

### Payment Timeline

**Typical processing:**
- 7-10 business days after submission
- May take longer for complex claims
- Direct deposit faster than checks

## Community Care Travel

### When It Applies

**If you receive VA Community Care:**
- Appointments at non-VA facilities
- Authorized by VA
- Same travel pay rules apply

### How to Claim

**Process:**
1. Get community care authorization
2. Attend appointment
3. File travel claim via BTSSS
4. Select "Community Care" as facility type

### What's Covered

**Same rates and rules:**
- Mileage reimbursement
- Deductible (if applicable)
- Special transportation (if pre-authorized)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I get paid for cancelled appointments?**
A: No. Must attend the appointment to be reimbursed.

**Q: Can I claim for telehealth appointments?**
A: No. Travel pay is for in-person appointments only.

**Q: What if I took public transportation?**
A: Actual cost reimbursed (with receipts) up to mileage equivalent.

**Q: Can I get travel pay for community care?**
A: Yes, same rules apply for VA-authorized community care.

**Q: Is there a limit on how much I can claim?**
A: No annual limit. Each qualifying appointment can be claimed.

**Q: Can a family member file for me?**
A: Yes, with proper authorization.

**Q: What if I have multiple stops?**
A: Mileage calculated from home to first stop, continuing to other stops, back home.

**Q: Does travel pay affect my VA benefits?**
A: No. It's reimbursement, not taxable income.

**Q: Can I claim parking?**
A: Generally no. VA facility parking should be free. Tolls may be claimable.

**Q: What if I carpool with another veteran?**
A: Each veteran can claim their own travel (one drives, both file).

## Special Situations

### Beneficiary Attendant Travel

**If you need someone to travel with you:**
- Medically necessary attendant
- May qualify for attendant travel pay
- Requires documentation
- Contact travel office

### Catastrophically Disabled

**If rated catastrophically disabled:**
- Additional travel benefits may apply
- Special transportation easier to get approved
- Contact travel office for details

### Homeless Veterans

**If homeless:**
- Still eligible for travel pay
- Can use shelter address
- Special assistance available

### C&P Exam Travel

**For Compensation & Pension exams:**
- ALWAYS qualifies for travel pay
- No deductible
- File immediately after exam
