# VA Prosthetics: Getting Equipment and Devices Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **What's covered:** Prosthetic limbs, wheelchairs, hearing aids, orthotics, medical equipment, home modifications
- **Who qualifies:** Veterans enrolled in VA healthcare; service-connected conditions get priority
- **Cost:** FREE for most veterans—no copays for prosthetics and sensory aids
- **Quality:** VA provides state-of-the-art technology, including advanced prosthetics
- **Insider tip:** VA has specialized Prosthetic and Sensory Aids Service (PSAS) representatives—they're your advocates for getting what you need

## What Does VA Prosthetics Cover?

VA's Prosthetics and Sensory Aids Service (PSAS) provides one of the most comprehensive assistive technology programs in the country.

### Prosthetic Limbs

**Upper extremity:**
- Below-elbow prostheses
- Above-elbow prostheses
- Partial hand prostheses
- Myoelectric (robotic) arms
- Advanced/bionic prostheses

**Lower extremity:**
- Below-knee prostheses
- Above-knee prostheses
- Microprocessor knees
- Running/sports prostheses
- Swimming prostheses
- Activity-specific prostheses

### Orthotic Devices

- Leg braces
- Back braces
- Custom orthotics (shoe inserts)
- Ankle-foot orthoses (AFO)
- Knee-ankle-foot orthoses (KAFO)
- Spinal orthoses
- Upper extremity orthoses

### Mobility Equipment

**Wheelchairs:**
- Manual wheelchairs
- Power wheelchairs
- Sports wheelchairs
- Shower/commode chairs
- Transport chairs

**Other mobility:**
- Scooters
- Walkers
- Canes
- Crutches
- Rollators

### Sensory Aids

**Hearing:**
- Hearing aids
- Cochlear implants
- Assistive listening devices
- Tinnitus devices

**Vision:**
- Low vision aids
- Magnifiers
- Closed-circuit TVs
- Electronic devices for blind veterans
- Guide dogs

**Communication:**
- Speech generating devices
- Augmentative communication aids
- Laryngeal speech devices

### Home Modifications

- Stair lifts
- Wheelchair ramps
- Bathroom modifications
- Kitchen modifications
- Widened doorways

### Other Equipment

- CPAP/BiPAP machines
- Hospital beds
- Patient lifts
- Diabetic supplies
- Ostomy supplies
- Wound care supplies
- Respiratory equipment

## Who Qualifies?

### Primary Eligibility

**All enrolled veterans** can receive prosthetics and sensory aids for:
- Service-connected conditions (always covered)
- Conditions being treated by VA
- Conditions that improve ability to live independently

### Priority Categories

| Priority | Description | Coverage |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Service-connected conditions | Full coverage, priority scheduling |
| 2 | Non-service-connected, enrolled | Coverage per clinical need |
| 3 | Aid to prevent hospitalization | Full coverage |
| 4 | Improve quality of life | Coverage as resources allow |

### Special Programs

**Veterans with Spinal Cord Injuries/Disorders:**
- Comprehensive SCI/D program
- Full prosthetics support
- Specialized rehabilitation

**Blind Veterans:**
- Blind Rehabilitation Centers
- Full sensory aids
- Training and support

**Polytrauma Veterans:**
- Polytrauma Rehabilitation Centers
- Comprehensive equipment
- Integrated care

## How to Get Prosthetics and Equipment

### Step 1: Medical Evaluation

Your VA provider determines clinical need:
1. Schedule appointment with primary care or specialty provider
2. Discuss your functional needs
3. Provider documents medical necessity
4. Provider places order with PSAS

### Step 2: PSAS Consultation

The Prosthetic and Sensory Aids Service:
1. Reviews the order
2. May schedule evaluation appointment
3. Assesses specific equipment needs
4. Determines best device options

### Step 3: Evaluation and Fitting

**For prosthetic limbs:**
- Meet with certified prosthetist
- Take measurements and molds
- Discuss options and goals
- Custom fabrication begins

**For wheelchairs:**
- Seating and mobility evaluation
- Assessment by rehab therapist
- Selection of appropriate chair
- Custom modifications as needed

**For hearing aids:**
- Audiology evaluation
- Hearing aid fitting
- Programming and adjustment
- Follow-up care

### Step 4: Receive Equipment

- Delivery to home or VA facility
- Training on use and maintenance
- Adjustment period
- Follow-up appointments

### Step 5: Ongoing Support

- Repairs and replacements
- Upgrades as technology improves
- Maintenance supplies
- Re-evaluation as needs change

## Cost

### What's FREE

**For most veterans:**
- All prosthetics (limbs, braces)
- All mobility equipment
- Hearing aids and accessories
- Vision aids
- Batteries and supplies
- Repairs and maintenance

### Copays

**There are NO copays for:**
- Prosthetics
- Orthotic devices
- Wheelchairs
- Hearing aids
- Sensory aids
- Supplies for these devices

This is true regardless of your VA priority group.

### What's NOT Covered

Generally not covered:
- Comfort items without medical need
- Purely cosmetic items
- Items not clinically necessary
- Items from non-VA sources without authorization

- Activity-specific devices
- Home modifications

### 2. Not Requesting Evaluation

- Tell your VA provider
- Request PSAS consultation
- Be specific about your needs

### 4. Not Using Supplies Benefit

VA provides supplies:
- Batteries for hearing aids
- Liners for prosthetics
- Wheelchair accessories
- Replacement parts

### 5. Ignoring Maintenance

- Prosthetics need adjustment
- Wheelchairs need service
- Hearing aids need tuning
- All equipment has lifespan

### 6. Not Knowing About Home Mods

VA can provide:
- Wheelchair ramps
- Bathroom modifications
- Stair lifts
- Other accessibility improvements

Ask about home modification programs.

### 7. Buying Privately When VA Would Cover

Before buying equipment:
- Check if VA covers it
- Get VA evaluation
- VA equipment is usually free

### 8. Not Appealing Denials

If PSAS denies equipment:
- Request explanation
- Gather supporting documentation
- File appeal
- Contact Patient Advocate

## Repairs and Replacements

### Repairs

VA repairs all VA-provided equipment:
- Call your PSAS representative
- Explain the problem
- Repairs arranged (often in-home)
- No cost to veteran

### Replacements

Equipment is replaced when:
- Beyond economical repair
- No longer meeting needs
- Technology has significantly improved

### Emergency Repairs

For urgent equipment failure:
- Contact PSAS immediately
- Explain the emergency
- Expedited repair or loaner arranged

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need a prescription for VA prosthetics?**
A: Your VA provider places the order, which serves as the prescription.

**Q: Can I get sports or recreational equipment?**
A: Yes! VA provides running blades, swimming prosthetics, sports wheelchairs, and other recreational equipment.

**Q: What about high-tech bionic arms?**
A: VA provides advanced prosthetics including myoelectric arms and the LUKE arm for qualifying veterans.

**Q: Is there a limit on how many prosthetics I can get?**
A: No arbitrary limit. You receive what's clinically necessary, including activity-specific devices.

**Q: Can VA modify my home for accessibility?**
A: Yes. VA can provide ramps, bathroom modifications, stair lifts, and other home modifications.

**Q: What about hearing aid batteries?**
A: VA provides free batteries for VA-issued hearing aids.

**Q: Can I go to a private prosthetist?**
A: Generally, you must use VA or VA-authorized providers. In some cases, VA can authorize outside care.

**Q: How often can I get new equipment?**
A: Based on clinical need. When equipment no longer meets needs or can't be repaired, it's replaced.

**Q: What if I don't like my prosthetic?**
A: Communicate with your prosthetist. Adjustments and alternative options are available.

**Q: Does VA provide service dogs?**
A: For blind veterans, VA provides guide dogs through accredited schools. Service dogs for other conditions may be supported differently.

**Q: Can I get a power wheelchair?**
A: If clinically indicated (manual wheelchair isn't feasible), yes.

**Q: What about CPAP machines?**
A: Covered for diagnosed sleep apnea. Supplies (masks, tubing) also provided.

## If You Have Problems

### Denied Equipment

1. **Request explanation** in writing
2. **Gather supporting evidence:**
   - Doctor's letter
   - Therapy notes
   - Functional assessments
3. **File appeal** with PSAS
4. **Contact Patient Advocate**
5. **Escalate** if needed

### Long Wait Times

1. **Contact PSAS** to check status
2. **Ask about expediting** if urgent need
3. **Request loaner equipment** if available
4. **Contact Patient Advocate** if delays are unreasonable

### Equipment Not Working

1. **Contact PSAS** immediately
2. **Request repair** or replacement
3. **Ask for loaner** while yours is repaired
4. **Emergency contact** for urgent issues
