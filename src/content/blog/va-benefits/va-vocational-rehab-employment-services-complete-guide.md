# VA Vocational Rehab Employment Services: Job Placement and Career Help Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **What it is:** Comprehensive employment assistance for veterans with service-connected disabilities
- **Key benefit:** Free job placement services, resume help, interview coaching, and career counseling
- **Who qualifies:** Veterans with 10%+ service-connected disability rating with employment barriers
- **Cost:** Completely free—all services covered by VA
- **Insider tip:** VR&E can provide employment services even if you don't need education/training—Employment Through Long-Term Services track

## What Are VR&E Employment Services?

VA Vocational Rehabilitation and Employment (VR&E, Chapter 31) provides five tracks of assistance. The employment-focused tracks help veterans:
- Find suitable employment
- Get job placement assistance
- Receive career counseling
- Obtain resume and interview help
- Access workplace accommodations

**Employment services tracks:**
1. **Reemployment:** Return to previous employer
2. **Employment Through Long-Term Services:** Training then employment
3. **Rapid Access to Employment:** Direct job placement without training
4. **Self-Employment:** Start your own business

## Who Qualifies?

### Basic Eligibility

**You qualify for VR&E if:**
- 10%+ service-connected disability rating AND
- Received discharge other than dishonorable AND
- Have an employment handicap

**OR:**
- 20%+ rating with serious employment handicap
- Active duty member with expected 20%+ rating (pending discharge)

### Employment Handicap Defined

**VA determines employment handicap based on:**
- Your disability limits job options
- You can't work in your previous field
- You need training or services to find suitable work
- Your disability affects ability to prepare for, obtain, or keep employment

### Who Gets Employment Services

**Employment services are appropriate if:**
- You're job-ready but need placement help
- You need interview or resume assistance
- You want to return to previous employer
- You've completed VR&E training and need job help

## Types of Employment Services

### Rapid Access to Employment Track

**For veterans who are already job-ready:**
- No additional training needed
- Just need help finding job
- Resume assistance
- Job search support
- Interview coaching

**Services include:**
- Career counseling
- Job placement services
- Resume writing
- Interview preparation
- Job search strategies
- Labor market information
- Networking assistance

### Employment Through Long-Term Services

**For veterans who need training THEN employment:**
- Education or training first
- Employment services after completion
- Full career support

**After training, you receive:**
- Job placement assistance
- Career coaching
- Workplace accommodation help
- Up to 18 months post-employment support

### Reemployment Track

**For veterans returning to previous employer:**
- Help negotiating return
- Workplace accommodation assistance
- Rights under USERRA
- Adjustment counseling

### Self-Employment Track

**For veterans starting businesses:**
- Business plan development
- Training in business skills
- Equipment and supplies
- Marketing assistance
- (See separate self-employment guide)

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Determine Eligibility

**Verify you have:**
- 10%+ service-connected rating (or pending)
- Employment handicap
- Discharge other than dishonorable

### Step 2: Apply Online

**Submit VA Form 28-1900:**
- Available at VA.gov/careers-employment/vocational-rehabilitation
- Or through eBenefits
- Or in person at VA Regional Office

### Step 3: Meet with VR&E Counselor

**Initial evaluation:**
- Discuss your situation
- Review service history
- Assess employment barriers
- Determine appropriate track

### Step 4: Develop Employment Plan

**Individualized Written Rehabilitation Plan (IWRP):**
- Sets employment goals
- Identifies services needed
- Establishes timeline
- Outlines VA support

### Step 5: Receive Services

**Work with counselor:**
- Access employment services
- Meet regularly for guidance
- Track progress toward goals

## What VR&E Provides for Employment

### Financial Support During Job Search

**Subsistence allowance:**
- Monthly payment while in program
- Rate varies by training status and dependents
- Helps cover living expenses

**2024 Rates (full-time equivalent):**
- No dependents: ~$750/month
- With dependents: Higher rates apply

### Employment Incentives for Employers

**VR&E offers employers:**
- Tax credits for hiring veterans
- On-the-job training subsidies
- Non-paid work experience programs
- Special employer services

**These incentives help veterans get hired.**

### Tools and Equipment

**For employment, VR&E may provide:**
- Interview clothing
- Work-appropriate attire
- Tools needed for job
- Licensing or certification fees
- Professional organization dues

### Transportation Support

**Help with:**
- Transportation to interviews
- Commuting assistance
- Vehicle modifications (if needed)

## Employer Programs Through VR&E

### Non-Paid Work Experience (NPWE)

**What it is:**
- Internship-like experience
- No wages (subsistence allowance instead)
- Employer evaluates veteran
- Often leads to permanent hire

**Benefits:**
- Gain experience
- Prove abilities
- Network with employer
- Low-risk for employer

### On-the-Job Training (OJT)

**What it is:**
- Paid training with employer
- VA supplements wages
- Structured skill development
- Leads to permanent position

**How it works:**
- Employer pays reduced wage initially
- VA covers difference
- Wages increase as skills develop
- Full wage after training complete

### Special Employer Incentives

**Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC):**
- Employers get tax credit for hiring veterans
- VR&E counselors can inform employers
- Increases veteran's competitiveness

## Post-Employment Support

### 18-Month Follow-Along

**After job placement:**
- VR&E stays in contact
- Help with workplace issues
- Accommodation adjustments
- Career advancement support
- Problem resolution

### When Issues Arise

**If problems develop:**
- Contact VR&E counselor
- Get help resolving issues
- Additional services if needed
- Support during transitions

### Job Loss Support

**If you lose the job:**
- Reopen case (within 18 months)
- Additional placement services
- Assess what happened
- New job search support

## Common Questions

**Q: Do I need to use education benefits before employment services?**
A: No. Rapid Access to Employment provides direct job placement without training.

**Q: Can I get VR&E employment help if I'm already employed?**
A: Maybe. If your job is unsuitable due to disability, you may qualify for help finding better employment.

**Q: How long can I receive employment services?**
A: Typically 12 years from discharge or rating determination, plus 18 months post-employment support.

**Q: Will VR&E find me a job?**
A: They provide extensive help, but you must actively participate in job search.

**Q: Can I use VR&E and GI Bill together?**
A: Usually one or the other, but VR&E often provides more comprehensive support.

**Q: What if I don't like my VR&E counselor?**
A: Request a different counselor through the regional office supervisor.

**Q: Is there a minimum disability rating for employment services?**
A: 10% with employment handicap, or 20% with serious employment handicap.

**Q: Can National Guard/Reserve members use VR&E?**
A: Yes, if you have a service-connected rating from active duty.

**Q: What industries does VR&E help with?**
A: All industries. They match you with suitable employment based on abilities and interests.

**Q: Can VR&E help with federal job applications?**
A: Yes! They're excellent at federal resume preparation and USAJOBS applications.

## If You're Denied Employment Services

### Common Denial Reasons

1. **No employment handicap found** - Disability doesn't limit employment
2. **Already suitably employed** - Current job is appropriate
3. **Infeasibility** - Goal not achievable within program
4. **Administrative issues** - Missing information

### How to Appeal

1. **Request reconsideration** from counselor
2. **Administrative review** by supervisor
3. **Board of Veterans Appeals** if needed
4. **Provide additional evidence** of employment barriers
