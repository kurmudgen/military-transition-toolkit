# Vet Centers: Free Readjustment Counseling for Combat Veterans Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **What it is:** Free, confidential counseling centers for combat veterans and families
- **Who qualifies:** Combat veterans, MST survivors, drone crew, mortuary affairs personnel, and their families
- **Cost:** Completely free—no enrollment, no copays
- **Key benefit:** Not part of VA healthcare record—truly confidential
- **Insider tip:** Vet Centers are separate from VA hospitals—a more relaxed, community-based option

## What Are Vet Centers?

Vet Centers are community-based counseling centers that provide a wide range of social and psychological services to eligible veterans and their families. They were created to help veterans readjust to civilian life.

**Key differences from VA Medical Centers:**
- Separate from VA healthcare system
- Records not shared with VA hospitals
- Smaller, community-based locations
- More flexible, less bureaucratic
- Combat/trauma focused

**There are over 300 Vet Centers nationwide, plus mobile units.**

## Who Qualifies?

### Combat Veterans

**If you served in:**
- Any combat zone or area of hostility
- Active duty, Guard, or Reserve
- Any branch of service
- Any era (WWII through present)

**This includes:**
- Afghanistan (OEF/OFS)
- Iraq (OIF/OND)
- Persian Gulf
- Vietnam
- Korea
- WWII
- Other combat zones

### Military Sexual Trauma (MST) Survivors

**If you experienced MST:**
- Any service member (combat or not)
- Active duty, Guard, or Reserve
- Any era
- Male or female

**MST includes:**
- Sexual assault
- Sexual harassment

### Other Qualifying Service

**Also eligible:**
- Drone/unmanned aircraft crew members
- Mortuary affairs personnel
- Veterans who served in areas of high casualty/hostility
- Bereaved family members of service members who died on duty

### Family Members

**Family can receive services:**
- If veteran is eligible
- Family members can access counseling
- Couples/family counseling available
- Bereavement counseling

## How Vet Centers Differ from VA

### Confidentiality

**Vet Center records:**
- Separate from VA medical records
- Not shared with VA hospitals
- Won't appear in VA health record
- Truly confidential (with limits for safety)

**VA Medical Center:**
- Part of comprehensive VA record
- Shared across VA system

### Environment

**Vet Centers:**
- Smaller, community-based
- Less clinical feeling
- More relaxed atmosphere
- Often in strip malls, office buildings

**VA Medical Centers:**
- Larger hospital settings
- More clinical
- Multiple services under one roof

### Access

**Vet Centers:**
- No enrollment required
- Walk-ins welcome
- Flexible hours (evenings, weekends often available)
- No copays ever

**VA Medical Centers:**
- Usually require enrollment
- Appointment-based
- May have copays
- Standard business hours primarily

### Focus

**Vet Centers:**
- Readjustment focused
- Combat/trauma specialized
- Short and long-term counseling
- Community connection

**VA Medical Centers:**
- Comprehensive healthcare
- Medical and mental health
- Specialty care
- Hospital services

- Call 1-877-WAR-VETS (927-8387)
- Search "Vet Center near me"

### Step 2: Make Contact

**You can:**
- Walk in (no appointment needed for initial visit)
- Call to schedule
- Request call back online

### Step 3: Initial Visit

**First visit:**
- Meet with counselor
- Discuss your situation
- No pressure to commit
- Explore what might help

### Step 4: Begin Services

**If you choose to continue:**
- Schedule regular appointments
- Individual, group, or family sessions
- As much or as little as you need

## Common Concerns

### "Will This Affect My Security Clearance?"

**Generally no:**
- Seeking mental health help is not disqualifying
- Shows good judgment
- Vet Center records are separate from VA system
- Discuss specific concerns with counselor

### "Will VA Find Out?"

**Vet Center records are separate:**
- Not automatically shared with VA medical
- You control what's shared
- Truly confidential (with safety exceptions)

### "I Don't Have PTSD"

**You don't need a diagnosis:**
- Readjustment counseling for any issue
- Don't have to have PTSD
- Just need help adjusting to civilian life

### "It's Been Years Since I Served"

**No time limit:**
- Vietnam veterans welcome
- Korean War veterans welcome
- WWII veterans welcome
- Service years or decades ago still qualifies

### "I Don't Want to Be in a Group"

**Individual counseling available:**
- Groups are optional
- Many veterans prefer one-on-one
- Your choice

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to bring my DD-214?**
A: Helpful but not always required. They can verify service other ways.

**Q: How much does it cost?**
A: Completely free. No copays, no fees.

**Q: Do I need VA healthcare enrollment?**
A: No. Vet Centers are separate from VA healthcare.

**Q: Will my commander find out?**
A: If still serving, there are some reporting requirements. Ask the counselor.

**Q: Can my spouse come to counseling?**
A: Yes. Family members of eligible veterans can receive services.

**Q: Are there female counselors?**
A: Yes. You can request a counselor of a specific gender.

**Q: What if I deployed but wasn't in combat?**
A: You may still qualify. Discuss your specific service with the Vet Center.

**Q: Is this only for mental health?**
A: Primarily readjustment counseling, but they connect you with other resources.

**Q: How long can I receive services?**
A: No time limit. As long as you benefit from services.

**Q: Can I use Vet Center and VA mental health?**
A: Yes. You can use both.
