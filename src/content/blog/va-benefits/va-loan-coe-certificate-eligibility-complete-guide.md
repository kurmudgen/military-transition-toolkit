# VA Loan Certificate of Eligibility (COE): How to Get Yours Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **What it is:** Official document proving you're eligible for a VA home loan
- **Why you need it:** Lenders require COE to process VA loans
- **Fastest way:** Online through VA.gov (often instant for veterans)
- **Through lender:** Most can pull your COE electronically in minutes
- **Insider tip:** Get your COE BEFORE house hunting—know your entitlement upfront

## What Is a Certificate of Eligibility?

The COE is your proof that you qualify for a VA home loan. It tells lenders:
- You meet VA's service requirements
- How much entitlement you have
- Any previous VA loan usage
- Your eligibility status

**The COE is NOT loan approval—it's just proof of eligibility.**

## What the COE Shows

### Information on Your COE

**Personal information:**
- Name
- Social Security Number (partial)
- Branch of service
- Service dates

**Eligibility details:**
- Total entitlement available
- Entitlement used (if any)
- Entitlement code (reason for eligibility)
- Funding fee status (exempt or not)

### Understanding Entitlement

**Basic entitlement:** $36,000
**Bonus (tier 2) entitlement:** Additional amount based on county limits

**Full entitlement:** No loan limits for $0 down
**Partial entitlement:** Previous use reduces available entitlement

## Who Qualifies for a COE?

### Veterans

**Wartime service:**
- 90 continuous days during wartime period

**Peacetime service:**
- 181 continuous days during peacetime

**Gulf War era (includes current):**
- 24 months continuous OR
- Full period of active duty (90+ days minimum)

### Active Duty Service Members

**Currently serving:**
- After 90 continuous days
- Statement of service required

### Guard and Reserve

**6 years of service, OR:**
- 90 days of active duty (Title 10), OR
- Called to active duty and served 90 days

### Surviving Spouses

**Eligible if:**
- Spouse died from service-connected disability, OR
- Spouse was MIA/POW for 90+ days, OR
- Spouse died on active duty
- Must not have remarried (exceptions for remarriage after age 57)

### Discharge Requirements

**Must have discharge under conditions other than dishonorable:**
- Honorable
- General (Under Honorable Conditions)
- Other (may require review)

**Dishonorable discharge:** Not eligible

## Three Ways to Get Your COE

### Method 1: Online (Fastest)

**Through VA.gov:**
1. Go to [VA.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/how-to-request-coe](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/how-to-request-coe/)
2. Sign in with ID.me, Login.gov, or DS Logon
3. Click "Request your COE"
4. Follow prompts
5. Download immediately (if eligible and records are available)

**Timeline:** Often instant to 24 hours

**Works best for:**
- Veterans with clear service records
- Those in VA system already
- Recent separations with electronic records

### Method 2: Through Your Lender

**Lender pulls COE:**
1. Apply for VA loan with lender
2. Lender accesses VA's system (Web LGY)
3. COE generated electronically
4. You receive copy from lender

**Timeline:** Often same day

**Advantages:**
- You don't have to do anything
- Lender handles it
- Quick and convenient
- Most common method

### Method 3: By Mail

**Submit VA Form 26-1880:**
1. Download form from VA.gov
2. Complete all sections
3. Attach proof of service (DD-214, etc.)
4. Mail to VA Regional Loan Center

**Mail to:**
VA Regional Loan Center
Attn: COE
P.O. Box 1880
Atlanta, GA 30301

**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks

**When to use mail:**
- Online doesn't work
- Complex service history
- Need to submit additional documents
- Discharge upgrade situation

## Documents Needed by Situation

### Veterans (DD-214)

**You need:**
- DD-214 (Member 4 copy preferred)
- Shows service dates and discharge type

**If you don't have DD-214:**
- Request from National Personnel Records Center
- eVetRecs system
- Can take weeks

### Active Duty Members

**You need:**
- Statement of Service from commanding officer
- Must include:
  - Name and SSN
  - Date of birth
  - Date entered active duty
  - Duration of prior active duty service
  - Name of commander providing info

**Template available from VA**

### Guard and Reserve

**You need one of:**
- DD-214 (if activated)
- Statement of service
- Retirement points summary
- NGB Form 22 (National Guard)
- DFAS earnings statement

### Surviving Spouses

**You need:**
- Veteran's DD-214
- Marriage certificate
- Veteran's death certificate
- VA determination of service-connected death (if applicable)

## Common Eligibility Issues

### Problem: Service Record Not Found

**Solution:**
- Request DD-214 from NPRC
- Provide alternative documentation
- Unit records, orders, etc.

### Problem: Insufficient Service Time

**Solution:**
- Calculate total qualifying service
- Include all active duty periods
- Guard/Reserve activated time counts

### Problem: Previous VA Loan Showing

**Solution:**
- If loan paid off/sold, request entitlement restoration
- Submit request through lender or VA
- May need closing documents from previous loan

### Problem: Discharge Upgrade Pending

**Solution:**
- Wait for upgrade decision
- VA considers character of discharge
- May be eligible with General discharge

### Problem: Can't Access Online

**Solution:**
- Use lender method
- Submit by mail with VA Form 26-1880

## Restoring Your Entitlement

### Full Restoration

**When possible:**
- Previous VA loan paid in full AND
- Property sold

**How to restore:**
1. Provide payoff information
2. Provide proof of sale
3. VA updates your entitlement
4. New COE shows full entitlement

### One-Time Restoration

**Situation:**
- Paid off VA loan
- Still own the property (didn't sell)

**You can restore ONCE:**
- One-time exception
- Use it strategically
- Can't restore again this way

### Entitlement Not Restored

**If you:**
- Defaulted on VA loan
- VA paid claim to lender
- Entitlement reduced

**To restore:**
- Pay back VA's loss
- Then entitlement restored
- Check with VA for amount owed

## Understanding Your Entitlement Amount

### Full Entitlement

**If COE shows full entitlement:**
- No previous VA loans, OR
- Previous loans fully restored
- No loan limits for $0 down

### Partial Entitlement

**If COE shows used entitlement:**
- Have existing VA loan, OR
- Didn't restore after previous loan
- May have loan limits

**Calculating remaining entitlement:**
- Basic: $36,000
- Bonus: Varies by county
- Minus: Entitlement charged to existing loans

## Funding Fee Exemption on COE

### Exempt Status

**COE may show funding fee exempt if:**
- You receive VA disability compensation
- Purple Heart recipient
- Surviving spouse receiving DIC

### How to Get Exemption Added

**If not showing but you qualify:**
1. Provide VA disability award letter
2. VA updates COE
3. New COE shows exempt status

**This can save thousands of dollars!**

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need a COE to apply for a VA loan?**
A: Yes, lenders require it. Most can pull it for you during application.

**Q: Does getting a COE cost anything?**
A: No. It's free to request and obtain.

**Q: How long is a COE valid?**
A: No expiration, but lender may want updated one for loan processing.

**Q: Can I check my eligibility before getting COE?**
A: Yes, use VA's eligibility estimator online.

**Q: What if my COE shows incorrect information?**
A: Contact VA to correct. Provide documentation.

**Q: Can I have multiple COEs?**
A: You only have one at a time, but can get updated versions.

**Q: Does the COE guarantee loan approval?**
A: No. COE just proves VA eligibility. Lender still evaluates your credit, income, etc.

**Q: Can I use the COE for multiple loan applications?**
A: Yes. Shop multiple lenders with same COE.

**Q: What's an entitlement code?**
A: Two-digit code showing your eligibility basis (active duty, Guard, etc.).

**Q: My spouse is the veteran—can I get the COE?**
A: Veteran must request. Lender will see both names during loan process.

## Common COE Codes Explained

### Entitlement Codes

| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| 01 | Regular military |
| 05 | Veteran has previous VA loan |
| 06 | Guard/Reserve (6 years) |
| 07 | Guard/Reserve (activated) |
| 09 | Active duty current |
| 10 | Surviving spouse |
| 11 | Restored entitlement |

### What Codes Mean for You

**01/09:** Full entitlement, standard process
**05:** May have reduced entitlement—check amounts
**06/07:** Standard Guard/Reserve eligibility
**10:** Surviving spouse—special requirements apply
**11:** Entitlement was restored—full or partial
