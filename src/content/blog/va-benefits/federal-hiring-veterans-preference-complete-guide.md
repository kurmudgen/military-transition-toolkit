# Federal Hiring: Veterans' Preference Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **What it is:** Point system and special hiring authorities that give veterans advantages in federal hiring
- **5-Point Preference:** For veterans with honorable/general discharge
- **10-Point Preference:** For disabled veterans, Purple Heart recipients, and certain family members
- **Special Authorities:** VRA, 30% Disabled, Schedule A—can bypass competitive hiring
- **Insider tip:** Veterans' preference applies differently to competitive vs. excepted service—know the rules

## What Is Veterans' Preference?

Veterans' preference is a federal hiring advantage established to recognize the sacrifices of those who served. It provides:
- Points added to examination scores
- Special hiring authorities
- Protections during reductions in force (layoffs)
- Rights in the hiring process

**Veterans' preference is the law (5 U.S.C. § 2108) and applies to virtually all federal agencies.**

## Types of Veterans' Preference

### 5-Point Preference (TP)

**Who qualifies:**
- Served on active duty during specified periods
- Discharged under honorable conditions
- Campaign medal holders

**Time periods include:**
- August 2, 1990 to present (Gulf War era)
- Any war
- Campaign or expedition with authorized medal

**What you get:**
- 5 points added to passing exam score
- Preference in competitive hiring

### 10-Point Preference (Multiple Types)

#### Disabled Veteran (CP/CPS)

**CP (Compensable Disability):**
- Service-connected disability rated 10-29%
- 10 points added to score

**CPS (Compensable Disability, 30%+):**
- Service-connected disability rated 30%+
- 10 points added to score
- Additional protections
- Can use 30% Disabled Hiring Authority

#### Purple Heart (XP)

**Purple Heart recipients:**
- Awarded Purple Heart for combat wounds
- 10 points regardless of disability rating
- Presumed to have disability

#### Derived Preference (XP)

**For certain family members:**
- Spouse of veteran with 100% disability
- Widow/widower of veteran
- Mother of deceased/disabled veteran

### How Points Are Applied

**Competitive examination process:**
1. Applicant takes exam or is evaluated
2. If "passing" score achieved
3. Points added to final score
4. Higher score = higher on hiring list

**Note:** Points only apply if you first achieve a passing score.

## Special Hiring Authorities

### Veterans Recruitment Appointment (VRA)

**What it is:**
- Direct hire authority
- No competition required
- Up to GS-11 or equivalent

**Who qualifies:**
- Disabled veterans (any rating)
- Veterans who served 3+ years active duty
- Campaign medal recipients
- Armed Forces Service Medal recipients
- Recent veterans (within 3 years of discharge)

**Benefits:**
- Bypasses competitive examination
- Agency can hire directly
- Faster hiring process

**Limitations:**
- GS-11 max (or equivalent)
- 2-year initial appointment (then converts)

### 30% or More Disabled Veteran

**What it is:**
- Direct hire authority
- Any grade level
- Non-competitive appointment

**Who qualifies:**
- 30%+ service-connected disability rating
- Retired from active duty with disability
- Purple Heart recipient

**Benefits:**
- Any grade level (no GS-11 limit)
- Direct hire without competition
- Immediate career or career-conditional appointment
- Very powerful hiring authority

### Schedule A (Disability)

**What it is:**
- Excepted service appointment
- For people with significant disabilities

**Who qualifies:**
- Veterans with severe disabilities
- Need certification from VA or licensed professional

**Benefits:**
- Non-competitive hiring
- Can convert to competitive service after 2 years

### Veterans Employment Opportunities Act (VEOA)

**What it is:**
- Apply to positions open only to current federal employees

**Who qualifies:**
- Preference eligible OR
- Veterans who served 3+ years and separated under honorable conditions

**Benefits:**
- Access to internal federal job announcements
- Merit promotion opportunities

## How to Claim Veterans' Preference

### On USAJOBS

**When creating profile:**
1. Go to USAJOBS.gov
2. Create or edit profile
3. Under "Eligibilities," select veteran status
4. Upload required documents

### Required Documents

**For 5-Point Preference:**
- DD-214 (Member 4 copy)
- Showing character of discharge
- Showing campaign medals (if applicable)

**For 10-Point Preference:**
- DD-214
- SF-15 (Application for 10-Point Preference)
- VA disability letter (current)
- Purple Heart citation (if applicable)

**For Derived Preference:**
- SF-15
- Marriage certificate (spouse)
- Death certificate (widow/widower)
- VA letter showing veteran's disability

### SF-15 Form

**Required for:**
- All 10-point preference claims
- Derived preference claims

**Get it at:** OPM.gov/forms/SF-15.pdf

## Veterans' Preference in Hiring Process

### Competitive Service

**How it works:**
1. Job announced on USAJOBS
2. Applicants submit applications
3. HR evaluates qualifications
4. Qualified applicants ranked
5. Veterans' preference points added
6. List sent to hiring manager
7. Selection made using "rule of three" or category rating

**Category Rating System:**
- Applicants placed in categories (Best Qualified, Well Qualified, Qualified)
- Veterans' preference applied within categories
- Preference eligibles must be selected from highest category before others

### Excepted Service

**Veterans' preference applies when:**
- Agency has adopted preference policy
- Required by law for certain positions

**Some excepted agencies don't apply preference:**
- Check specific agency policies
- Intelligence agencies may differ
- Legislative/judicial branches exempt

### Pass Over Rules

**Agencies cannot pass over preference eligible without:**
- OPM approval (for 30%+ disabled)
- Legitimate, documented reasons
- Following proper procedures

**30%+ disabled veterans:**
- Cannot be passed over without OPM approval
- Strong protection in hiring

## Reduction in Force (RIF) Protection

### How RIF Retention Works

**Retention register groups:**
1. Career tenure
2. Within tenure: Veterans' preference status
3. Within preference: Length of service
4. Within service: Performance

**Veterans get:**
- Higher retention standing
- Protection during layoffs
- Extra service credit (disabled)

### Preference in RIF

**Preference eligibles:**
- Retained over non-preference with same tenure
- Additional years credit for disability

**Additional credit:**
- CPS (30%+): 4 additional years
- CP (10-29%): 2 additional years

### VA Hiring Programs

- Veterans' preference in all competitive announcements

### DoD Programs

**Department of Defense:**
- Operation Warfighter (internships)

- Veteran affinity groups

## Common Questions

**Q: Does veterans' preference guarantee a job?**
A: No. It provides advantages but doesn't guarantee selection.

**Q: What if I have 40% disability and a 5-point medal?**
A: You get 10 points (disability). Points don't stack.

**Q: Can preference be used for promotions?**
A: No. Only for initial appointment and RIF protection.

**Q: Does preference apply to contractor jobs?**
A: No. Only federal government positions.

**Q: What about GS-12 and above with VRA?**
A: VRA is limited to GS-11. Use 30% Disabled authority for higher grades.

**Q: Can I use veterans' preference if I'm still on active duty?**
A: Generally no. Must be discharged/separated to claim preference.

**Q: Does preference apply to temporary positions?**
A: Sometimes. Depends on appointment type and duration.

**Q: What if my DD-214 isn't available yet?**
A: Submit Statement of Service from commander initially.

**Q: Can veterans' preference be combined with Schedule A?**
A: You can use whichever authority is most advantageous.

**Q: Does veterans' preference apply to SES positions?**
A: No. Senior Executive Service is excepted.

## How to Strengthen Your Application

### Optimize Your Federal Resume

**Federal resume requirements:**
- Much longer than private sector (4-6 pages)
- Include all experience details
- Specific duties and accomplishments
- Supervisor contact information
- Hours per week

### Match Qualifications

**Read the announcement carefully:**
- Specialized experience required
- Use exact language from announcement
- Provide specific examples
- Quantify accomplishments

### Upload All Documents

**Common mistakes:**
- Missing DD-214
- Outdated VA letter
- Wrong DD-214 copy
- Missing SF-15 for 10-point

### Answer Assessment Questions Honestly

**Self-assessment:**
- Rate yourself accurately
- Provide examples to support ratings
- Don't inflate qualifications

## If Preference Is Violated

### Filing a Complaint

**If you believe preference was violated:**
1. Contact agency HR first
2. File with Merit Systems Protection Board (MSPB)
3. Department of Labor VETS for certain claims

### VEOA Appeals

**Veterans Employment Opportunities Act:**
- File complaint with Department of Labor
- Then to MSPB if unresolved
- Must file within 60 days

### What to Document

**Keep records of:**
- Job announcement
- Your application materials
- Communications with agency
- Selection outcomes
- Any evidence of violation
