# CHAMPVA: Healthcare for Dependents and Survivors Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **What it is:** Healthcare coverage for spouses and children of certain disabled veterans
- **Who qualifies:** Dependents of 100% P&T disabled veterans or surviving family of service-connected deaths
- **Cost:** Very low—25% cost-share for most services, no monthly premium
- **Coverage:** Similar to civilian insurance—medical, mental health, prescriptions, dental
- **Insider tip:** CHAMPVA is secondary to most other insurance—enroll even if you have other coverage

## What Is CHAMPVA?

The Civilian Health and Medical Program of the Department of Veterans Affairs (CHAMPVA) provides healthcare coverage for eligible dependents who are NOT eligible for TRICARE.

**CHAMPVA covers:**
- Medical care
- Surgical care
- Prescription medications
- Mental health services
- Durable medical equipment
- Dental (limited)
- Vision (limited)

**It's like health insurance, but specifically for qualifying veteran family members.**

## Who Qualifies?

### Dependents of 100% P&T Veterans

**If the veteran is:**
- Rated 100% Permanent and Total (P&T) disabled

**Eligible dependents include:**
- Spouse
- Children under 18
- Children 18-23 if full-time student
- Helpless children (permanently disabled before 18)

### Survivors of Veterans

**If the veteran:**
- Died from service-connected disability, OR
- Was rated 100% P&T at time of death

**Eligible survivors:**
- Surviving spouse (if not remarried before 55)
- Children (same age rules as above)

### Dependents of Veterans Who Died on Active Duty

**If service member died on active duty:**
- After September 10, 2001
- Survivors may qualify for CHAMPVA

### Important: NOT TRICARE Eligible

**You CANNOT be eligible for TRICARE:**
- Active duty family members have TRICARE
- Retirees have TRICARE
- CHAMPVA is for those WITHOUT TRICARE access

**If eligible for TRICARE, you cannot use CHAMPVA.**

## What's Covered

### Medical Services

**Covered (25% cost-share):**
- Doctor visits
- Specialist care
- Hospital stays
- Surgery
- Laboratory tests
- X-rays and imaging
- Emergency care
- Maternity care
- Physical therapy
- Mental health care

### Prescription Drugs

**Covered (25% cost-share or less):**
- Generic medications
- Brand name medications
- Specialty drugs
- Mail order pharmacy (lower cost)

**CHAMPVA Meds by Mail:**
- 90-day supply at lower cost
- Convenient home delivery

### Mental Health

**Covered services:**
- Outpatient therapy
- Inpatient psychiatric care
- Substance abuse treatment
- Counseling

### Preventive Care

**Often 100% covered:**
- Well-child visits
- Immunizations
- Preventive screenings
- Annual exams

### Durable Medical Equipment

**Covered with approval:**
- Wheelchairs
- Hospital beds
- Oxygen equipment
- Prosthetics

### Limited Coverage

**Dental:**
- Limited to specific conditions
- Extractions related to medical treatment
- Some oral surgery

**Vision:**
- Medical eye conditions covered
- Routine exams may be limited
- Glasses/contacts generally not covered

## What's NOT Covered

**CHAMPVA doesn't cover:**
- Cosmetic surgery (non-medical)
- Experimental treatments (most)
- Long-term care (nursing home)
- Personal comfort items
- Custodial care
- Most dental (except as noted)
- Routine vision exams

## How Much Does It Cost?

### No Monthly Premium

**CHAMPVA has no premium:**
- Don't pay monthly fee to be enrolled
- Only pay when you use services

### Cost-Share (25%)

**Standard cost-share:**
- You pay 25% of allowable charges
- CHAMPVA pays 75%
- After deductible is met

### Annual Deductible

**2024 deductibles:**
- $50 per person per year
- $100 per family per year

**After deductible:**
- Cost-share applies
- Very affordable care

### Catastrophic Cap

**Annual limit:**
- $3,000 per family per year
- After this, CHAMPVA pays 100%

### Cost Example

**Scenario: Doctor visit costs $200**
- Annual deductible: $50 (if not met)
- Remaining: $150
- Your cost-share (25%): $37.50
- **Total you pay: $87.50**

**After deductible met:**
- Visit costs $200
- Your cost-share: $50
- **You pay: $50**

## How to Enroll

### Step 1: Gather Documents

**You'll need:**
- VA Form 10-10d (Application)
- Veteran's DD-214
- VA rating decision (showing 100% P&T)
- Marriage certificate (spouse)
- Birth certificates (children)
- Social Security numbers
- Medicare card (if applicable)
- Other health insurance information

### Step 2: Complete Application

**VA Form 10-10d:**
- Application for CHAMPVA Benefits
- Complete all sections
- Include all supporting documents

### Step 3: Submit Application

**Mail to:**
VHA Office of Integrated Veteran Care
CHAMPVA Eligibility
PO Box 469028
Denver, CO 80246-9028

**Or fax:** 1-303-331-7809

### Step 4: Wait for Determination

**Processing time:**
- 6-8 weeks typical
- May be longer during busy periods
- Check status: 1-800-733-8387

### Step 5: Receive CHAMPVA Card

**If approved:**
- Receive authorization letter
- Get CHAMPVA ID card
- Begin using benefits

## Using CHAMPVA

### Finding Providers

**CHAMPVA is not a network plan:**
- See any provider who accepts CHAMPVA
- No referrals needed for specialists
- Choose your own doctors

**Tips for finding providers:**
- Call provider and ask "Do you accept CHAMPVA?"
- Many providers accept CHAMPVA like other insurance
- Some may not know what it is—explain it's similar to insurance

### Filing Claims

**If provider bills CHAMPVA:**
- Provider files claim directly
- You pay your cost-share

**If you pay upfront:**
- Get itemized receipt
- Submit claim form (VA Form 10-7959a)
- Receive reimbursement

### Prescription Process

**At pharmacy:**
- Present CHAMPVA card
- Pay cost-share

**CHAMPVA Meds by Mail:**
1. Get prescription from doctor
2. Mail or upload to Meds by Mail
3. Receive medications at home
4. Pay lower cost-share

## CHAMPVA and Medicare

### Primary vs. Secondary

**If you have Medicare:**
- Medicare is PRIMARY
- CHAMPVA is SECONDARY
- CHAMPVA pays what Medicare doesn't

### Must Enroll in Medicare B

**If Medicare eligible:**
- MUST enroll in Medicare Part B
- CHAMPVA won't pay what Part B would have covered
- Coordination of benefits required

### Exception: Under 65 Medicare

**If on Medicare due to disability (under 65):**
- Same rules apply
- Must have Part B

### Cost Savings

**With Medicare + CHAMPVA:**
- Medicare pays first (80%)
- CHAMPVA pays most of remaining
- Very low out-of-pocket costs

## CHAMPVA and Other Insurance

### Coordination of Benefits

**If you have other insurance:**
- Other insurance usually primary
- CHAMPVA is secondary
- CHAMPVA may pay remaining balance

**Report all insurance to CHAMPVA.**

### Through Employer

**If spouse has employer coverage:**
- Employer plan is primary
- CHAMPVA can be secondary
- Good to have both

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I use VA facilities with CHAMPVA?**
A: Sometimes. VA can provide care when space available. Contact local VA.

**Q: Is there a network of providers?**
A: No. CHAMPVA is not a network plan. See any provider who accepts it.

**Q: What if a provider doesn't know what CHAMPVA is?**
A: Explain it's similar to insurance. Provide billing information from your card.

**Q: Can I have CHAMPVA and Medicare?**
A: Yes. Medicare is primary, CHAMPVA secondary.

**Q: What if I get married again?**
A: Surviving spouses who remarry before 55 lose eligibility. After 55, may retain.

**Q: How long does coverage last for children?**
A: Until 18, or 23 if full-time student, or indefinitely if helplessly disabled.

**Q: Can I see specialists without referral?**
A: Yes. No referral required for CHAMPVA.

**Q: What about pre-existing conditions?**
A: CHAMPVA covers pre-existing conditions. No exclusions.

**Q: Is pregnancy covered?**
A: Yes. Maternity care is covered like other medical care.

**Q: Does CHAMPVA cover my stepchildren?**
A: Yes, if legally adopted or claimed as dependent.

## Tips for Success

### 1. Keep Your Card Handy

Always carry CHAMPVA card to appointments.

### 2. Verify Provider Accepts CHAMPVA

Call ahead to confirm before scheduling.

### 3. Use Meds by Mail

Save money on prescriptions with mail order.

### 4. Report Changes

Notify CHAMPVA of address changes, new insurance, or status changes.

### 5. Keep Records

Save EOBs and receipts for your records.

### 6. Understand Primary/Secondary

Know which insurance is primary to avoid billing issues.

## If Application Is Denied

### Common Reasons

1. **Not 100% P&T** - Veteran's rating not permanent and total
2. **TRICARE eligible** - Can't have CHAMPVA if TRICARE eligible
3. **Documentation missing** - Need all required documents
4. **Relationship not verified** - Missing marriage/birth certificates

### Appeal Process

1. Review denial letter
2. Provide missing documentation
3. Request reconsideration
4. Appeal to VA if needed
