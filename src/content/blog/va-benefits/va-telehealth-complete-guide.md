# VA Telehealth: How to Use Virtual Care Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **What it is:** Healthcare appointments via video or phone instead of in-person visits
- **Who can use it:** All enrolled veterans; available for most appointment types
- **Cost:** Same as in-person visits (free for most veterans)
- **Technology:** VA Video Connect app, tablets, smartphones, or computers
- **Insider tip:** VA can provide free tablets or internet assistance for veterans who need them

## What Is VA Telehealth?

VA telehealth allows you to have healthcare appointments without traveling to a VA facility. You can see your doctor, therapist, or specialist from:
- Your home
- Your workplace
- A local VA clinic (for specialty appointments)
- Any private location

**Types of VA telehealth:**
- **Video visits:** Face-to-face via video
- **Phone appointments:** Voice-only consultations
- **Secure messaging:** Asynchronous communication through My HealtheVet
- **Remote patient monitoring:** Tracking health data from home

**Services available via telehealth:**
- Primary care
- Mental health (therapy, psychiatry)
- Specialty care consultations
- Medication management
- Nutrition counseling
- Social work services
- Post-surgical follow-ups
- Chronic disease management

## Who Can Use VA Telehealth?

### Eligibility

If you're enrolled in VA healthcare, you can use telehealth for appropriate appointments. There are no additional eligibility requirements.

### Best Candidates for Telehealth

Telehealth works especially well if you:
- Live far from a VA facility
- Have transportation challenges
- Have mobility limitations
- Prefer home-based care
- Have a busy schedule
- Want to avoid waiting rooms
- Need frequent follow-ups

### When In-Person Is Still Needed

Some situations require in-person visits:
- Physical examinations
- Lab work and blood draws
- Imaging (X-rays, MRIs)
- Procedures and surgeries
- Initial comprehensive evaluations (sometimes)
- Complex conditions requiring hands-on assessment

## VA Telehealth Options

### 1. VA Video Connect

**What it is:** VA's primary video visit platform

**How it works:**
1. Schedule video appointment with your provider
2. Receive link via email or text
3. Click link at appointment time
4. Join video call from device

**Requirements:**
- Smartphone, tablet, or computer
- Camera and microphone
- Internet connection
- VA Video Connect app (mobile) or web browser (computer)

**App download:**
- iOS: Apple App Store
- Android: Google Play Store

### 2. Phone Appointments

**What it is:** Voice-only appointments

**How it works:**
1. Schedule phone appointment
2. Provider calls you at scheduled time
3. Conduct appointment by phone

**Best for:**
- Quick follow-ups
- Medication adjustments
- Simple check-ins
- When video isn't available

### 3. My HealtheVet Secure Messaging

**What it is:** Secure messaging with your care team

**How to use:**
1. Log into My HealtheVet (myhealth.va.gov)
2. Navigate to Secure Messaging
3. Send message to your care team
4. Receive response (typically 1-3 business days)

**Best for:**
- Non-urgent questions
- Prescription refill requests
- Appointment requests
- Sharing information

### 4. VA Health Chat

**What it is:** Text-based chat for quick questions

**Available for:**
- General health information
- Navigation help
- Basic questions

### 5. Remote Patient Monitoring

**What it is:** Tracking health data from home

**How it works:**
- Receive monitoring equipment from VA
- Take daily measurements (blood pressure, weight, blood sugar)
- Data transmitted to VA
- Care team monitors and responds

**Conditions commonly monitored:**
- Diabetes
- Heart conditions
- COPD
- Hypertension
- Post-surgical recovery

## How to Get Started

### Step 1: Enroll in VA Healthcare

If not already enrolled:
1. Apply at VA.gov/health-care/apply
2. Complete enrollment
3. Receive confirmation

### Step 2: Create Accounts

**My HealtheVet:**
1. Go to myhealth.va.gov
2. Create account
3. Upgrade to Premium (in-person or online identity verification)

**VA.gov:**
1. Go to VA.gov
2. Create account with ID.me or Login.gov
3. Verify identity

### Step 3: Request Telehealth Appointment

**Options:**
- Call your VA clinic and request video or phone appointment
- Use My HealtheVet Secure Messaging to request
- Ask during current appointment to schedule future telehealth
- Use VA.gov online scheduling

### Step 4: Prepare Technology

**Before first appointment:**
1. Download VA Video Connect app (if using mobile)
2. Test camera and microphone
3. Ensure stable internet connection
4. Find quiet, private location
5. Test app before appointment

### Step 5: Attend Appointment

**On appointment day:**
1. Click the link from your email/text
2. Allow camera and microphone access
3. Wait in virtual waiting room
4. Provider joins when ready
5. Conduct appointment as normal

## Technology Requirements

### Minimum Requirements

**Smartphone/Tablet:**
- iOS 11 or higher, or Android 6.0 or higher
- Camera (front-facing)
- Microphone
- Internet connection (WiFi or cellular data)

**Computer:**
- Windows 10 or MacOS 10.13 or higher
- Web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
- Webcam
- Microphone/speakers or headset
- Broadband internet

### Internet Speed

| Activity | Minimum Speed |
|----------|--------------|
| Video visit | 1 Mbps up/down |
| HD video | 3+ Mbps |
| Audio only | 256 Kbps |

### Troubleshooting

**Common issues:**
- Camera/microphone not detected: Check permissions
- Poor video quality: Check internet connection, close other apps
- Can't join call: Try different browser, restart device
- Audio issues: Use headphones, check volume settings

**Get help:**
- VA Video Connect Help Desk: 866-651-3180
- Option 3 for technical support
- Available Monday-Saturday

## VA Technology Assistance Programs

### VA Tablets

VA provides free tablets to veterans who:
- Need telehealth but lack devices
- Have clinical need for remote care
- Don't have other technology access

**How to get one:**
1. Talk to your VA provider
2. Request referral for tablet
3. Receive tablet and training

### Digital Divide Consult

VA's program to address technology barriers:
- Device provision
- Internet assistance
- Digital literacy training
- Ongoing technical support

### VA Connected Care

Coordination of telehealth services including:
- Equipment needs assessment
- Internet connectivity solutions
- Training and support

**To access:**
1. Contact your VA care team
2. Express interest in telehealth
3. Complete technology assessment
4. Receive needed resources

## Cost and Coverage

### Telehealth Costs

Telehealth appointments cost the same as in-person:
- Free for Priority Groups 1-6
- Standard copays for Groups 7-8
- Free for service-connected conditions
- Free for combat veterans (5 years)

### What's Covered

All services available via telehealth are covered:
- Primary care
- Mental health
- Specialty consultations
- Follow-up care
- Medication management

### Travel Pay

**Important:** You may still qualify for travel pay for telehealth:
- If you travel to a VA facility for telehealth
- If you use Clinical Video Telehealth (at a VA site)
- Mileage reimbursement applies

## Types of Telehealth Visits

### Primary Care

**Common uses:**
- Chronic disease management
- Medication refills and adjustments
- Post-hospitalization follow-up
- Health maintenance discussions
- Non-urgent symptoms

**How it works:**
- Video or phone with your primary care provider
- Review medications, symptoms, test results
- Adjust treatment as needed
- Order labs/imaging for in-person if needed

### Mental Health

**Common uses:**
- Individual therapy
- Psychiatric medication management
- Group therapy
- Substance abuse counseling
- PTSD treatment

**Particularly effective:**
- More convenient = better attendance
- Privacy of home setting
- Reduced barriers to care
- Evidence shows comparable outcomes to in-person

### Specialty Care

**Available specialties:**
- Cardiology
- Dermatology (store-and-forward)
- Neurology
- Endocrinology
- Rheumatology
- Many others

**How it works:**
- Often connected to local CBOC
- Specialist at larger VA
- You at local clinic or home

## Clinical Video Telehealth (CVT)

### What It Is

Telehealth conducted at a VA facility:
- You go to nearby VA clinic (CBOC)
- Specialist at distant VA Medical Center
- Connected via video

### Why Use CVT

- Access to specialists not at your local VA
- Equipment/rooms set up for medical exams
- Staff to assist with technology
- May be required for certain specialties

### How to Access

1. Your provider identifies need for specialty
2. Referral to telehealth specialty
3. Appointment scheduled at local VA
4. You attend local VA, specialist joins remotely

## Store-and-Forward Telehealth

### What It Is

Asynchronous telehealth where data is collected and reviewed later:
- Images captured at one location
- Sent to specialist for review
- Specialist reviews and responds

### Common Uses

- **Dermatology:** Photos of skin conditions
- **Retinal imaging:** Eye disease screening
- **Wound care:** Images of healing progress
- **Pathology:** Specimen images

### How It Works

1. Visit local VA for imaging/photos
2. Images sent to specialist
3. Specialist reviews and provides recommendation
4. Your local provider shares results

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is telehealth as good as in-person care?**
A: For many conditions, yes. Studies show comparable outcomes, especially for mental health and chronic disease management.

**Q: Can I get prescriptions via telehealth?**
A: Yes. Providers can prescribe medications during telehealth visits, including controlled substances when appropriate.

**Q: What if I don't have internet or a device?**
A: VA can provide tablets and help with internet access through its Connected Care programs.

**Q: Is telehealth private?**
A: Yes. VA telehealth uses encrypted, secure technology. Find a private location for your appointments.

**Q: Can I do telehealth from work?**
A: Yes, if you have a private space. Many veterans do lunchtime appointments.

**Q: What if there's a technical problem during my appointment?**
A: Your provider will try to reconnect or switch to phone. Contact VA Video Connect Help Desk: 866-651-3180.

**Q: Do I still get travel pay for telehealth?**
A: If you travel to a VA facility for telehealth, yes. Home-based telehealth doesn't include travel pay.

**Q: Can my spouse or caregiver join?**
A: Yes, with your permission. They can be in the room or join remotely.

**Q: Is all care available via telehealth?**
A: Not all care. Physical exams, labs, imaging, and procedures require in-person visits.

**Q: How do I cancel or reschedule?**
A: Same as in-person: call your clinic or use My HealtheVet.

## Real Examples

### Example 1: Rural Veteran with PTSD

**Profile:** Army veteran living 2 hours from nearest VA

**Situation:**
- Weekly therapy needed for PTSD
- 4-hour round trip impossible regularly
- Work schedule conflicts

**Solution:**
- Weekly video therapy from home
- Using VA Video Connect on smartphone
- Quarterly in-person visits for comprehensive care

**Outcome:** Maintained consistent therapy, significant PTSD improvement

### Example 2: Elderly Veteran with Chronic Conditions

**Profile:** Vietnam veteran, 75 years old, diabetes and heart disease

**Situation:**
- Monthly check-ins needed
- Mobility challenges
- No transportation

**Solution:**
- VA provided tablet
- Monthly video visits with primary care
- Remote patient monitoring (blood pressure, glucose)
- Care team monitors data daily

**Outcome:** Better disease control, fewer ER visits, maintained independence

### Example 3: Working Veteran

**Profile:** Air Force veteran, works full-time

**Situation:**
- Needs mental health follow-up
- Can't take time off work
- Missing appointments due to schedule

**Solution:**
- Lunch-hour phone appointments
- Psychiatry every 3 months via video
- Secure messaging for between-visit questions

**Outcome:** Consistent medication management, no missed work
