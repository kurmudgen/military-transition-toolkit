# A&P Mechanic License for Veterans: Complete Guide

- **Best military backgrounds:** All aircraft maintenance MOS/rates—direct translation

## What Is an A&P Certificate?

An Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certificate is the FAA certification required to perform maintenance on aircraft. It consists of:

**Airframe (A):** Structure, systems, and components of the aircraft body
**Powerplant (P):** Engines and related systems
**A&P (Both):** Full certification—most common and versatile

With an A&P, you can maintain, repair, and inspect aircraft. Adding an Inspection Authorization (IA) allows you to approve aircraft for return to service after major repairs.

## Why Veterans Excel in Aviation Maintenance

Military aviation maintenance directly translates:

**Same aircraft often:** Many military aircraft (C-130, Blackhawk, F-18) have civilian variants or similar systems.

**Rigorous training:** Military aviation maintenance training exceeds most civilian programs.

**Safety culture:** Military emphasizes maintenance safety and quality—exactly what FAA requires.

**Documentation discipline:** Military maintenance records transfer to civilian requirements.

**Tool control:** FOD awareness and accountability are second nature.

## Military to A&P Pathway

### Direct Pathway (No School Required)
Veterans with 18-30 months military aviation maintenance experience can apply directly for FAA exams without attending A&P school.

**Requirements:**
- 18 months airframe OR 18 months powerplant OR 30 months combined
- Documentation of experience (DD-214, training records, experience statements)
- Pass FAA written, oral, and practical exams

### School Pathway
If you don't have qualifying experience or prefer structured prep:
- FAA-approved A&P school (12-24 months typically)
- GI Bill covers approved programs
- School prepares you for exams

## Certification Requirements

### FAR 147 Experience Requirements
To qualify for exams without school:

| Certificate | Experience Required |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Airframe only | 18 months airframe experience |
| Powerplant only | 18 months powerplant experience |
| A&P (both) | 30 months combined experience |

### Military Experience That Counts
Almost all aviation maintenance qualifies:
- Aircraft structural repair
- Engine maintenance/repair
- Electrical systems
- Avionics (may count toward airframe)
- Hydraulics, fuel, flight controls
- Survival equipment (limited)

### Exam Requirements
**Three phases for each rating (Airframe and Powerplant):**
1. **Written exam:** Computer-based, multiple choice (~100 questions)
2. **Oral exam:** Examiner asks questions, you demonstrate knowledge
3. **Practical exam:** Perform actual maintenance tasks

## Timeline to Certification

### Direct Pathway with Military Experience (1-3 months)
- **Week 1-2:** Gather documentation, submit 8610-2 to FSDO
- **Week 3-6:** Study for written exams
- **Week 7-8:** Take written exams (General, Airframe, Powerplant)
- **Week 9-12:** Study for oral/practical, schedule with DME
- **Week 10-14:** Complete oral and practical exams

### A&P School (12-24 months)
- **Months 1-6:** General and airframe curriculum
- **Months 7-12:** Powerplant curriculum
- **Months 13-18:** Advanced topics, exam prep
- **Month 19-24:** Complete FAA exams

### Documentation Needed

1. **DD-214:** Shows MOS and service dates
2. **Training records:** Formal school documentation
3. **Experience letters:** From supervisors documenting tasks performed
4. **VMET:** Verification of Military Experience and Training
5. **OJT logs:** If available

### FAA Form 8610-2 Process
1. Complete FAA Form 8610-2 (Application for A&P Certificate)
2. Gather all military documentation
3. Submit to local FSDO (Flight Standards District Office)
4. FSDO reviews and approves eligibility
5. Receive authorization to test

1. **Going to A&P school when unnecessary:** If you have 30+ months experience, test directly
2. **Not gathering documentation before separation:** Get experience letters signed while still in

5. **Ignoring general aviation:** Regional/GA jobs can lead to major airlines

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to go to A&P school if I was a military aircraft mechanic?**
A: Usually no. With 30+ months experience, you can test directly without school.

**Q: How do I prove my military experience qualifies?**
A: DD-214, training records, and experience letters documenting specific tasks performed.

**Q: How long does FSDO approval take?**
A: Typically 2-4 weeks. Some FSDOs are faster than others.

**Q: Are the FAA exams hard?**
A: Written exams are straightforward with proper study. Oral/practical require solid hands-on knowledge.

**Q: Can I work while waiting for my A&P?**
A: You can work under an A&P's supervision, but cannot sign off work without your own certificate.

**Q: Is helicopter experience valid for A&P?**
A: Yes. A&P covers all aircraft. Helicopter experience qualifies.

**Q: What about avionics—does that count?**
A: Avionics work counts toward airframe experience. Separate avionics certification exists but isn't FAA-required.

**Q: Which airlines pay the most?**
A: Major airlines (Delta, United, American, Southwest) pay top wages with excellent benefits after seniority.

**Q: Is aviation maintenance a good long-term career?**
A: Yes. Massive shortage, rising pay, excellent job security. Airlines are competing for A&Ps.

**Q: Should I get my IA (Inspection Authorization)?**
A: After 3+ years with A&P, IA is valuable for advancement and opens more opportunities.
