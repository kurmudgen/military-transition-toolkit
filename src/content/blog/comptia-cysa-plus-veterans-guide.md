## What Is CompTIA CySA+ Certification?

CompTIA CySA+ (Cybersecurity Analyst) validates skills needed for SOC analyst, threat intelligence analyst, and security operations roles. It focuses on defensive security—detecting, analyzing, and responding to threats rather than exploiting them.

CySA+ covers threat management, vulnerability management, security operations and monitoring, and incident response. It's the natural progression after Security+ for those pursuing blue team (defensive) security careers.

**For DoD:** CySA+ is approved for DoD 8570/8140 CSSP Analyst, CSSP Incident Responder, and CSSP Infrastructure Support positions.

## Why Veterans Excel in Security Operations

Military defensive cybersecurity training directly applies:

**Threat detection:** You've monitored networks for intrusions and anomalies. SOC analysts do the same with commercial tools.

**Incident response:** Military incident response procedures parallel civilian IR frameworks.

**Intelligence analysis:** Threat intelligence work uses the same analytical skills as military intelligence.

**Log analysis:** You've reviewed security logs and alerts. CySA+ validates this skill formally.

**Teamwork under pressure:** SOCs operate 24/7 with high-stakes incidents. Your military experience prepared you for this.

## Certification Requirements

### Education Prerequisites
- **Minimum education:** None required
- **Recommended:** Security+ or 4 years hands-on security experience
- **GI Bill eligible:** Yes, for training programs

### Experience Requirements
- **CompTIA recommendation:** 4 years hands-on security experience
- **Alternative:** Security+ plus practice and study
- **Military equivalent:** Any cybersecurity/IA MOS time counts

### Exam Requirements
- **Exam code:** CS0-003
- **Questions:** Maximum 85 questions
- **Time limit:** 165 minutes
- **Passing score:** 750/900 (approximately 83%)
- **Question types:** Multiple choice, drag-and-drop, performance-based
- **Renewal:** Every 3 years

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is CySA+ worth it in 2025?**
A: Yes. It's the standard blue team certification after Security+. SOC analyst roles often require or prefer it.

**Q: CySA+ vs PenTest+—which is better?**
A: Different focus. CySA+ is defensive (blue team); PenTest+ is offensive (red team). Choose based on career goal.

**Q: Should I get CySA+ or CEH first?**
A: CySA+ for defensive roles (SOC, IR). CEH for offensive roles (pen testing). CySA+ is more accessible and relevant for most entry roles.

**Q: How hard is CySA+ compared to Security+?**
A: Harder. It's more specialized and assumes Security+ knowledge. Performance-based questions require actual analysis skills.

**Q: Does my clearance matter for CySA+ jobs?**
A: Yes. Cleared SOC analysts for government/contractor work are in high demand.

**Q: Is CySA+ DoD 8570 approved?**
A: Yes. CySA+ meets CSSP Analyst, CSSP Incident Responder, and CSSP Infrastructure Support requirements.

**Q: Can I pass CySA+ without Security+?**
A: Yes, but harder. Security+ provides foundation. If you have equivalent experience, you can attempt CySA+ directly.

**Q: What SIEM should I learn?**
A: Splunk is most common (free for learning). Also learn Elastic/ELK basics. Many employers use commercial SIEMs that work similarly.

**Q: How much log analysis is on the exam?**
A: A lot. You'll analyze logs, identify indicators, and make decisions based on data. Practice is essential.

**Q: Should I learn scripting for CySA+?**
A: Basic scripting helps (Python, PowerShell) but isn't required for the exam. Very valuable for the job.

**Q: What's the difference between CS0-002 and CS0-003?**
A: CS0-003 is current (2023+). Updated for modern threats and tools. Take the current version.
