## CHAPTER 1: Property Tax Exemptions & Benefits

### 100% DISABLED VETERAN PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTION

**Who Qualifies:**
- 100% service-connected permanent and total disability rating from VA
- Principal place of residence in Virginia
- Honorable discharge

**What You Get:**
COMPLETE exemption from real property taxes on your primary residence and up to one acre of land.

**Real-World Savings by County:**

| County | Avg Home Value | Annual Tax Saved |
|--------|---------------|------------------|
| Fairfax | $650,000 | $7,150 |
| Loudoun | $575,000 | $5,750 |
| Arlington | $700,000 | $7,700 |
| Prince William | $450,000 | $4,950 |
| Virginia Beach | $375,000 | $4,125 |

**Over 20 years: $82,500-$154,000 in savings**

**Surviving Spouse Benefits:**
Unmarried surviving spouses can continue receiving the exemption and can even transfer it to a new home if they move.

## CHAPTER 2: Income Tax Benefits

### VIRGINIA INCOME TAX SUBTRACTION FOR VETERANS

**2025 Enhancement:**
The subtraction increases from $30,000 to $40,000 for tax year 2025.

**Who Qualifies:**
- Virginia resident
- Age 55 or older with service-connected disability, OR
- Receiving VA disability compensation

**What You Get:**
Subtract up to $40,000 from Virginia taxable income

**Example Savings:**
- $40,000 subtraction × 5.75% VA tax rate = **$2,300 annual savings**
- Over 20 years = **$46,000**

**Combined with federal VA compensation (tax-free), this significantly reduces tax burden.**

## CHAPTER 3: VMSDEP Education Benefits

### VIRGINIA MILITARY SURVIVORS & DEPENDENTS EDUCATION PROGRAM

**What It Covers:**
Waiver of ALL tuition and mandatory fees for up to 8 semesters at Virginia public colleges and universities.

**Who Qualifies:**

**Tier 1 - Surviving Dependents:**
- Spouse or child of service member killed in action or died from service-connected disability
- Spouse or child of veteran rated 90-100% permanently disabled

**Tier 2 - POW/MIA Dependents:**
- Dependents of service members who are/were prisoners of war or missing in action

**What's Covered:**
✓ Full tuition waiver (in-state and out-of-state rates)
✓ Mandatory fees waived
✓ Up to 8 semesters (4 academic years)

**Value:**
- 4 years at UVA: ~$80,000
- 4 years at Virginia Tech: ~$70,000
- 4 years at William & Mary: ~$85,000

**Multiple Children Benefit:**
Each qualifying dependent child can use the benefit.

## CHAPTER 4: Employment Preference

### VETERANS' PREFERENCE IN STATE HIRING

**Preference Points:**
- Veterans: 5 points added to passing exam score
- Disabled veterans: 10 points added

**How It Works:**
Points added to civil service exam scores, moving veterans higher on hiring lists.

**Covered Positions:**
All Virginia state government positions using competitive examination process.

## CHAPTER 5: Vehicle Tax Exemptions

### PERSONAL PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTION (100% Disabled Veterans)

**Effective 2021:**
100% service-connected disabled veterans qualify for exemption on ONE vehicle (car or pickup truck).

**What's Exempt:**
- Personal property tax (annual vehicle tax)
- Registration fees

**Annual Savings:**
$200-$800+ depending on vehicle value and locality

**Application:**
Apply through local Commissioner of Revenue with VA rating documentation.

## CHAPTER 8: Application Process

### PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTION APPLICATION

**Step 1:** Contact your local Commissioner of Revenue
**Step 2:** Complete exemption application form
**Step 3:** Provide:
- DD-214
- VA rating letter (100% P&T)
- Proof of residency

**Deadline:** Typically December 5th annually (check local deadline)

**Processing:** 2-4 weeks

### VMSDEP APPLICATION

**Step 1:** Verify eligibility (90-100% disabled parent or KIA parent)
**Step 2:** Apply to Virginia college/university
**Step 3:** Contact school's financial aid office
**Step 4:** Submit:
- Parent's DD-214
- VA rating letter or death certificate
- Student's birth certificate
- Proof of Virginia residency

**Deadline:** Varies by school, apply when applying for admission
