# Mississippi Military Retirement: Best Cities, Housing Costs & Quality of Life

Mississippi ranks among the most tax-friendly states for military retirees with 100% exemption on military retirement pay, generous property tax exemptions for disabled veterans, and one of the lowest costs of living in the nation.

But where should you actually live? Mississippi's cities vary significantly in veteran population, job opportunities, housing costs, and proximity to military installations and VA healthcare.

This guide ranks Mississippi's best cities for veterans with real data on home prices, veteran populations, and quality of life factors.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Best overall for veterans**: Gulfport-Biloxi (military presence, coast, jobs, VA hospital)
**Best for affordability**: Hattiesburg, Jackson
**Best for military retirees**: Biloxi (Keesler AFB proximity)
**Best small city**: Hattiesburg (college town, military presence)
**Best suburban lifestyle**: Southaven (Memphis metro, affordable)

**Median home price range**: $145,000 (Jackson) to $265,000 (Gulfport-Biloxi coast)
**State average**: $198,000 (lowest in the South)

**Overall Grade: B**

**Why it ranks #3**: State capital, VA hospital, state government jobs with veteran preference, most affordable housing, but high crime.

#### Veteran Population
- **Hinds County veterans**: 13,000+

**Jackson (city)**:
- **Median home price**: $145,000 (most affordable major city)
- **Property tax with exemption**: $0 for 100% disabled
- **Property tax without exemption**: ~$1,885/year (1.3% rate)
- **Rental costs**: $800-$1,200/month
- **Many areas to avoid** due to crime - research carefully

**Madison (suburb - highly recommended)**:
- **Median home price**: $335,000
- **Among Mississippi's safest and most affluent areas**
- **Excellent schools**

**Ridgeland/Flowood (suburbs)**:
- **Median home price**: $275,000-$300,000
- **Growing, safer alternatives to Jackson proper**

**Brandon (Rankin County)**:
- **Median home price**: $245,000
- **Family-friendly, good schools**

#### Cost of Living
- **Overall index (Jackson city)**: 77.8 (22.2% below national average)
- **Housing**: 40% below national average in city
- **Overall**: Among the lowest in the nation
- **Suburbs are more expensive but still affordable**

#### Employment Opportunities

**Major employers**:
- **State of Mississippi government** (largest employer - veteran hiring preference)
- **University of Mississippi Medical Center** (UMMC - 10,000+ employees, major hospital)

**Average salary**: $48,000

**State hiring preference** is valuable in Jackson - many state agencies headquartered here.

#### VA Healthcare Access
- **G.V. (Sonny) Montgomery VA Medical Center**: 1500 East Woodrow Wilson Avenue, Jackson, MS 39216

#### Quality of Life
- **Climate**: Mild winters (45-60°F), hot, humid summers (90-95°F)
- **Outdoor recreation**: Ross Barnett Reservoir, Natchez Trace Parkway
- **Culture**: State capital amenities, museums, Mississippi Museum of Art, music history

- **Community**: Declining population in city, growing suburbs

**Pros**:
- VA hospital in city
- State government jobs with veteran preference

- Tax-free military retirement income
- Property tax exemption for 100% disabled
- Suburbs (Madison) are safe with excellent schools
- Central Mississippi location

**Cons**:
- Very high crime in Jackson proper (research neighborhoods carefully)
- Declining urban infrastructure
- Lower wages
- Hot, humid summers
- Limited private-sector veteran employment
- Must live in suburbs for safety (which are more expensive)

**Bottom line**: Jackson is best for veterans seeking state government employment with hiring preference or those requiring frequent VA hospital access. However, strongly consider living in suburbs (Madison, Ridgeland, Brandon) rather than Jackson proper due to crime. The VA hospital and state jobs are the main draws. If you can afford $275K-$335K for a home in Madison, you'll get excellent schools and safety with easy access to VA hospital and state jobs.

## Climate and Weather Considerations

### Overall Mississippi Climate
- **Winters**: Mild (40-60°F) - rarely below freezing except far north
- **Summers**: Hot and humid (85-95°F, high humidity)
- **Hurricane risk**: Coastal areas and inland flooding
- **Tornado risk**: Spring tornadoes possible statewide

### By Region

**Gulf Coast (Gulfport-Biloxi)**
- Mildest winters (50-65°F)
- Hot, humid summers (85-92°F)
- Hurricane risk (direct hits and storm surge)
- Year-round outdoor activities

**Central Mississippi (Jackson, Hattiesburg)**
- Mild winters (45-60°F)
- Hot summers (90-95°F)
- Inland hurricane effects (flooding, wind)
- Four seasons (mild)

**North Mississippi (Tupelo, Southaven)**
- Cooler winters (35-55°F, occasional snow)
- Hot summers (90-95°F)
- Tornado risk in spring

### Veteran Health Considerations

**Heat and humidity**: Mississippi summers are challenging for veterans with cardiovascular issues or respiratory conditions. Air conditioning is essential.

**Hurricane preparedness**: Coastal residents need evacuation plans, flood insurance, and hurricane insurance.

**Mild winters**: Excellent for veterans with cold-weather injuries or arthritis.

The best city for you depends on your priorities: military community (Gulfport-Biloxi), affordability (Hattiesburg), state jobs (Jackson suburbs), or Memphis access (Southaven).
