## Pricing Models Overview

### Model 1: 100% Free (Nonprofit/Government)
**Competitors:** SkillBridge, VA TAP, O2O, Hire Heroes, VetJobs, FourBlock, ACP, Bunker Labs, Service2School

**Monetization:**
- Government funding (SkillBridge, VA TAP)
- Grants and donations (O2O, Hire Heroes, Service2School)
- Corporate sponsorships (FourBlock, ACP, VetsinTech)
- Volunteer-driven (ACP mentors, Service2School counselors)

**Strengths:**
- Zero barrier to entry
- High veteran trust
- Scalable through partners/volunteers

**Weaknesses:**
- Sustainability challenges (see: Veterati shutdown)
- Limited features/support
- Dependent on external funding
- Can't invest heavily in technology

---

### Model 2: Freemium (Free Core + Paid Premium)
**Competitors:** LinkedIn (1-year free Premium for veterans, then $30-40/month)

**Free Tier:**
- Basic LinkedIn profile and job search
- Limited InMail, profile views

**Premium Tier ($30-40/month):**
- See who viewed profile
- Unlimited LinkedIn Learning
- InMail credits
- Applicant insights

**Strengths:**
- Sustainable business model
- Free tier drives adoption
- Premium converts motivated users

**Weaknesses:**
- Paywall friction for some features
- Time-limited free access for veterans

---

### Model 3: B2B (Employers Pay, Veterans Free)
**Competitors:** Military.com, VetJobs, RecruitMilitary, Shift.org

**Employer Pricing:**
- **Military.com:** Job postings (Monster feed), employer branding, ads
- **VetJobs:** Job postings (exact pricing not public)
- **RecruitMilitary:** Career fair booth fees, virtual fair access
- **Shift.org:** SkillBridge facilitation fees, recruiting services
- **Hire Heroes USA:** $145/job posting, packages available

**Strengths:**
- Veterans access 100% free
- Scalable revenue
- Aligns incentives (employers want to hire)

**Weaknesses:**
- Veterans are product, not customer
- Privacy concerns (data shared with employers)
- Employer needs may conflict with veteran needs

---

### Model 4: Paid Services (Professional Fees)
**Competitors:** CareerProPlus, professional resume writers

**Pricing:**
- **CareerProPlus:** Custom quotes ($300-800+ estimated based on industry standards)
- **Resume writers:** $200-1000+ per resume
- **Career coaches:** $100-300/hour

**Strengths:**
- High-quality personalized service
- Professional expertise
- Immediate revenue

**Weaknesses:**
- Expensive for most veterans
- One-time transaction (no recurring revenue)
- Excludes budget-conscious users
- Passive (do it for you vs empower you)

---

### Model 5: For-Profit Specialized Services
**Competitors:** Veterans United Home Loans

**Pricing:**
- **Veterans United:** Origination fees (higher than average), interest on loans
- **Value to veterans:** $0 down VA loans, no PMI

**Strengths:**
- Sustainable high-revenue business
- Aligned incentives (helping veterans get homes)
- Defensible moat (VA loan specialization)

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited to specific vertical (home loans)
- Not a platform, single service

## Value Perception Analysis

### What Veterans Will Pay For:
1. ‚úÖ **Expert human review** - VA claims, resume, LinkedIn ($49-149)
2. ‚úÖ **1-on-1 coaching** - Career guidance, interview prep ($99-299)
3. ‚úÖ **Time savings** - Tools that automate tedious work
4. ‚úÖ **Confidence** - Practice, feedback, preparation
5. ‚úÖ **Outcomes** - Features that directly lead to job offers, benefits approvals

### What Veterans Won't Pay For:
1. ‚ùå **Basic information** - Articles, guides (expect free)
2. ‚ùå **Job boards** - Accustomed to free listings
3. ‚ùå **Community access** - Peer forums should be free
4. ‚ùå **Checklists/templates** - Basic tools expected free
5. ‚ùå **Paywalls on essentials** - VA claims info, benefits data

### Price Sensitivity:
- **Transitioning veterans** are often on tight budgets (military pay ‚Üí civilian gap)
- **BUT** willing to pay for **proven ROI** (e.g., $149 VA claims help if it unlocks $1000/month disability)
- **Price anchoring:** CareerProPlus charges $300-800 for resume = MTT's $49 feels like steal
- **Free tier essential:** Must offer robust free version to build trust and adoption

## MTT's Pricing Strategy

### Phase 1: Launch (Months 1-6)
**100% FREE for all users**
- Build user base and trust
- Gather feedback on willingness to pay
- Validate features veterans value most
- Goal: 5,000-10,000 active users

### Phase 2: Freemium Introduction (Months 7-12)
**Launch Premium tier ($9.99/month)**
- Free tier remains robust (VA Claims, state benefits, resume builder, community)
- Premium adds expert reviews, unlimited tracking, AI features
- Introduce professional services marketplace (coaching, resume review)
- Target: 5-10% conversion = 250-1,000 premium users
- Revenue: $30K-120K ARR

### Phase 3: Scale & Optimize (Year 2+)
**Optimize pricing and packaging**
- Test annual pricing ($99/year = 17% discount)
- Experiment with tiered premium (Premium vs Premium Pro)
- Expand professional services marketplace
- Corporate sponsorships (employers pay to sponsor transition tools)
- Target: 50,000 users, 5% premium = 2,500 premium @ $120K/yr = $300K ARR

## Pricing Recommendations

### 1. Start Free, Add Premium Thoughtfully
- Launch with generous free tier (build trust and user base)
- Premium should feel like "nice to have," not "must have"
- Free tier must deliver core value (VA claims, state benefits, resume)

### 2. Price Premium at $9.99/month
- Psychologically under $10 = impulse buy
- $99/year = better value, encourage annual commitment
- Competitive with LinkedIn Premium but more valuable (broader transition focus)

### 3. Professional Services = √Ä La Carte
- $49 resume review (75% cheaper than CareerProPlus)
- $79 LinkedIn optimization
- $99 coaching sessions (partner with Hire Heroes, ACP coaches)
- $149 VA claims expert filing (biggest ROI for veterans)

### 4. Corporate Sponsorships (B2B2C)
- Employers sponsor features ("Brought to you by Amazon")
- Employers pay for premium access for their veteran candidates
- Revenue without compromising veteran experience

### 5. Never Paywall These Features:
- VA Claims Builder (basic)
- State Benefits Comparison
- Retirement Calculator
- Community Forum
- Basic resume builder
- Transition checklist
- Military skills translator
