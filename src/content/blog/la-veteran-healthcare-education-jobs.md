# Louisiana Veterans: Healthcare Facilities, Education Benefits & Job Resources

Louisiana serves approximately 257,000 veterans - about 6% of the state's adult population. Whether you're transitioning to civilian life or already living in Louisiana, you need to know what healthcare facilities, education benefits, and job opportunities are available.

This guide provides actual addresses, phone numbers, and actionable information for Louisiana veterans.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Healthcare**: Louisiana has 3 VA medical centers (New Orleans, Shreveport, Alexandria/Pineville), multiple community-based clinics, and 5 state veterans homes - excellent VA healthcare network for veterans across the state

**Education**: In-state tuition at all Louisiana public universities and colleges for GI Bill users; Louisiana State University and Tulane both have strong veteran support programs; Title 29 tuition exemption for qualifying dependents (free college)

**Employment**: State hiring preference with direct hire authority, two major military installations (Barksdale AFB, Fort Johnson), defense contractor presence, veteran-friendly employers statewide

**Veteran Population Hotspots**: Jefferson Parish (16,500+ veterans), Caddo Parish/Shreveport (15,000+), Orleans Parish/New Orleans (13,500+), East Baton Rouge Parish (substantial veteran population)

## Education Benefits for Louisiana Veterans

### In-State Tuition for Veterans (Choice Act)

Under the Veterans Access, Choice, and Accountability Act (VACAA), veterans using GI Bill benefits qualify for **in-state tuition** at Louisiana public schools regardless of residency duration.

**Eligibility**:
- Using Post-9/11 GI Bill or Montgomery GI Bill
- Living in Louisiana while attending school
- Enrolling within 3 years of discharge (Post-9/11 GI Bill)

**Benefit**: Eliminates out-of-state tuition surcharge (typically $8,000-12,000/year)

**Applies to**: All Louisiana public universities, colleges, and community colleges

**Example savings**:
- Louisiana State University out-of-state tuition: ~$30,000/year
- Louisiana State University in-state tuition: ~$12,000/year
- **Savings with Choice Act**: $18,000/year

### Louisiana Title 29 Tuition Exemption (State Benefit)

Louisiana's **Title 29** provides exceptional education benefits for eligible dependents of veterans - one of the best state education benefits in the nation.

**Who qualifies**:
- Children of veterans who are POW/MIA, killed in action, or 100% service-connected disabled
- Spouses of POW/MIA veterans
- Surviving spouses and children of deceased veterans

**Benefit**: Covers **all tuition and school-imposed fees** at Louisiana public universities and colleges (does not cover student-imposed fees)

**Duration**: Good for four years of schooling to be completed in not more than five years, or until the student reaches age 25 (no age limit for surviving spouses)

**Value**: $10,000-15,000/year per dependent at major universities
- **Total value**: $40,000-60,000 per dependent over 4 years

**How to apply**: Contact Louisiana Department of Veterans Affairs and your university's veteran services office

This is one of Louisiana's most valuable but often overlooked veteran benefits - essentially free college for qualifying dependents.

### Louisiana HB167 (Gap Coverage for Disabled Veterans)

Veterans living in Louisiana using Chapter 33 (Post-9/11) GI Bill benefits with **less than 100% GI Bill coverage** AND a **VA service-connected disability** may qualify for additional coverage of tuition and fees under Louisiana's HB167.

**Example**: If you have 80% Post-9/11 GI Bill eligibility and a 30% service-connected disability, Louisiana may cover the 20% gap.

**Contact**: Your university veteran services office to apply

## Employment Resources for Louisiana Veterans

### State Hiring Preference

Louisiana law grants veterans significant advantages in state employment.

**What you get**:
- **5 to 10 points added** to passing scores on civil service examinations
- **Absolute appointment preference** within a given grade
- **Higher retention standing** in case of layoffs
- **Direct hire authority** (no exam required in certain circumstances)

**Direct hire authority**: A state employment hiring authority may hire a veteran for a probationary appointment without requiring a civil service examination, provided the veteran meets the job's minimum qualifications. The veteran must have been honorably discharged in the last 12 months and have served at least 90 days of active duty (other than for training).

**Who qualifies**:
- Veterans who served honorably in the U.S. Armed Forces
- Spouses, surviving spouses, and surviving parents of veterans (in certain circumstances)
- Louisiana resident

**How it works**: If you meet minimum qualifications and pass the exam (if required), your veteran status gives you priority over non-veteran applicants with similar scores.

**Where to apply**: [jobs.la.gov](https://jobs.la.gov) or contact Louisiana Department of State Civil Service

**Major state employers**:
- Louisiana Department of Corrections
- Louisiana Department of Transportation and Development (DOTD)
- Louisiana Department of Wildlife and Fisheries
- Louisiana State Police
- State universities and colleges
- Louisiana National Guard (civilian positions)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Enroll in VA healthcare immediately** - Louisiana has 3 VA medical centers and multiple clinics - excellent coverage
2. **Use in-state tuition benefits** - Save $10,000-18,000/year at Louisiana public universities with Choice Act
3. **Explore Title 29 benefits** - Free college tuition for qualifying dependents (one of best state benefits)
4. **Leverage state hiring preference** - Veterans get 5-10 point preference and direct hire authority for state jobs
5. **Target military installations and major employers** - Barksdale AFB, Fort Johnson, oil & gas, petrochemical industries offer thousands of jobs
6. **Connect with your parish Veterans Service Officer** - Free help claiming every benefit you've earned

Louisiana genuinely supports its veteran population with tangible, valuable benefits - especially strong VA healthcare access, excellent education benefits (Title 29), and substantial employment opportunities in oil & gas, petrochemical, defense, and state government sectors.
