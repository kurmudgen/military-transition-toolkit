## 1. QUICK COMPARISON OVERVIEW

## 2. TEXAS: #1 STATE FOR VETERANS NATIONALLY

**Overall Ranking: #1 in U.S.**

### PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS

**100% Disabled Veterans:**
- **FULL EXEMPTION** from ALL property taxes
- Includes school, county, city, special district taxes
- No property tax bill whatsoever
- Applies to one primary residence

**Real-World Value:**
- $350,000 home in Dallas County
- Normal annual property taxes: ~$7,000-$8,000
- With 100% disability: **$0**
- **Annual savings: $7,000-$8,000**
- **Lifetime savings: $210,000-$240,000 over 30 years**

**Disabled Veterans (10%-90%):**
- Partial exemptions based on percentage:
  - 10-29%: $5,000 of assessed value
  - 30-49%: $7,500 of assessed value
  - 50-69%: $10,000 of assessed value
  - 70-100%: $12,000 of assessed value (or full exemption)

**Over-65 Veterans:**
- **$12,000 additional exemption** when you turn 65
- Stacks with disability exemption

**Surviving Spouses:**
- Exemption continues for widow/widower
- One of the BEST survivor benefits in the country

### EDUCATION BENEFITS (HAZLEWOOD ACT)

**THE NATION'S BEST EDUCATION BENEFIT**

**Hazlewood Act:**
- **Up to 150 credit hours FREE** at Texas public universities
- Covers tuition and most fees
- Can attend UT Austin, Texas A&M, UT Dallas, etc.
- Worth $80,000-$120,000 total

**Eligibility:**
- Texas resident for 12 consecutive months
- Honorably discharged
- Designated Texas as home of record OR entered service in Texas

**Transferable to Dependents:**
- Can transfer ALL 150 hours to children
- Use your GI Bill, give Hazlewood to kids
- Children can attend ANY Texas public university free

**Hazlewood Legacy Act (for Children):**
- Children of 100% disabled or deceased veterans get their own 150 hours
- Does NOT use parent's Hazlewood hours
- Can stack: Parent's hours + Legacy hours = 300 total hours for family

**Real-World Value:**
- UT Austin: $11,000/year tuition
- 4 years (120 hours): **$44,000 saved**
- Transfer to child: **Another $44,000**
- **Total family value: $88,000+**

### INCOME TAX

**NO STATE INCOME TAX**
- Military retirement: **Not taxed**
- VA disability: **Not taxed** (federal law)
- Civilian income: **Not taxed**

**Annual Savings vs. California:**
- $50,000 military pension
- CA tax: ~$1,500-$2,000
- TX tax: **$0**
- **Savings: $1,500-$2,000/year**

### OTHER BENEFITS

**Employment Preference:**
- Veterans preference for state jobs
- Many cities have veterans hiring programs

**Free License Plates:**
- Veteran plates available
- Disabled veteran plates (free)

**Property Tax Deferral:**
- Can defer property taxes at low interest if needed

**Hunting/Fishing:**
- Discounted licenses for veterans

### WHO SHOULD CHOOSE TEXAS

✅ 100% disabled veterans (best property tax exemption in nation)
✅ Veterans with children (Hazlewood is unbeatable)
✅ Military retirees (no income tax + strong benefits)
✅ Veterans wanting strong job market (Austin, Dallas, Houston, San Antonio)

**Best Cities:**
- San Antonio (Military City USA, huge veteran population)
- Austin (tech jobs, UT Austin for Hazlewood)
- Dallas/Fort Worth (job market, affordable suburbs)
- Houston (energy sector jobs, Texas Medical Center)
- El Paso (low cost of living, Fort Bliss proximity)

## 3. FLORIDA: #2 STATE FOR VETERANS

**Overall Ranking: #2 in U.S.**

### PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS

**100% Disabled Veterans:**
- **FULL EXEMPTION** from property taxes
- Includes all county, city, school taxes
- Must be FL resident
- Applies to homestead property

**Real-World Value:**
- $300,000 home in Hillsborough County (Tampa)
- Normal taxes: ~$4,500/year
- With 100% disability: **$0**
- **Annual savings: $4,500**

**10% or Higher Disability + Combat Veteran:**
- **$5,000 additional homestead exemption**
- Stacks with standard homestead exemption

**Disabled Veterans (less than 100%):**
- Percentage-based discount on ad valorem taxes
- Varies by county

**Homestead Exemption (All Residents):**
- **$50,000 exemption** for all homeowners
- First $25,000 off total value
- Second $25,000 off assessed value above $50,000
- Veterans get this PLUS veteran exemptions

### INCOME TAX

**NO STATE INCOME TAX**
- Military retirement: Not taxed
- VA disability: Not taxed
- Civilian income: Not taxed
- Social Security: Not taxed

**Value: Same as Texas - save $1,000-$5,000/year depending on income**

### EDUCATION BENEFITS

**Florida GI Bill Scholarship:**
- Free tuition at Florida public colleges/universities
- For honorably discharged Florida veterans
- Does not cover fees (~$1,000-$2,000/year)

**Value:**
- University of Florida tuition: ~$6,000/year
- 4 years: **$24,000 saved**

### OTHER BENEFITS

**Homestead Protection:**
- Property cannot be seized by creditors (strong asset protection)
- Valuable for business owners/entrepreneurs

**Wartime Veterans' Preference:**
- Hiring preference for state jobs

**Driver License:**
- Veteran designation available
- Disabled veteran plates (free parking at meters in some cities)

**Hunting/Fishing:**
- Free licenses for 100% disabled veterans

### WHO SHOULD CHOOSE FLORIDA

✅ 100% disabled veterans (full property exemption)
✅ Military retirees (no income tax, warm weather)
✅ Veterans seeking no state income tax
✅ Veterans who value homestead protection
✅ Retirees (no tax on Social Security + warm climate)

**Best Cities:**
- Tampa (MacDill AFB, veteran services, job market)
- Jacksonville (NAS Jax, large veteran population, growing city)
- Orlando (tourism jobs, VA hospital, central location)
- Pensacola (NAS Pensacola, lower cost of living, beach)
- Miami (international jobs, VA healthcare, diverse)

**Read the complete Florida guide for full details**

## 4. TENNESSEE: TAX-FREE HAVEN

**Overall Ranking: Top 10 Nationally**

### INCOME TAX

**NO STATE INCOME TAX**
- Military retirement: Not taxed
- Wages: Not taxed
- VA disability: Not taxed

**Note:** TN used to tax dividends/interest (Hall Tax) but **eliminated it in 2021**
- Now TRUE zero income tax state

### PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS

**100% Disabled Veterans:**
- **$175,000 of appraised value exempt**
- Applies to primary residence

**Real-World Value:**
- $200,000 home in Nashville
- Tax rate: ~$3.00 per $100
- Normal tax: $6,000/year
- After $175,000 exemption: Only $750/year
- **Savings: $5,250/year**

**Disabled Veterans (any percentage):**
- $75,000 exemption for property owned by disabled veteran

**Surviving Spouses:**
- Exemption continues if veteran was receiving it at time of death

### EDUCATION BENEFITS

**Helping Heroes Grant:**
- Free tuition and fees at TN public colleges
- For post-9/11 veterans
- Covers tuition not covered by federal GI Bill
- Allows stacking: Use GI Bill for housing, TN pays tuition

**Valor Act:**
- Free community college for veterans

### OTHER BENEFITS

**Sales Tax Holiday:**
- Disabled veterans can shop sales-tax-free during certain periods

**Employment:**
- Veterans preference in state hiring

### WHO SHOULD CHOOSE TENNESSEE

✅ Military retirees (no income tax)
✅ 100% disabled veterans (strong property exemption)
✅ Veterans seeking low cost of living + no taxes
✅ Music/healthcare/logistics industry workers (TN specialties)

**Best Cities:**
- Nashville (growing tech hub, music city, VA hospital)
- Memphis (FedEx jobs, VA hospital, low cost of living)
- Chattanooga (fiber internet, outdoor lifestyle, low cost)
- Knoxville (University of TN, Oak Ridge jobs, mountains)

## 5. VIRGINIA: COMPREHENSIVE BENEFITS

**Overall Ranking: Top 10 Nationally**

### INCOME TAX

**Military Retirement Subtraction:**
- Up to **$40,000 of military retirement income exempt** (age 55+)
- Under 55: Up to $20,000 exempt if totally disabled

**VA Disability:**
- 100% exempt (federal law)

**Example (Age 55+):**
- $50,000 military pension
- $40,000 exempt, $10,000 taxable
- VA tax on $10,000: ~$500-$575
- **Savings: ~$2,000/year vs. full taxation**

### PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS

**100% Disabled Veterans:**
- **Varies by locality** (cities/counties set own exemptions)
- Many jurisdictions offer full or partial exemptions
- Examples:
  - Fairfax County: Up to $10,000 exemption
  - Virginia Beach: Partial exemption
  - Richmond: Varies

**Check your specific county/city for details**

### EDUCATION BENEFITS

**Virginia Military Survivors and Dependents Education Program (VMSDEP):**
- **Free tuition at Virginia public colleges**
- For dependents of:
  - 100% service-connected disabled veterans
  - Veterans killed in action
  - POW/MIA
- Covers undergraduate tuition (not fees)

**Value:**
- UVA tuition: ~$18,000/year
- Virginia Tech tuition: ~$13,000/year
- 4 years: **$52,000-$72,000**

**Purple Heart Recipients:**
- Children get free in-state tuition at state schools

### OTHER BENEFITS

**Property Tax Relief for Elderly/Disabled:**
- Additional relief programs by locality

**Hiring Preference:**
- Strong veterans preference for state jobs
- Virginia has many federal contractors (veteran-friendly hiring)

**Free Hunting/Fishing:**
- For 100% disabled veterans

### WHO SHOULD CHOOSE VIRGINIA

✅ Veterans with children (VMSDEP is excellent)
✅ Military retirees age 55+ (strong income tax break)
✅ Veterans seeking federal contracting jobs (Pentagon, Hampton Roads, Quantico)
✅ 100% disabled in localities with good exemptions

**Best Cities:**
- Virginia Beach (Oceana, huge military community, beach)
- Norfolk (Naval Station Norfolk, veteran-heavy)
- Richmond (state capital, growing tech)
- Arlington (Pentagon, federal contractors, high salaries)

## 6. GEORGIA: STRONG PROPERTY TAX BENEFITS

**Overall Ranking: Top 15 Nationally**

### PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS

**100% Disabled Veterans:**
- **$110,572 exemption** from assessed value (2024 amount, adjusts yearly)
- Applies to state, county, municipal, and school taxes

**Real-World Value:**
- $200,000 home
- Exemption: $110,572
- Taxable value: $89,428
- At 1% rate: Save ~$1,100/year

**Disabled Veterans (Any Percentage):**
- $91,513 standard homestead exemption

**Surviving Spouses:**
- Exemption continues for widow/widower

### INCOME TAX

**Military Retirement Pay:**
- Up to **$35,000 exempt** for retirees age 62+
- Up to **$17,500 exempt** for retirees age 60-61
- No exemption under age 60

**Future:** GA is phasing in increases to these amounts

**Example (Age 65):**
- $50,000 pension
- $35,000 exempt, $15,000 taxable
- Tax savings: ~$1,700/year

### EDUCATION BENEFITS

**HERO Scholarship:**
- Free tuition at Georgia public colleges/universities
- For children of disabled veterans or those killed in action
- Covers tuition and fees

**Georgia Military College State Service Scholarship:**
- Free tuition for Georgia National Guard members

### OTHER BENEFITS

**Free Hunting/Fishing:**
- For 100% disabled veterans

**License Plates:**
- Disabled veteran plates with parking privileges

### WHO SHOULD CHOOSE GEORGIA

✅ Military retirees age 62+ (decent income exemption)
✅ 100% disabled veterans (strong property exemption)
✅ Veterans seeking job growth (Atlanta metro is booming)
✅ Veterans with families (lower cost of living than VA/NC)

**Best Cities:**
- Atlanta (job market, Emory VA, airport hub)
- Columbus (Fort Benning/Moore, military community)
- Savannah (port jobs, historic city, beach access)
- Augusta (Fort Eisenhower, Charlie Norwood VA)

## 7. NORTH CAROLINA & SOUTH CAROLINA COMPARISON

### **NORTH CAROLINA**

**Property Tax:**
- **100% disabled:** $45,000 exemption (applies to first $45K of home value)
- Disabled veterans: Varies by county (typically $10,000-$25,000)

**Income Tax:**
- **Up to $6,370 of military retirement exempt** (2024)
- Modest compared to other states

**Education:**
- NC National Guard Scholarship (for Guard members)
- Scholarship for children of deceased/disabled veterans

**Best For:** Veterans seeking moderate cost of living + military bases (Fort Liberty, Camp Lejeune, Cherry Point)

**Best Cities:**
- Fayetteville (Fort Liberty, large veteran population)
- Jacksonville (Camp Lejeune, coastal)
- Raleigh/Durham (Research Triangle jobs, VA hospital)

---

### **SOUTH CAROLINA**

**Property Tax:**
- **100% disabled:** Full exemption on first **$50,000** of home value
- All honorably discharged veterans: $50,000 exemption

**Income Tax:**
- **Up to $17,500 military retirement exempt**
- Better than NC

**Education:**
- Free tuition at SC state colleges for children of 100% disabled/deceased veterans
- LIFE Scholarship (merit-based) available to veteran dependents

**Property Tax Comparison:**
- SC offers ALL veterans $50,000 exemption (not just disabled)
- Better than NC for non-disabled veterans

**Best Cities:**
- Charleston (Joint Base Charleston, historic, growing)
- Columbia (state capital, Fort Jackson proximity)
- Greenville (BMW, growing economy, low cost)

**WINNER: South Carolina has better benefits than North Carolina**

## 12. DECISION MATRIX & ACTION PLAN

### **DECISION TREE**

**Are you 100% service-connected disabled?**
→ YES: Choose **Texas or Florida** (full property exemption + no income tax)
→ NO: Continue

**Are you a military retiree?**
→ YES: Choose **Texas, Florida, or Tennessee** (no income tax saves most)
→ NO: Continue

**Do you have children who will attend college?**
→ YES: Choose **Texas** (Hazlewood) or **Virginia** (VMSDEP)
→ NO: Continue

**What's your priority?**
→ Lowest taxes: **TX, FL, TN** (no income tax)
→ Job market: **TX, FL, GA, VA**
→ Cost of living: **TN, MS, AR**
→ Warm weather: **FL, TX, LA**

### **ACTION PLAN**

**STEP 1: Calculate Your Potential Savings (Use Online Calculators)**

**Property Tax Savings:**
- Find property tax rate in target city/county
- Apply exemption amount
- Calculate annual savings

**Example (100% disabled, $250K home):**
- TX: $0 tax (save ~$6,000/year)
- FL: $0 tax (save ~$3,500/year)
- TN: ~$750/year (save ~$4,500/year)

**Income Tax Savings:**
- Calculate state tax on pension/income
- Apply exemptions
- Compare to zero-tax states

**STEP 2: Visit Target States**

□ Spend 1-2 weeks in top 2-3 states
□ Check job market, cost of living, VA healthcare access
□ Talk to local veterans organizations
□ Research neighborhoods

**STEP 3: Establish Residency**

□ Driver's license (usually requires proof of address)
□ Vehicle registration
□ Voter registration
□ Update DEERS/VA records

**STEP 4: File for Benefits**

□ Property tax exemption (file with county tax assessor)
□ Income tax exemption (file on state return)
□ Education benefits (apply through state veterans office or university)
□ Veteran license plates

**Timeline:** Most benefits require 6-12 months residency before applying
