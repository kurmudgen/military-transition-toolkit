# Arizona vs Nevada Veterans Comparison: Complete Guide 2025

Arizona and Nevada both offer excellent financial benefits for military retirees with different tax approaches, both warm climates, and growing veteran communities. These southwestern states are increasingly popular destinations for veterans seeking retirement. This guide provides comprehensive comparison for your relocation decision.

## Military Retirement Tax Treatment

### Arizona: Military Retirement Fully Exempt

Arizona provides complete military retirement tax exemption:

**Arizona Military Retirement Benefits:**
- Military pension: 100% exempt from state income tax
- VA disability compensation: 100% exempt
- State income tax rate: 2.55%-4.5% (progressive)
- Effective tax on military retirement: 0%

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Military retirement tax: $0
- Other income tax: $780 (at 3.9% effective rate)
- **Total state tax: $780**

### Nevada: No State Income Tax at All

Nevada offers the ultimate tax benefit - NO state income tax on any income:

**Nevada Tax Benefits:**
- Military pension: 0% state tax (no state income tax)
- VA disability compensation: 0% tax
- All other income: 0% state tax
- Effective tax rate: 0% on all income
- Funded by: Gaming, sales tax, property tax

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Military retirement tax: $0
- Other income tax: $0
- **Total state tax: $0**

**Tax Winner: Nevada** (No state income tax vs Arizona's tax on other income)
**Annual savings choosing Nevada: $780+ on $65,000 income**

## Final Verdict

### Choose Arizona if:
- You want lowest cost of living (2-3% cheaper)
- You prefer larger veteran community
- You value better VA healthcare access
- You want more diverse job market
- You prefer higher average salaries
- You want secure water supply long-term

### Choose Nevada if:
- You want NO state income tax (even on other income)
- You enjoy entertainment/Las Vegas lifestyle
- You prefer smaller veteran population
- You value gaming/entertainment options
- You want slightly more upscale lifestyle options

**The Numbers:** Arizona offers better overall value for most veterans due to:
- Lower cost of living (2-3%)
- Better job market and salaries
- Superior VA healthcare access
- Larger, more established veteran communities
- Better water security

The tax advantage of Nevada (no tax on other income) amounts to $780-$930 annually for a retiree with modest additional income. However, Arizona's cost of living advantage (2-3%) typically offsets this benefit for the average veteran.

**For most military retirees, Arizona is the superior choice** due to comprehensive advantages in VA healthcare, cost of living, job market, and community infrastructure. Nevada appeals primarily to high-earning retirees seeking maximum tax efficiency and entertainment-focused lifestyles.
