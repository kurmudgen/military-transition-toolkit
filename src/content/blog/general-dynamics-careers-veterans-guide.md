# General Dynamics Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

General Dynamics builds the machines that project American power: nuclear submarines, Abrams tanks, Stryker vehicles, and the IT systems that connect it all. For veterans—especially those from the Navy, Army armor/infantry, or IT fields—General Dynamics offers careers directly connected to your service.

With four major business units spanning shipbuilding, combat vehicles, aerospace, and IT services, GD provides diverse opportunities across the defense spectrum.

## Understanding General Dynamics

### Business Structure

General Dynamics comprises four groups:

**Aerospace:**
- Gulfstream business jets

**Combat Systems:**
- Land vehicles and weapons
- Abrams tanks, Stryker vehicles
- Munitions and guns

**Marine Systems:**
- Nuclear submarines (Electric Boat)
- Surface combatants (NASSCO, Bath)
- Ship repair

**Technologies:**
- GDIT (IT services)
- Mission Systems
- C4ISR

### Company Profile

**General Dynamics by the numbers:**
- $39+ billion annual revenue
- 100,000+ employees
- 5th largest defense contractor
- Diverse portfolio

### Veteran Value Proposition

GD values military service because:
- You've operated GD equipment
- You understand the mission
- Security culture familiarity
- Technical training applies

## General Dynamics Hiring Process

### Step 1: Identify Business Unit

Determine target area:
- Shipbuilding (Electric Boat, BIW, NASSCO)
- Land Systems
- GDIT
- Mission Systems

Each has separate career portal.

### Step 2: Apply

**Where to apply:**
- gd.com/careers (general)
- gdeb.com (Electric Boat)
- gdbiw.com (Bath Iron Works)
- gdit.com/careers (GDIT)
- gdms.com (Mission Systems)
- gdland.com (Land Systems)

### Step 3: Screening

Review for:
- Basic qualifications
- Clearance eligibility
- Experience match
- Physical requirements (trades)

### Step 4: Interviews

Process varies by unit:

**Trades positions:**
- Skills assessment
- Physical capability
- Background check

**Professional positions:**
- Phone screen
- Technical interview
- Panel interview

### Step 5: Assessment

May include:
- Trade skills test
- Technical assessment
- Drug screening
- Background investigation

### Step 6: Offer

If selected:
- Compensation discussion
- Benefits overview
- Start date
- Clearance processing if needed
