[MAIN CONTENT - 7-8 minutes]

**Section 1: Your Market Value (90 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Salary comparison by agency level]

**31B civilian salary ranges:**
- Local police: $55,000-$70,000/year average
- State trooper: $60,000-$80,000/year
- Federal law enforcement: $73,000-$95,000/year (GS-11 to GS-13)
- Corrections officer: $45,000-$65,000/year
- Court security: $50,000-$70,000/year

**Reality check:** The average 31B salary data shows $35K-$64K, but that includes part-time and lower-tier positions. Full-time sworn law enforcement starts at $55K minimum.

**Top-paying markets:**
- California: $70,000-$110,000/year (LAPD, LASD, Bay Area agencies)
- Washington state: $65,000-$90,000/year
- New York City: $60,000-$85,000/year
- Texas: $55,000-$75,000/year

**Your advantage:** Many agencies have critical shortages and are actively recruiting veterans with military police experience.

**Section 2: Federal Law Enforcement (The High Pay Path) (120 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Federal agency logos with salary ranges]

**U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP)**
- Border Patrol Agent: $73,000-$95,000/year (GL-5 to GL-7 start)
- Excellent benefits, federal pension
- Hiring surge happening now
- 31B experience highly valued
- Locations: Southwest border states primarily

**U.S. Marshals Service**
- Deputy U.S. Marshal: $73,000-$95,000/year
- Fugitive apprehension, court security, witness protection
- Competitive but 31B background fits perfectly
- Federal benefits and pension

**Federal Bureau of Prisons (BOP)**
- Correctional Officer: $45,000-$65,000/year
- Easiest federal hire for 31Bs
- Path to Special Investigative Agent ($73K-$95K)
- Locations nationwide at federal prisons

**Transportation Security Administration (TSA)**
- Federal Air Marshal: $50,000-$95,000/year
- TSA Officer: $40,000-$55,000/year (easier entry)
- Travel benefits

**DEA, ATF, Secret Service**
- Special Agent: $73,000-$95,000+ (GS-11 start)
- More competitive, often require degree
- 31B experience + degree = very competitive
- Timeline: 6-12 month hiring process

**VA Police**
- Police Officer at VA facilities: $50,000-$70,000/year
- Federal benefits, stable hours
- Less competitive than other federal

**Application tips:**
- USAJobs.gov for all federal positions
- Veterans' preference adds 5-10 points to score
- Hiring process: 6-12 months (be patient)
- Most require U.S. citizenship

**Section 3: State and Local Police (The Traditional Path) (90 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Map showing best-paying states]

**PAYS Program Agencies (Guaranteed Interview):**
- Los Angeles Police Department
- New York City Police Department
- Las Vegas Metro Police Department
- Many others participate

**What PAYS means:** After leaving service, you're guaranteed a job interview. Not a guarantee of hire, but you skip the initial screening.

**Major city departments:**

**LAPD (Los Angeles):**
- Starting: $74,000/year
- After 3 years: $100,000+/year
- Hiring bonus: Up to $10,000
- Critical need for officers

**NYPD (New York):**
- Starting: $58,580/year
- After 5.5 years: $100,000+/year
- Free academy training
- Excellent benefits

**Chicago PD:**
- Starting: $55,000/year
- After 18 months: $95,000+/year
- Pension system

**Houston PD:**
- Starting: $60,000/year
- Lower cost of living than coastal cities

**State Trooper Positions:**
- California Highway Patrol: $80,000-$120,000/year
- Texas DPS: $60,000-$85,000/year
- New York State Police: $65,000-$90,000/year
- Florida Highway Patrol: $50,000-$70,000/year

**Requirements:**
- Police academy (4-6 months, agency pays you)
- Usually some college credits required
- Physical fitness test
- Background investigation (must be clean)
- Psychological evaluation

**Section 4: Corrections - The Fast Hire Option (90 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Corrections salary and advancement path]

This is the path nobody talks about, but it's the fastest hire for 31Bs.

**Federal Bureau of Prisons:**
- Correctional Officer: $45,000-$65,000/year
- Hiring constantly
- 31B experience = preferred candidate
- 6-month timeline from application to start
- Path to Special Investigative Agent ($73K+)

**State Corrections:**
- Salary: $40,000-$60,000/year (varies by state)
- Usually hiring immediately
- Benefits and pension
- Shift work (nights/weekends)

**County Jails:**
- Deputy Sheriff (jail division): $45,000-$65,000/year
- Often the entry point to patrol
- Many departments promote from jail to road patrol after 1-2 years

**Why corrections makes sense:**
- Fastest hire (30-60 days possible)
- Your 31B experience translates directly
- Stepping stone to patrol or federal
- Stable income while you apply elsewhere

**Reality check:** Corrections is hard work. Dangerous, stressful, and often thankless. But it's stable employment and a path to better positions.

**Section 5: Court Security and Specialized Roles (60 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Specialized law enforcement positions]

**U.S. Court Security Officers:**
- Salary: $50,000-$70,000/year
- Protect federal courthouses
- Less stressful than patrol
- Federal contractor positions (not federal employee)

**Campus Police:**
- University police: $45,000-$65,000/year
- State university = good benefits
- Lower stress than city patrol
- Summers often slower

**Hospital Security/Police:**
- Hospital police officer: $45,000-$60,000/year
- Dealing with psych patients and aggressive visitors
- Growing field

**Private Security Leadership:**
- Security director: $60,000-$90,000/year
- Corporate security manager: $65,000-$95,000/year
- Your 31B leadership experience matters here

**Section 6: Requirements and Certifications (60 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Checklist of requirements]

**Most agencies require:**
- High school diploma minimum
- Some college credits (30-60 credits common)
- Age 21+ at hire
- U.S. citizen
- Valid driver's license
- Clean criminal record
- Credit check (no major issues)

**Disqualifying factors:**
- Felony convictions
- Domestic violence convictions
- Serious financial problems (collections, bankruptcy)
- Drug use within certain timeframes
- Dishonorable discharge

**Certifications that help:**
- State Police Officer Standards and Training (POST) certification
- EMT certification (some agencies require)
- Degree in Criminal Justice (not required but helps)

**Use your GI Bill:**
- Many officers get degrees while working
- Criminal Justice, Public Administration, or any bachelor's
- Helps with promotion to sergeant, lieutenant, detective

**Section 7: The Reality of Law Enforcement Life (60 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Work schedule reality]

Let's be honest about what you're getting into:

**Schedule:**
- Shift work: Days, nights, weekends, holidays
- 10-12 hour shifts common
- Rotating schedules disrupt sleep
- Overtime often mandatory (but paid well)

**Stress:**
- Dealing with people on their worst days
- Public scrutiny and body cameras
- Lawsuits and complaints
- Political pressures

**Physical:**
- Physically demanding well into your 40s-50s
- Foot chases, fights, long periods in vehicles
- Injuries common

**But also:**
- Strong sense of purpose
- Brotherhood/sisterhood similar to military
- Good benefits and pension (25-30 year retirement common)
- Job security

**Who should do this:**
- You want to help your community
- You can handle stress and criticism
- You're okay with shift work
- You want a pension retirement

**Who shouldn't:**
- You have PTSD that isn't managed
- You can't handle confrontation well
- You need consistent M-F schedule
- You're burned out on enforcement

**Section 8: 90-Day Action Plan (60 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Timeline with action items]

**Days 1-30:**
- Research agencies in your target area
- Check PAYS program agencies
- Start college courses if you need credits
- Get any documents (DD-214, certifications)

**Days 31-60:**
- Apply to 5-10 agencies simultaneously
- Take civil service exams
- Physical fitness prep (running, push-ups, sit-ups)
- Budget for background investigation costs

**Days 61-90:**
- Complete applications and testing
- Background interviews
- Polygraph examination (for federal)
- Medical and psychological evaluations

**Timeline reality:** Federal takes 6-12 months. Local/state takes 3-6 months.
