# Best Companies for 68X Mental Health Specialists

## BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

68X Mental Health Specialists transition to premium positions at hospital systems, community mental health centers, telehealth companies, and corporate employee assistance programs. Compensation as Licensed Mental Health Counselor ranges $48,000-$85,000+ depending on setting, with significant upside in private practice ($60,000-$150,000+). Top employers include Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, Kaiser Permanente, UnitedHealth, Mindstrong, Talkspace, and large hospital systems. Your military experience in crisis intervention, trauma response, and mental health assessment positions you for leadership roles in mental health operations and clinical services earning $85,000-$180,000+.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What's the best path for earning potential?**
A: Private practice ownership ($150,000-$300,000+) or telehealth director roles ($150,000-$180,000+). Telehealth offers fastest path (5-7 years); private practice higher ceiling but more risk.

**Q: Should I work for hospital/clinic or telehealth?**
A: **Hospital**: Stability, benefits, clinical prestige. **Telehealth**: Remote flexibility, faster growth, better compensation, higher advancement ceiling.

**Q: Can I work for VA and build private practice concurrently?**
A: Yes, common model. VA part-time + private practice can exceed $120,000-$200,000 combined income.

**Q: How quickly can I advance to supervisor roles?**
A: Typically 5-7 years with demonstrated leadership. VA faster track due to federal ladder. Telehealth fastest (3-5 years to manager roles).

**Q: What certifications increase earning potential?**
A: Advanced trauma (EMDR, trauma-focused CBT): +15-20%. Substance abuse specialist: +10-15%. Specializations provide meaningful earning advantage.

**Q: Are mental health positions remote?**
A: Telehealth: 100% remote. VA/Hospital: On-site. Private practice: Flexible. Increasing hybrid options post-2024.
