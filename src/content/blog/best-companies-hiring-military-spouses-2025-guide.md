# Best Companies Hiring Military Spouses (2025): Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

Hundreds of major employers actively recruit military spouses through the **Military Spouse Employment Partnership (MSEP)** and independent programs. These companies offer **remote work options, PCS flexibility, career development**, and culture that values military family experience. MSEP partners have collectively hired **275,000+ military spouses** since 2011. This guide highlights the best employers across industries and explains how to leverage military spouse programs to get hired.

## The Military Spouse Career Challenge

Some companies see military spouses as risky hires. Smart companies see:
- Proven adaptability
- Strong work ethic
- Diverse perspectives
- Reliable, mission-oriented employees

The companies in this guide have figured this out. They actively recruit military spouses because they've seen the ROI.

## MSEP: Military Spouse Employment Partnership

### What is MSEP?

The Military Spouse Employment Partnership connects military spouses with employers who commit to:
- Recruiting and hiring military spouses
- Offering career advancement opportunities
- Providing flexible/portable employment options
- Supporting work-life balance

### By the Numbers

- **700+** partner employers
- **275,000+** military spouses hired since 2011
- **All industries** represented
- **Remote and in-person** positions

### How to Use MSEP

1. **Create profile:** myseco.militaryonesource.mil
2. **Search jobs:** MSEP job board
3. **Apply directly:** To partner companies
4. **Mention MSEP:** In applications when relevant

## How to Get Hired

### Strategy 1: Leverage MSEP

1. Register at myseco.militaryonesource.mil
2. Search MSEP partner jobs
3. Mention MSEP partnership in cover letter
4. Apply through both MSEP and company site

### Strategy 2: Target Specific Programs

Many companies have dedicated military spouse programs:
- Look for "military" or "military spouse" pages on career sites
- Apply through military-specific portals when available
- Reference programs in applications

### Strategy 3: Hiring Our Heroes

Apply for HOH Corporate Fellowship:
- Matches with partner companies
- 12-week fellowship with salary
- High conversion rate to full-time
- hiringourheroes.org

### Strategy 4: Career Fairs

Attend military spouse career fairs:
- Virtual fairs increasingly common
- Meet recruiters who understand military life
- Hiring Our Heroes events
- Installation-based fairs

### Application Tips

**In Your Resume:**
- Highlight adaptability and diverse experience
- Include military spouse-relevant skills
- Use keywords from job posting

**In Your Cover Letter:**
"As a military spouse, I've developed exceptional adaptability and thrive in new environments. I'm drawn to [Company] because of your commitment to military families and [specific reason related to role]."

**In Interviews:**
- Speak confidently about your background
- Emphasize what you bring, not just your spouse's service
- Ask about remote/flexibility options if important

## Industry-Specific Tips

### Technology

- Highlight any technical skills
- Certifications matter (CompTIA, AWS, etc.)
- Portfolio important for design/development
- Remote work is norm in tech

### Healthcare

- Licensure portability matters (see compact states)
- Telehealth positions often more flexible
- Non-clinical roles (admin, IT) may be more portable
- Major health systems have positions everywhere

### Finance/Banking

- Customer service is common entry point
- Certifications help (FINRA, CPA prep)
- Many remote positions available
- Career paths are clear

### Defense Contracting

- Security clearance is major asset
- Positions exist at most major installations
- Technical skills in demand
- Remote work growing
