---# 3E0X2 - Electrical Power Production Career Transition Guide

## Overview

Electrical Power Production specialists operate and maintain electrical power generation equipment, including generators, switchgear, transformers, and power distribution systems.

**Daily:** Operate power plants/generators, maintain prime power equipment, troubleshoot electrical distribution, perform preventive maintenance, monitor power quality, emergency power operations.

## Civilian Paths

**Power Plant Operator:** Utility/industrial power generation. $60K-$105K. Excellent career. Recession-proof. Union jobs.
**Stationary Engineer:** Building power systems. $55K-$95K. Commercial buildings, hospitals, universities.
**Generator Technician:** Backup power systems service. $50K-$90K. Growing field (data centers, critical facilities).
**Electrical Power Systems Technician:** Industrial power distribution. $55K-$95K. Manufacturing, utilities.

## Certifications

**Stationary Engineer License:** State-specific. Required for power plant operation. Opens $60K-$100K+ roles.
**Power Plant Operator License:** Utility company requirement. Competitive but excellent pay/benefits.
**Electrician License:** Journeyman. Complements power production expertise.

## Top Companies

Utility companies (Duke Energy, NextEra, Dominion), data center operators (Google, Microsoft, Amazon), hospitals, universities, industrial plants, generator service companies (Cummins, Caterpillar, Kohler).

## Action Plan

Get stationary engineer license or power plant operator certification. Apply to utilities, data centers, hospitals. Strong demand for power production specialists.
