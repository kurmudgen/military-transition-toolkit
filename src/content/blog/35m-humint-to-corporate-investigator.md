## Corporate Investigation Career Paths

### Position 1: Corporate Fraud Investigator

**Civilian job titles:**
- Fraud Investigator
- Corporate Investigator
- Special Investigator
- Compliance Investigator

**Salary ranges:**
- **Entry fraud investigator:** $60,000-$80,000
- **Fraud investigator (3-5 years):** $80,000-$110,000
- **Senior investigator:** $100,000-$140,000

**What translates directly:**
- Interview and interrogation skills
- Evidence analysis and assessment
- Source credibility evaluation
- Documentation and reporting
- Pattern recognition and analysis
- Covert investigation techniques
- Information verification and cross-referencing
- Risk assessment

**Certifications needed:**
- **CFE (Certified Fraud Examiner)** - Optional but valuable
  - 4 hours exam, $300-$400
  - Requires 2 years fraud investigation experience
  - Can pursue after starting role
- **Background investigator license** - State-dependent, $100-$500
- **Bachelor's degree preferred** - Not always required with 35M experience

**Reality check:**
Corporate fraud investigators investigate employee misconduct, vendor fraud, financial crimes, embezzlement, insurance fraud. Work for corporations, insurance companies, investigative firms.

Entry: $65K-$80K. Larger companies/insurance: $80K-$95K entry.

Your 35M interview skills and analysis expertise give massive advantage. You understand deception indicators and credibility assessment at sophisticated level.

Hours: Typically 40-50 hour weeks. Some travel to conduct investigations.

Work environment: Mix of office work and fieldwork. Professional but less dangerous than military operations.

**Best for:** 35M veterans wanting to leverage interviewing/analysis skills for stable civilian career.

- Highlight security clearance prominently
