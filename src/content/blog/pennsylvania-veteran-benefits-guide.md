# Pennsylvania Veteran Benefits: Complete State Programs Guide (2025)

Pennsylvania, home to over 750,000 veterans, offers significant state benefits including property tax exemptions, blind veteran benefits, and education assistance. With a long military history and strong veteran support infrastructure, Pennsylvania provides meaningful programs for those who served.

This guide covers all Pennsylvania veteran benefits with eligibility requirements and application procedures.

## Property Tax Exemptions

### Disabled Veterans Real Estate Tax Exemption

**100% Permanently and Totally Disabled Veterans:**
- **Exemption:** Complete exemption from real estate taxes
- **Coverage:** Primary residence
- **Savings:** Entire property tax bill (often $2,000-$6,000+ annually)

**Eligibility:**
- 100% permanent and total service-connected disability
- Or wartime service with non-service-connected total disability
- Own and occupy home as primary residence
- Pennsylvania resident

### Blind Veterans Exemption

**Blind Veterans:**
- Complete exemption from real estate taxes
- Must have VA-rated blindness

### Surviving Spouse Exemption

**Surviving Spouse of Qualifying Veteran:**
- Continues receiving exemption
- Must not remarry
- Must continue occupying the home

### How to Apply

1. Obtain VA letter certifying permanent and total disability
2. Contact your county Board of Assessment Appeals
3. Complete Application for Disabled Veterans Real Estate Tax Exemption
4. Submit DD-214 and VA letter
5. Apply by March 15 for current tax year

## Income Tax Benefits

### Military Retirement Pay Exclusion

**Pennsylvania Tax Benefit:**
- Military retirement pay is fully exempt from Pennsylvania income tax
- 100% exclusion for military retirees
- No age or income restrictions

**Active Duty Pay:**
- Active duty military pay is also exempt from PA income tax

### Combat Pay

**Tax-Free:**
- Combat zone pay exempt (federal and state)

## Education Benefits

### Educational Gratuity Program

**For Children of Deceased Veterans:**
- Up to $500 per term
- At Pennsylvania state or state-aided schools
- For children of veterans who died in service or from service-connected disability

### Pennsylvania National Guard Education Assistance Program

**Benefit:**
- 100% tuition at Pennsylvania state schools
- For eligible Guard members
- Must maintain satisfactory performance

### POW/MIA Dependent Education

**Children of POW/MIA:**
- Free tuition at state schools
- Full education benefits

### In-State Tuition for Veterans

**All Veterans:**
- Qualify for in-state tuition regardless of prior residency
- Effective immediately upon enrollment
- Covers all state system schools

**GI Bill Users:**
- In-state tuition guaranteed under federal law

## Vehicle Benefits

### Disabled Veteran License Plates

**Free Plates for:**
- 100% Disabled Veterans (DV plate)
- Purple Heart recipients
- Medal of Honor recipients
- Former POWs
- Legion of Merit recipients

**Additional Benefits:**
- Free registration
- Free plate
- Accessible parking (if mobility impaired)

### Special Plates Available

**Various Recognition Plates:**
- Combat Veteran plates
- Branch-specific plates
- Campaign plates (Iraq, Afghanistan, etc.)

### How to Apply

1. Visit PennDOT
2. Complete Form MV-145
3. Provide DD-214 and VA documentation

## Hunting and Fishing

### Free/Discounted Licenses

**100% Disabled Veterans:**
- Free resident hunting license
- Free resident fishing license
- Free archery/muzzleloader stamps

**Reduced Fee Licenses:**
- Disabled veterans (less than 100%) may qualify for reduced fees

### State Game Lands

**Disabled Veterans:**
- Special hunting opportunities
- Accessible hunting areas

### How to Apply

1. Visit Pennsylvania Game Commission website or agent
2. Provide VA disability documentation
3. Show Pennsylvania ID

## Employment Preferences

### State Employment Preference

**Pennsylvania Veterans' Preference:**
- 10 points added to passing civil service scores (veterans)
- Additional points for disabled veterans
- Preference in state government hiring

**How to Claim:**
- Indicate veteran status on application
- Provide DD-214
- Submit VA disability documentation if applicable

### PA CareerLink Veterans Services

**Priority of Service:**
- Veterans receive priority at PA CareerLink offices
- Dedicated veterans employment representatives
- Job search assistance
- Resume development
- Career counseling

**Locations:**
- PA CareerLink offices throughout state
- Visit pacareerlink.pa.gov

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Pennsylvania tax military retirement pay?

No. Pennsylvania fully exempts military retirement pay from state income tax.

### What disability rating do I need for property tax exemption?

100% permanent and total disability is required. Wartime veterans with non-service-connected total disability may also qualify.

### Can my children get education assistance?

Yes, through the Educational Gratuity Program if you died in service or from service-connected disability (up to $500/term).

### How many state veterans homes does Pennsylvania have?

Six, located throughout the state from Philadelphia to Erie.

### How do I get free hunting/fishing licenses?

Contact the PA Game Commission with proof of 100% service-connected disability.
