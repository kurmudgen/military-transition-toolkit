## CHAPTER 1: Property Tax Exemptions & Benefits

### Overview

California's property tax exemptions for disabled veterans can significantly reduce or eliminate your property tax burden. The exemption amounts are among the most generous in the nation, with values that increase annually with inflation.

### The Main Property Tax Benefits

1. **Basic Disabled Veterans' Exemption** ($175,298 for 2025)
2. **Low-Income Disabled Veterans' Exemption** ($262,950 for 2025)
3. **Surviving Spouse Benefits** (carryover exemptions)
4. **Homeowners' Exemption** (additional $7,000 - all homeowners)

Let's break down each benefit in detail.

---

### üí∞ DISABLED VETERANS' EXEMPTION - BASIC (California Revenue & Taxation Code Section 205)

#### Who Qualifies

‚úì Honorably discharged veteran
‚úì **100% service-connected disability rating from VA**, OR
‚úì **100% disability compensation due to Individual Unemployability (IU)**
‚úì Property is veteran's principal place of residence
‚úì California resident

#### What You Get

**For 2025: $175,298 exemption of assessed value**

This amount is adjusted annually for inflation by the California State Board of Equalization.

#### How It Works

The exemption reduces the **assessed value** of your home by $175,298, which then reduces your property tax bill.

#### Example Calculation

**Scenario:** Home in San Diego County

- Home assessed value: $700,000
- Basic exemption: -$175,298
- **Taxable value: $524,702**
- County tax rate: ~1.1%
- **Annual tax without exemption:** $7,700
- **Annual tax with exemption:** $5,772
- **Annual savings: $1,928**

**Over 20 years: $38,560 in savings**

---

### üè† LOW-INCOME DISABLED VETERANS' EXEMPTION (California Revenue & Taxation Code Section 205.5)

#### Who Qualifies

All requirements of basic exemption PLUS:

‚úì **Household income does not exceed $78,718** (for 2025 assessment year)

**Income Includes:**
- Veteran's income
- Spouse's income
- Income of any co-tenants
- **All VA benefits and Social Security benefits are included**

#### What You Get

**For 2025: $262,950 exemption of assessed value**

50% larger exemption than the basic level.

#### Example Calculation

**Scenario:** Home in Los Angeles County

- Home assessed value: $800,000
- Low-income exemption: -$262,950
- **Taxable value: $537,050**
- County tax rate: ~1.2%
- **Annual tax without exemption:** $9,600
- **Annual tax with exemption:** $6,445
- **Annual savings: $3,155**

**Over 20 years: $63,100 in savings**

üí° **Pro Tip:** The low-income exemption threshold is adjusted annually. Even if you don't qualify this year, you might qualify in future years as the threshold increases.

### üíç SURVIVING SPOUSE BENEFITS

#### Who Qualifies

Unmarried surviving spouse of:
- Qualifying disabled veteran who received the exemption at time of death
- Veteran who died of service-connected causes

Requirements:
‚úì **Have not remarried**
‚úì Property was deceased veteran's principal residence at time of death
‚úì Property remains surviving spouse's principal residence

#### What You Get

**Full continuation** of the veteran's property tax exemption:
- Same exemption amount the veteran was receiving
- Basic or low-income level (depending on veteran's qualification)
- Continues indefinitely if spouse doesn't remarry

#### Special Provisions

If the surviving spouse sells the property and purchases a new principal residence in California:
- Can transfer the **same dollar amount of exemption**
- Must apply within one year of purchasing new residence
- Unremarried status must continue

‚ö†Ô∏è **Important:** Remarriage **permanently terminates** the exemption.

---

### üè° ADDITIONAL HOMEOWNERS' EXEMPTION (All California Homeowners)

#### Who Qualifies

- ANY California homeowner
- Property is principal residence
- Owner-occupied

#### What You Get

**$7,000 reduction in assessed value** (all homeowners, not just veterans)

#### Why It Matters for Veterans

You can **stack this with** the Disabled Veterans' Exemption.

**Example Stacking:**
- Home assessed value: $700,000
- Homeowners' Exemption: -$7,000
- Low-Income DV Exemption: -$262,950
- **Total exemptions: $269,950**
- **Taxable value: $430,050**

On a $700,000 home at 1.1% tax rate:
- Tax without exemptions: $7,700
- Tax with both exemptions: $4,731
- **Total annual savings: $2,969**

### üìù ANNUAL FILING REQUIREMENTS

#### Basic Disabled Veterans' Exemption

‚úì **One-time filing** - continues automatically as long as:
- Property remains your principal residence
- You remain eligible
- You notify assessor of any changes in eligibility

#### Low-Income Disabled Veterans' Exemption

‚ö†Ô∏è **Annual filing required** - must file EVERY YEAR by February 15

**Why:** Income must be verified annually to ensure continued qualification.

**Failure to file by deadline** = loss of low-income exemption for that year (reverts to basic exemption if you qualify).

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL DEADLINES

**Filing Deadline: February 15** (for most initial applications)

**Late Filing:** Allowed in some circumstances up to filing deadline for following year, but requires:
- Good cause for late filing
- Documentation of disability dating back to intended claim year

üí° **Pro Tip:** File early (January is ideal) to ensure processing before deadline and avoid any complications.

## CHAPTER 2: College Fee Waiver & Education Benefits

### Overview

The California College Fee Waiver for Veteran Dependents is one of the most valuable education benefits in any state, potentially providing over $100,000 in free education for your children or spouse. This benefit covers tuition and mandatory fees at ANY public college or university in California.

### üéì COLLEGE FEE WAIVER FOR VETERAN DEPENDENTS (California Education Code Section 69950)

#### What Is It?

The College Fee Waiver **waives mandatory system-wide tuition and fees** at:

‚úì All **California Community Colleges** (115 campuses)
‚úì All **California State University (CSU)** campuses (23 universities)
‚úì All **University of California (UC)** campuses (10 universities)

#### Who Qualifies - Four Plans

**PLAN A** (Most Common):

**Unmarried children** of:
- Veteran who is totally service-connected disabled (100%), OR
- Veteran whose death was officially rated as service-connected

**Age requirement:** Over 14 and under 27 years old

**Spouses** of:
- Wartime veteran who is totally service-connected disabled (100%)

**Income:** **NO income restrictions**

‚ö†Ô∏è **Important:** Cannot receive Plan A and VA Chapter 35 benefits simultaneously

---

**PLAN B**:

**Children (no age limit)** of:
- Veteran with service-connected disability (any percentage), OR
- Veteran who had service-connected disability at time of death, OR
- Veteran who died of service-related causes

**Income requirement:** **Cannot exceed state poverty threshold**

**For 2025-2026 academic year: $22,273 household income limit**

**Income includes:**
- Student's adjusted gross income
- PLUS value of support provided by parents

üí° **Pro Tip:** Most families qualify under Plan A if the veteran is 100% disabled. Plan B is available for children of veterans with lower disability ratings but has strict income limits.

---

**PLAN C & D** (Less Common):

Contact County Veterans Service Office for eligibility - these plans cover specific wartime service periods and other unique circumstances.

#### Step 1: Determine Eligibility

‚ñ° Verify veteran parent's disability rating or service-connected death

- Complete school-specific fee waiver forms
- Must reapply each academic year

#### Step 5: Verify Waiver Applied

- Check tuition bill to confirm fees are waived
- Contact financial aid office if discrepancies
- Keep copy of authorization letter

## CHAPTER 3: CalVet Home Loan Program

### Overview

The CalVet Home Loan Program, administered by the California Department of Veterans Affairs, offers veterans one of the most competitive home financing options available‚Äîoften with **0% down payment**, **below-market interest rates**, and unique protections not available with conventional or even VA loans.

### üè° CALVET HOME LOAN PROGRAM

#### What Is It?

A state-funded home loan program exclusively for California veterans, offering:

‚úì **Up to 100% financing** (zero down payment)
‚úì **Below-market interest rates**
‚úì **No monthly PMI** (Private Mortgage Insurance)
‚úì **Flexible credit requirements**
‚úì **Outstanding insurance protection**

---

### WHO QUALIFIES

‚úì Honorably discharged veteran
‚úì Minimum service: **90 days active duty** (wartime or peacetime, excluding training)
‚úì California resident
‚úì Meet credit and income requirements
‚úì **No current CalVet home loan**
‚úì Property will be owner-occupied in California

üí° **Pro Tip:** CalVet does **not** have a minimum credit score requirement and manually underwrites all loans for maximum flexibility.

---

### 2025 LOAN LIMITS

**Standard Counties:** $806,500

**High-Cost Counties:** Up to $1,209,750

**High-Cost Counties Include:**
- Los Angeles
- Orange
- San Diego
- San Francisco

- Santa Clara
- Alameda
- Marin
- And others

### KEY BENEFITS

#### 1. **Zero Down Payment (0% Down)**

CalVet uses the federal VA guarantee to offer up to **100% financing**.

**Conventional Loan Comparison:**
- Conventional: 10-20% down required
- CalVet: **$0 down possible**

**Example:**
- $600,000 home purchase
- Conventional 10% down: **$60,000 required**
- CalVet 0% down: **$0 required**
- **Savings: $60,000 cash preserved**

---

#### 2. **Below-Market Interest Rates**

CalVet offers competitive rates typically **25-35 basis points (0.25%-0.35%) lower** than conventional rates.

**2025 Example Rates:**
- Conventional 30-year mortgage: ~7.0%
- CalVet rate: **~5.125%-5.375%**

**Interest Savings Example** ($500,000 loan, 30 years):

| **Loan Type** | **Interest Rate** | **Monthly Payment** | **Total Interest Paid** |
|--------------|------------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Conventional | 7.0% | $3,327 | $698,720 |

**Lifetime savings: Over $200,000**

---

#### 3. **No Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)**

Conventional loans with less than 20% down require PMI ($100-$300/month typically).

CalVet loans **never require PMI**, even with 0% down.

**Annual savings: $1,200-$3,600**

---

#### 4. **Flexible Credit Requirements**

- **No minimum credit score requirement**
- Manual underwriting for all applications
- Evaluates full financial picture, not just credit score
- More forgiving of past financial issues

üí° **Pro Tip:** If you've been denied conventional financing due to credit score, CalVet may still approve you.

---

#### 5. **Outstanding Insurance Protection**

CalVet provides **exceptional insurance coverage** included with your loan:

**Fire and Hazard Insurance:**
- CalVet provides comprehensive coverage
- Competitive rates
- Streamlined claims process

**Disaster Coverage:**
- **Earthquake insurance** (optional but available)
- **Flood insurance** (where required)
- Coverage often better than standalone policies

**Life Insurance Protection:**
- Decreasing term life insurance available
- Pays off loan balance if you pass away
- Protects your family

#### What It Means

Veterans who pass state civil service examinations receive **preferential placement at the top rank** of the eligibility list, giving you significant advantage in state hiring.

### WHO QUALIFIES FOR VETERANS' PREFERENCE

---

### HOW VETERANS' PREFERENCE WORKS IN CALIFORNIA

#### Effective January 1, 2014: **Rank Placement System** (No Longer Points)

Previously, California added points to veterans' exam scores. Now, qualifying veterans who **pass the examination** are placed at the **top rank** of the resulting eligibility list.

#### What This Means

**Traditional Scoring (Non-Veterans):**
- Take civil service exam
- Ranked by score (highest to lowest)
- Agencies hire from top of list

**With Veterans' Preference:**
- Pass the exam (any passing score)
- Automatically placed in **Top Rank**
- Hired before non-veterans with higher scores

**Example:**
- Veteran scores 75 (passing)
- Non-veteran scores 95 (higher)
- **Veteran is ranked above non-veteran** due to preference

üí° **Pro Tip:** You don't need the highest score‚Äîyou just need to pass. Veterans' Preference does the rest.

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT LIMITATIONS

**Veterans' Preference is NOT granted once you achieve permanent civil service status.**

This means:
- Preference applies to **entrance exams only**
- Once you're hired into permanent civil service position, preference no longer applies to promotional exams
- Use your preference strategically when first entering state employment

**Dishonorably Discharged Veterans:**
- NOT eligible for Veterans' Preference
- Must have honorable discharge or general under honorable conditions

‚úì **Open examinations** (anyone can apply)
‚úì **Open, non-promotional examinations**

‚ùå **NOT applicable to:**
