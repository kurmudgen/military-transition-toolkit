## Your Path from Combat Medicine to High-Paying Healthcare Careers
**By Jacob, Navy E6 | Formation Labs**

## TOP 10 CAREER PATHS FOR 68W COMBAT MEDICS

### 1. REGISTERED NURSE (RN)
**Salary:** $65K-$95K | **Specialized RN:** $100K-$130K+
**Path:** Bridge program or traditional BSN (2-4 years)
**Military Credit:** Some programs grant credit for 68W training
**Why It's Great:** Highest-paid healthcare role accessible to medics

### 2. PARAMEDIC
**Salary:** $45K-$75K | **Flight Paramedic:** $60K-$90K+
**Fastest Path:** National Registry of EMTs → Paramedic school (1-2 years)
**Direct Translation:** Combat lifesaver → civilian trauma care
**Your Edge:** Combat experience highly valued in EMS

### 3. PHYSICIAN ASSISTANT (PA)
**Salary:** $95K-$130K | **Top Markets:** $140K-$160K+
**Path:** Bachelor's + PA school (6-7 years total if starting)
**IPAP Option:** Interservice Physician Assistant Program (while in service)
**ROI:** Extremely high, approaching physician income

### 4. EMERGENCY ROOM TECHNICIAN
**Salary:** $40K-$60K | **With experience:** $55K-$70K
**Immediate Entry:** Can start within months
**Perfect Fit:** ER pace similar to combat medicine
**Stepping Stone:** While pursuing RN or PA

### 5. SURGICAL TECHNOLOGIST
**Salary:** $40K-$60K | **OR experience:** $55K-$75K
**Training:** 12-24 month program
**Military Skills:** Sterile technique, surgical assists translate directly
**Job Security:** Always in demand

### 6. RESPIRATORY THERAPIST
**Salary:** $55K-$80K | **Specialized:** $75K-$95K
**Path:** Associate's degree (2 years)
**Critical Skill:** High demand post-COVID
**Certification:** RRT credential increases pay

### 7. OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH & SAFETY SPECIALIST
**Salary:** $60K-$95K | **Industrial:** $75K-$110K+
**Unique Fit:** Safety training + medical background
**Industries:** Construction, manufacturing, oil/gas
**Your Advantage:** Risk assessment mentality

### 8. MEDICAL DEVICE SALES
**Base:** $60K-$80K | **With Commission:** $100K-$200K+
**No Degree Required:** Clinical experience often substitutes
**Perfect Combo:** Medical knowledge + discipline + resilience
**Top Earners:** $250K+ in surgical device sales

### 9. FIREFIGHTER/PARAMEDIC
**Salary:** $50K-$80K | **Captain+:** $80K-$120K+
**Combined Role:** Many depts require paramedic cert
**Strong Benefits:** Pension, healthcare, job security
**Military Culture:** Similar teamwork and mission focus

### 10. MEDICAL LABORATORY TECHNICIAN
**Salary:** $45K-$65K | **Specialized:** $60K-$80K
**Path:** Associate's or Bachelor's in MLT (2-4 years)
**Stable:** Less physically demanding, good for long career
**Advancement:** Can become lab manager ($75K-$100K+)

## CERTIFICATION FAST-TRACK

**IMMEDIATE (0-6 months):**
□ National Registry EMT-Basic (if not already)
□ BLS, ACLS, PALS certifications
□ State EMT license

**SHORT-TERM (6-18 months):**
□ Paramedic certification
□ Surgical Tech program
□ Phlebotomy certification

**MID-TERM (2-3 years):**
□ Associate's in Nursing (ADN)
□ Respiratory Therapy degree
□ Radiologic Tech program

**LONG-TERM (4-7 years):**
□ Bachelor's in Nursing (BSN)
□ Physician Assistant school
□ Bachelor's + grad school for specialized roles

## USING YOUR GI BILL STRATEGICALLY

**Best ROI Options:**
1. **PA School** - $95K-$130K career, 6-7 years total
2. **BSN (Nursing)** - $75K-$95K, 4 years
3. **Paramedic + Associate's** - $55K-$75K, 2 years faster

**Don't Waste GI Bill On:**
- EMT-Basic (cheap, get employer to pay)
- Short certifications (use Vocational Rehab instead)
- For-profit schools (poor outcomes)
