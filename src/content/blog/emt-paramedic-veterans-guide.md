## What Is EMT/Paramedic Certification?

Emergency Medical Technicians (EMTs) and Paramedics provide pre-hospital emergency care. Certification levels:

**EMT-Basic (EMT-B):** Basic life support, vital signs, CPR, basic airway management, splinting, patient transport
**Advanced EMT (AEMT):** IV therapy, some medications, advanced airway
**Paramedic:** Advanced life support, cardiac monitoring, intubation, full medication administration

Certification is through the National Registry of EMTs (NREMT), with state licensing required to practice.

## Certification Requirements

### EMT-Basic
- Complete approved EMT course (120-180 hours)
- Pass cognitive (written) exam
- Pass psychomotor (practical) exam
- State license application

### Paramedic
- EMT certification (prerequisite)
- Complete paramedic program (1,200-1,800+ hours)
- Pass NREMT Paramedic exams
- State license application
- Clinical and field internship hours

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can 68W medics become EMTs without school?**
A: Many states allow 68Ws to test directly or take shortened bridge courses. Check your state's requirements.

**Q: Is EMT or Paramedic better?**
A: EMT is faster to obtain. Paramedic offers more scope, better pay. Most career EMS providers are paramedics.

**Q: Should I become a firefighter/paramedic?**
A: Fire departments often offer the best EMS compensation. Consider fire academy if interested.

**Q: How does military medic compare to civilian EMT?**
A: Military medics often have more trauma experience but may need training in medical emergencies and civilian protocols.

**Q: Is EMS a good long-term career?**
A: EMS can be a career or stepping stone to nursing, PA, or medical school. Many advance to fire leadership or healthcare administration.

**Q: What about flight paramedic?**
A: Requires 3-5 years ground experience plus additional certifications. High demand, competitive.

**Q: Do I need EMT before paramedic?**
A: Yes. EMT is prerequisite for paramedic school.

**Q: What's the NREMT recertification requirement?**
A: Every 2 years, through continuing education or re-examination.
