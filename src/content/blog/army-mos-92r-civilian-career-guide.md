## What Does a 92R Parachute Rigger Do?

As a 92R, you were responsible for the most safety-critical equipment in airborne operations—parachutes. Soldiers, aircrew, and cargo depended on your technical precision for survival.

You inspected incoming parachutes checking for damage, wear, or defects. You packed personnel parachutes (main and reserve) following precise folding sequences. You prepared cargo parachutes and airdrop systems for equipment and supply drops. You repaired damaged parachutes using industrial sewing machines and hand stitching. You maintained parachute rigging facilities including humidity control, packing tables, and specialized tools.

You understood aerodynamics, fabric construction, harness systems, deployment sequences, failure modes, and emergency procedures. You worked with T-11 personnel parachutes, MC-6 paracommander systems, cargo parachutes (G-11, G-12, G-13), and specialized extraction systems.

You didn't just "fold parachutes"—you performed precision technical work where human lives depended on your attention to detail, followed complex technical procedures, and maintained zero-defect quality standards every single day.

## Top Civilian Career Paths for 92R Veterans

### Parachute Rigger (Civilian Skydiving Operations)

**Civilian job titles:**
- FAA Certificated Parachute Rigger (Senior or Master)
- Skydiving Center Rigger
- Tandem Parachute Rigger
- Sport Parachute Rigger

**Salary ranges:**
- **Entry Rigger (with FAA cert):** $35,000-$48,000
- **Senior Parachute Rigger:** $45,000-$65,000
- **Master Rigger (dropzone manager):** $55,000-$75,000
- **Contract Rigger (experienced, specialized):** $60,000-$80,000

**What translates directly:**
Everything. Your military rigging experience translates directly to civilian skydiving operations.

**Certifications needed:**
- **FAA Senior Parachute Rigger Certificate** (requires testing with FAA examiner, your military training qualifies you for experience requirements)
- **FAA Master Parachute Rigger** (requires 3 years experience packing + 100 parachutes of each type)
- **USPA membership** (United States Parachute Association—professional organization)

**Reality check:**
Civilian sport parachute rigging pays less than military but offers lifestyle benefits—working at dropzones (often scenic locations), being part of skydiving community, flexible schedules (seasonal in many locations), and continuing hands-on rigging work.

Military riggers transition easily because civilian sport parachutes are simpler than military systems. Your T-11 and MC-6 experience far exceeds the complexity of sport mains and reserves.

Many dropzones need riggers, especially those offering tandem skydiving (tourist operations). Tandem rigs require frequent repacking (every 180 days per FAA regs) providing consistent work. Dropzones value military riggers for professionalism, attention to detail, and safety focus.

Pay varies by location and season. Busy dropzones in popular areas (California, Florida, Hawaii, Arizona) offer year-round work. Northern dropzones are seasonal (April-October). Some riggers supplement income doing reserve repacks (required every 180 days) for skydivers maintaining their own gear.

**Best for:** 92Rs who love rigging work, want to stay in skydiving industry, prefer lifestyle over high pay, and enjoy being part of tight-knit skydiving community.

Path for 92Rs: Pursue A&P license using GI Bill (aviation maintenance technology programs at community colleges), gain maintenance experience at airlines/repair stations, then apply to FAA inspector positions.

The FAA values military aviation backgrounds, especially safety-critical roles like parachute rigging. Your attention to detail, technical knowledge, and regulatory compliance experience strengthens applications.

**Best for:** 92Rs willing to pursue A&P license and aviation maintenance experience, want federal career with excellent compensation, and are interested in aviation safety oversight rather than hands-on rigging.

## Required Certifications & Training

### High Priority (get these):

**FAA Senior Parachute Rigger Certificate**
- **What it is:** FAA certification to pack personnel parachutes (main and reserve)
- **Requirements:** Must have packed at least 20 parachutes of type being rated for, pass written/oral/practical tests with FAA Designated Parachute Rigger Examiner (DPRE)
- **Cost:** $100-$300 for testing (examiner fees vary)
- **Time:** Testing only (your military experience meets experience requirements)
- **Value:** Required to work as parachute rigger at dropzones or pack reserves for skydivers. Essential for civilian rigging careers.

**FAA Master Parachute Rigger**
- **Requirements:** 3 years experience as senior rigger + packed at least 100 parachutes of each of two types
- **Value:** Higher certification allowing more parachute types and specialized systems. Your military experience likely qualifies you to test immediately.

**FAA Airframe & Powerplant (A&P) License**
- **What it is:** FAA mechanic certification for aircraft maintenance
- **Cost:** $5,000-$15,000 (aviation maintenance technology programs at community colleges)
- **Time:** 18-24 months (accelerated programs available)
- **Value:** Opens aviation maintenance, inspection, and FAA inspector careers. Significant investment but high ROI for aviation careers.

### Medium Priority (if it fits your path):

**ASQ Certified Quality Inspector (CQI)**
- **What it is:** American Society for Quality certification for quality inspectors
- **Cost:** $300-$600 (exam + study materials)
- **Value:** Professional credential for QC/QA careers in manufacturing

**AS9100 Quality Management Training**
- **What it is:** Aerospace quality management system standard (ISO 9001 for aerospace)
- **Cost:** $500-$1,500 for training courses
- **Value:** Valuable for aerospace QC/QA roles

**USPA Coach / Instructor Ratings**
- Only pursue if serious about skydiving instruction career
- Requires extensive jump experience first

**Technical Degree (Aviation Maintenance, Manufacturing Technology, Quality Assurance)**
- **Cost:** $0 with GI Bill
- **Time:** 2 years associate degree
- **Value:** Strengthens aviation and manufacturing career applications

### Lower Priority:

**Lean Six Sigma Certifications**
- Useful for quality and manufacturing management roles
- Cost: $500-$2,000

**Specific aircraft type training** (Boeing, Airbus, etc.)
- Only pursue if offered by employer
