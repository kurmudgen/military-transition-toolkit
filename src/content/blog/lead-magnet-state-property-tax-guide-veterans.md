## ALL 50 STATES PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS

### STATES WITH FULL EXEMPTION (100% P&T)
**Texas, Florida, Nevada, Tennessee, South Dakota, Illinois, Michigan, Indiana, New York, Virginia, Alabama, Louisiana, Mississippi, Oklahoma, Wyoming, West Virginia, New Mexico, Rhode Island, Wisconsin, South Carolina**

### STATES WITH PARTIAL EXEMPTIONS

**ALASKA:** $150,000 assessed value exemption
**ARIZONA:** Up to $4,748 (2025), income limits apply
**CALIFORNIA:** $175,298 basic / $262,950 low-income
**COLORADO:** $192,620 (50%+ disabled)
**GEORGIA:** $120,000 (100% disabled)
**HAWAII:** $124,000 basic home exemption
**IOWA:** $4,850 annually (100% disabled)
**KANSAS:** Up to full market value (varies by county)
**KENTUCKY:** $40,500 (100% P&T)
**MAINE:** $10,000 (100% P&T, veteran not serving)
**MARYLAND:** Varies by county, generally full exemption
**MASSACHUSETTS:** $1,000-$1,500 (limited)
**MONTANA:** $204,000 reduction (100% P&T)
**NEBRASKA:** $5,000-$6,000 value exemption
**NEW HAMPSHIRE:** All veterans exemption
**NORTH CAROLINA:** Excluded from property taxes (100% P&T)
**NORTH DAKOTA:** $240,000 reduction (100% P&T)
**OREGON:** $242,470 assessed value (2024-25)
**UTAH:** Varies by county
