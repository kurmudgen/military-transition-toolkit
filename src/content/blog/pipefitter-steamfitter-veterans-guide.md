## What Is a Pipefitter/Steamfitter?

Pipefitters and steamfitters install and maintain piping systems that carry water, steam, chemicals, and gases. They work on:

- High-pressure steam systems
- Industrial process piping
- HVAC piping
- Power plant systems
- Refinery and chemical plant piping
- Fire suppression systems

**Pipefitters** typically work on high-pressure, industrial piping. **Steamfitters** specialize in steam systems. Many belong to the United Association (UA) union.

## Why Veterans Excel in Pipefitting

Military training creates excellent pipefitters:

**Mechanical aptitude:** You've worked on complex systems.

**Precision work:** Military maintenance requires exactness—pipefitting demands the same.

**Physical capability:** Pipefitting is demanding work in challenging environments.

**Safety discipline:** High-pressure systems require military-grade safety awareness.

**Blueprint reading:** Isometric drawings are similar to military technical drawings.

## Apprenticeship Requirements

### UA Apprenticeship
- **Age:** 18+ years
- **Education:** High school diploma or GED
- **Math:** Algebra capability
- **Physical:** Able to perform physical work
- **Interview:** Selection committee

### Application Process
1. Apply to local UA union
2. Aptitude test (math, reading comprehension)
3. Interview
4. Drug test
5. Acceptance into program

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What's the difference between pipefitter and plumber?**
A: Plumbers work on water supply and drainage (residential/commercial). Pipefitters work on industrial piping, steam, and high-pressure systems. Pipefitting is generally higher paid.

**Q: How much do union pipefitters make?**
A: Union journeymen earn $70,000-$110,000+ depending on location and specialty. Nuclear and travel work can exceed $150,000.

**Q: Is the UA Veterans in Piping program really free?**
A: Yes. 18-week accelerated training at military installations for transitioning service members.

**Q: Do I need to know welding?**
A: Welding is learned during apprenticeship. Prior welding experience helps but isn't required.

**Q: What's shutdown/turnaround work?**
A: Intensive maintenance periods at refineries and plants. Long hours, excellent pay, temporary locations.

**Q: Can I get into nuclear work?**
A: Yes, especially with security clearance. Nuclear pipefitting pays significantly more.

**Q: Is pipefitting hard on your body?**
A: Yes. Physical work in various conditions. Good fitness and ergonomics help.

**Q: How competitive is UA apprenticeship entry?**
A: Competitive in major metros, less so in industrial areas. Veterans often receive preference.
