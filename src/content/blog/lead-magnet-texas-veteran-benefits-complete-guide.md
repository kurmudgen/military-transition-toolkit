## CHAPTER 1: Property Tax Exemptions & Benefits

### Overview

Texas's property tax exemptions for veterans are among the most generous in the nation. Depending on your disability rating and circumstances, you could reduce your property tax by thousands annually or eliminate it entirely.

### The Five Main Property Tax Benefits

1. **$5,000 - $12,000 Exemption** (10%+ service-connected disability)
2. **Additional $12,000 for Age 65+** (disabled veterans)
3. **Total Exemption** (100% P&T service-connected disability)
4. **Surviving Spouse Benefits** (carryover exemptions)
5. **Deployed Service Member Exemption**

Let's break down each benefit in detail.

---

### üí∞ DISABILITY RATING-BASED EXEMPTIONS (Texas Tax Code Section 11.22)

#### Who Qualifies

- Honorably discharged veterans
- Service-connected disability of 10% or higher
- Texas resident

#### What You Get (Exemption Amount by Disability Rating)

| **Disability Rating** | **Exemption Amount** | **Typical Annual Savings*** |
|----------------------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| 10-29% | $5,000 | $125-$300 |
| 30-49% | $7,500 | $187-$450 |
| 50-69% | $10,000 | $250-$600 |
| 70-99% | $12,000 | $300-$720 |
| 100% P&T | **Total Exemption** | $3,000-$10,000+ |

*Based on varying county tax rates (typically 2.5%-6%)

#### Example Calculation (50% Disabled Veteran)

- Property assessed value: $350,000
- With $10,000 exemption: $340,000 taxable value
- Annual savings at 3% tax rate: **$300**
- Combined with homestead exemption: **$500-$700+ annual savings**

üí° **Pro Tip:** These exemptions apply to your residence homestead property. You must occupy the property as your primary residence to qualify.

---

### üè† 100% DISABLED VETERAN TOTAL EXEMPTION (Texas Tax Code Section 11.131)

#### Who Qualifies

- Honorably discharged veteran
- 100% service-connected disability rating from VA **OR**
- Awarded 100% disability compensation due to Individual Unemployability
- Property must be residence homestead

#### What You Get

**ZERO property taxes** on your primary residence homestead.

This is Texas's most valuable veteran benefit available.

#### Real-World Impact by County

| **County** | **Avg Home Value** | **Avg Annual Tax** | **Your Savings** |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Harris (Houston) | $375,000 | $6,750 | $6,750 |
| Dallas | $425,000 | $8,500 | $8,500 |
| Travis (Austin) | $550,000 | $10,450 | $10,450 |
| Bexar (San Antonio) | $325,000 | $5,850 | $5,850 |
| Tarrant (Fort Worth) | $380,000 | $7,220 | $7,220 |
| Collin (Plano) | $475,000 | $9,025 | $9,025 |

**Over 20 years of homeownership, this benefit could save you $120,000-$200,000+.**

#### Special Provisions for 100% Disabled Veterans

**Donated Residence:**
A charity may donate a residence homestead to a disabled veteran at no cost to the veteran, and the residence homestead is exempt from taxation if the charity's donation is stated in a recorded deed and the veteran hasn't previously received a donated residence homestead in Texas.

**Age of Home:**
The property is exempt whether you live in a brand new home or one built 100 years ago.

---

### üë¥ ADDITIONAL $12,000 EXEMPTION FOR VETERANS AGE 65+

#### Who Qualifies

- Age 65 or older
- Service-connected disability of at least 10%
- Residence homestead property

#### What You Get

An **additional $12,000** exemption on top of your disability-rating exemption.

#### Example Stacking Benefits

**Scenario:** 70-year-old veteran with 50% disability rating

- Base exemption (50% disability): $10,000
- Age 65+ disabled veteran: $12,000
- **Total exemption: $22,000**
- Standard homestead: $100,000
- **Grand total: $122,000 in exemptions**

On a $350,000 home, this reduces your taxable value to $228,000, saving approximately **$750-$1,500 annually** depending on your county's tax rate.

---

### üíç SURVIVING SPOUSE BENEFITS (Texas Tax Code Section 11.22)

#### Who Qualifies

Surviving spouses of veterans who:
- Received a property tax exemption for disabled veterans at time of death
- Have not remarried
- Continue to reside in the property

#### What You Get

**Full continuation** of the veteran's property tax exemption, including:
- Same exemption amount the veteran was receiving
- 100% exemption if veteran was 100% disabled
- Continues indefinitely if spouse doesn't remarry

#### Transferability Provision

If the surviving spouse sells the property, they can transfer the same dollar amount of exemption to a new residence homestead in Texas (if they continue to not remarry).

**Example:**
- Veteran had 100% exemption on home valued at $400,000 (full exemption)
- Veteran passes away
- Surviving spouse continues to receive **full exemption**
- Spouse sells home and buys new $500,000 home
- **Full exemption transfers to new home**

‚ö†Ô∏è **Important:** Remarriage terminates the exemption permanently.

---

### üéñÔ∏è PARTIAL EXEMPTION FOR DONATED HOMESTEAD

If a charitable organization donates a residence homestead to a partially disabled veteran at no cost, the veteran qualifies for a **partial exemption** from taxation equal to a percentage of appraised value based on disability rating:

- 10-29% disability = 10% of appraised value exempt
- 30-49% disability = 30% of appraised value exempt
- 50-69% disability = 50% of appraised value exempt
- 70%+ disability = 100% of appraised value exempt

---

### ü™ñ DEPLOYED SERVICE MEMBER EXEMPTION (Texas Tax Code Section 11.131)

#### Who Qualifies

- Texas service members deployed outside the U.S.
- Service in active duty in any branch of Armed Forces
- Serving in designated combat zone or qualified hazardous duty area

#### What You Get

Complete exemption from taxation on residence homestead for the **entire year** in which the service member is deployed for at least 90 days.

**Example:**
- Deployed to Afghanistan March 1 - December 31 (10 months)
- Property tax bill: $6,000 normally
- **Your cost: $0**

üí° **Pro Tip:** You must apply for this exemption each year you're deployed. It's not automatic.

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL DEADLINE: APRIL 30th

**You MUST apply by April 30 to receive veteran exemptions for that tax year.**

#### Late Applications

After April 30, you can still file a late application if you weren't aware of the exemption or couldn't reasonably determine qualification. Late applications may be accepted up to:
- **One year after the delinquency date** for taxes on the property

#### Important Notes

- File early to avoid missing the deadline
- Some appraisal districts offer online applications
- Always request confirmation of receipt

## CHAPTER 2: The Hazlewood Act & Education Benefits

### Overview

The Hazlewood Act is Texas's signature veteran education benefit and one of the most generous state-level education programs in the entire country. This benefit alone can save you or your family members over $50,000 in tuition costs.

### üéì THE HAZLEWOOD ACT (Texas Education Code Section 54.341)

#### What Is It?

The Hazlewood Act provides qualified Texas veterans, spouses, and dependent children with an education benefit of **up to 150 semester credit hours** of tuition exemption at Texas public institutions of higher education.

#### Who Qualifies (Veterans)

To qualify as a "Texas Veteran," you must meet ALL of these criteria:

‚úì **Served at least 181 days** of active duty service (excluding training)
‚úì **Honorable discharge** or general discharge under honorable conditions
‚úì **Texas connection:** EITHER
  - Texas was your home of record when you entered service, OR
  - Texas was your place of entry into service (listed on DD-214), OR
  - You've been a Texas resident for at least 12 consecutive months AND plan to continue living in Texas
‚úì **Residency:** Currently reside in Texas
‚úì **Federal benefits:** Have exhausted federal VA education benefits OR don't have federal benefits that cover tuition and fees only

üí° **Pro Tip:** National Guard and Reserve members who served on federal active duty orders for at least 181 consecutive days (not including training) also qualify.

#### What It Covers

‚úì **Tuition** - Full exemption
‚úì **Most mandatory fees** - Covered
‚úì **Lab fees** - Covered
‚úì **Building use fees** - Covered

‚ùå **What's NOT Covered:**
- Room and board
- Books and supplies
- Parking
- Student service fees (some exceptions)
- Deposits
- Living expenses

#### Covered Institutions

- **Public Universities:** UT system, Texas A&M system, University of Houston, Texas Tech, UNT, Texas State, and all other public universities
- **Community Colleges:** All Texas public community colleges
- **Technical Colleges:** Texas State Technical College system
- **Public Health-Related Institutions:** UT Health Science Centers, Texas A&M Health Science Center, etc.

‚ùå **NOT Covered:**
- Private universities (Baylor, SMU, TCU, Rice, etc.)
- Out-of-state institutions
- For-profit schools

---

#### Example: University of Texas at Austin

- **Tuition per semester:** ~$5,500
- **Mandatory fees:** ~$500
- **Total per semester:** ~$6,000
- **Two semesters per year:** ~$12,000

- **Tuition per semester:** ~$5,200
- **Mandatory fees:** ~$800
- **Total per semester:** ~$6,000
- **Two semesters per year:** ~$12,000
- **Four-year degree:** **~$48,000**

#### Example: Houston Community College

- **Tuition per semester (15 credit hours):** ~$1,500
- **Fees:** ~$300
- **Total per semester:** ~$1,800
- **Associates degree (4 semesters):** **~$7,200**

**Total potential value of 150 credit hours:** $40,000-$60,000+ depending on institution.

---

### üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß‚Äçüë¶ HAZLEWOOD LEGACY PROGRAM

One of the most valuable aspects of the Hazlewood Act is the ability to transfer unused benefits to your children.

#### How Transfer Works

Eligible Texas veterans may assign or transfer **unused hours** of Hazlewood exemption to their children, subject to these conditions:

**Eligible Children:**
- Biological children
- Stepchildren
- Adopted children
- Claimed as dependents on your federal tax return

**Requirements for Children:**
‚úì **Age:** Must be 25 years old or younger on the first day of semester/term
‚úì **Residency:** Must be Texas resident (defined as living in Texas for 12 months)
‚úì **Veteran Status:** Parent must be eligible for Hazlewood Act
‚úì **Not withdrawn:** Cannot have been convicted of felony
‚úì **Good standing:** Must maintain good academic standing

#### Transfer Limitations

- Veterans can transfer up to 150 hours **total** (not per child)
- Once hours are used by a child, they cannot be reclaimed
- Multiple children can share the benefit, but total cannot exceed 150 hours

**Example Scenario:**
- Veteran has 150 unused hours
- Child #1 uses 60 hours (Associates degree)
- Child #2 uses 90 hours (remaining balance for Bachelor's)
- Veteran has 0 hours remaining

üí° **Pro Tip:** If you plan to use some hours yourself AND transfer some to children, use yours first. You can always transfer unused hours later, but once transferred, you can't take them back.

---

### üéñÔ∏è HAZLEWOOD EXEMPTION FOR CHILDREN OF DECEASED/DISABLED VETERANS

#### Separate Program for Children

Children of certain veterans qualify for Hazlewood benefits **independent of any transfer:**

**Qualifying Scenarios:**
1. Veteran parent **killed in action**
2. Veteran parent **died from service-connected causes**
3. Veteran parent **100% disabled** from service-connected causes
4. Veteran parent **died from any cause** after being 100% service-connected disabled

**Children's Eligibility:**
- Parent was Texas resident at time of death or disability determination
- Child under age 26 on first day of semester
- Child meets academic and residency requirements

**Benefits:**
- Up to 150 credit hours tuition exemption
- Independent of parent's Hazlewood usage
- Can attend any Texas public institution

---

### üë∞ HAZLEWOOD FOR SPOUSES

#### Who Qualifies

Spouses of veterans qualify for Hazlewood if the veteran:
- Qualifies for Hazlewood Act themselves
- Is 100% service-connected disabled and unable to use the benefit due to disability

#### Limitations

Spouses receive fewer exemptions than veterans and children:
- Must use benefit while married to veteran
- Benefit terminates upon divorce
- Limited to specific circumstances

üí° **Pro Tip:** If both you and your spouse are veterans, you EACH qualify for up to 150 hours (300 hours combined for your household).

---

### üìö OTHER TEXAS EDUCATION BENEFITS

#### 1. **Tuition Exemption for Surviving Spouses and Children (Texas Education Code 54.203)**

Provides tuition exemption at Texas public colleges for:
- Surviving spouses of members who died in service
- Surviving spouses who have not remarried
- Children under age 26 of deceased service members

#### 2. **Top 10% Automatic Admission**

Texas law guarantees automatic admission to Texas public universities for students graduating in the top 10% of their Texas high school class (some universities limit percentage to top 6% at certain schools like UT Austin).

**Why This Matters for Veterans' Children:**
Your children automatically qualify for admission to excellent universities if they perform well in Texas high schools.

#### 3. **Concurrent Enrollment Exemption**

High school students who are children of disabled veterans or killed in action receive tuition exemption for college courses taken concurrently during high school.

## CHAPTER 3: Texas Veterans Land Board Programs

### Overview

The Texas Veterans Land Board (VLB), administered by the Texas General Land Office, offers unique programs that exist nowhere else in America. These programs provide low-interest loans for land, homes, and home improvements exclusively to Texas veterans.

### üè° TEXAS VETERANS LAND BOARD: THE FIVE PROGRAMS

1. Veterans Housing Assistance Program (VHAP)
2. Veterans Land Loan Program (VLB Land)
3. Veterans Home Improvement Program (VHIP)
4. Texas State Veterans Homes
5. Texas State Veterans Cemeteries

---

### üè† VETERANS HOUSING ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (VHAP)

#### What Is It?

A low-interest loan program to help Texas veterans purchase a home with **little to no money down**.

#### Who Qualifies

‚úì Veteran with honorable discharge or active duty service member
‚úì Served minimum service requirements (varies by era)
‚úì Texas resident
‚úì Meet income and credit requirements
‚úì Have not had VLB loan previously charged off or foreclosed

#### Key Benefits

**Low Interest Rates:**
- Fixed-rate loans
- Rates typically 0.25%-0.75% below conventional rates
- As of 2025: rates approximately 5.5%-6.5% (varies)

**Low/No Down Payment:**
- Can finance up to 100% of purchase price in some cases
- Typical down payment: 0%-5%
- Much better than conventional loans (usually 10-20% down)

**Loan Amounts:**
- Up to the conforming loan limit (2025: $806,500 for single-family homes in most counties)
- Higher limits in high-cost counties
- No maximum sales price

**Disabled Veteran Rate Reduction:**
Veterans with 30% or greater VA disability rating receive **0.5% interest rate reduction**.

**Example Rate Comparison:**
- Conventional 30-year mortgage: 7.25%
- VLB loan (standard): 6.25%
- VLB loan (30%+ disabled): **5.75%**

#### Real-World Savings Example

**Scenario:** $350,000 home purchase, 30-year mortgage

| **Loan Type** | **Interest Rate** | **Monthly Payment** | **Total Interest Paid** |
|--------------|------------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Conventional | 7.25% | $2,388 | $509,680 |
| VLB Standard | 6.25% | $2,154 | $425,440 |
| VLB Disabled (30%+) | 5.75% | $2,042 | $385,120 |

**Savings with VLB Disabled Rate: $124,560 over life of loan**

#### How to Apply

1. **Contact VLB-approved lender** (list at VLB website)
2. **Get pre-qualified** for loan amount
3. **Find a home** within loan limits
4. **Submit application** through VLB lender
5. **Close on your home** just like conventional loan

üìû **Contact:** 1-800-252-8387 | VLBinfo@glo.texas.gov

### üå≥ VETERANS LAND LOAN PROGRAM

The **only program of its kind in the nation**, allowing Texas veterans to purchase land at favorable rates with as little as 5% down.

#### Who Qualifies

‚úì Texas veteran (honorable discharge)

‚úì Texas resident

**Disabled Veteran Rate Reduction:**
30%+ VA disability rating = **0.5% interest rate reduction**

- **Total interest:** $86,480

**VLB advantage:** $25,000 less down payment + lower monthly payment + longer term

### üìä HOW VETERANS' PREFERENCE WORKS IN TEXAS

#### 1. **State Agency Hiring Goal: 20%**

Every Texas state agency must establish a goal to hire **veterans for at least 20%** of total positions.

This creates strong incentive to hire veterans.

#### 2. **Mandatory Interview Requirements**

**If 6 or fewer applicants interviewed:**
- At least **1 must be a veteran** (if qualified veteran applied)

**If 7+ applicants interviewed:**
- At least **20% must be veterans** (if qualified veterans applied)

**Example:**
- Agency interviews 10 candidates
- Minimum 2 must be qualified veterans

#### 3. **Competitive Exam Points**

**Standard Veteran:** +10 points added to exam score
**Disabled Veteran:** +15 points added to exam score

**Example:**
- Exam score: 85/100
- Disabled veteran: 100/100 (85 + 15)
- Moves you to top of candidate pool

#### 4. **Direct Hire Authority**

State agencies may:
- Designate positions as **"veteran-only"** positions
- **Directly hire** veterans without advertising position
- Fast-track veteran applicants through hiring process

#### 5. **Retention Preference**

During layoffs/reductions in force:
- Veterans receive preference in retention decisions
- Last hired, first fired does NOT apply to veterans with preference

- Sign and date
- Make copies for your records

**Step 5: Submit Application**

**Options:**
- **In person** (recommended for first-time - can ask questions)
- **By mail** (with delivery confirmation)
- **Online** (if available in your county)

**Step 6: Follow Up**

- Request confirmation of receipt
- Note case/confirmation number
- Ask for estimated processing time
- Save all documentation

**Step 7: Verify Exemption**

- Should receive written confirmation
- Verify exemption appears on property tax statement
- Keep confirmation letter permanently

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL DEADLINE: April 30

**Must apply by April 30** to receive exemption for that tax year.

**Late applications** possible up to one year after delinquency date in some cases, but don't risk it.

### üéì HAZLEWOOD ACT APPLICATION

#### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Verify Eligibility**

‚ñ° Served 181+ days active duty
‚ñ° Honorable discharge
‚ñ° Texas home of record OR place of entry OR 12-month Texas residency

**Step 2: Choose Texas Public Institution**

- University, community college, or technical school
- Must be public institution in Texas

**Step 3: Apply and Get Accepted**

- Complete standard admission application
- Get accepted to institution

**Step 4: Contact School's Veterans Office**

- Usually in registrar or financial aid office
- Request Hazlewood exemption coordinator

**Step 5: Complete Hazlewood Application**

- School-specific form (varies by institution)
- Attach DD-214
- Provide proof of Texas connection
- Proof of Texas residency

**Step 6: Submit to School**

- Deadline: Usually 2-4 weeks before semester starts
- Submit early to ensure processing

**Step 7: Register for Classes**

- Complete registration
- Verify Hazlewood applied to tuition bill

**Step 8: Verify Each Semester**

- Hazlewood does NOT automatically renew
- **Must reapply each semester**
- Set calendar reminders
