# Arkansas Military Retirement: Best Cities, Housing Costs & Quality of Life

Arkansas ranks among the best states for military retirees thanks to zero state income tax on military retirement, full property tax exemptions for 100% disabled veterans, and a cost of living 11-17% below the national average.

But where should you actually live? Arkansas's cities vary significantly in veteran population density, job opportunities, housing costs, and access to VA healthcare.

This guide ranks Arkansas's best cities for veterans with real data on home prices, veteran populations, and quality of life factors.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Best overall for veterans**: Fayetteville/Northwest Arkansas (jobs, education, growth)
**Best for affordability**: Fort Smith, Jonesboro, Conway
**Best for healthcare access**: Little Rock (largest VA medical center)
**Best small-city feel**: Conway, Hot Springs, Jonesboro
**Best outdoor lifestyle**: Fayetteville, Hot Springs

**Median home price range**: $176,000 (Fort Smith) to $480,000+ (Bentonville)
**State average**: ~$260,000-$280,000

## Top 10 Best Cities for Veterans in Arkansas

- **Median price per square foot**: $217

#### Cost of Living
- **Overall index**: Moderate (below national average but higher than state average)
- **Housing**: Above Arkansas average due to growth and demand
- **Groceries**: Near state average
- **Utilities**: Average

#### Employment Opportunities

**Major employers**:
- University of Arkansas (10,000+ employees) - education, research, administration
- Washington Regional Medical Center - healthcare
- Walmart headquarters nearby (Bentonville)
- Tyson Foods nearby (Springdale)
- Growing tech and startup scene
- Numerous Walmart suppliers and vendors

**Average salary**: Higher than Arkansas average due to corporate presence

**Veteran-specific advantages**: University employment opportunities, strong corporate veteran hiring programs nearby

#### VA Healthcare Access

- **Phone**: (479) 443-4301 | 1-800-691-8387
- **Services**: Full-service VA hospital including surgery, mental health, specialty care
- **Vet Center**: Fayetteville (counseling services)
- **State Veterans Home**: Arkansas Veterans Home at Fayetteville (1179 North College Avenue)

**This is one of only two full VA medical centers in Arkansas** - exceptional access.

#### Quality of Life

- **Outdoor recreation**: Ozark Mountains, mountain biking (world-class trails), hiking, lakes, Devils Den State Park
- **Culture**: University of Arkansas athletics (SEC football), Walton Arts Center, Fayetteville Square, craft breweries, food scene
- **Education**: Excellent K-12 schools, University of Arkansas (Yellow Ribbon school)
- **Crime**: Low to moderate (safer than larger Arkansas cities)

#### Veterans Day Recognition
Strong veteran community with active American Legion and VFW posts.

**Pros**:

**Bottom line**: If you value education (for yourself or dependents), healthcare access, and quality of life over affordability, Fayetteville is unmatched in Arkansas. The VA medical center alone makes it worth the premium.

## Climate and Weather Considerations

### Overall Arkansas Climate
- **Winters**: Mild (35-50°F average)
- **Summers**: Hot and humid (85-95°F, high humidity)
- **Rainfall**: 45-55 inches annually (wettest in spring)

### By Region

**Northwest Arkansas (Fayetteville, Bentonville, Rogers)**
- Slightly cooler than rest of state
- Occasional snow/ice (1-3 times per winter)
- Four distinct seasons
- Beautiful fall foliage in Ozarks

**Central Arkansas (Little Rock, Conway)**
- Hot, humid summers
- Mild winters, rare snow
- Tornado risk (Arkansas is in "Dixie Alley")
- Spring is tornado season (March-May)

**River Valley (Fort Smith)**
- Very hot summers
- Mild winters
- Lower humidity than eastern Arkansas

**Veteran Health Considerations**:
- **Heat and humidity**: Can exacerbate respiratory and cardiovascular conditions
- **Tornado risk**: Ensure home has safe room or know your shelter plan
- **Mild winters**: Excellent for veterans with cold-weather injuries or arthritis
