# Michigan Veteran Benefits: Complete State Programs Guide (2025)

Michigan honors its 550,000+ veterans with substantial state benefits including property tax exemptions, education assistance, and employment preferences. From the Upper Peninsula to Detroit, Michigan has developed comprehensive programs to support those who served.

This guide covers all Michigan veteran benefits with eligibility requirements and application procedures.

## Property Tax Exemptions

### Disabled Veteran Property Tax Exemption

**100% Service-Connected Disabled Veterans:**
- **Exemption:** Complete exemption from property taxes
- **Coverage:** Primary residence
- **Savings:** Full property tax bill (often $3,000-$8,000+ annually)

**Requirements:**
- 100% service-connected disability rating
- Own and occupy home as primary residence
- Michigan resident

### Surviving Spouse Exemption

**Surviving Spouse of 100% Disabled Veteran:**
- Complete property tax exemption continues
- Must have been receiving exemption prior to veteran's death
- Must remain unremarried
- Must continue occupying the home

### Principal Residence Exemption

**All Homeowners (Including Veterans):**
- 18 mills exemption on school operating taxes
- Automatic for primary residence

### How to Apply

1. Obtain VA letter certifying 100% service-connected disability
2. Contact your local Assessor's office
3. Complete Affidavit for Disabled Veterans Exemption (Form 5107)
4. Submit DD-214 and VA letter
5. Apply by February deadline for current year

## Education Benefits

### Michigan National Guard Tuition Assistance

**State Tuition Assistance Program (STAP):**
- Up to 100% tuition at Michigan public colleges
- For eligible National Guard members
- Based on annual funding

**Federal Tuition Assistance:**
- Additional federal TA available
- Combined with STAP for full coverage

### Children of Veterans Tuition Grant

**Eligibility:**
- Children of 100% disabled veterans
- Children of veterans who died from service-connected causes
- Children of POW/MIA veterans

**Benefit:**
- Up to $2,800 per year
- At Michigan public colleges
- Renewable for 4 years

**How to Apply:**
- Through Michigan Student Aid
- FAFSA required

### In-State Tuition for Veterans

**All Veterans:**
- Qualify for in-state tuition regardless of prior residency
- Effective immediately upon enrollment
- Covers all Michigan public universities and community colleges

**GI Bill Users:**
- In-state tuition guaranteed under federal law

## Vehicle Benefits

### Disabled Veteran License Plates

**Free Plates for:**
- 100% Disabled Veterans
- Purple Heart recipients
- Medal of Honor recipients
- Former POWs
- Combat Veterans

**Additional Benefits:**
- Free registration (100% disabled)
- Free plate
- Accessible parking (if mobility impaired)

### How to Apply

1. Visit Michigan Secretary of State
2. Provide DD-214 and VA documentation
3. Complete Form TR-54B

## Hunting and Fishing

### Free Hunting/Fishing License

**100% Disabled Veterans:**
- Free all-species fishing license
- Free hunting licenses
- Annual application required

**50%+ Disabled Veterans:**
- Half-price hunting and fishing licenses
- Significant savings

### Recreation Passport

**Disabled Veterans:**
- Free Michigan Recreation Passport (100% disabled)
- Access to state parks, trails, and boat launches

### How to Apply

1. Visit Michigan DNR website or license agent
2. Provide VA disability documentation
3. Show Michigan ID

## Financial Benefits

### Military Retirement Pay Exclusion

**Michigan Tax Benefit:**
- Military retirement pay is fully exempt from Michigan income tax
- 100% exclusion for military retirees
- No age or income restrictions

This is one of the most generous military retirement tax policies in the country.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Michigan tax military retirement pay?

No. Michigan fully exempts military retirement pay from state income tax.

### What disability rating do I need for property tax exemption?

100% service-connected disability is required for the complete property tax exemption.

### Can my children get tuition assistance?

Yes, through the Children of Veterans Tuition Grant if you're 100% disabled or died from service-connected causes.

### How do I get free hunting/fishing licenses?

Visit a DNR license agent with proof of 100% service-connected disability.

### How do I access the Veterans Trust Fund?

Contact your county veterans service officer. They process emergency grant applications.
