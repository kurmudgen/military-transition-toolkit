# Missouri Veterans: Healthcare Facilities, Education Benefits & Job Resources

Missouri serves approximately 430,000 veterans - about 9.1% of the state's adult population. Whether you're transitioning to civilian life or already living in Missouri, you need to know what healthcare facilities, education benefits, and job opportunities are available.

This guide provides actual addresses, phone numbers, and actionable information for Missouri veterans.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Healthcare**: Missouri has 5 VA medical centers, 10+ community-based clinics, and 7 state veterans homes

**Education**: Veterans Education Program (partial tuition coverage), strong GI Bill support at major universities including Mizzou and Missouri S&T

**Employment**: State hiring preference, major employers (Boeing, Emerson, Cerner/Oracle Health), Fort Leonard Wood civilian opportunities, and veteran-focused job programs

**Veteran Population Hotspots**: St. Louis County (55,000+), Jackson County/Kansas City (45,000+), St. Charles County (20,000+)

## Education Benefits for Missouri Veterans

### Missouri Returning Heroes Education Act

Missouri's primary education benefit for veterans - provides **50% tuition reimbursement** at eligible institutions.

**Eligibility**:
- Served in armed forces after September 11, 2001
- Received honorable discharge
- Missouri resident for at least 12 months
- Enrolled at participating Missouri institution
- Must have completed degree/certificate program

**Important**: This is a **reimbursement** program, not upfront payment. You pay tuition, complete your coursework, and receive 50% back after successful completion.

**Benefit amount**:
- 50% of tuition costs
- Up to 50 credit hours of undergraduate or graduate coursework
- Does not cover fees, books, or housing

**Qualifying schools**: Public universities, community colleges, and some private institutions in Missouri

**How it works with GI Bill**: You can use both! GI Bill pays first, then Missouri Returning Heroes covers 50% of remaining costs (if any).

**How to apply**:
1. Apply for admission to eligible Missouri school
2. Use your GI Bill benefits (if applicable)
3. Complete your coursework successfully
4. Apply for reimbursement through Missouri Department of Higher Education & Workforce Development
5. Receive 50% tuition reimbursement

**Contact**: Missouri Department of Higher Education & Workforce Development - (573) 751-2361

### Wartime Veteran's Survivor Grant

**For surviving spouses and children** of veterans who died in service or from service-connected disabilities.

**Benefit**: Full tuition waiver at Missouri public institutions

**Eligibility**:
- Spouse or child of veteran who died in service OR from service-connected disability
- Missouri resident
- Enrolled at Missouri public university or community college

**How to apply**: Contact your school's financial aid office with documentation (DD-1300, VA determination letter, etc.)

### In-State Tuition for Veterans

Veterans using GI Bill benefits qualify for **in-state tuition** at Missouri public schools under the Veterans Access, Choice, and Accountability Act (VACAA), regardless of residency status.

**Eligibility**:
- Using Post-9/11 GI Bill or Montgomery GI Bill
- Living in Missouri while attending school
- Enrolling within 3 years of discharge

## Employment Resources for Missouri Veterans

### State Hiring Preference

Missouri law provides **hiring preference** to veterans for state government jobs.

**Who qualifies**:
- Honorably discharged veterans
- Disabled veterans (additional preference)

**How it works**: Veterans who meet minimum qualifications receive additional points in the hiring process, giving priority over non-veteran applicants with similar qualifications.

**Where to apply**: [mo.gov/jobs](https://mo.gov/jobs/) or Missouri Office of Administration
