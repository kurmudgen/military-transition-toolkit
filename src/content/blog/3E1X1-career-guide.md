---# 3E1X1 - Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning & Refrigeration (HVAC/R) Career Transition Guide

## Overview

HVAC/R specialists install, maintain, and repair heating, air conditioning, refrigeration, and ventilation systems for Air Force facilities and equipment.

**Daily:** Install/repair HVAC systems, troubleshoot refrigeration, maintain chillers/boilers, ductwork, controls, EPA refrigerant handling, preventive maintenance, emergency repairs.

## Civilian Paths

**HVAC Technician:** Residential/commercial HVAC. $45K-$85K. High demand. Recession-resistant. Can start own company.
**Refrigeration Technician:** Commercial refrigeration (supermarkets, restaurants). $50K-$90K. Specialized, well-paid.
**Chiller Technician:** Large commercial systems. $55K-$95K. Hospitals, office buildings, industrial.
**HVAC Service Manager:** Lead technician teams. $65K-$110K.

## Certifications

**EPA 608 Universal:** Refrigerant handling. REQUIRED for HVAC career. Should already have from Air Force.
**HVAC Excellence/NATE:** Industry certifications. Demonstrate competency. Valued by employers.
**State Contractor License:** Allows you to run own HVAC company. Requirements vary by state.
**Journeyman/Master HVAC:** State-specific licensing. Opens higher-paying opportunities.

## Salary

| Experience | Residential | Commercial | Industrial |
|---|---|---|---|
| Entry | $35K-$50K | $40K-$55K | $45K-$60K |
| Journeyman | $50K-$70K | $60K-$85K | $70K-$95K |

**Own Business:** HVAC contractors can earn $100K-$250K+ but requires business skills, licensing, startup capital.

## Top Companies

Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Johnson Controls, facility maintenance companies, commercial HVAC contractors, property management companies, hospitals, schools, industrial plants.
