# Illinois vs Wisconsin Veterans Guide: Comparison 2025

Illinois and Wisconsin represent two significant midwestern states with distinct tax philosophies, diverse employment opportunities, and strong veteran communities. Illinois offers major urban centers while Wisconsin emphasizes affordability. This guide compares these Great Lakes destinations for transitioning veterans.

## Military Retirement Tax Treatment

### Illinois: Military Retirement Exempt

Illinois provides complete military retirement exemption:

**Illinois Military Retirement Benefits:**
- Military pension: 100% exempt from state income tax
- VA disability compensation: Exempt
- State income tax rate: 4.95% (flat)
- Effective tax on military retirement: 0%

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Military retirement tax: $0
- Other income tax: $990 (at 4.95%)
- **Total state tax: $990**

### Wisconsin: Military Retirement Not Exempt

Wisconsin taxes military retirement:

**Wisconsin Tax Treatment:**
- Military pension: Subject to state income tax
- VA disability compensation: Exempt
- State income tax rate: 3.54%-7.65% (progressive)
- Effective tax on military retirement: ~5.5%

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Military pension tax: $2,475 (at 5.5%)
- Other income tax: $1,200 (at 6%)
- **Total state tax: $3,675**

**Tax Winner: Illinois** (Saves $2,685 annually on military retirement)

## Final Verdict

### Choose Illinois if:
- You want military retirement tax exemption
- You seek major urban center (Chicago)
- You want higher salaries (career focus)
- You prefer milder winters
- You value urban cultural attractions

### Choose Wisconsin if:
- You want lower overall cost of living (3%)
- You prefer affordable housing
- You like college town culture
- You don't mind harsh winters
- You seek outdoor recreation focus

**The Numbers:** Illinois' military retirement tax advantage ($2,685/year) is offset by Wisconsin's lower housing costs and overall expenses. Over 10 years, costs are essentially equivalent, making this decision lifestyle-driven rather than financial.

Illinois appeals to career-focused veterans in Chicago, while Wisconsin appeals to budget-conscious retirees willing to accept harsh winters for affordability and outdoor recreation.
