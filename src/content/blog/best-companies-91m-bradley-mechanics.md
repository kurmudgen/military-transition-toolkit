# Best Companies for 91M Bradley Mechanics

## BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

91M Bradley Vehicle Mechanics transition to premium positions at manufacturing plants, government contractors, automotive companies, and heavy equipment manufacturers. Compensation ranges $50,000-$110,000+ as technician, with advancement to supervisor and management roles earning $100,000-$200,000+. Top employers include General Dynamics (Bradley manufacturer), Caterpillar, Cummins, Toyota, automotive suppliers, and maintenance service contractors. Your military background in hydraulics, complex system diagnostics, and preventive maintenance positions you for rapid advancement to supervisory roles. Most 91Ms transition within 1-2 months.

## Union vs. Non-Union Comparison

**Union (UAW - Ford/GM; USW - Steel)**: $52,000-$74,000 starting; Year 5: $95,000-$125,000; Year 10: $130,000-$200,000+
- Higher wages, stronger pension, job security
- Seniority-based advancement
- Comprehensive benefits

**Non-Union**: $50,000-$70,000 starting; Year 5: $72,000-$110,000; Year 10: $105,000-$180,000
- Faster merit-based advancement
- More management flexibility
- Generally lower total compensation than union

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Which company pays highest for 91M mechanics?**
A: Union automotive (Ford/GM) typically highest at year 5+ ($95,000-$125,000). General Dynamics competitive due to Bradley background. Caterpillar strong across range.

**Q: Should I choose union or non-union?**
A: **Union**: Higher pay ($15,000-$30,000 more at peak), stronger pension, seniority. **Non-union**: Faster advancement, merit-based, less seniority politics. Choose based on priorities.

**Q: How quickly can I advance to supervisor?**
A: Typically 4-6 years with demonstrated competence. Caterpillar and Cummins faster (3-5 years). Union advancement more seniority-based.

**Q: What's the job outlook?**
A: Strong. Manufacturing rebounding, EV transition creating technical demand, aging workforce retiring. Good long-term stability and growth.

**Q: Do I need additional education to advance to management?**
A: Not always, but MBA or engineering technology degree accelerates path to plant manager level. ASE certifications and Six Sigma valuable for advancement.
