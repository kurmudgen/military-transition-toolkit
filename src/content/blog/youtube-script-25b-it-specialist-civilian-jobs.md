**Section 3: The Certifications That Actually Matter (90 seconds)**

Not all certs are equal. Here's what employers actually look for, in order of impact:

[Visual suggestion: Certification tier list]

**Tier 1 - Get These First:**

**CompTIA Security+ ($425 value - FREE for vets)**
- Required for most DoD IT contracts
- Entry requirement for 90% of cybersecurity jobs
- Covers network security, risk management, threat analysis
- **Get it through:** DoD COOL, VET TEC, or ACT Now Education

**CCNA - Cisco Certified Network Associate ($300 value - FREE)**
- Industry standard for networking
- Opens $70K+ positions immediately
- **Get it through:** Onward to Opportunity, GI Bill reimbursement

**Tier 2 - Get Within First Year:**

**CompTIA CySA+ or CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker)**
- Cybersecurity analyst level
- Required for many mid-level security roles
- Salary jump: $20K-$30K

**AWS Certified Solutions Architect or Azure Administrator**
- Cloud is where the money is
- $95K-$130K salary range
- AWS reimburses exam costs for veterans

**Tier 3 - Career Advancement:**

**CISSP - Certified Information Systems Security Professional**
- Senior-level certification
- Average salary: $145,000
- Requires 5 years experience (but military counts)

**DON'T WASTE MONEY ON:**
- CompTIA A+ (you're overqualified as 25B)
- Random vendor-specific certs without job demand
- Expensive bootcamps when free options exist

**Section 4: Free Certification Programs (90 seconds)**

Here's how to get $5,000+ in certifications for zero cost:

[Visual suggestion: List of free programs]

**DoD COOL (Credentialing Opportunities Online)**
- Covers Security+, CCNA, Linux+, and 100+ other certs
- Use while still in or within 180 days of separation
- Pays for exam vouchers and sometimes study materials

**Microsoft MSSA (Software & Systems Academy)**
- Free 17-week intensive program
- Choose: Cloud application development OR server/cloud administration
- Includes Microsoft certifications
- 90%+ job placement rate
- 750+ hiring partners
- Must apply within 180 days of separation
- **Website:** microsoft.com/mssa

**Onward to Opportunity (O2O)**
- Syracuse University program
- Free training for: CCNA, Security+, Project+, PMP, CISSP
- Career coaching included
- Job placement assistance
- For transitioning service members and military spouses

**VET TEC**
- Covers high-tech training programs
- Cybersecurity, coding, data science
- You get housing allowance while training (BAH rates)
- Doesn't use GI Bill unless you fail to complete
- **Apply through:** VA.gov

**FedVTE (Federal Virtual Training Environment)**
- Department of Homeland Security program
- 850+ hours of FREE cybersecurity courses
- Self-paced online training
- Available to ALL veterans
- **Website:** fedvte.usalearning.gov

**AWS re/Start**
- Free 12-week cloud computing training
- Exam costs reimbursed
- Career coaching and job placement
- Cohort-based, intensive program

**Cost to you:** $0. Seriously use these programs.

**Section 6: The Security Clearance Multiplier (60 seconds)**

If you have an active security clearance, this changes everything.

[Visual suggestion: Salary comparison cleared vs non-cleared]

**The numbers:**
- Same job, no clearance: $80K
- Same job with Secret: $95K-$105K
- Same job with TS/SCI: $110K-$130K+

**Maintaining your clearance:**

If you're getting out, find a cleared position within 2 years to keep it active. After 2 years, it becomes inactive and companies have to reinvestigate (expensive for them, limits your options).

**Cleared job boards:**
- ClearanceJobs.com
- ClearedConnections.com
- Indeed.com (filter by security clearance)

**Reality check:** Most major defense contractors only hire cleared IT personnel. Your clearance can be worth $20K-$40K in additional salary.

**Section 8: Common Mistakes to Avoid (60 seconds)**

**Mistake #1:** Taking the first job offered
- First offer is rarely the best offer
- Apply to 20+ companies, compare offers
- Negotiate (IT employers expect it)

**Mistake #2:** Undervaluing yourself
- Don't apply for $45K help desk if you have Security+ and CCNA
- You're qualified for $70K+ roles immediately

**Mistake #3:** Paying for certifications
- Everything can be free for veterans
- Never pay out of pocket for certs listed above

**Mistake #4:** Ignoring remote work
- Many IT jobs are 100% remote now
- You can live in low cost of living area, earn Silicon Valley salary

**Mistake #5:** Not using your clearance
- If you have one, only apply to cleared positions (higher pay)
