# Georgia vs Tennessee Veterans Guide: Complete Comparison 2025

Georgia and Tennessee represent two of the most economically vibrant southeastern states with strong veteran populations, excellent tax benefits, and affordable living. Both states offer military retirement exemptions but with different structures. This comprehensive guide helps you choose between these southeastern destinations.

## Military Retirement Tax Treatment

### Georgia: Military Retirement Fully Exempt

Georgia provides one of America's most generous military retirement tax benefits:

**Georgia Military Retirement Benefits:**
- Military pension: 100% exempt from state income tax (unlimited amount)
- VA disability compensation: 100% exempt
- State income tax rate: 5.75% (flat rate)
- Effective tax on military retirement: 0%

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Military retirement tax: $0 (unlimited exemption)
- Other income tax: $1,150 (at 5.75%)
- **Total state tax: $1,150**

### Tennessee: Military Retirement NOT Exempt (BUT No State Income Tax)

Tennessee uses a unique tax structure:

**Tennessee Tax Benefits:**
- Military pension: Partially exempt ($41,400 standard deduction for most retirees)
- VA disability compensation: 100% exempt
- State income tax rate: 0% (no state income tax)
- Effective tax on military retirement: 0% for most retirees

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Military retirement tax: $0 (standard deduction covers most pensions)
- Other income tax: $0 (no state income tax)
- **Total state tax: $0**

**Tax Winner: Tennessee** (No state income tax at all; Georgia taxes other income)
**Annual savings choosing Tennessee: $1,150+ on $20,000 additional income**
