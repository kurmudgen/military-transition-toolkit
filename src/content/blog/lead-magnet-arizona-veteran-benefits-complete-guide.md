---# Complete Arizona Veteran Benefits Guide 2025
## Your Total Roadmap to Maximizing Arizona's Veteran Benefits
**By Jacob, Navy E6 | Formation Labs**

## ABOUT THIS GUIDE

Arizona ranks among the top veteran-friendly states with no state tax on military retirement pay, generous property tax exemptions, and the Purple Heart Tuition Waiver providing free college education. This guide covers every benefit available to Arizona veterans.

**What You'll Learn:**
- Property tax exemption worth up to $4,748 (2025) with income-based eligibility
- Purple Heart and disabled veteran tuition waivers (free college education)
- No state income tax on military retirement pay
- Employment preference and state hiring programs
- License fee waivers and DMV benefits
- Hunting and fishing license discounts

## CHAPTER 1: Property Tax Exemptions

### DISABLED VETERAN PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTION

**2025 Maximum Exemption:** $4,748 in assessed value

**Who Qualifies:**
- Honorably discharged veteran
- Service-connected OR non-service-connected disability
- Income limits: $38,843 per household ($46,600 with minor children)
- Property assessed value cannot exceed $31,347 (LPV $313,470)

**Exemption Amount:**
Proportional to disability rating percentage.

**Examples:**
- 50% disability = 50% of $4,748 = $2,374 exemption
- 100% disability = Full $4,748 exemption

**Annual Savings:**
$100-$300+ depending on disability percentage and local tax rates

**Application Deadline:**
First Monday in January through last day of February

### 100% COMBAT VETERAN FULL EXEMPTION (NEW LEGISLATION)

**Who Qualifies:**
- Combat veteran
- 100% service-connected disability from VA
- Property is primary residence

**What You Get:**
FULL property tax exemption (entire property value exempt)

**This is new legislation providing complete tax elimination for qualifying combat veterans.**

**Potential Savings:**
$2,000-$6,000+ annually depending on home value

## CHAPTER 2: Education Benefits

### PURPLE HEART TUITION WAIVER

**Who Qualifies:**
- Purple Heart recipient
- Arizona resident OR stationed in Arizona when injured
- 50% or higher VA disability rating

**What's Covered:**
ALL tuition expenses waived for undergraduate degree at Arizona public universities and community colleges.

**Value:**
- University of Arizona: ~$40,000 (4 years)
- Arizona State University: ~$42,000 (4 years)
- Northern Arizona University: ~$38,000 (4 years)

**Not Covered:**
Books, fees, room and board

**Application:**
Through Arizona Department of Veterans' Services (ADVS)

### TUITION WAIVER FOR DEPENDENTS OF DECEASED/DISABLED VETERANS

**Who Qualifies:**

**Tier 1:** Unremarried spouse or child (up to age 30) whose sponsor was:
- Arizona National Guard member OR U.S. Armed Forces member
- Arizona resident or stationed in Arizona
- Killed in line of duty or died from injuries in line of duty

**Tier 2:** Current Arizona National Guard members who received Purple Heart after 9/11/2001

**Tier 3:** Former Arizona National Guard members medically discharged due to injury/disability

**What's Covered:**
Full tuition waiver for undergraduate degree

**Value:**
$38,000-$42,000 per student

**Federal Benefits:**
Can be used concurrently with tuition waiver (use GI Bill for housing, books)

## CHAPTER 3: Income Tax Benefits

### NO STATE INCOME TAX ON MILITARY RETIREMENT PAY

**Arizona does NOT tax:**
✓ Military retirement pay
✓ Survivor Benefit Plan (SBP) payments
✓ VA disability compensation (already federally tax-free)

**Comparison to Other States:**
Many states tax military retirement. Arizona's exemption saves retirees $1,000-$5,000+ annually depending on retirement pay amount.

**Example:**
$30,000 annual military retirement pay in state with 5% income tax = $1,500 annual tax
In Arizona = $0 tax

**20-year savings: $30,000+**

## CHAPTER 4: Employment Preference

### ARIZONA VETERAN EMPLOYMENT PREFERENCE

**State Jobs:**
Veterans receive preference points in state hiring:
- 5 points for veterans
- 10 points for disabled veterans

**Application:**
Must include DD-214 with state job applications to receive preference

**Covered Positions:**
All Arizona state government positions

## CHAPTER 5: Vehicle & License Benefits

### DISABLED VETERAN LICENSE PLATES

**Who Qualifies:**
Arizona resident veteran with service-connected disability

**Benefits:**
- Special DV license plates
- Reduced registration fees
- Some localities offer free parking

**Application:**
Arizona MVD with DD-214 and VA rating letter

### DRIVER LICENSE FEE WAIVER

Veterans with service-connected disabilities may qualify for reduced or waived driver's license fees.

**Check with local MVD office for current policies.**

## CHAPTER 6: Hunting & Fishing Benefits

### DISABLED VETERAN HUNTING/FISHING LICENSE

**Who Qualifies:**
50%+ service-connected disability

**Benefit:**
Significantly reduced fees on combination hunting/fishing licenses

**Regular price:** $60-$150
**Disabled veteran price:** $20-$40

**Annual savings:** $40-$110

## CHAPTER 7: Additional Benefits

### ARIZONA VETERAN PROPERTY TAX ASSISTANCE PROGRAM

For veterans facing financial hardship, some counties offer property tax payment assistance or deferrals.

**Contact your county assessor for specific programs.**

### VETERANS' DONATION PROGRAM

License plate donation program where proceeds fund veteran services.

### STATE VETERANS' HOMES

Arizona operates state veterans' homes providing skilled nursing care:
- Phoenix Veterans' Home
- Tucson Veterans' Home

**Eligibility:**
Veterans needing skilled nursing care

**Costs:**
Based on income and VA benefits

## CHAPTER 8: Application Process

### PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTION APPLICATION

**Timeline:**
Apply between first Monday of January and last day of February

**Required Documents:**
□ DD-214
□ VA benefit summary letter (disability rating)
□ Proof of residency
□ Proof of income (last year's tax return)

**Where to Apply:**
County Assessor's office in county where property located

**Processing:**
4-6 weeks

### TUITION WAIVER APPLICATION

**Step 1:** Verify eligibility with Arizona DVS
**Step 2:** Complete ADVS Tuition Waiver Verification Form
**Step 3:** Submit to ADVS: benefits@azdvs.gov
**Step 4:** Receive verification letter
**Step 5:** Submit to school financial aid office

**Contact:**
Arizona DVS: (602) 234-5804
