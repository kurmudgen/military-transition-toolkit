# Tennessee Veteran Benefits: Complete State Programs Guide (2025)

Tennessee honors its 470,000+ veterans with substantial state benefits including property tax relief, education assistance, and special privileges. As a state with no income tax on wages, Tennessee is already tax-friendlyâ€”and the veteran-specific benefits make it even better.

This guide covers all Tennessee veteran benefits with eligibility requirements and application procedures.

## Property Tax Exemptions

### Disabled Veteran Property Tax Relief

**100% Permanently and Totally Disabled Veterans:**
- **Exemption:** First $175,000 of home value exempt from property taxes
- **Savings:** Approximately $1,500-$3,000+ annually (varies by county)
- **Includes:** Home and up to 1 acre

**Eligibility:**
- 100% permanent and total service-connected disability
- Own and occupy home as primary residence
- Tennessee resident

### Surviving Spouse Relief

**Surviving Spouse of Service Member Killed in Action:**
- Same $175,000 exemption
- Must remain unremarried
- Must continue occupying the home

**Surviving Spouse of 100% Disabled Veteran:**
- Continues receiving exemption
- Must remain unremarried
- Must have been married at time of veteran's death

### How to Apply

1. Obtain VA letter certifying permanent and total disability
2. Visit your county Trustee's office
3. Complete Property Tax Relief Application
4. Submit DD-214, VA letter, and deed
5. Apply by April 5 for current tax year

## Education Benefits

### Tennessee STRONG Act

**Dependent Children/Spouse Education Benefit:**
- Free tuition and mandatory fees at Tennessee public colleges
- For dependents of 100% disabled veterans or veterans killed in action

**Eligibility for Dependents:**
- Child must be under age 23 (or 25 if veteran was killed in action)
- Spouse remains eligible while unmarried
- Veteran must have been Tennessee resident

**Coverage:**
- Tuition at all Tennessee public universities and community colleges
- Mandatory fees
- Does not cover books, room, or board

### Tennessee National Guard Tuition Assistance

**Active Guard Members:**
- Up to 100% tuition at Tennessee public colleges
- Based on availability of funds

### In-State Tuition for Veterans

**All Veterans:**
- Qualify for in-state tuition immediately
- No residency waiting period
- Covers GI Bill users under VACA

## Vehicle Benefits

### Disabled Veteran License Plates

**Free Plates for:**
- 100% Disabled Veterans
- Purple Heart recipients
- Medal of Honor recipients
- Former POWs
- Pearl Harbor Survivors

**Benefits:**
- Free registration
- Free plate
- Applies to one vehicle

### How to Apply

1. Visit Tennessee County Clerk's office
2. Provide DD-214 and VA disability documentation
3. Complete registration form

## Hunting and Fishing

### Free Hunting/Fishing License

**Permanently and Totally Disabled Veterans:**
- Free Lifetime Sportsman License
- Includes hunting, fishing, and trout privilege
- Valued at $200+

**Service-Connected Disabled Veterans (Less Than 100%):**
- Discounted licenses available
- Contact TWRA for specific discounts

### How to Apply

1. Visit TWRA website or license agent
2. Provide VA permanent and total disability documentation
3. Apply for license

## Employment Preferences

### State Employment Preference

**Tennessee Veterans' Preference:**

**How to Claim:**

## Financial Benefits

### No State Income Tax on Wages

**Benefit:**
- Tennessee has no state income tax on wages or salary
- All military retirement pay is effectively tax-free at state level
- Disability compensation also tax-free

**Note:** Tennessee previously had the "Hall Tax" on investment income, but this was fully repealed in 2021.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Tennessee tax military retirement pay?

No. Tennessee has no state income tax on wages, so military retirement pay is not taxed.

### What disability rating do I need for property tax relief?

You need 100% permanent and total disability rating from the VA for the full $175,000 exemption.

### Can my kids get free college tuition?

Yes, if you're 100% disabled or died from service-connected causes. Apply through Tennessee STRONG Act.

### How do I get a lifetime hunting/fishing license?

Visit TWRA with proof of 100% permanent and total disability rating. The license is free and valid for life.

### Are Guard/Reserve members eligible?

Depends on the benefit. Many require qualifying active duty service. Guard members who served on active duty may qualify.

## Bottom Line

Tennessee's combination of no state income tax and generous veteran benefits makes it one of the most financially attractive states for veterans. The property tax relief for 100% disabled veterans is among the highest in the nation.

2. **$175,000 property tax exemption** - For 100% disabled veterans
3. **STRONG Act education benefits** - Free tuition for dependents
4. **Free lifetime hunting/fishing** - For permanently disabled veterans
