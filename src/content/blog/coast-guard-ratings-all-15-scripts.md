---# Coast Guard Ratings - All 15 Career Transition Scripts

Complete career guidance for top 15 Coast Guard ratings.

---

## BM - Boatswain's Mate

**Civilian careers:** Maritime operations ($50K-$80K), Harbor master ($65K-$95K), Port operations ($55K-$85K), Merchant marine deck officer (with license $70K-$120K), Tug boat operator ($60K-$90K)

**Best path:** Pursue merchant marine license or harbor/port operations

**Top employers:** Shipping companies, ports, maritime authorities, towing companies

---

## MK - Machinery Technician

**Civilian careers:** Marine engineer (with license $75K-$120K), Industrial mechanic ($60K-$90K), Power plant operator ($70K-$105K), HVAC technician ($55K-$85K), Manufacturing maintenance ($65K-$95K)

**Best path:** Marine engineering license or power plant operations

**Top employers:** Shipping companies, power plants, utilities, maritime companies

---

## OS - Operations Specialist

**Civilian careers:** Air traffic controller ($60K-$140K if under 31), Emergency dispatcher ($45K-$65K), Operations coordinator ($50K-$75K), Maritime traffic controller ($55K-$85K)

**Best path:** FAA air traffic control or maritime operations

**Top employers:** FAA, ports, vessel traffic services, emergency dispatch centers

---

## IT - Information Systems Technician

**Civilian careers:** Network administrator ($65K-$95K), Cybersecurity analyst ($80K-$130K), IT support ($55K-$80K), Systems administrator ($70K-$100K)

**Best path:** Cybersecurity with Security+/CCNA certs

**Top employers:** Defense contractors, tech companies, corporate IT

**Certifications:** Security+, CCNA, CISSP through DoD COOL

---

## HS - Health Services Technician

**Civilian careers:** Paramedic ($48K-$65K), Medical assistant ($35K-$50K), Registered Nurse via accelerated programs ($70K-$98K), Emergency medical services ($45K-$65K)

**Best path:** Accelerated BSN programs or paramedic certification

**Top employers:** Hospitals, clinics, EMS services, urgent care

---

## ME - Maritime Enforcement Specialist

**Civilian careers:** Federal law enforcement ($73K-$110K), Border patrol ($73K-$95K), Customs officer ($60K-$90K), Police officer ($55K-$85K), Harbor police ($60K-$90K)

**Best path:** Federal law enforcement (CBP, FBI, DEA)

**Top employers:** CBP, FBI, DEA, local police, port authorities

---

## ET - Electronics Technician

**Civilian careers:** Electronics technician ($60K-$90K), Marine electronics ($65K-$95K), Industrial electronics ($65K-$95K), Avionics tech ($65K-$100K)

**Best path:** Marine or industrial electronics technician

**Top employers:** Maritime companies, industrial facilities, airlines

---

## SK - Storekeeper

**Civilian careers:** Supply chain coordinator ($50K-$75K), Warehouse manager ($55K-$80K), Inventory specialist ($45K-$70K), Procurement specialist ($55K-$85K)

**Best path:** Corporate supply chain or federal logistics

**Top employers:** Logistics companies, federal government, corporate supply chain

**Certifications:** APICS CPIM through DoD COOL

---

## YN - Yeoman

**Civilian careers:** Office administrator ($45K-$65K), Executive assistant ($50K-$75K), HR specialist ($50K-$75K), Administrative specialist ($45K-$70K)

**Best path:** Corporate admin or federal administrative specialist (GS-5 to GS-9)

**Top employers:** Federal government, Fortune 500, healthcare admin

---

## DC - Damage Controlman

**Civilian careers:** Firefighter ($50K-$80K), Industrial firefighter ($60K-$90K), Maritime safety specialist ($60K-$95K), Fire inspector ($55K-$80K), Shipyard safety ($60K-$85K)

**Best path:** Municipal firefighter or industrial/maritime safety

**Top employers:** Fire departments, shipyards, maritime companies, industrial facilities

---

## GM - Gunner's Mate

**Civilian careers:** Weapons technician ($55K-$85K), Defense contractor ordnance ($70K-$110K with clearance), Law enforcement armorer ($50K-$75K), Firearms instructor ($45K-$75K)

**Best path:** Defense contractor or law enforcement armorer

**Top employers:** Defense contractors, police departments, military installations (civilian)

---

## FS - Food Service Specialist

**Civilian careers:** Chef ($45K-$75K), Restaurant manager ($50K-$75K), Institutional food service ($45K-$70K), Catering manager ($50K-$75K), Cruise ship chef ($50K-$85K)

**Best path:** Maritime food service or institutional management

**Top employers:** Cruise lines, maritime companies, hotels, hospitals

---

## MST - Marine Science Technician

**Civilian careers:** Environmental scientist ($60K-$90K), Marine biologist (with degree $55K-$85K), Environmental compliance ($60K-$95K), Oceanographer (with degree $65K-$100K), Oil spill response ($55K-$85K)

**Best path:** Environmental compliance or marine/environmental science (use GI Bill for degree)

**Top employers:** EPA, environmental consulting firms, oil companies, NOAA

---

## AMT - Aviation Maintenance Technician

**Civilian careers:** Aircraft mechanic with A&P ($65K-$100K), Helicopter mechanic ($60K-$90K), Aviation maintenance ($60K-$90K), Airline mechanic ($75K-$155K at majors)

**Best path:** Get FAA A&P license, work toward airline or helicopter operations

**Top employers:** Airlines, helicopter operators (EMS, tourism), Coast Guard contractors

---

## AST - Aviation Survival Technician (Rescue Swimmer)

**Civilian careers:** Emergency medical services ($45K-$70K), Firefighter/paramedic ($55K-$85K), Search and rescue ($50K-$80K), Lifeguard supervisor ($40K-$65K), Safety specialist ($60K-$90K)

**Best path:** Fire department (paramedic/rescue) or EMS leadership

**Top employers:** Fire departments, EMS services, search & rescue organizations

**Note:** Your rescue swimmer background is unique - leverage in interviews
