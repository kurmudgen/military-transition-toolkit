[INTRO]
Captain Dan Torres lost both legs to IED in Afghanistan 2015. Through adaptive sports and determination, he became Paralympic track athlete, motivational speaker, and advocate for wounded warriors, earning income through speaking ($5K-20K per event), sponsorships, and veterans advocacy work.

[MAIN CONTENT]
**Injury:** Infantry officer, IED blast, double above-knee amputation, 2 years recovery at Walter Reed, medically retired.
**Recovery:** Discovered adaptive sports through Wounded Warrior Project, began running with prosthetics, competed in Invictus Games 2017, qualified for Paralympics 2020.
**Career:** Professional adaptive athlete, motivational speaker (100+ events annually), brand ambassador for prosthetics company, veterans advocate, board member of adaptive sports organizations.
**Income:** Speaking fees ($5K-20K per event), sponsorships, book deals, corporate partnerships. Combined income $150K-250K annually.
**Impact:** Inspired thousands of wounded veterans, advocated for VA adaptive sports programs, raised millions for veteran causes.

[CONCLUSION]
Dan turned his devastating injury into a platform to inspire and help other veterans. His story shows that even the hardest transitions can lead to meaningful purpose and impact.
