# CISSP for Veterans: Your Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

The Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) is the gold-standard certification for senior cybersecurity professionals and one of the highest-paying credentials veterans can earn. The exam costs $749, with total preparation typically running $1,000-$5,000 depending on your study approach. CISSP requires five years of professional security experience (with possible waivers), and most experienced professionals prepare for 3-6 months. While challenging, CISSP is GI Bill eligible and can boost salary to $120,000-$180,000+, making it one of the best ROI certifications available.

## What Is CISSP?

CISSP, administered by (ISC)², is the premier certification for experienced security practitioners, managers, and executives. It validates deep expertise across eight security domains and demonstrates the ability to design, implement, and manage enterprise cybersecurity programs.

CISSP is not entry-level—it's designed for professionals who have moved beyond implementation into architecture, management, and leadership. The certification is globally recognized and often required for Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) and senior security architect positions.

The exam covers eight domains:
1. Security and Risk Management
2. Asset Security
3. Security Architecture and Engineering
4. Communication and Network Security
5. Identity and Access Management (IAM)
6. Security Assessment and Testing
7. Security Operations
8. Software Development Security

CISSP meets DoD 8570.01-M requirements for IAT Level III and IAM Level II positions—the highest levels.

## Why Veterans Excel at CISSP

Military experience provides exceptional preparation for enterprise security leadership:

**Risk Management Experience**: Military operations involve constant risk assessment and mitigation—core CISSP concepts.

**Policy and Compliance Background**: Military personnel understand following and enforcing security policies, frameworks, and regulations.

**Leadership and Management Skills**: NCOs and officers have managed teams, resources, and complex operations.

**Classified Information Handling**: Experience with security clearances directly translates to information security concepts.

**Relevant MOS/Ratings That Align**:
- Army: 17A (Cyber Operations Officer), 255A (Information Services Technician), 35Q (Cryptologic Network Warfare Specialist)
- Navy: 1810 (Cryptologic Warfare Officer), IDC (Information Dominance Corps)
- Air Force: 17D (Cyberspace Operations Officer), 1N4X1A (Network Intelligence Analyst)
- Marines: 0602 (Communications Officer), 0689 (Cyber Security Technician)

Many senior NCOs and officers have the experience and mindset ideal for CISSP success.

## Requirements & Prerequisites

**Experience Requirement**:
- Five years cumulative paid work experience in two or more of the eight CISSP domains
- Four-year college degree OR approved credential (Security+, CCNA Security, etc.) waives one year
- Maximum one-year waiver possible = minimum four years required experience

**The Associate of (ISC)² Path**:
- If you lack required experience, you can pass the exam and become an "Associate of (ISC)²"
- You have six years to earn the required experience
- Great option for transitioning veterans building civilian experience

**Endorsement Requirement**:
- After passing, another CISSP must endorse your application
- (ISC)² can act as endorser if you don't know one
- Application reviewed within 4-6 weeks

## GI Bill & VA Funding Options

### Post-9/11 GI Bill

CISSP training is covered through VA-approved programs:
- University cybersecurity certificate programs
- VA-approved boot camps (SANS, InfoSec Institute, etc.)
- Exam fees when part of approved training

**How to Use It**:
1. Verify benefits at VA.gov
2. Search WEAMS for CISSP-focused programs
3. Apply for benefits and enroll
4. Complete training and certification

### VR&E (Chapter 31)

Veterans with disability ratings may receive:
- Boot camp or training coverage
- Exam fee ($749)
- Study materials
- Support services

VR&E counselors often approve CISSP for veterans with significant IT/security experience.

### Credentialing Assistance (Active Duty)

While $749 exceeds typical per-exam limits, boot camp programs may qualify:
- Check with Education Center for program approvals
- May require supervisor recommendation for advanced certifications

### Other Funding Options

**(ISC)² Scholarships**: Various scholarships available for veterans and underrepresented groups
**Employer Sponsorship**: Many employers pay for CISSP—it directly benefits them
**Military Associations**: Some veteran organizations offer certification funding
**Tuition Assistance + Personal Funds**: Combine programs for full coverage

## The Exam: What to Expect

**Format**: Computerized Adaptive Testing (CAT)
**Number of Questions**: 125-175 questions (adaptive)
**Time Limit**: 4 hours
**Passing Score**: 700 out of 1,000
**Question Types**: Multiple choice and advanced innovative items (drag-and-drop, hotspot)
**Testing**: Pearson VUE centers only (no remote proctoring)

**The CAT Experience**:
- Exam adapts to your ability level
- Questions get harder as you demonstrate competence
- Minimum 125 questions = you either passed or failed early
- Maximum 175 questions = exam needed more data
- Most people find it very difficult—that's normal

**Key Insight**: CISSP tests your ability to think like a security manager, not a technician. It asks "what should you do first?" and "what's the BEST answer?"—requiring judgment, not just knowledge.

## Certification Maintenance

**Renewal Period**: Every 3 years
**CPE Requirement**: 120 Continuing Professional Education credits over 3 years (40/year minimum)
**Annual Maintenance Fee**: $125/year

**Ways to Earn CPEs**:
- Work experience (max 40/year)
- Training and education
- Conferences and events
- Teaching/mentoring
- Writing articles or books
- Volunteer work
- Earning other certifications

**Pro Tip**: Many activities you already do count toward CPEs—document them throughout the year.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Treating It Like a Technical Exam**: CISSP tests managerial thinking, not technical implementation

2. **Studying Only One Resource**: Use multiple books, videos, and practice exams

3. **Neglecting the "Why"**: Understand concepts deeply, not just definitions

4. **Insufficient Practice Questions**: Do 1,500-3,000+ practice questions minimum

5. **Ignoring the CAT Format**: Practice adaptive testing and pacing strategies

6. **Underestimating Domain Breadth**: All eight domains appear—you can't skip any

7. **Not Thinking Like a Manager**: Ask "What would a CISO do?" not "What would I do?"

8. **Rushing to Take the Exam**: Take extra time if needed—$749 is expensive to repeat

## Your 90-Day Action Plan

### Days 1-30: Assessment & Foundation

- [ ] Verify you meet experience requirements (or plan Associate path)
- [ ] Research GI Bill/VR&E coverage for training programs
- [ ] Obtain official (ISC)² study guide and practice tests
- [ ] Join r/cissp subreddit and Discord communities
- [ ] Watch Destination Certification MindMaps (free on YouTube)
- [ ] Create study schedule: minimum 15-20 hours per week
- [ ] Begin reading study guide—cover 2-3 domains
- [ ] Take baseline practice exam to identify weak areas
- [ ] Understand the CAT format and scoring

### Days 31-60: Deep Domain Study

- [ ] Complete study guide—all eight domains
- [ ] Watch Kelly Handerhan or Thor Teaches videos for weak areas
- [ ] Take domain-specific practice quizzes
- [ ] Read 11th Hour CISSP for reinforcement
- [ ] Practice 50-100 questions daily
- [ ] Focus on risk management thinking
- [ ] Join weekly study groups (online or local)
- [ ] Review official (ISC)² practice tests

### Days 61-90: Exam Preparation & Execution

- [ ] Complete Boson or similar premium practice exams
- [ ] Score consistently 75%+ on practice tests before scheduling
- [ ] Schedule exam at Pearson VUE (allow 2-week buffer)
- [ ] Final review of all domains using MindMaps
- [ ] Practice full-length CAT simulations
- [ ] Light review 2-3 days before exam
- [ ] Take and pass the exam
- [ ] Begin endorsement process
- [ ] Submit CPE credits and pay AMF
- [ ] Update LinkedIn and pursue senior opportunities
