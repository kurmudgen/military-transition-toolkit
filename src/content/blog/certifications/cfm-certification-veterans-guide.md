# CFM (Certified Facility Manager) for Veterans: Your Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

The Certified Facility Manager (CFM) from IFMA (International Facility Management Association) is the premier credential for facility management professionals. The exam costs $550-$750, with preparation ranging from self-study to professional courses ($500-$3,500). Most candidates prepare in 8-16 weeks. Veterans with military facilities, base operations, or installation management experience are exceptionally well-prepared. CFM holders earn $80,000-$130,000, with director-level positions exceeding $160,000.

## What Is CFM Certification?

The CFM certification from IFMA validates expertise across eleven core competencies of facility management:

1. Occupancy and Human Factors
2. Operations and Maintenance
3. Sustainability
4. Facility Information Management and Technology
5. Risk Management
6. Communication
7. Performance and Quality
8. Leadership and Strategy
9. Real Estate and Property Management
10. Project Management
11. Finance and Business

CFM demonstrates strategic facility management capability—not just building maintenance, but comprehensive facility leadership.

## Why Veterans Excel at Facility Management

Military experience provides exceptional preparation:

**Installation Operations**: Military bases are massive facilities requiring comprehensive management.

**Multi-Disciplinary Coordination**: Base operations coordinate numerous functions—exactly what FM requires.

**Emergency Planning**: Military emergency readiness translates to facility continuity planning.

**Resource Management**: Budget, personnel, and asset management experience.

**Directly Related Experience**:
- Base operations officers and NCOs
- Installation management
- Facilities engineering (Navy/Marine Corps)
- Civil engineering (Air Force)
- Post/Camp/Station operations
- Any supervisory role managing buildings/grounds

## Requirements & Prerequisites

**CFM Eligibility Requirements**:

**Option 1**: CFM credential by examination
- Meet education and experience requirements:
  - Bachelor's degree + 3 years FM experience
  - Associate's + 5 years FM experience
  - High school + 8 years FM experience

**Option 2**: CFM by Demonstrated Competency
- Bachelor's degree + 10 years FM experience
- Portfolio demonstration process

**Military Experience Counts**: Your installation/facilities experience qualifies as FM experience.

## GI Bill & VA Funding Options

### VR&E (Chapter 31)

VR&E may cover:
- CFM exam fees
- Study materials
- Prep courses

### Credentialing Assistance (Active Duty)

CFM exam fits within CredA limits:
- $550-$750 typically covered
- Check with your Education Center

## The Exam: What to Expect

### Exam Format

**Format**: Computer-based, multiple choice
**Number of Questions**: 180 questions
**Time Limit**: 4 hours
**Passing Score**: Scaled, approximately 500 out of 800
**Testing**: Prometric centers

### Exam Content Areas

1. Occupancy and Human Factors (12%)
2. Operations and Maintenance (14%)
3. Sustainability (10%)
4. Facility Information Management & Technology (6%)
5. Risk Management (10%)
6. Communication (6%)
7. Performance and Quality (8%)
8. Leadership and Strategy (12%)
9. Real Estate and Property Management (6%)
10. Project Management (8%)
11. Finance and Business (8%)

### Question Style

- Scenario-based situations
- Best practice recommendations
- Application of knowledge
- Multi-step problem solving

## Certification Maintenance

**Recertification**: Every 3 years
**CFM Maintenance Points**: 120 points required
**Annual Fee**: None (recertification fee at 3 years)

**Point Categories**:
- Education and training
- Professional contributions
- Teaching and presenting
- Publications

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Underestimating Breadth**: CFM covers all eleven competencies—study them all

2. **Ignoring Finance/Business**: This isn't just technical—business acumen matters

3. **Skipping Sustainability**: Increasingly important in modern FM

4. **Not Using IFMA Resources**: Official resources align directly with exam

5. **Rushing the Process**: Take time to absorb the comprehensive content

6. **Ignoring Leadership Content**: 12% of the exam—strategic thinking required
