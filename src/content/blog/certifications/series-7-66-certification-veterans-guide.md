# Series 7 and Series 66 for Veterans: Your Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

The Series 7 (General Securities Representative) and Series 66 (Uniform Combined State Law) licenses allow you to sell securities and provide investment advice. Must be sponsored by a FINRA member firm to take exams. Exam fees total ~$500 (usually employer-paid). Training takes 2-4 months. Veterans' trustworthiness and analytical skills excel in financial services. Entry-level financial advisors earn $50,000-$70,000, with successful advisors reaching $150,000-$500,000+. Many firms actively recruit veterans with paid training programs.

## What Are Series 7 and Series 66?

**Series 7 (General Securities Representative)**:
- Sell stocks, bonds, mutual funds, options
- Most comprehensive securities license
- Required for full-service financial advisors
- FINRA-administered exam

**Series 66 (Uniform Combined State Law)**:
- Combines Series 63 (state law) and Series 65 (investment advisor)
- Required to give investment advice for fees
- Needed in addition to Series 7 for advisory roles
- State-level registration

**Together**: Series 7 + 66 allows you to work as a full-service financial advisor.

## Requirements & Prerequisites

**Key Requirement**: You MUST be sponsored by a FINRA member firm before taking Series 7.

**Series 7 Prerequisites**:
- Sponsorship by FINRA-registered broker-dealer
- SIE (Securities Industry Essentials) exam or exemption
- Clean background check (Form U4)

**Series 66 Prerequisites**:
- Usually taken after/with Series 7
- No separate sponsorship required
- State registration through firm

**SIE Exam**: Can be taken without sponsorship, tests basic securities knowledge. Good way to prepare while job hunting.

## The Exams

### SIE (Securities Industry Essentials)

**Questions**: 75 (10 unscored)
**Time**: 1 hour 45 minutes
**Passing Score**: 70%
**Content**: Basic securities concepts, regulations, markets

### Series 7

**Questions**: 125 (10 unscored)
**Time**: 3 hours 45 minutes
**Passing Score**: 72%
**Content**: Securities products, client accounts, trading, regulations

**Topics Include**:
- Equity securities
- Debt securities
- Packaged securities and options
- Customer accounts
- Securities regulations

### Series 66

**Questions**: 100 (10 unscored)
**Time**: 2 hours 30 minutes
**Passing Score**: 73%
**Content**: State law, investment advisor regulations, ethics

## License Maintenance

- Continuing education required
- Firm-element training annually
- Regulatory-element every 3 years
- Background updated through Form U4
- State registrations maintained through firm

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Trying to Take Series 7 Without Sponsorship**: You need a firm first
2. **Underestimating Series 7 Difficulty**: It's challenging—plan 4-8 weeks minimum
3. **Choosing Wrong Firm**: Culture and training quality vary significantly
4. **Ignoring SIE Preparation**: Take SIE first to show commitment to employers
5. **Not Building Network Early**: Start connecting before you're licensed
