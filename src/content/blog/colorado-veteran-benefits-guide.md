# Colorado Veteran Benefits: Complete State Programs Guide (2025)

Colorado, home to major military installations including Peterson Space Force Base, Fort Carson, Buckley Space Force Base, and the Air Force Academy, offers strong benefits to its 400,000+ veteran residents. From property tax exemptions to military retirement income exclusions, Colorado provides meaningful support.

This guide covers all Colorado veteran benefits with eligibility requirements and application procedures.

## Property Tax Exemptions

### Disabled Veteran Property Tax Exemption

**100% Permanently Disabled Veterans:**
- **Exemption:** 50% of first $200,000 of actual home value
- **Maximum Benefit:** $100,000 assessed value exempt
- **Tax Savings:** Approximately $700-$1,400+ annually (varies by county)

**Eligibility:**
- 100% permanent disability rating (service-connected or non-service-connected)
- Or 100% individual unemployability (IU)
- Own and occupy home as primary residence
- Colorado resident

### Surviving Spouse Exemption

**Surviving Spouse of Qualifying Veteran:**
- Same 50% exemption continues
- Must have been married to veteran at time of death
- Must remain unremarried
- Must continue occupying the home

### How to Apply

1. Obtain VA letter or other disability documentation
2. Contact your county Assessor's office
3. Complete Disabled Veteran Property Tax Exemption application
4. Submit DD-214 and disability documentation
5. Apply by July 1 for current tax year

## Income Tax Benefits

### Military Retirement Pay Exclusion

**Colorado Tax Benefit (Age 55-64):**
- Exclude up to $20,000 of military retirement pay
- From Colorado state income taxes

**Colorado Tax Benefit (Age 65+):**
- Exclude up to $24,000 of retirement income (including military)
- Part of general retirement income exclusion

### Colorado National Guard Benefits

**State Income Tax Exclusion:**
- Up to $5,500 exclusion for Guard drill pay
- For enlisted members with income under $50,000

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Colorado tax military retirement pay?

Partially. Colorado excludes up to $20,000 (ages 55-64) or $24,000 (65+) of retirement income from state taxes.

### What disability rating do I need for property tax exemption?

100% permanent disability (service-connected or non-service-connected) qualifies you for the 50% exemption.

### Can I get free hunting/fishing licenses?

100% disabled veterans receive free licenses. 70%+ disabled veterans receive significant discounts.

### How do I get veteran designation on my license?

Visit any DMV with your DD-214 and request the free designation.

### Are Guard/Reserve members eligible?

Depends on the benefit. Many require qualifying service. Active Guard members may qualify for Guard-specific benefits.
