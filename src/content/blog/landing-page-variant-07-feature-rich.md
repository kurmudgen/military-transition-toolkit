## A/B Test Hypothesis

### What We're Testing
**Primary:** Does showcasing comprehensive features convert better than pain points, benefits, or other angles?

**Secondary:** Do detailed feature descriptions build confidence and reduce purchase hesitation?

**Tertiary:** Does "all features included" positioning justify the price better than other approaches?

### Expected Winner
**Moderate confidence‚Äîperformance will vary by audience segment.**

**Will perform well with:**
- **Research-oriented users** who want to understand exactly what they're getting
- **High-detail personalities** (common among officers and senior enlisted)
- **Comparison shoppers** evaluating multiple transition resources
- **Users who value "complete solution"** over single-feature tools

**May underperform with:**
- **Decision-fatigued users** who find feature lists overwhelming
- **Emotional decision-makers** who need story/trust first
- **Mobile users** (long page, lots of detail)

**Prediction:** Middle-tier performer (5th-7th place) for general audience, but will be #1-2 for certain segments (officers, analytical personalities, comparison shoppers).

### Success Metrics
- **Primary:** Free trial sign-up rate (target: 10-12%)
- **Secondary:** Time on page (expect highest of all variants‚Äîlots to read)
- **Engagement:** Screenshot views (lightbox clicks), feature accordion expansions
- **Quality:** Trial-to-paid conversion (expect higher‚Äîusers who convert from this page know exactly what they're buying, less buyer's remorse)
- **Segment performance:** Track by referral source (hypothesis: paid search comparison traffic will love this)

### Statistical Significance Needed
- Minimum 300 visitors per variant (higher volume needed due to detail)
- 95% confidence level
- Run for 14+ days (longer page = more time needed to evaluate)
- Segment by traffic source and device (hypothesis: desktop will outperform mobile for this variant)
- Track engagement metrics: scroll depth, screenshot views, accordion expansions
