# Minnesota Military Retirement: Best Cities, Housing Costs & Quality of Life

Minnesota ranks among the top states for veterans thanks to the property tax Market Value Exclusion for disabled veterans (up to $300,000), partial military retirement tax exemption, exceptional schools, and one of the strongest economies in the nation.

But where should you actually live? Minnesota's cities vary significantly in housing costs, job opportunities, weather severity, and access to VA healthcare.

This guide ranks Minnesota's best cities for veterans with real data on home prices, veteran populations, and quality of life factors.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Best overall for veterans**: Minneapolis/Twin Cities (jobs, healthcare, quality of life, but expensive)
**Best for affordability**: Duluth, Mankato, St. Cloud
**Best for healthcare access**: Minneapolis (VA medical center) and Rochester (Mayo Clinic)
**Best college town**: St. Cloud (St. Cloud State, affordable)
**Best small-city balance**: Rochester (Mayo Clinic employment, manageable size)

**Median home price range**: $185,000 (Duluth) to $465,000 (Minneapolis)
**State average**: $355,000

## Climate and Weather Considerations

### Overall Minnesota Climate
- **Winters**: Very cold to brutal (0 to -20°F common, wind chills to -40°F)
- **Summers**: Warm to hot (75-90°F)
- **Snowfall**: 40-100+ inches annually
- **Seasons**: Four very distinct seasons

### By Region

**Twin Cities (Minneapolis/St. Paul)**
- Cold winters (0-25°F typical)
- 50-60 inches of snow annually
- Warm, sometimes humid summers (80-90°F)
- Spring and fall are beautiful but short

**Southern Minnesota (Rochester, Mankato)**
- Slightly milder winters (5-30°F)
- Less snow than northern Minnesota (40-50 inches)
- Hotter summers

**Northern Minnesota (Duluth)**
- Very cold winters (-10 to 20°F)
- Lake effect snow (80-100 inches)
- Cool summers (60-75°F) - most comfortable
- Beautiful fall colors

**Western Minnesota (Moorhead)**
- Very cold winters with prairie winds
- Less snow but brutal wind chills
- Hot summers

### Veteran Health Considerations

**Extreme cold**: Minnesota winters are among the coldest in the continental U.S. Veterans with cold-weather injuries, circulation issues, or respiratory conditions should consider this carefully.

**Heating costs**: Winter heating bills can be significant, especially in older homes.

**Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)**: Long, dark winters can affect mental health. VA provides mental health services and light therapy.

**Benefits of cold**: Excellent for veterans who dislike heat and humidity. Summers are comfortable with low humidity most days.
