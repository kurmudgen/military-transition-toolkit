# Plumbing License for Veterans: Career Path Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **Time to get licensed:** 4-5 years (apprenticeship) or accelerated with military credit
- **Total cost:** $0-$10,000 (apprenticeships are paid training)
- **Average starting salary:** $40,000-$55,000 (journeyman: $55,000-$80,000)
- **Best military backgrounds:** UT (Navy), 12K (Army Plumber), 3E4X1 (Air Force), 1169 (Marine Corps)
- **Job outlook:** 2% growth (stable, essential trade)

## What Is a Plumbing License?

Plumbers install, maintain, and repair water, drainage, and gas piping systems in residential, commercial, and industrial settings. Licensing varies by state but typically includes:

**Apprentice Plumber:** Learning under supervision (4-5 years typically)
**Journeyman Plumber:** Licensed to work independently
**Master Plumber:** Can design systems, pull permits, own business, supervise

Most states require 8,000+ hours of experience plus passing exams for journeyman and master levels.

## Why Veterans Excel in Plumbing

Military training creates excellent plumbers:

**Mechanical aptitude:** Military equipment maintenance develops hands-on skills that transfer directly.

**Physical capability:** Plumbing is physically demanding—lifting, crawling, working in tight spaces.

**Problem-solving:** Plumbing troubleshooting requires logical diagnosis under pressure.

**Blueprint reading:** Military technical training often includes schematic reading—essential for plumbing.

**Safety awareness:** Plumbing involves hazards (gas, trenching, tools). Your military safety training applies.

## Certification/License Requirements

### Apprentice Requirements
- **Age:** 18+ years
- **Education:** High school diploma or GED
- **Math:** Basic math/algebra
- **Physical capability:** Able to perform plumbing work
- **Valid driver's license**

### Journeyman License Requirements
- **Hours:** Typically 8,000 hours (4-5 years)
- **Education:** Related classroom instruction
- **Exam:** Written and sometimes practical
- **Military credit:** Many states offer hour reductions

### Master Plumber Requirements
- **Journeyman license:** Prerequisite
- **Additional experience:** Usually 2-4 more years
- **Exam:** More advanced
- **Business knowledge:** Often required

### Getting Military Credit

1. **Document your experience:**
   - DD-214 with MOS code
   - Training certificates
   - VMET (Verification of Military Experience and Training)
   - OJT logs if available

2. **Contact state licensing board:**
   - Request military experience evaluation
   - Submit documentation
   - Ask about accelerated licensure

3. **Contact UA Local:**
   - Many accept military plumbing experience
   - Veterans in Piping provides direct pathway

### Experience Translation

**Military experience:** "Maintained water distribution, wastewater collection, and fuel distribution systems on naval base"

**Civilian translation:** "Installed, maintained, and repaired water supply and drainage systems serving 5,000+ users, including water heaters, fixtures, and underground distribution piping"

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does it take to become a journeyman plumber?**
A: Typically 4-5 years (8,000+ hours). Military credit and accelerated programs can reduce this.

**Q: What's the UA Veterans in Piping program?**
A: Free 18-week training at military bases for transitioning service members. Leads directly to union apprenticeship with advanced standing.

**Q: Is the journeyman exam hard?**
A: Moderately difficult. Requires code knowledge and practical understanding. Serious study required.

**Q: Union or non-union—which is better for veterans?**
A: Union typically pays more with excellent benefits. The UA actively recruits veterans and offers dedicated programs.

**Q: Can I use my GI Bill for plumbing training?**
A: Yes. Trade schools are covered, and registered apprenticeships qualify for GI Bill housing allowance.

**Q: Do I need math skills for plumbing?**
A: Yes—measuring, pipe sizing, grade calculations. Basic algebra is sufficient.

**Q: Is plumbing physically demanding?**
A: Yes. Lifting, crawling in tight spaces, working in weather. Military fitness helps.

**Q: Can I start my own plumbing business?**
A: Yes, with master plumber license. Many veterans become successful plumbing contractors.

**Q: What's the difference between plumber and pipefitter?**
A: Plumbers work on water/drainage systems. Pipefitters work on high-pressure industrial systems. Both are covered under UA.

**Q: What tools do I need to buy?**
A: Employers typically provide power tools. Expect to invest $500-$1,500 in hand tools.
