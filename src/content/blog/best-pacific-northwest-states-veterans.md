# Best Pacific Northwest States for Veterans 2025

The Pacific Northwest offers progressive communities, exceptional outdoor recreation, growing economies, and increasingly veteran-friendly policies. Washington and Oregon dominate the region as top veteran destinations, each with distinct tax structures and lifestyle appeal. This guide compares these Pacific Northwest states for military retirees.

## Top 2 Pacific Northwest States for Veterans

### 1. WASHINGTON - BEST FOR TAX-FREE INCOME

**Why Washington Leads:**
- No state income tax: Any source (ultimate advantage)
- Military retirement: 100% tax-free
- All other income: Tax-free
- Property tax: 0.90% (very low)
- Cost of living: Moderate-high (mainly Seattle effect)
- Job market: Excellent (tech, aerospace, healthcare)
- VA healthcare: Excellent statewide
- Outdoor recreation: Mountains, water, hiking

**Financial Advantages:**
- **Zero state income tax** on all sources—unique advantage
- Military retirement completely tax-free
- Other income completely tax-free (critical advantage at higher incomes)
- Low property taxes
- Sales tax: 10.25% average (funding alternative to income tax)

**Tax Comparison - Military Retiree with $50K Income + $10K Other Income:**

**Washington:**
- State income tax: $0
- Sales tax impact: ~$800/year
- **Total state tax: ~$800**

**Oregon:**
- State income tax: ~$3,368
- Sales tax: $0
- **Total state tax: ~$3,368**

**Annual Advantage: Washington saves $2,568+**

**Best Cities:**
- **Seattle:** Tech hub (Amazon, Microsoft), excellent but expensive ($725K)
- **Tacoma:** Military base presence, more affordable ($420K), excellent VA
- **Spokane:** Eastern Washington, affordable, less rainy ($380K)

**Outdoor Recreation:**
- Cascades Mountains: Hiking, skiing
- Puget Sound: Water recreation, boating
- Rainier National Park access
- 7 National Forests
- Rain forest access (Olympic Mountains)

**Recommendation:** Washington optimal for high-earning professionals, especially those in tech/aerospace sectors where no state income tax provides substantial advantage.

---

### 2. OREGON - BEST FOR OVERALL LIFESTYLE + AFFORDABILITY

**Why Oregon Appeals:**
- Military retirement: Taxed (but lower rate structure)
- Cost of living: 3-4% lower than Washington
- Housing: Median $425K (more affordable than Washington)
- No sales tax: Unlike Washington's 10.25%
- Job market: Growing (especially Portland tech)
- Outdoor recreation: Beaches, mountains, national parks
- VA healthcare: Good statewide
- Progressive communities: Strong veteran support

**Financial Considerations:**
- Military retirement taxed at effective 3.75% rate
- Other income taxed at progressive rates (4.75%-9.9%)
- No sales tax (saves ~$1,000/year vs Washington)
- Housing more affordable
- Overall cost of living 3-4% lower

**Best Cities:**
- **Portland:** Tech growth, urban culture, progressive, affordable ($500K)
- **Eugene:** College town, outdoor recreation, affordable ($380K)
- **Bend:** Outdoor recreation hub, moderate cost ($480K)

**Outdoor Recreation:**
- Cascade Mountains: Skiing, hiking
- 360+ miles of Pacific beaches
- Oregon Coast beauty
- Crater Lake
- Willamette Valley wine country
- Desert hiking/recreation

**Unique Feature:** Coast recreation—unmatched in Washington.

**Recommendation:** Oregon optimal for outdoor enthusiasts and those prioritizing lifestyle + affordability balance over maximum tax savings.

## Best Choice by Veterans Profile

### High-Earning Tech Professional ($80K+ Income)
**Best: Washington**
- No state income tax on high income
- Tech opportunities (Seattle, Bellevue)
- Aerospace presence
- Urban amenities
- 10-year tax savings: $40,000+
- Recommendation: Seattle metro area

### Military Retiree, Fixed Income Only ($45K Pension)
**Essentially Tied:**
- Washington: $0 tax, but higher living costs
- Oregon: ~$1,688 tax (at lower effective rate), 3% lower costs
- Overall annual difference: ~$800
- Choose based on lifestyle: WA for tech culture, OR for coast/wine
- Recommendation: Tacoma (WA) or Eugene (OR)

### Outdoor Recreation Focus
**Best: Oregon**
- Pacific beaches (unmatched advantage)
- Wine country recreation
- Slightly lower cost
- Diverse recreation options
- Recommendation: Bend, Eugene, or Portland

### Working Veteran in Tech/Aerospace
**Best: Washington**
- Higher salaries (especially tech)
- No income tax on high earnings
- Tech ecosystem (Seattle, Bellevue, Puget Sound)
- Aerospace (Boeing headquarters)
- Recommendation: Seattle metro, Tacoma (Boeing)

### Retired from Tech, Seeking Lifestyle
**Best: Oregon**
- Lower cost than Washington
- Excellent lifestyle amenities
- Beach access + wine country
- Less urban/tech-focused culture
- Recommendation: Portland, Eugene, Bend

## Conclusion

**Washington and Oregon represent the Pacific Northwest's best options for veterans**, each with distinct advantages:

**Washington** excels for **high-income professionals** where no state income tax creates substantial savings. Tech professionals, aerospace workers, and those earning $80K+ benefit most from Washington's unique tax structure.

**Oregon** excels for **lifestyle-focused retirees** seeking balance of affordability, outdoor recreation (especially beaches), and progressive communities. Fixed-income military retirees save money and enjoy more diverse recreation.

**For most military retirees:** Oregon likely provides better overall value (3% lower costs, beach access, wine country).

**For high-earning tech/aerospace veterans:** Washington's no state income tax provides $30,000-$50,000+ over 10 years, justifying higher living costs.

Both states offer excellent VA healthcare, outdoor recreation, progressive veteran-friendly communities, and quality of life unmatched in other regions. The choice depends primarily on income level and lifestyle priorities.
