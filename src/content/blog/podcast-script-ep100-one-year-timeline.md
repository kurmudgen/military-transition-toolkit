[MAIN CONTENT]

**12 Months Before EAS: Foundation Phase**

**Career Planning:**
- Identify 2-3 target industries or career paths
- Research salary ranges for your target roles
- Connect with 5-10 veterans in those careers on LinkedIn
- Schedule 2-3 informational interviews

**Education:**
- Decide if you're using GI Bill immediately or later
- If using GI Bill: Research schools and programs
- If not using GI Bill: Explore other education options (state benefits, employer tuition assistance)

**VA Benefits:**
- Download VA Benefit Summary Letter (va.gov)
- Begin documenting service-connected medical conditions
- Schedule medical appointments for any undocumented conditions
- Start gathering buddy statements if you plan to file VA claims

**Administrative:**
- Check leave balance (target: 60-90 days saved for terminal leave)
- Request DD-214 preview to ensure accuracy
- Update DEERS and ID card information
- Register for TAP (aim to attend in next 2-6 months)

---

**10-11 Months Before EAS: TAP and Initial Prep**

**TAP Program:**
- Attend TAP (don't wait until last minute)
- Take specialized tracks (entrepreneurship, higher education, technical)
- Get contact information from instructors and career counselors
- Complete Individual Transition Plan (ITP)

**Resume and Online Presence:**
- Write first draft of civilian resume
- Get resume reviewed by TAP counselor or veteran career coach
- Create LinkedIn profile (if you don't have one)
- Optimize LinkedIn (professional photo, compelling headline, detailed experience)

**Networking:**
- Connect with 20-30 people on LinkedIn in target industries
- Join veteran professional groups (LinkedIn, local meetups)
- Attend first veteran hiring event or conference

**VA Claims:**
- File VA disability claim if you haven't already (or at least start documentation process)
- Complete all C&P exams scheduled
- Ensure all service medical records are in your file

---

**8-9 Months Before EAS: Active Job Search Begins**

**Job Search:**
- Apply to 10-15 jobs per week
- Customize resume/cover letter for each application
- Track applications in spreadsheet
- Follow up on applications after 1-2 weeks

**Networking:**
- Schedule 3-5 informational interviews per month
- Attend veteran hiring events or career fairs
- Connect with recruiters at target companies
- Ask for referrals from people in your network

**Skills Development:**
- Complete any certifications relevant to your target career
- Take online courses to fill skill gaps (Coursera, LinkedIn Learning, etc.)
- Build portfolio or work samples if applicable (e.g., coding projects for tech roles)

**Financial Planning:**
- Calculate post-military budget (account for loss of BAH, BAS, etc.)
- Build 3-6 month emergency fund if possible
- Research health insurance options (employer coverage, TRICARE Transitional, ACA)
- Consult with financial advisor if available

---

**6-7 Months Before EAS: Interviews and Offers**

**Job Search:**
- Apply to 15-20 jobs per week
- By now you should have had several phone screens and interviews
- Prepare for technical or case interviews if applicable
- Practice behavioral interview questions (STAR method)

**Interviews:**
- Schedule interviews (use leave days if necessary)
- Send thank-you notes within 24 hours of each interview
- Follow up on outstanding applications

**Certification and Training:**
- Complete any remaining certifications
- If considering SkillBridge: Submit application now (requires 180-day lead time)

**Financial:**
- Finalize post-military budget
- Apply for credit cards or loans if needed (easier while on active duty)
- Max out TSP contributions if possible (up to IRS limits)

**VA Benefits:**
- Follow up on pending VA claims
- Enroll in VA healthcare (apply at va.gov/health-care/apply)
- Research TRICARE Transitional Assistance Management Program (TAMP) eligibility

---

**4-5 Months Before EAS: Securing Employment**

**Job Search:**
- Ideally you have 1-2 job offers by now
- If not: Accelerate applications, increase networking, consider broadening your search
- Negotiate offers (salary, start date, benefits, relocation)
- Accept offer that best fits your goals

**If No Offers Yet:**
- Don't panic—many veterans find jobs within 1-3 months of separation
- Increase applications to 20-25 per week
- Expand geographic search if location-flexible
- Consider contract or temporary roles to get foot in door

**Administrative:**
- Submit terminal leave request (60-90 days)
- Begin out-processing checklist (varies by service/installation)
- Schedule separation physical
- Update SGLI beneficiary information

**Relocation:**
- If relocating: Start researching housing in new area
- Visit new city if possible to scout neighborhoods
- Research schools if you have kids
- Plan moving logistics (military moves, civilian moves, or DIY)

---

**2-3 Months Before EAS: Final Prep**

**Employment:**
- Finalize start date with new employer
- Complete new hire paperwork
- If starting during terminal leave: Get command approval in writing
- If no job yet: File for unemployment on EAS date (may qualify depending on state)

**Administrative:**
- Complete out-processing checklist
- Get copies of all medical and service records
- Attend final briefings (finance, legal, benefits)
- Clear all units, return all equipment
- Schedule DD-214 pickup

**Benefits:**
- Confirm VA healthcare enrollment
- If no VA claim filed yet: File now (you can file up to 1 year after EAS date, but sooner is better)
- Research TRICARE Transitional coverage (if eligible)
- Apply for state veteran benefits (property tax exemptions, education benefits, etc.)

**Financial:**
- Confirm final pay details (leave payout if selling any back, final paycheck date)
- Set up civilian health insurance if starting after TAMP ends
- Rollover TSP to IRA or keep in TSP (decide based on your financial plan)

---

**1 Month Before EAS: Final Countdown**

**Terminal Leave Begins:**
- Stop reporting to duty
- Use time for final job search, interviews, or starting civilian job
- Relocate if necessary

**Administrative:**
- Pick up DD-214 (keep multiple certified copies—store in safe place)
- Get VA Medical Records (MyHealtheVet or request from VA facility)
- Transfer GI Bill benefits to dependents if applicable (must be done while on active duty)
- Update driver's license, voter registration, addresses

**Healthcare:**
- If civilian job: Enroll in employer health insurance during new hire period
- If no job: Research ACA options or TRICARE Transitional
- Fill prescriptions for 90-day supply before leaving military
- Schedule VA healthcare appointments if enrolled

**Housing:**
- Move out of military housing (if applicable)
- Set up utilities, internet, etc. at new residence
- Update address with USPS, banks, credit cards, VA

---

**EAS Date: Transition Day**

**Official Separation:**
- Final paycheck received (includes any sold-back leave)
- No longer active duty
- TRICARE coverage ends (TAMP begins if eligible)
- Benefits transition from active duty to veteran status

**Immediate Actions:**
- File for unemployment if you don't have a job yet (check state requirements)
- Confirm VA healthcare enrollment is active
- Begin using GI Bill if starting school soon
- If you have a job: Focus on starting strong in civilian career

---

**After EAS: First 90 Days Civilian**

**Career:**
- Excel in your new civilian role
- Build relationships with coworkers
- Ask questions, learn company culture
- Don't expect to feel comfortable immediately (transition takes time)

**VA Benefits:**
- Follow up on pending VA claims (check status at va.gov)
- Attend C&P exams if scheduled
- File for increases or secondary conditions if applicable

**Personal Adjustment:**
- Expect some culture shock (civilian workplace very different from military)
- Stay connected with veteran community (reduces isolation)
- Seek counseling if struggling with transition (VA or private)
- Give yourself 6-12 months to fully adjust
