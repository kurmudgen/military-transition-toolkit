# Best Mountain West States for Veterans 2025

The Mountain West represents America's premier region for outdoor recreation combined with growing economies, military retirement tax exemptions, and increasingly attractive living costs. This guide ranks the best Mountain West states for transitioning military retirees seeking outdoor lifestyle and career opportunities.

## Top 5 Mountain West States for Veterans

### 1. COLORADO - BEST FOR OUTDOOR-FOCUSED VETERANS

**Why Colorado Leads:**
- Military retirement: 100% exempt from state income tax
- Cost of living: Low-moderate (outdoor recreation + tech economy)
- Climate: 300+ days of sunshine, excellent outdoor conditions
- Job market: Excellent (especially tech in Denver, Boulder)
- VA healthcare: Excellent statewide network
- Outdoor recreation: World-class skiing, hiking, climbing

**Financial Profile:**
- Military retirement: Tax-free
- State income tax: 4.4% (flat on other income)
- Cost of living: 108 index (moderate for region)
- Housing median: $525,000 (high due to tech growth)
- Property tax: 0.51% (lowest in nation)
- **10-year cost for $50K income: ~$460,000**

**Best Cities:**
- **Denver:** Tech opportunities, urban amenities, nearby mountains, higher cost
- **Colorado Springs:** Military base presence, affordable ($420K), excellent outdoor access
- **Fort Collins:** Tech growth, college town culture, outdoor recreation

**Outdoor Recreation:**
- World-class skiing (Vail, Aspen, Keystone)
- Hiking: Rocky Mountain trails
- Rock climbing: Premier destinations
- Mountain biking: Renowned systems
- Elevation: 5,280-14,000+ feet

**Recommendation:** Colorado ideal for tech-focused veterans willing to pay premium for outdoor recreation + career growth.

---

### 2. UTAH - BEST VALUE MOUNTAIN WEST OPTION

**Why Utah Excels:**
- Military retirement: Taxed (4.65% rate)
- Cost of living: Lower than Colorado (103 index)
- Outdoor recreation: National parks, deserts, mountains
- Community: Strong, family-oriented
- Job market: Growing (especially Salt Lake City tech)
- VA healthcare: Good and growing
- Housing: Median $425,000 (more affordable than Colorado)

**Financial Profile:**
- Military retirement: Taxed at 4.65%
- Lower cost of living offsets tax burden
- Property tax: 0.60% (very low)
- Housing more affordable than Colorado
- **10-year cost for $50K income: ~$420,000 (vs $460K Colorado)**

**Best Cities:**
- **Salt Lake City:** Growing tech hub, excellent skiing nearby, urban culture
- **Provo:** Affordable, college town, growing tech sector
- **St. George:** Very affordable, Zion access, retirement-focused

**Outdoor Recreation:**
- 5 national parks (Zion, Arches, Canyonlands, Bryce, Capitol Reef)
- Skiing: Alta, Snowbird, Powder Mountain
- Hiking: Some of America's best trails
- Desert recreation: Red rock country
- Lower elevation (4,200-9,000 feet): Easier adaptation

**Unique Advantage:** National parks access within state—unmatched recreation diversity.

**Recommendation:** Utah ideal for outdoor enthusiasts seeking better value than Colorado.

---

### 3. IDAHO - EMERGING TECH HUB + AFFORDABILITY

**Why Idaho Appeals:**
- Military retirement: 100% exempt
- Cost of living: Moderate (105 index)
- Emerging tech hub: Boise tech boom (2nd fastest-growing)
- Outdoor recreation: Excellent
- VA healthcare: Good statewide
- Housing: $365,000 (moderate for region)
- Community: Growing, welcoming

**Financial Advantages:**
- Military retirement tax-free
- Emerging tech opportunities (without full California costs)
- Property tax: 0.87%
- Growing job market
- Outdoor recreation included

**Best Cities:**
- **Boise:** Tech boom, affordable urban living, outdoor access ($420K)
- **Pocatello:** Very affordable, college town, mountains ($240K)
- **Coeur d'Alene:** Outdoor recreation, beautiful lakes, moderate cost ($380K)

**Outdoor Recreation:**
- Mountains: Sawtooth, White Cloud ranges
- Skiing: Sun Valley, Schweitzer
- Rivers: Salmon, Snake rivers
- Lakes: Payette, Coeur d'Alene
- Fishing: World-class fly fishing

**Consideration:** Idaho housing prices rapidly rising due to tech boom—long-term value less certain.

**Recommendation:** Idaho optimal for tech-focused outdoor enthusiasts before prices rise further.

---

### 4. MONTANA - MOST OUTDOOR-FOCUSED STATE

**Why Montana Attracts:**
- Military retirement: 100% exempt
- Cost of living: Moderate (104 index)
- Outdoor recreation: Most wilderness in lower 48
- National parks: Glacier, Yellowstone access
- Community: Outdoor culture deeply embedded
- VA healthcare: Growing
- Housing: Median $340,000
- Climate: True seasons, excellent outdoor conditions

**Financial Profile:**
- Military retirement tax-free
- Very low property tax
- Moderate housing costs
- Strong outdoor culture
- **10-year cost: ~$365,000-$380,000**

**Best Cities:**
- **Missoula:** College town, growing tech, culture ($400K)
- **Billings:** Moderate cost, practical ($320K)
- **Bozeman:** Premier outdoor recreation, expensive ($480K)

**Outdoor Recreation:**
- National parks: Glacier and Yellowstone access
- Mountains: Northern Rockies
- Rivers: Yellowstone, Missouri
- Fishing: Excellent
- Hiking: Some of nation's best trails

**Lifestyle:** True mountain culture, "frontier" feel, less development pressure

**Recommendation:** Montana ideal for retirees prioritizing outdoor living over career opportunities.

---

### 5. WYOMING - LOWEST COST + TAX-FREE

**Why Consider Wyoming:**
- No state income tax: Any source (unique advantage)
- Military retirement: 100% tax-free
- Cost of living: Very low (96 index)
- Housing: Very affordable ($280K median)
- Property tax: 0.61% (very low)
- VA healthcare: Limited (major drawback)
- Outdoor recreation: Excellent
- Lifestyle: Frontier, less developed

**Financial Advantages:**
- No state income tax—unique advantage
- Military retirement completely tax-free
- Very low property taxes
- Very affordable housing
- **10-year cost: ~$340,000 (lowest Mountain West)**

**Drawbacks:**
- Very limited VA healthcare
- Smaller veteran community
- Remote, rural lifestyle
- Long distances between services

**Best Cities:**
- Casper: Moderate size, affordable
- Laramie: College town, outdoor recreation, affordable

**Recommendation:** Wyoming only for budget-conscious retirees comfortable with remote living and private healthcare.

## Conclusion

The Mountain West offers **unmatched outdoor recreation combined with growing economic opportunities** for veterans.

**Colorado** emerges as best overall for those balancing career and outdoor recreation.

**Utah** provides best value for outdoor enthusiasts on budget.

**Montana** optimal for retirement-focused outdoor living.

**Idaho** emerging as best tech + outdoor combination before costs rise.

**Wyoming** lowest cost but requires private healthcare resources.

For a military retiree with $60,000 annual income, the Mountain West enables:
- World-class outdoor recreation
- Four-season climate
- Growing tech opportunities
- Excellent quality of life
- Annual costs $34,000-$46,000 depending on state choice

The region's combination of tax benefits, outdoor recreation, growing economies, and veteran-friendly communities makes it increasingly attractive for next-generation military retirees.
