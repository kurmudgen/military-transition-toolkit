# Military Transition Timeline: Complete Checklist from 12 Months Out

## Bottom Line Up Front

A successful military transition requires planning that starts **12 months before separation**. The number one regret veterans have: "I wish I'd started earlier." This timeline covers everything: TAP, VA claims, job search, benefits, finances, and personal preparation. Follow this checklist and you'll separate prepared, not scrambling.

## 12 Months Before Separation

### Career Preparation

- [ ] **Register for TAP (Transition Assistance Program)**
  - Mandatory 5-day workshop
  - Schedule early—slots fill up
  - Can take multiple times

- [ ] **Complete self-assessment**
  - What do you want to do?
  - What are your transferable skills?
  - What industries interest you?

- [ ] **Research career options**
  - Look at jobs matching your MOS/rating
  - Research civilian salary ranges
  - Identify required certifications

- [ ] **Start networking**
  - LinkedIn profile (start building now)
  - Connect with veterans in desired field
  - Join veteran networking groups

### VA Benefits

- [ ] **File Intent to File**
  - Locks your effective date
  - Takes 5 minutes
  - Do it NOW

- [ ] **Start gathering medical documentation**
  - Review service treatment records
  - Document all injuries and conditions
  - Request copies of medical records

- [ ] **Research VA disability claims**
  - Understand rating criteria
  - Identify conditions to claim
  - Begin gathering evidence

### Financial Preparation

- [ ] **Calculate your budget**
  - Current income vs. expected civilian income
  - Account for lost benefits (housing, healthcare)
  - Build financial buffer

- [ ] **Review GI Bill benefits**
  - Post-9/11 vs. Montgomery GI Bill
  - Transfer to dependents deadline
  - Understand housing allowance (BAH)

- [ ] **Check TSP and retirement accounts**
  - Review contribution amounts
  - Understand rollover options
  - Don't withdraw—let it grow

### Personal

- [ ] **Discuss transition with family**
  - Location preferences
  - Education plans
  - Timeline expectations

- [ ] **Research locations**
  - Where do you want to live?
  - Job market in that area
  - Cost of living comparison

## 9 Months Before Separation

### Career

- [ ] **Complete TAP workshop**
  - Attend all sessions
  - Take advantage of resources
  - Ask questions

- [ ] **Write first resume draft**
  - Translate military experience
  - No acronyms or jargon
  - Focus on accomplishments

- [ ] **Create LinkedIn profile**
  - Professional photo
  - Detailed experience
  - Start connecting

- [ ] **Research certifications**
  - What credentials does your target industry require?
  - Can you get them through military programs?
  - Timeline for certification

### VA Benefits

- [ ] **Request complete military medical records**
  - Submit SF-180 to NPRC
  - Can take months—start now
  - Request all locations served

- [ ] **Attend VA benefits briefing**
  - Usually part of TAP
  - Ask questions about claims process
  - Get contact information

- [ ] **Connect with VSO**
  - DAV, VFW, American Legion
  - Free claims assistance
  - Start building relationship

### Education

- [ ] **Research schools if planning education**
  - GI Bill Yellow Ribbon schools
  - Programs in your career field
  - Online vs. in-person options

- [ ] **Take CLEP/DSST exams**
  - Free while on active duty
  - Earn college credits
  - Multiple subjects available

### Financial

- [ ] **Build emergency fund**
  - 6+ months of expenses
  - Account for transition uncertainty
  - Separate from regular savings

- [ ] **Pay down debt**
  - Credit cards especially
  - Vehicle loans if possible
  - Reduces stress during transition

## 6 Months Before Separation

### Career

- [ ] **Start applying for jobs**
  - Apply to target companies
  - Tailor resume for each application
  - Track applications

- [ ] **Attend career fairs**
  - Hire Heroes USA events
  - Military-focused job fairs
  - Networking events

- [ ] **Practice interviewing**
  - Mock interviews through TAP
  - Practice translating military experience
  - Prepare for common questions

- [ ] **Get certifications if needed**
  - Many free through military programs
  - Credentialing Assistance program
  - COOL program (Navy/Marine)

### VA Benefits

- [ ] **Begin VA disability claim process**
  - File BDD claim (Benefits Delivery at Discharge)
  - BDD available 180-90 days before separation
  - Allows for rating decision at separation

- [ ] **Schedule medical appointments**
  - Document all conditions
  - Get everything in your record
  - Request copies of treatment notes

- [ ] **Gather buddy statements**
  - Contact fellow service members
  - Document in-service events
  - Written statements with signatures

### Healthcare

- [ ] **Understand TRICARE options**
  - Transitional TRICARE (180 days)
  - TRICARE Reserve Select
  - CHAMPVA (if service-connected)

- [ ] **Schedule dental work**
  - Use active duty dental coverage
  - Complete all needed procedures
  - Dental records for VA

### Housing

- [ ] **Research housing options**
  - VA home loan if buying
  - Rental market in target area
  - Timeline for move

- [ ] **Start saving for move**
  - DITY move or government move?
  - Deposit for rental
  - Closing costs if buying

### Legal/Admin

- [ ] **Update legal documents**
  - Will
  - Power of attorney
  - Beneficiary designations

- [ ] **Gather important documents**
  - DD-214 (issued at separation)
  - Training certificates
  - Awards and decorations

## 4 Months Before Separation

### Career

- [ ] **Continue job applications**
  - Apply to 10+ positions weekly
  - Follow up on applications
  - Adjust strategy as needed

- [ ] **Intensify networking**
  - Informational interviews
  - LinkedIn engagement
  - Industry events

- [ ] **Target specific companies**
  - Research veteran-friendly employers
  - Find internal connections
  - Tailor applications

### VA Benefits

- [ ] **Complete BDD claim submission**
  - Submit all evidence
  - Complete required exams
  - Track claim status

- [ ] **Attend C&P exams**
  - Don't reschedule unless necessary
  - Describe worst symptoms
  - Bring documentation

### Healthcare

- [ ] **Complete all medical appointments**
  - Final documentation
  - Prescriptions for 90 days
  - Referrals for ongoing care

- [ ] **Register for VA healthcare**
  - Can do before separation
  - Enrollment priority based on rating
  - Establishes care continuity

### Financial

- [ ] **Finalize budget**
  - Realistic civilian income expectations
  - Healthcare costs included
  - Transition expenses budgeted

## 3 Months Before Separation

### Career

- [ ] **Accelerate job search**
  - Multiple interviews per week if possible
  - Consider interim employment
  - Don't be picky about first job if needed

- [ ] **Negotiate job offers**
  - Know your worth
  - Consider total compensation
  - Understand benefits packages

### VA Benefits

- [ ] **Ensure BDD claim is complete**
  - All exams done
  - All evidence submitted
  - Track through VA.gov

- [ ] **Review GI Bill enrollment**
  - If using for education
  - Verify enrollment certification
  - Understand payment timeline

### Housing

- [ ] **Secure housing**
  - Sign lease or close on home
  - Coordinate move dates
  - Notify housing office

### Admin

- [ ] **Schedule final out appointments**
  - Check out briefings
  - Medical clearance
  - Equipment turn-in

## 2 Months Before Separation

### Career

- [ ] **Accept job offer (if received)**
  - Negotiate start date
  - Coordinate with separation date
  - Confirm everything in writing

- [ ] **If no offer yet, continue search**
  - Increase applications
  - Expand geographic area
  - Consider temporary work

### VA Benefits

- [ ] **Verify VA healthcare enrollment**
  - Confirm facility assignment
  - Schedule first appointment
  - Transfer prescriptions

### Move Preparation

- [ ] **Coordinate move**
  - Schedule packers/movers
  - Notify utility companies
  - Change address

- [ ] **Declutter**
  - Less stuff = easier move
  - Donate/sell unneeded items
  - Reduce stress

### Admin

- [ ] **Begin out-processing**
  - Follow installation checklist
  - Clear all appointments
  - Return equipment

## 1 Month Before Separation

### Career

- [ ] **Confirm employment start date**
  - First day logistics
  - Onboarding requirements
  - Dress code/expectations

- [ ] **If no job, finalize backup plan**
  - Unemployment benefits eligibility
  - Temporary work options
  - Continue job search plan

### VA Benefits

- [ ] **Verify claim status**
  - BDD claim should be nearly complete
  - Follow up on any issues
  - Prepare for rating decision

### Out-Processing

- [ ] **Complete final out-processing**
  - All appointments cleared
  - DD-214 worksheet reviewed
  - Final pay calculated

- [ ] **Review DD-214 carefully**
  - All service dates correct
  - Awards and decorations listed
  - RE code correct
  - Narrative reason for separation

### Personal

- [ ] **Attend separation ceremony (if applicable)**
  - Invite family
  - Proper closure

- [ ] **Mental preparation**
  - Transition is emotional
  - Plan for adjustment period
  - Support resources identified

## Separation Week

### Documents

- [ ] **Receive DD-214**
  - Review for accuracy
  - Make multiple copies
  - Store safely

- [ ] **Collect all records**
  - Medical records
  - Personnel records
  - Training certificates

### Benefits Activation

- [ ] **Activate post-separation benefits**
  - VA healthcare
  - GI Bill (if enrolling)
  - Transitional TRICARE

### Final Move

- [ ] **Execute move**
  - Supervise movers
  - Final walk-through
  - Close out housing

## First 90 Days After Separation

### Career

- [ ] **Start new job or continue search**
  - First job doesn't have to be perfect
  - Gain civilian experience
  - Keep networking

### VA Benefits

- [ ] **Receive rating decision (BDD claims)**
  - Review carefully
  - Appeal if necessary
  - Set up direct deposit

- [ ] **Attend first VA appointments**
  - Establish care
  - Transfer prescriptions
  - Mental health check-in

### Financial

- [ ] **Monitor budget**
  - Adjust as needed
  - Build savings
  - Manage healthcare costs

### Personal

- [ ] **Allow for adjustment**
  - Culture shock is normal
  - Connect with other veterans
  - Seek support if struggling

## Key Deadlines Summary

| Deadline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| 12 months | Start TAP, file Intent to File |
| 6 months | Start job search, begin VA claim |
| 180 days | BDD claim window opens |
| 90 days | BDD claim window closes, intensify job search |
| 30 days | Complete out-processing |
| Separation | Receive DD-214 |
| 1 year post | Deadline to file VA claim for day-after-separation effective date |

## Common Transition Mistakes

1. **Starting too late** - Begin 12 months out
2. **Not filing Intent to File** - Costs you backpay
3. **Waiting on job search** - Start at 6 months minimum
4. **Ignoring VA benefits** - File your claims
5. **Not translating experience** - Learn civilian language
6. **Poor financial planning** - Build that buffer
7. **Isolation** - Connect with veteran community
8. **Skipping TAP** - It's actually useful
9. **Not negotiating salary** - Know your worth
10. **Rushing** - Quality transition takes time

## Resources

- **TAP:** Your installation's transition office
- **VA Benefits:** va.gov
- **Job Search:** Hire Heroes USA, American Corporate Partners
- **Financial:** Military OneSource financial counseling
- **Mental Health:** VA Vet Centers, Give an Hour

Your transition doesn't have to be chaotic. Start early, follow this timeline, and you'll be ready for civilian life.
