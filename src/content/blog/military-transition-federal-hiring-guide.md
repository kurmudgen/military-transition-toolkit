# Federal Jobs for Veterans: Complete USAJobs Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

Veterans have **significant hiring advantages** for federal jobs. Understanding **veterans' preference**, **special hiring authorities**, and **federal resume requirements** can make the difference between getting hired and getting lost in the system. Federal jobs offer stability, benefits, and retirement—but the application process is different from private sector. This guide shows you exactly how to navigate it.

## Why Consider Federal Employment

### Benefits

**Job Security:** Federal employees have strong job protections

**Benefits Package:**
- Health insurance (FEHB)
- Retirement pension (FERS)
- Thrift Savings Plan (401k-equivalent with 5% match)
- Generous leave (13-26 days annual leave + sick leave)
- 11 paid federal holidays

**Work-Life Balance:**
- Many telework options
- Flexible schedules
- Family-friendly policies

**Career Progression:**
- Clear grade/step advancement
- Training opportunities
- Transfer between agencies

### Salary

Federal salaries are set by the General Schedule (GS):

| GS Level | Typical Experience | Base Salary Range (2024) |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| GS-5 | Entry level | $34,000 - $44,000 |
| GS-7 | Some experience | $42,000 - $55,000 |
| GS-9 | 2-3 years | $51,000 - $67,000 |
| GS-11 | 4-5 years | $62,000 - $80,000 |
| GS-12 | Senior | $74,000 - $96,000 |
| GS-13 | Expert | $88,000 - $114,000 |
| GS-14 | Manager | $104,000 - $135,000 |
| GS-15 | Senior Manager | $122,000 - $159,000 |

**Note:** Locality pay adjustments add 15-40%+ in major metro areas.

## Veterans' Preference

### What It Is

Veterans' preference gives qualified veterans an advantage in federal hiring. It's not a guarantee, but it means:
- Additional points added to your score
- Preference in selection when equally qualified
- Some positions restricted to veterans

### Types of Preference

**5-Point Preference:**
- Served during a war, campaign, or expedition
- OR served on active duty between certain dates
- Adds 5 points to passing examination scores

**10-Point Preference:**
- Disabled veterans (any service-connected disability)
- Purple Heart recipients
- Spouses, widows, or mothers of certain veterans
- Adds 10 points to passing examination scores

**Sole survivor preference**
- Additional protections for sole surviving children

### Claiming Veterans' Preference

You'll need:
- DD-214 (Member 4 copy)
- SF-15 (if claiming 10-point preference)
- VA disability letter (if claiming disabled veteran preference)

## Special Hiring Authorities

Veterans have access to hiring paths not available to the public:

### VRA (Veterans Recruitment Appointment)

- For veterans who served 180+ days after September 11, 2001
- OR veterans with service-connected disability
- Allows hiring at GS-11 or below without competition
- 2-year appointment converting to career

### 30% or More Disabled Veteran

- Direct hire authority for veterans with 30%+ disability rating
- Non-competitive appointment
- Very powerful—agencies can hire you directly
- Requires VA disability letter

### VEOA (Veterans Employment Opportunities Act)

- Allows veterans to apply to jobs open only to federal employees
- Look for jobs open to "Status" or "Merit Promotion" candidates
- Significant advantage—less competition

### Schedule A (Disability)

- Non-competitive hiring for people with disabilities
- 30%+ disability rating qualifies
- Different from 30% disabled veteran authority but can be combined

### Military Spouse Preference

- If your spouse is active duty
- Non-competitive appointment authority
- Available at some agencies

## Using USAJobs

### Creating Your Profile

1. **Create account** at usajobs.gov
2. **Build your profile:**
   - Upload documents (DD-214, SF-50, transcripts)
   - Create resume(s)
   - Set up search agents

3. **Complete hiring preferences:**
   - Claim veterans' preference
   - Indicate special hiring authorities
   - List eligibilities

### Understanding Job Announcements

Every announcement contains critical information:

**"Who May Apply":**
- "All U.S. Citizens" = Open to everyone
- "Current Federal Employees" = Internal only (unless VEOA eligible)
- "Veterans" = Open to vets with specific authorities

**"How You Will Be Evaluated":**
- Lists what they're looking for
- Use these keywords in your resume

**"Required Documents":**
- Missing documents = automatic rejection
- DD-214, SF-15, transcripts, etc.

**"Specialized Experience":**
- Minimum qualifications
- Must demonstrate you meet these

### Applying for Jobs

1. **Find jobs** matching your skills
2. **Read the entire announcement** (critically important)
3. **Tailor your resume** to match the job
4. **Answer questionnaire** honestly but favorably
5. **Upload all required documents**
6. **Submit before deadline**

## The Federal Resume

### How It's Different

**Private sector resume:** 1-2 pages, brief
**Federal resume:** 3-6+ pages, detailed

Federal resumes require:
- Complete employment history
- Hours worked per week
- Supervisor contact information
- Detailed descriptions of duties and accomplishments
- Specific dates (month/year)

### Required Information

For each position:
- Job title
- Organization name
- Address
- Dates employed (MM/YYYY - MM/YYYY)
- Hours per week
- Supervisor name and phone
- May we contact? (Yes/No)
- Salary
- Grade/rank (if federal/military)
- Detailed duties and accomplishments

### Resume Content Strategy

**Match the job announcement:**
- Use keywords from the announcement
- Address each "specialized experience" requirement
- Mirror their language

**Quantify accomplishments:**
- How many people supervised
- Dollar amounts managed
- Percentage improvements
- Volume/throughput

**Include everything relevant:**
- All relevant experience
- Training and education
- Awards and recognition
- Volunteer work (if relevant)

### Common Federal Resume Mistakes

1. **Too short** - Federal resumes need detail
2. **Missing required information** - Hours, supervisor, dates
3. **Not matching keywords** - Use announcement language
4. **Vague descriptions** - Be specific and quantified
5. **Missing documents** - Check requirements carefully

## The Application Process

### Timeline

Federal hiring is slow:
- Application review: 2-4 weeks
- Interviews: 2-6 weeks after closing
- Selection: 2-4 weeks after interviews
- Background check: 2-8 weeks (or longer for clearances)
- Start date: 2-4 weeks after background
- **Total: 2-6+ months is normal**

### Questionnaire

Most applications include a self-assessment questionnaire:
- Answer honestly but favorably
- "Expert" ratings must be supportable by your resume
- HR compares your answers to your resume

**Tip:** If you rate yourself "Expert" on something, your resume better show it.

### Interview

Federal interviews are typically:
- Panel format (3-5 interviewers)
- Structured questions (same for all candidates)
- Behavioral-based (STAR method answers)
- Scored on a rubric

**Prepare for:**
- "Tell us about a time when..."
- Job-specific technical questions
- Questions about the position duties

### Selection

After interviews:
- Panel scores candidates
- Veterans' preference applied
- Selecting official makes final decision
- HR extends tentative offer
- Background/clearance processed
- Final offer and start date

## Finding the Right Jobs

### Job Series

Federal jobs are classified by "series" (occupation codes):

**Common for veterans:**
- 0080: Security Administration
- 0083: Police
- 0301: Misc. Administration
- 0340: Program Management
- 0343: Management Analyst
- 0346: Logistics Management
- 0391: Telecommunications
- 1102: Contract Specialist
- 2210: Information Technology

### Search Strategies

**On USAJobs:**
- Search by keyword (your skills)
- Search by series
- Filter by location
- Filter by agency
- Save searches with email alerts

**Target agencies:**
- Department of Defense (largest employer of vets)
- Department of Veterans Affairs
- Department of Homeland Security
- All military branches (civilian positions)

## Tips for Success

### Apply to Many Positions

Federal hiring is competitive. Apply to:
- Multiple positions
- Multiple agencies
- Multiple locations (if flexible)

### Use All Your Eligibilities

If you have veterans' preference and 30% disability:
- Apply under all applicable authorities
- Check all boxes that apply
- Maximize your opportunities

### Network

Even in federal hiring, networking helps:
- Contact agency HR about positions
- Reach out to veterans in agencies
- Attend federal career fairs

### Be Patient

Federal hiring takes time. Keep applying while waiting.

### Don't Give Up

If not selected:
- Ask for feedback (some agencies provide it)
- Improve your resume
- Apply again

## Key Takeaways

1. **You have significant advantages** - Use veterans' preference and special authorities
2. **Federal resumes are different** - 3-6 pages, detailed, formatted specifically
3. **Read announcements carefully** - Requirements are requirements
4. **Match keywords** - Use their language
5. **Apply to many jobs** - It's competitive
6. **Be patient** - Federal hiring takes months
7. **Claim all eligibilities** - Check every box that applies

Federal employment offers excellent benefits and job security. Your veteran status gives you a real advantage—use it.
