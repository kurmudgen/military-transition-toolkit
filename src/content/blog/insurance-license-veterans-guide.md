# Insurance License for Veterans: Complete Career Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Total cost:** $200-$800 (education + exams + fees)

- **Job outlook:** 6% growth through 2032

## What Is an Insurance License?

Insurance licenses authorize you to sell insurance products to consumers. Different license types cover different products:

**Life Insurance:** Term, whole life, universal life policies
**Health Insurance:** Medical, dental, vision, disability
**Property Insurance:** Homeowners, renters, commercial property
**Casualty Insurance:** Auto, liability, umbrella policies
**Property & Casualty (P&C):** Combined property and casualty license

Most states require separate licenses for Life/Health and Property/Casualty lines.

## License Types

### Life and Health (L&H)
- **Life Insurance:** Death benefit policies
- **Health Insurance:** Medical coverage
- **Often combined:** Single exam, single license

### Property and Casualty (P&C)
- **Property:** Homes, buildings, belongings
- **Casualty:** Auto, liability, accidents
- **Combined:** Most common license

### Specialty Lines
- **Surplus Lines:** Non-standard risks
- **Crop Insurance:** Agricultural coverage
- **Title Insurance:** Real estate transactions

## State Requirements

Requirements vary by state but typically include:

### Pre-License Education
| License Type | Hours Required | Notes |
|--------------|----------------|-------|
| Life Insurance | 20-50 hours | Varies by state |
| Health Insurance | 20-50 hours | Often combined with Life |
| Property & Casualty | 40-60 hours | Usually separate |

### State Examination
- Proctored computer exam
- Multiple choice questions
- 70% passing score typical
- Results immediate

### Background Check
- Required in all states
- Fingerprinting may be required
- Felonies may disqualify

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long to get licensed?**
A: 2-8 weeks including education, exam, and paperwork. Can be faster with intensive study.

**Q: Is the exam hard?**
A: Moderate difficulty. Pass rates around 60-70%. Study properly and most pass first time.

**Q: Captive or independent agent?**
A: Captive offers more support and often salary—good for starting. Independent offers higher commissions and freedom—better for experienced agents.

**Q: Can I make good money in insurance?**
A: Yes, but it takes time. First year is building. Top agents earn $150,000+, but it takes 3-5 years typically.

**Q: Is insurance sales hard?**
A: Yes. Requires prospecting, handling rejection, and consistent effort. Military discipline helps.

**Q: Should I get both L&H and P&C licenses?**
A: Depends on career path. Many successful agents have both for complete client service.

**Q: What about USAA or military-focused companies?**
A: Great option for veterans. Your military background is valued. USAA, Armed Forces Insurance, and others hire veterans.

**Q: Is there recurring income?**
A: Yes. Renewals provide ongoing income from existing clients. Long-term agents have significant renewal books.
