# North Carolina vs South Carolina Veterans: Complete Comparison Guide 2025

Both Carolinas offer strong veteran benefits, lower costs of living than many states, and expanding job markets. However, they differ significantly in tax treatment, economic opportunities, and climate. This guide helps veterans choose between these two southeastern destinations.

## Military Retirement Tax Treatment

### North Carolina: Partial Military Retirement Exemption

North Carolina offers a partial tax exemption for military retirees:

**NC Military Retirement Benefits:**
- Military pension: 100% exempt (effective tax rate: 0%)
- VA disability: 100% exempt
- State income tax rate: 4.75-4.99% (progressive)
- No income tax on military retirement
- Effective state tax on other income: 4.9%

**Tax Savings on $36,000 military pension:**
- North Carolina state tax: $0
- Additional income ($20,000): $980
- **Total state tax: $980**

### South Carolina: Military Retirement Exemption PLUS Lower Tax Rate

South Carolina provides similar military exemption with lower overall tax rates:

**SC Military Retirement Benefits:**
- Military pension: 100% exempt (effective tax rate: 0%)
- VA disability: 100% exempt
- State income tax rate: 0%-7% (progressive)
- No income tax on military retirement
- Effective state tax on other income: 4.5%

**Tax Savings on $36,000 military pension:**
- South Carolina state tax: $0
- Additional income ($20,000): $900
- **Total state tax: $900**

**Tax Winner: South Carolina** (Saves $80 annually on $20,000 additional income)
