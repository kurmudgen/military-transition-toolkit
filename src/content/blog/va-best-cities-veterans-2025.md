# Best Cities for Veterans in Virginia 2025: Cost of Living & Benefits

## Bottom Line Up Front

Virginia Beach ranks #1 for veterans in Virginia with 14.4% veteran population (highest in state), median home price of $376,500, $3,765 annual property tax savings for 100% disabled veterans, proximity to Hampton VA Medical Center (20 minutes), and massive defense contractor presence at Naval Station Norfolk and Oceana Naval Air Station. The Hampton Roads region (Virginia Beach, Norfolk, Chesapeake, Hampton) collectively offers the strongest veteran ecosystem in Virginia. Arlington/Northern Virginia offers highest salaries ($110,000+ average for defense contractors) but highest costs (median home $700,000). Roanoke is the most affordable option at $200,000 median home price with excellent Salem VA Medical Center access.

## How We Ranked These Cities

**Ranking Criteria (Weighted):**

1. **Veteran Population** (20%) - Higher veteran populations indicate stronger veteran communities, support networks, and veteran-friendly culture

2. **Cost of Living** (20%) - Housing costs, property taxes, overall affordability

3. **VA Healthcare Access** (15%) - Proximity to VA medical centers and CBOCs, quality of care, wait times

4. **Employment Opportunities** (15%) - Defense contractors, federal jobs, veteran hiring programs, average salaries

5. **Tax Benefits** (10%) - Property tax savings for disabled veterans, local tax variations

6. **Military Community** (10%) - Proximity to active duty installations, military spouse opportunities, transition resources

7. **Quality of Life** (10%) - Education, crime rates, recreation, weather, culture

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Which Virginia city has the most veterans?**

A: Virginia Beach has the most veterans (66,220) and highest percentage (14.4%). Norfolk has 22,850 veterans (9.6%). Hampton has 14,500 (10.6%). Chesapeake has 25,300 (10%).

**Q: Where should I live if I work at the Pentagon?**

A: Arlington (10 minutes) or Alexandria (10 minutes) for shortest commute. Consider Fairfax County or Prince William County for affordability with 30-45 minute commute.

**Q: I'm a 100% disabled veteran on a budget. Where should I live?**

A: Roanoke ($200K median home, Salem VA 10 minutes, Davis & McDaniel Veterans Care Center in-city). Hampton is also affordable ($285K) with Hampton VAMC in-city.

**Q: Which city has the best VA hospital?**

A: Hunter Holmes McGuire VA Medical Center in Richmond is rated highest (Level 1A complexity). Hampton VAMC (468 beds) is also excellent. Salem VAMC is Virginia's psychiatric specialty center.

**Q: I'm a Navy veteran. Where should I live?**

A: Virginia Beach (#1) or Norfolk (#2). Naval Station Norfolk access, massive Navy community, best military culture in Virginia.

**Q: Which city has the best schools for military families?**

A: Arlington (A+ rating, but expensive $700K median home) or Chesapeake (A- rating, affordable $330K median home). Chesapeake is best value for school quality.

**Q: Can I afford Arlington on military retirement income alone?**

A: Difficult unless you have significant retirement income or VA disability compensation. Arlington median home ($700K) requires approximately $150K household income to afford comfortably. Consider more affordable Northern Virginia areas like Prince William County.

**Q: I need a job. Which city has the best veteran employment?**

A: Arlington/Alexandria (Pentagon, defense contractors, $110K-$185K salaries) for highest-paying jobs. Virginia Beach/Norfolk for most veteran-specific opportunities and military-friendly employers.

**Q: I'm attending college on GI Bill. Where should I live?**

A: Live near your school:
- UVA: Charlottesville
- Virginia Tech: Blacksburg (not on this list, but consider nearby Roanoke)
- VCU: Richmond
- GMU: Fairfax/Arlington area
- ODU: Norfolk/Virginia Beach
- Liberty: Lynchburg

**Q: Which city has the lowest crime rate?**

A: Arlington and Chesapeake have the lowest crime rates on this list. Both are well below national average.

**Q: I want beach lifestyle. Where should I live besides Virginia Beach?**

A: Norfolk (waterfront, less expensive), Chesapeake (nearby beaches, suburban), or Hampton (Buckroe Beach, most affordable).

**Q: Are there any Virginia cities good for Air Force veterans?**

A: Hampton (Langley AFB in-city) is best for Air Force. Otherwise, consider Northern Virginia near Pentagon/Joint Base Andrews (Maryland).

**Q: Which city has the worst traffic?**

A: Arlington has worst traffic (Northern Virginia congestion). Virginia Beach has seasonal summer traffic. Norfolk has tunnel bottlenecks. Roanoke has minimal traffic.

**Q: I'm moving to Virginia from another state. Which city should I pick?**

A: Depends on priorities:
- **Career growth:** Arlington/Alexandria
- **Military community:** Virginia Beach
- **Affordability:** Roanoke
- **Balance:** Richmond or Chesapeake
- **Family/schools:** Chesapeake or Arlington (if you can afford it)

**Q: Do any cities offer extra veteran benefits beyond state benefits?**

A: Most Virginia cities follow state veteran benefits (property tax exemptions, income subtraction). Virginia Beach has dedicated Office of Veteran Services providing extra assistance. Arlington offers free document preparation assistance and translation services.

**Q: Can I use my VA home loan in any of these cities?**

A: Yes. VA home loans work nationwide. All these cities have VA-approved lenders. In competitive markets (Arlington, Alexandria, Richmond), consider getting pre-approved and writing strong offers. Some sellers prefer conventional financing over VA loans due to appraisal requirements.

**Q: Which city is best for veteran entrepreneurs?**

A: Richmond (state capital, diverse economy, lower costs than Northern Virginia) or Arlington/Alexandria (defense contractor opportunities, high-income clients, Pentagon proximity). Virginia Beach also has strong veteran entrepreneur networks.
