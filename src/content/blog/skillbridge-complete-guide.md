# DoD SkillBridge: Complete Program Guide for 2025

SkillBridge is one of the most valuable transition benefits available to separating service members. It allows you to gain civilian work experience, training, and employment opportunities during your last 180 days of service—while still receiving your full military pay and benefits.

This guide covers everything you need to know about SkillBridge: eligibility, finding programs, getting command approval, and maximizing the opportunity.

## What is SkillBridge?

### The Basics

**SkillBridge** is a DoD program that allows service members in their last 180 days of service to participate in civilian job training, employment skills training, and internships with approved employers.

**Key Benefits:**
- **Continue receiving military pay and benefits** during the program
- **Gain civilian work experience** before separation
- **Earn certifications and credentials** at employer's expense
- **High job offer rates** - many participants receive offers from host companies
- **No cost to you** - companies provide training, you keep your military compensation

### Why It Matters

**Traditional transition:**
- Separate from military
- Start job search
- Wait for offers
- Begin civilian career

**SkillBridge transition:**
- Begin work/training while still in military
- Build civilian network and experience
- Often receive job offer before separation
- Seamless transition to civilian employment

## Eligibility Requirements

### Who Can Participate

**You must be:**
- Active duty service member
- Within 180 days of separation or retirement
- Expected to receive honorable discharge
- Have completed TAP/transition requirements
- Approved by your command

**All branches participate:**
- Army
- Navy
- Air Force
- Marine Corps
- Coast Guard
- Space Force

### Command Approval

**Your commander has final say.** SkillBridge is not a right—it's an opportunity that requires commander approval.

**Factors commanders consider:**
- Unit operational requirements
- Manning levels
- Your job responsibilities
- Timing of request
- Mission impact

**Tips for approval:**
- Plan early (start 12+ months before separation)
- Demonstrate responsible transition planning
- Show how you'll complete hand-off duties
- Be flexible on timing if needed
- Present professionally

## Application Process

### Timeline

**12+ Months Before Separation:**
- Research programs
- Identify target opportunities
- Begin discussions with leadership
- Complete TAP early if required

**9-12 Months:**
- Apply to SkillBridge programs
- Many programs fill quickly
- Competitive programs (MSSA) require early application
- Await acceptance

**6-9 Months:**
- Receive program acceptance
- Work with chain of command on approval
- Complete SkillBridge agreement paperwork
- Coordinate with program and unit

**180-Day Window:**
- Begin SkillBridge (up to 180 days before separation)
- Complete program requirements
- Transition to civilian role

### Paperwork Required

**Standard documents:**
- DD Form 2648 (Pre-separation Counseling Checklist)
- Individual Transition Plan (ITP)
- SkillBridge Agreement (program-specific)
- Command endorsement
- Service-specific forms

**Each service has specific processes:**
- Check your branch's transition office
- Work with installation career counselor
- Follow proper channels

### Getting Accepted

**What programs look for:**
- Relevant background/interest
- Strong military record
- Professional application
- Genuine interest in the field
- Ability to commit to program duration

**Application tips:**
- Tailor resume to program
- Write strong cover letter explaining interest
- Be responsive to communications
- Interview professionally
- Follow up appropriately

## Maximizing SkillBridge

### Choosing the Right Program

**Consider:**
- Does this align with your career goals?
- What's the job offer rate?
- What certifications/training are included?
- Is the location workable?
- How long is the program?
- Does the company interest you long-term?

**Don't just take any program:**
- SkillBridge is limited to 180 days
- Choose something valuable
- Target programs with high conversion rates

### During the Program

**Treat it like a real job (it is):**
- Perform at your best
- Be professional and reliable
- Build relationships
- Learn everything possible
- Ask about full-time opportunities

**Network:**
- Connect with colleagues on LinkedIn
- Build relationships beyond immediate team
- Attend company events
- Meet people in your target field

**Document everything:**
- Track certifications earned
- Save performance feedback
- Document projects and accomplishments
- Build your civilian resume

### Job Offer Strategy

**If you want an offer from the host company:**
- Express your interest early
- Ask about conversion process
- Understand their hiring timeline
- Perform exceptionally

**If you're exploring options:**
- SkillBridge doesn't obligate you to take their offer
- Use the experience on your resume
- Continue networking elsewhere
- Apply to other positions

## Common Challenges and Solutions

### Challenge 1: Command Won't Approve

**Possible reasons:**
- Unit is undermanned
- Critical mission period
- Not planned far enough in advance

**Solutions:**
- Start planning earlier
- Propose shorter program
- Offer to remain available for critical events
- Work with transition office to advocate
- Be flexible on timing

### Challenge 2: Can't Find Programs in My Area

**Solutions:**
- Many programs are remote/hybrid now
- Consider relocating early
- Look at corporate programs with multiple locations
- Consider training programs vs internships

### Challenge 3: Program is Full

**Solutions:**
- Apply earlier next cycle
- Get on waitlist
- Identify backup programs
- Contact program directly to express interest

### Challenge 4: Program Duration Exceeds My Timeline

**Solutions:**
- Some programs are flexible
- Negotiate shorter participation
- Look for shorter programs
- Consider partial participation

## SkillBridge + Terminal Leave

### Can You Combine Them?

**Yes, in most cases:**
- SkillBridge during last 180 days
- Terminal leave at the very end
- Must coordinate with command

**Example Timeline:**
- 120-day SkillBridge program
- 30 days terminal leave
- Separation date

**Command discretion applies.** Work with leadership on acceptable combination.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How early should I start planning?

12+ months before separation. Popular programs fill quickly, and command approval takes time.

### Can I do SkillBridge if I'm retiring?

Yes. Same 180-day window applies to retirees.

### Do I keep my BAH and benefits?

Yes. You continue receiving all military pay and benefits during SkillBridge.

### Can I be recalled during SkillBridge?

Technically yes, you're still on active duty. However, this is rare.

### Is there a cost to participate?

No cost to you. Companies provide training. You keep military compensation.

### What if the company doesn't offer me a job?

No problem. SkillBridge doesn't obligate either party. You've gained experience and can apply elsewhere.

### Can I do more than one SkillBridge program?

Generally no. The 180-day window is your total SkillBridge time.

### Do Reserve/Guard members qualify?

Only if on active duty orders with 180+ days remaining and expected honorable discharge.

## Bottom Line

SkillBridge is one of the most valuable transition benefits available. It allows you to gain civilian experience, training, and often a job offer—all while still receiving military pay and benefits.

**Keys to Success:**
1. **Start early** - Begin planning 12+ months out
2. **Choose wisely** - Pick a program aligned with your goals
3. **Get command buy-in** - Work professionally with your chain of command
4. **Perform excellently** - Treat it as a real job interview
5. **Network actively** - Build connections during the program

Don't leave this benefit unused. SkillBridge can transform your transition from stressful to seamless.
