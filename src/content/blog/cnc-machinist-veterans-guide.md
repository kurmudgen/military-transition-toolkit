## What Is CNC Machining?

CNC (Computer Numerical Control) machinists operate computer-controlled machine tools to produce precision parts. They:

- Set up and operate CNC lathes, mills, and grinders
- Read and interpret blueprints and technical drawings
- Write or modify CNC programs (G-code)
- Inspect parts for quality and specifications
- Maintain and troubleshoot machines

CNC machining is essential for manufacturing everything from medical devices to aerospace components.

## Why Veterans Excel in CNC Machining

Military technical training provides excellent foundation:

**Precision mindset:** You understand tolerances and specifications.

**Technical aptitude:** Military technical MOS develop mechanical understanding.

**Blueprint reading:** Many military jobs require technical drawing interpretation.

**Quality focus:** Military standards mirror precision manufacturing requirements.

**Computer skills:** CNC programming uses transferable computer skills.

## Types of CNC Machines

### Primary Machine Types
- **CNC Lathe/Turning Center:** Rotational parts
- **CNC Mill/Machining Center:** Complex shapes, pockets, holes
- **CNC Grinder:** Precision finishing
- **CNC EDM:** Electrical discharge machining
- **5-Axis CNC:** Complex multi-angle machining

### Industry Segments
- **Production machining:** High volume, repetitive
- **Job shop:** Varied work, prototyping
- **Tool and die:** Precision tooling
- **Aerospace:** Tight tolerances, exotic materials
- **Medical devices:** Precision, regulated

## Certification Options

### NIMS (National Institute for Metalworking Skills)
Industry-standard credentials:
- **CNC Mill Operations**
- **CNC Lathe Operations**
- **CNC Milling Programming Setup Operations**
- **CNC Turning Programming Setup Operations**
- **Measurement, Materials, and Safety**

### Other Certifications
- **Mastercam certification:** CAM software
- **HAAS certification:** Manufacturer-specific
- **AWS D17.1 welder certifications:** For welding-related machining
- **GD&T (Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing)**

## Certification Requirements

### NIMS Certification Process
1. Complete training (school or experience)
2. Pass written (theory) exam
3. Pass performance (practical) exam
4. Credential is industry-recognized

### Experience Alternative
- 4,000+ hours documented experience
- Can test for NIMS without formal training
- Military experience may count

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is CNC machining a dying trade?**
A: Basic machining is declining, but skilled CNC machinists and programmers remain in demand, especially for aerospace and medical devices.

**Q: Do I need a degree?**
A: No degree required for most positions. Certifications and demonstrated skills matter more.

**Q: What's the difference between operator and programmer?**
A: Operators run machines and may do setup. Programmers write the G-code and define toolpaths. Programmers earn more.

**Q: Is Workshops for Warriors really free?**
A: Yes, for qualifying veterans. Excellent program with NIMS certifications included.

**Q: Should I learn manual machining first?**
A: Helpful but not required. Understanding manual concepts helps with CNC, but you can start with CNC directly.

**Q: What CAD/CAM software should I learn?**
A: Mastercam is industry standard. Fusion 360 is free for learning and increasingly used. Both are valuable.

**Q: What tools do I need to buy?**
