## WHERE TO LIVE (Q451-Q460)

### Q451: What are the best states for veterans to live in? {#q451}

**A:** The best states for veterans typically include South Carolina, Florida, Texas, Virginia, and Tennessee based on factors like no/low taxes on military retirement, property tax exemptions for disabled veterans, quality of veteran services, cost of living, and access to VA facilities.

The "best" state depends on your priorities, but here's a comprehensive breakdown:

**Top 5 overall states for veterans (2024-2025):**

**1. South Carolina**
*Why it's #1:*
- No state tax on military retirement income
- Full property tax exemption for 100% disabled veterans
- Lower cost of living than national average
- 7 VA medical centers and clinics
- Strong veteran support services
- Mild climate
- Growing job market (especially Charleston, Greenville)

**2. Florida**
*Why it ranks high:*
- No state income tax at all
- Full property tax exemption for 100% disabled veterans
- Huge veteran population (1.5+ million)
- Excellent VA facilities (14 VA medical centers)
- No state tax on military retirement
- Warm climate year-round
- Strong veteran community

**3. Texas**
*Why it's popular:*
- No state income tax
- Full property tax exemption for 100% disabled veterans
- Partial exemptions for lower ratings
- Strong job market (especially Austin, Dallas, Houston)
- Low cost of living (varies by city)
- 8 VA medical centers
- Veteran-friendly culture

**4. Virginia**
*Why veterans choose it:*
- Increasing military retirement tax exemption ($20K in 2024, $40K by 2026+)
- Strong veteran services and support
- Proximity to military installations (networking, employment)
- Growing property tax exemptions for disabled veterans
- Excellent VA facilities
- Four-season climate
- Strong economy and job market

**5. Tennessee**
*Why it's rising:*
- No state income tax
- Low cost of living
- Growing property tax benefits for veterans
- VA healthcare access (Nashville, Memphis VA medical centers)
- No state tax on military retirement
- Affordable housing
- Growing job market

**Comparison by key factors:**

**Factor 1: State taxes on military retirement**

**No tax (best):**
- Alabama, Alaska, Florida, Hawaii, Illinois, Iowa, Kansas, Louisiana, Maine, Massachusetts, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, Nebraska, New Jersey, Nevada, New Hampshire, New Mexico, New York, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Virginia, Washington, West Virginia, Wisconsin, Wyoming

**Full tax (worst):**
- California, District of Columbia

**Partial exemptions:**
- Arizona, Colorado, Delaware, Georgia, Idaho, Kentucky, Maryland, Montana, Oklahoma, Oregon, Rhode Island, Utah

**Factor 2: Property tax exemptions**

**Full exemption for 100% disabled:**
- Alabama, Florida, Illinois, Iowa, Louisiana, Maryland, Michigan, Mississippi, Nebraska, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New Mexico, Oklahoma, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, Texas, Virginia, West Virginia (20 states total)

**Partial exemptions:**
- Most other states offer some level of exemption based on rating

**Factor 3: Cost of living**

**Most affordable:**
- Tennessee, Texas (outside major cities), South Carolina, Mississippi, Alabama, Arkansas

**Moderate:**
- Virginia, North Carolina, Georgia, Florida (outside Miami/South Florida)

**Expensive:**
- California, New York, Hawaii, Massachusetts, Washington (Seattle area)

**Factor 4: VA facility access**

**Excellent VA coverage:**
- Florida (14 VA medical centers)
- Texas (8 VA centers)
- California (9 VA centers)
- New York (multiple centers)

**Factor 5: Job market for veterans**

**Strongest job markets:**
- Texas (Austin, Dallas, Houston)
- Virginia (Northern Virginia, Hampton Roads)
- Colorado (Denver area)
- North Carolina (Research Triangle)
- Washington (Seattle)

**States by veteran population (support/community):**

**Highest veteran populations:**
1. California: 1.6 million
2. Texas: 1.5 million
3. Florida: 1.5 million
4. Pennsylvania: 800,000
5. Virginia: 750,000

**States to consider for specific priorities:**

**If you prioritize:**

*Low taxes + warm weather:*
- Florida, Texas, Tennessee

*Strong veteran community:*
- South Carolina, Virginia, Texas, Florida

*Outdoor recreation:*
- Colorado, Washington, Montana

*Job opportunities in tech:*
- Texas (Austin), Virginia (Northern VA), Colorado (Denver), Washington (Seattle)

*Affordability:*
- Tennessee, South Carolina, Alabama, Mississippi

*Best overall benefits package:*
- South Carolina, Florida, Texas

**States to avoid (for tax reasons):**

**Least veteran-friendly tax-wise:**
1. **California:** Full tax on military retirement, high state income tax (up to 13.3%), expensive
2. **District of Columbia:** Full tax on military retirement, high cost of living
3. **Oregon:** Partial exemption only, high state taxes

**However:** Even "worst" states may be right for you if job opportunities, family, or other factors outweigh tax considerations.

**How to decide where to relocate:**

**Step 1: Identify your priorities**
- Financial (taxes, cost of living)
- Family (proximity to relatives)
- Career (job market in your field)
- Healthcare (VA facility access)
- Climate (warm vs. four-seasons)
- Lifestyle (urban vs. rural, recreation)

**Step 2: Research top states matching your priorities**
- Use comparison tools
- Visit state veteran benefits websites
- Join veteran groups in potential states (Facebook, Reddit)

**Step 3: Visit before moving**
- Spend time in the area
- Tour neighborhoods
- Check out VA facilities
- Talk to local veterans

**Step 4: Run the numbers**
- Calculate total tax burden (state + property + sales tax)
- Estimate cost of living
- Compare salaries in your field
- Factor in property tax exemptions

**Example financial comparison:**

**Scenario: Retired O-5, $66K military retirement, 100% VA rating, $400K home**

*Option 1: California*
- State tax on retirement: ~$5,940/year
- Property tax: ~$4,800/year (no exemption)
- **Total: $10,740/year**

*Option 2: Florida*
- State tax on retirement: $0
- Property tax: $0 (100% exemption)
- **Total: $0/year**
- **Savings: $10,740/year vs. California**

Over 20 years: $214,800 savings by living in Florida vs. California

**Additional resources by state:**

Most states have veteran-specific resources:
- State Department of Veterans Affairs (search "[State] Veterans Affairs")
- Property tax information: County property appraiser websites
- State tax guidance: State Department of Revenue

**Bottom line:**

The best state for veterans depends on your individual situation, but South Carolina, Florida, Texas, Virginia, and Tennessee consistently rank highest for combination of tax benefits, cost of living, veteran services, and quality of life. Focus on states that don't tax military retirement, offer property tax exemptions, have strong VA healthcare systems, and align with your career goals and lifestyle preferences.
