# CBP Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide to Customs and Border Protection

Customs and Border Protection is the largest federal law enforcement agency—and one of the most aggressive recruiters of military veterans. With over 60,000 employees guarding America's borders, CBP offers veterans operational careers that mirror military service.

If you're looking for continued tactical work, outdoor operations, and law enforcement authority, CBP delivers opportunities unmatched in federal service.

## Why CBP for Veterans?

### Operational Mission

CBP's mission resonates with military experience:
- Border security operations
- Counter-narcotics
- Anti-terrorism
- Immigration enforcement
- Trade enforcement

### Largest LE Agency

Scale creates opportunity:
- 60,000+ employees
- 20,000+ Border Patrol Agents
- 25,000+ CBP Officers
- Multiple career paths
- Continuous hiring

### Military Skills Premium

Your training applies directly:
- Surveillance and reconnaissance
- Tactical operations
- Physical endurance
- Decision-making
- Team operations

### Aggressive Veteran Hiring

CBP actively recruits veterans:
- Targeted veteran outreach
- Veteran hiring authorities used
- Military experience valued for all positions
- Clearance compatibility

## CBP Career Paths

### Border Patrol Agent (BPA)

Primary border security position (20,000+ agents):

**Responsibilities:**
- Patrol and secure U.S. borders
- Apprehend illegal crossers
- Interdict narcotics
- Surveillance operations
- Search and rescue
- Traffic checkpoints

**Starting salary:** ~$59,000-$95,000 (includes overtime/differentials)
**Full performance:** $100,000-$125,000+

### CBP Officer (CBPO)

Ports of entry security (25,000+ officers):

**Responsibilities:**
- Inspect travelers at ports of entry
- Process imports/exports
- Counter-terrorism screening
- Drug interdiction
- Trade enforcement
- Immigration processing

**Starting salary:** ~$48,000-$80,000+
**Full performance:** $85,000-$110,000+

### Air and Marine Operations (AMO)

Aviation and maritime interdiction (1,800+ agents):

**Positions:**
- Air Interdiction Agent (pilot)
- Marine Interdiction Agent
- Aviation Enforcement Agent
- UAS operators

**Highly relevant for:** Military pilots, Coast Guard, Navy

### CBP Agriculture Specialist

Agricultural inspection:

**Responsibilities:**
- Inspect agricultural products
- Prevent pest introduction
- Protect U.S. agriculture
- Scientific/technical work

**Starting salary:** ~$48,000-$65,000

### Trade and Revenue Positions

Trade enforcement:

**Positions:**
- Import Specialist
- Trade Analyst
- Entry Specialist

**Focus:** Trade compliance, duty collection, fraud detection

## Border Patrol Agent Requirements

### Basic Requirements

**Age:**
- Under 40 at appointment
- Veteran preference may extend
- Mandatory retirement at 57

**Citizenship:**
- U.S. citizen

**Education:**
- No college degree required
- High school diploma/GED

**Experience alternative:**
- One year qualifying experience, OR
- Bachelor's degree

**Military service counts as qualifying experience.**

**License:**
- Valid driver's license

**Residency:**
- Must live within 100 miles of border (Southwest/Northern)

### Physical Requirements

**Border Patrol fitness test:**
- Pre-employment fitness test (BPFT)
- Push-ups, sit-ups, stepping test
- Must pass all components

### Medical Requirements

- Good physical health
- Vision correctable to 20/20
- Hearing within normal limits
- Drug screening
- Psychological evaluation

### Background Investigation

- Secret clearance level
- Polygraph required
- Financial review
- Extensive reference checks
- Drug history review

### Spanish Language

**Spanish proficiency is NOT required to apply but:**
- Tested during processing
- Intensive Spanish training at academy if needed
- Proficiency required for journey level

## CBP Officer Requirements

### Basic Requirements

**Age:**
- Under 40 at appointment
- Military credit may extend
- Mandatory retirement at 57

**Citizenship:**
- U.S. citizen

**Education:**
- Bachelor's degree, OR
- Three years of specialized experience, OR
- Combination

**Military experience counts toward specialized experience.**

**License:**
- Valid driver's license

### Physical Requirements

- Pre-employment physical
- Drug test
- Less physically demanding than BPA
- Standing for extended periods

### Background Investigation

Same as Border Patrol:
- Polygraph
- Secret clearance
- Financial review

## Hiring Process

### Border Patrol Agent Process

**Step 1: Apply** (cbp.gov/careers)
- Online application
- Assessment questionnaire

**Step 2: Entrance Exam**
- Logical reasoning test
- Spanish language assessment
- Computer-based at testing center

**Step 3: Structured Interview**
- Panel interview
- Behavioral questions
- Job preview

**Step 4: Medical and Fitness**
- Physical examination
- Fitness test (BPFT)
- Vision and hearing

**Step 5: Background Investigation**
- SF-86 submission
- Polygraph examination
- Financial and criminal checks

**Step 6: Training**
- 6 months at Border Patrol Academy
- Artesia, New Mexico
- Spanish language intensive
- Law enforcement training
- Immigration law

**Timeline:** 6-12 months typical

### CBP Officer Process

Similar process with some differences:
- Less emphasis on Spanish (but still helpful)
- Different entrance exam
- Training at FLETC (19 weeks)
- Additional OJT at port of assignment

**Highlight:**
- Surveillance/reconnaissance experience

### Physical Preparation

Train specifically for:

### Background Preparation

Be ready to document:
- Complete residence history
- Employment history
- Foreign contacts
- Drug use history (if any)

**Honesty is essential—polygraph will be administered.**

## Career Progression

### Border Patrol Path

**Years 1-2:** Academy, career ladder (GL-5/7/9/11/12)
**Years 2-4:** GS-12 journey level
**Years 4-7:** GS-13, potential FOS (Field Operations Supervisor)
**Years 7-15:** SBPA (Supervisory BPA), management opportunities
**Years 15+:** Patrol Agent in Charge, Chief positions

### CBP Officer Path

**Years 1-3:** Career ladder (GL-5/7/9/11)
**Years 3-5:** GS-12 journey level
**Years 5-10:** Supervisory opportunities
**Years 10+:** Port management, program management

### Specialized Paths

- BORTAC selection
- Air and Marine transition
- Training division
- Headquarters positions
- International liaison
