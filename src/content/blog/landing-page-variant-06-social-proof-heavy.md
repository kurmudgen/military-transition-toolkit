### Question 1: Are these reviews real or paid?
100% real. We don't pay for reviews. These are actual veterans using the platform and sharing their honest experience. We can connect you with users if you want to verify (with their permission).

### Question 2: Why are there "only" 25+ users?
We launched during a government shutdown when .mil verification was down. We've been growing organically through word-of-mouth from veteran to veteran. Quality over quantity‚Äîwe'd rather have 25 engaged users than 1,000 inactive accounts.

Every transition is different, but the tools work the same. If you use the state benefits guides, you'll find benefits. If you use the career tools, you'll get clarity. If you use the VA guidance, you'll file correctly. Results depend on your situation, but the tools work.

### Question 4: Can I talk to someone who's used this?
Email us (support@militarytransitiontoolkit.com) and we'll connect you with a current user who's willing to chat‚Äîwith their permission. We're a small, tight community. People are generally happy to help.

### Question 5: What if my experience isn't as good as theirs?
7-day free trial means you can test everything risk-free. If it doesn't help, don't pay. But 24 out of 25 users are happy enough to recommend it to other veterans, so odds are good you'll find value too.

No. Our average rating is 4.9/5, which means 1 out of 5 stars is "missing" on average. Not everyone gives 5 stars, and that's fine. We share real feedback, not just the glowing stuff.

### Question 7: Will you have more users? Will that change the platform?

## A/B Test Hypothesis

### What We're Testing
**Primary:** Does heavy social proof (testimonials, ratings, success stories) convert better than features, benefits, or other messaging?

**Secondary:** Does "join a community" positioning resonate more than "buy a product" positioning?

**Tertiary:** Do detailed success stories with specific results (dollar amounts, time saved) build enough trust to overcome skepticism?

### Expected Winner
**High confidence this will be a top-3 performer.**

**Why it should work:**
- Social proof is one of the strongest conversion drivers (Cialdini's influence principles)
- Veteran-to-veteran trust is powerful in military community
- Specific results (dollar amounts, time saved) build credibility
- "Join 25+" is small but authentic (not fake "10,000 users" claims)

**Potential risks:**
- "Only 25 users" might seem too small for some users (not enough social proof)
- Heavy testimonial pages can feel repetitive or oversell
- May underperform with skeptics who don't trust online reviews

**Prediction:** Top-3 performer, possibly #1 or #2. Will excel with users who value peer recommendations and community. Will perform especially well with referral traffic (veteran recommending to veteran).

### Success Metrics
- **Primary:** Free trial sign-up rate (target: 16%+ due to strong social proof)
- **Secondary:** Trial-to-paid conversion rate (target: 10%+ due to trust built)
- **Engagement:** Scroll depth through testimonials (target: 75%+)
- **Quality:** Referral rate after sign-up (expect highest of all variants‚Äîusers feel part of community)
- **Trust:** Return visit rate (users come back after reading testimonials to sign up later)

### Statistical Significance Needed
- Minimum 250 visitors per variant
- 95% confidence level
- Run for 10-14 days
- Track engagement with testimonials (clicks, scroll depth)
- Monitor referral traffic performance (hypothesis: this variant will excel with referrals)
