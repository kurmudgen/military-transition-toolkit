# Notary Public Certification for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front
- **Time to get certified:** 1 day to 6 weeks (varies by state)
- **Total cost:** $50-$500 (commission + supplies)
- **Income potential:** $0-$1,000/month (side income) to $50,000-$100,000+ (full-time loan signing)
- **Best military backgrounds:** Any MOS—administrative, legal, or customer service helpful
- **Job outlook:** Strong demand, especially for mobile and loan signing notaries

## What Is a Notary Public?

A notary public is a state-appointed official authorized to witness signatures, administer oaths, and authenticate documents. Notaries serve as impartial witnesses to deter fraud.

**Common Services:**
- Witness signatures on documents
- Administer oaths and affirmations
- Certify copies
- Take acknowledgments
- Perform jurats

**Notary Signing Agent (NSA):** Specialized notary who handles loan document signings, earning significantly more per appointment.

## Types of Notary Services

### Traditional Notary
- Basic notarizations ($5-$25 per signature)
- Walk-in clients at fixed location
- Lower earning potential
- Often supplemental to other work

### Mobile Notary
- Travel to client location
- Higher fees ($50-$150+ per appointment)
- Flexible schedule
- Growing demand

### Notary Signing Agent (NSA)
- Specialize in mortgage loan documents
- $75-$200 per signing (or more)
- Requires additional training
- Highest income potential

### Remote Online Notary (RON)
- Perform notarizations via video
- Growing rapidly post-COVID
- State authorization required
- Technology-enabled service

## State Requirements

Requirements vary significantly by state:

### Common Requirements
- **Age:** 18+ (some states higher)
- **Residency:** State resident or work in state
- **Background check:** No felony convictions typically
- **Education:** Some states require course
- **Exam:** Some states require passing exam
- **Bond:** Required in most states ($5,000-$25,000)
- **Commission application:** State filing

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much can a notary make?**
A: Basic notary: $200-$800/month part-time. NSA: $2,000-$8,000+/month. Full-time NSA can earn $50,000-$100,000+/year.

**Q: Is notary signing agent worth it?**
A: Yes. NSA training costs $150-$500 but signings pay $75-$200 each. ROI is quick if you're active.

**Q: How long does it take to become a notary?**
A: 1 day to 6 weeks depending on state. Some states have no education/exam; others require both.

**Q: Can I do this part-time?**
A: Yes. Many notaries work part-time around other jobs. Signings often scheduled evenings/weekends.

**Q: What about remote online notary (RON)?**
A: Growing rapidly. Requires state authorization and technology setup. Good supplement to in-person work.

**Q: Do I need a car?**
A: For mobile notary/NSA, yes. Most income is from traveling to clients.

**Q: How do I get signing appointments?**
A: Register with signing services (Snapdocs, Notary Rotary, etc.), network with title companies, direct marketing.

**Q: Is there liability risk?**
A: Some. E&O insurance ($50-$200/year) protects against errors. Follow proper procedures to minimize risk.
