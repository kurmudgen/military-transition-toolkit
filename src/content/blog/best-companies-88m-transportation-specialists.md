## How to Maximize Your Chances

### 1. Highlight Logistics Experience

**Don't write:** "88M Transportation Specialist, drove vehicles"

**Do write:** "Logistics operations professional managing vehicle fleet operations, supply chain coordination, route optimization, and compliance for mission-critical operations"

### 2. Emphasize Key Skills

- Fleet and vehicle management
- Logistics planning and coordination
- Supply chain operations
- Route planning and optimization
- Compliance and safety protocols
- Team leadership and supervision
- Numbers: "Managed 100+ vehicle fleet," "Coordinated 500+ shipments," "Optimized routes reducing costs 20%"

### 3. Consider Certifications

- **Commercial Driver's License (CDL):** If pursuing driver roles
- **APICS CSCP:** Supply chain certification
- **Lean Six Sigma:** Process improvement

### 4. Strategic Application

- Apply to 20-30+ companies
- Target both driver and logistics coordinator roles
- Look for advancement opportunities in company
- Don't just apply for driver positions - target management track

### 5. Geographic Flexibility

- Major hubs pay more: Texas, California, Georgia, Illinois
- Regional companies more flexible on location
- Remote logistics coordination roles emerging
- Consider cost of living vs. salary
