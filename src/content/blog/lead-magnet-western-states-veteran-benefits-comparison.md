## 1. QUICK COMPARISON OVERVIEW

**The West offers diverse veteran benefits**, from Nevada's tax-free environment to California's massive property exemptions. The region includes 7 states with no income tax and some of the highest property tax exemptions in the nation.

**TOP 5 WESTERN STATES FOR VETERANS:**
1. **Nevada** - No income tax + full property exemption for 100% disabled
2. **California** - $262,950 exemption (highest in nation for low-income 100% disabled)
3. **Arizona** - Purple Heart property tax exemption + strong disabled benefits
4. **Washington** - No income tax + strong property exemptions
5. **Colorado** - $300,000+ exemption for 100% disabled + growing job market

**NO INCOME TAX STATES (Save $1,000-$5,000/year):**
- Nevada
- Washington
- Wyoming
- Alaska
- **Bonus:** New Hampshire (East, but worth noting)
- **Bonus:** Texas, Florida, Tennessee (South, covered in Southern guide)

**HIGHEST PROPERTY EXEMPTIONS:**
- California: $262,950 (low-income 100% disabled)
- Colorado: $300,000+ (100% disabled)
- Nevada: Full exemption (100% disabled)

## 2. NEVADA: TAX-FREE PARADISE

**Overall Ranking: Top 10 Nationally**

### INCOME TAX

**NO STATE INCOME TAX**
- Military retirement: $0 tax
- Wages/salary: $0 tax
- VA disability: $0 tax (federal law)
- Investment income: $0 tax

**Annual Savings vs. California:**
- $70,000 combined income (pension + job)
- CA tax: ~$2,500-$3,500
- NV tax: **$0**
- **Lifetime savings: $75,000-$105,000 over 30 years**

### PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS

**100% Disabled Veterans:**
- **FULL EXEMPTION** from property taxes
- No cap on home value
- Applies to primary residence

**Real-World Value:**
- $350,000 home in Henderson (Las Vegas area)
- Normal annual property taxes: ~$3,200
- With 100% disability: **$0**
- **Annual savings: $3,200**

**Disabled Veterans (60%+):**
- Partial property tax abatement
- Sliding scale based on percentage

**Surviving Spouses:**
- Exemption transfers to widow/widower

### EDUCATION BENEFITS

**Nevada Tuition Assistance:**
- Limited state programs
- Rely primarily on federal GI Bill
- Community colleges affordable even without state aid

### OTHER BENEFITS

**No Sales Tax on Groceries/Prescription Drugs:**
- Helps stretch disability/retirement income

**Veteran License Plates:**
- Available with veteran designation

**Gaming/Entertainment Industry Jobs:**
- Las Vegas hospitality sector often veteran-friendly

### DOWNSIDES

- Limited education benefits compared to CA/WA
- Hot desert climate (difficult for some service-connected conditions)
- VA healthcare concentrated in Las Vegas/Reno

### WHO SHOULD CHOOSE NEVADA

✅ 100% disabled veterans (full property exemption + no income tax)
✅ Military retirees (zero tax on pension)
✅ Veterans seeking tax-free environment
✅ Veterans working in hospitality, gaming, or remote work

**Best Cities:**
- Henderson (Las Vegas suburb, VA nearby, family-friendly)
- Reno (growing tech sector, lower cost than Vegas, outdoor recreation)
- Las Vegas (VA hospital, job market, entertainment)

## 3. CALIFORNIA: HIGHEST EXEMPTION AMOUNTS

**Overall Ranking: Top 15 Nationally (Despite High Taxes)**

### PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS

**Low-Income 100% Disabled Veterans:**
- **$262,950 exemption** (2024 amount, adjusts annually)
- Household income must be under $108,046 (2024)
- Largest property tax exemption in the United States

**Real-World Value:**
- $400,000 home in San Diego County
- Tax rate: ~1.1%
- Normal tax: $4,400/year
- After $262,950 exemption: Only $1,507/year
- **Savings: $2,893/year**

**100% Disabled Veterans (Any Income):**
- **$161,083 basic exemption** (2024)
- No income limits
- Applies statewide

**Disabled Veterans (Any Percentage):**
- **$4,000 or $1,000 exemption** depending on property value

### INCOME TAX

**Military Retirement:**
- NO exemption (fully taxable)
- CA tax rates: 1% - 13.3% (highest in nation)

**Example:**
- $50,000 military pension
- CA tax: ~$1,500-$2,000/year
- **This is why many retirees leave CA**

### EDUCATION BENEFITS

**CalVet Fee Waiver:**
- Waives tuition and fees at California community colleges
- For veterans who served during wartime

**College Fee Waiver (Dependents):**
- Free tuition at UC/CSU for dependents of 100% disabled or deceased veterans
- Must meet income requirements
- **Massive value:** UC Berkeley = $14,000/year × 4 years = $56,000

**Cal Guard Education Assistance:**
- Up to $18,000 for CA National Guard members

### OTHER BENEFITS

**CalVet Home Loans:**
- Low-interest home loans
- Competitive rates
- Up to $1.5M loan amount

**Free Hunting/Fishing Licenses:**
- For 100% disabled veterans

**Hiring Preference:**
- Veterans preference for state jobs

### DOWNSIDES

- Highest income tax in the nation
- High cost of living (especially Bay Area, LA, San Diego)
- Property taxes still high even with exemptions
- Heavy regulation/bureaucracy

### WHO SHOULD CHOOSE CALIFORNIA

✅ 100% disabled, low-income veterans (highest exemption nationally)
✅ Veterans with children going to UC/CSU (dependent fee waiver)
✅ Veterans in high-paying tech/entertainment careers (can offset high taxes)
✅ Veterans who value climate, diversity, recreation

**Best Cities:**
- San Diego (huge military presence, VA hospital, weather)
- Sacramento (state capital, veterans programs, lower cost than coastal)
- Los Angeles (entertainment, VA West LA, job market)
- San Francisco Bay Area (tech jobs, high salaries, expensive)

## 4. ARIZONA: PURPLE HEART BENEFITS

**Overall Ranking: Top 15 Nationally**

### PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS

**Purple Heart Recipients:**
- **FULL PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTION**
- Applies to primary residence
- No disability rating required (only Purple Heart)
- **Extremely generous benefit**

**100% Disabled Veterans:**
- Property tax exemption on primary residence
- Applies to both county and state portions

**Real-World Value:**
- $300,000 home in Phoenix
- Normal property taxes: ~$2,700/year
- With Purple Heart or 100% disability: **$0**
- **Annual savings: $2,700**

**Widows of Military:**
- $4,289 exemption (2024)

### INCOME TAX

**Retired Military Pay:**
- Up to **$3,500 exempt** (65+)
- Up to **$2,500 exempt** (under 65)
- Very limited benefit

**AZ Tax Rates:** 2.55% - 4.5% (moderate)

### EDUCATION BENEFITS

**Free Tuition (Dependents):**
- Children of 100% disabled or deceased veterans
- Free tuition at Arizona state universities (ASU, U of A, NAU)
- Does not cover fees

**National Guard Tuition Assistance:**
- For AZ Guard members

### OTHER BENEFITS

**Veteran Property Valuation Protection:**
- Property cannot be re-assessed higher while veteran lives there

**Employment:**
- Veterans preference for state jobs

**Free Hunting/Fishing:**
- For 100% disabled veterans

### DOWNSIDES

- Hot climate (summer temps 110°F+)
- Limited income tax exemption
- VA healthcare concentrated in Phoenix/Tucson

### WHO SHOULD CHOOSE ARIZONA

✅ **Purple Heart recipients** (full property exemption regardless of disability rating)
✅ 100% disabled veterans (property exemption)
✅ Veterans seeking warm, dry climate
✅ Veterans with children attending ASU/U of A

**Best Cities:**
- Phoenix (VA hospital, job market, largest metro)
- Tucson (lower cost, U of Arizona, VA hospital)
- Mesa (Phoenix suburb, veteran-friendly, affordable)
- Prescott (small town, cooler climate, veteran community)

**Read the complete Arizona guide for full details**

## 5. WASHINGTON & OREGON: PACIFIC NORTHWEST COMPARISON

### **WASHINGTON**

**Income Tax:** **NO STATE INCOME TAX**
- Military retirement: Not taxed
- Wages: Not taxed
- Investment income: Capital gains tax on high earners ($250K+)

**Property Tax Exemptions:**
- **100% disabled:** Full exemption (first $300,000 of value in 2024)
- **80%+ disabled:** Partial exemption
- **61-79% disabled:** $40,000 exemption
- Senior veterans: Additional exemptions

**Real-World Value (100% Disabled):**
- $350,000 home in Tacoma
- Tax rate: ~1%
- Normal tax: $3,500/year
- After $300,000 exemption: Only $500/year
- **Savings: $3,000/year**

**Education:**
- Free tuition at WA state colleges for families making under $50K
- Veterans can use GI Bill at University of Washington, WSU

**Best Cities:**
- Seattle/Tacoma (JBLM, VA Puget Sound, tech jobs, high salaries)
- Spokane (lower cost, growing, VA hospital)
- Vancouver (Portland metro access, no income tax, no sales tax if shop in OR)

**Who Should Choose:** 100% disabled veterans, military retirees (no income tax), tech workers

---

### **OREGON**

**Income Tax:**
- **No exemption for military retirement** (fully taxable)
- OR tax rates: 4.75% - 9.9% (high)

**Property Tax Exemptions:**
- **100% disabled:** Full exemption
- **40%+ disabled:** $27,485 assessed value exemption (2024)

**Education:**
- Oregon Veteran Tuition Waiver: Free tuition at community colleges
- Limited compared to other states

**Sales Tax:** **NO SALES TAX** (big plus)

**Best Cities:**
- Portland (VA hospital, job market, no sales tax)
- Eugene (University of Oregon, lower cost)
- Salem (state capital, moderate cost)

**Comparison Verdict:**
- **Washington wins** for military retirees (no income tax vs. OR's high income tax)
- **Oregon wins** for shoppers (no sales tax vs. WA's 6.5%-10% sales tax)
- **Both** offer good property exemptions for disabled veterans

**Best Strategy:** Live in Vancouver, WA (no income tax) and shop in Portland, OR (no sales tax)

## 6. COLORADO: GROWING VETERAN HUB

**Overall Ranking: Top 20 Nationally**

### PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS

**100% Disabled Veterans:**
- **First $300,000+ of actual value exempt** (adjusted annually)
- 2024 amount: $307,000 exempt
- One of the highest exemptions in the U.S.

**Real-World Value:**
- $400,000 home in Colorado Springs
- Normal tax: ~$2,400/year
- After $307,000 exemption: Only $560/year
- **Savings: $1,840/year**

**50% or Higher Disabled:**
- 50% of first $200,000 exempt

**Gold Star Spouses:**
- Full property tax exemption

### INCOME TAX

**Military Retirement:**
- Up to **$40,000 exempt** for recipients age 55+
- Under 55: Up to $20,000 exempt

**Example (Age 65):**
- $60,000 pension
- $40,000 exempt, $20,000 taxable
- CO tax rate: 4.4% flat
- Tax: $880
- **Savings: $1,760/year vs. full taxation**

### EDUCATION BENEFITS

**COL Scholarship:**
- Free tuition for dependents of deceased/100% disabled Colorado Guard members

**In-State Tuition:**
- Veterans get in-state tuition immediately (don't need to wait for residency)

### OTHER BENEFITS

**Free Parks Pass:**
- For disabled veterans (annual parks pass normally $80)

**Free Hunting/Fishing:**
- For 100% disabled veterans

**Strong Job Market:**
- Defense contractors, aerospace, tech, outdoor industry

### WHO SHOULD CHOOSE COLORADO

✅ 100% disabled veterans (high property exemption)
✅ Military retirees 55+ (strong income exemption)
✅ Veterans seeking outdoor lifestyle (skiing, hiking, mountain living)
✅ Veterans in aerospace/defense/tech

**Best Cities:**
- Colorado Springs (Fort Carson, USAFA, Peterson SFB, defense contractors)
- Denver (VA hospital, job market, airport hub)
- Aurora (Buckley SFB, VA hospital, diverse)
- Fort Collins (CSU, outdoor lifestyle, growing tech)

- Among the lowest in the nation (even without exemptions)

**Free Hunting/Fishing:**
- For disabled veterans

### WHO SHOULD CHOOSE NEW MEXICO

✅ Veterans seeking low cost of living
✅ Military retirees (decent income exemption)
✅ Veterans who value Southwestern culture
✅ Veterans in national labs/defense (Los Alamos, Sandia, White Sands)

**Best Cities:**
- Albuquerque (VA hospital, Kirtland AFB, biggest city)

## 10. ALASKA & HAWAII: NON-CONTIGUOUS STATES

### **ALASKA**

**Income Tax:** **NO STATE INCOME TAX**
- Military retirement not taxed
- Zero state income tax

**Property Tax:**
- **100% disabled:** Full exemption up to $150,000 assessed value
- **50%+ disabled:** Partial exemption

**Education:**
- AK residents pay in-state tuition at University of Alaska

**Permanent Fund Dividend:**
- All AK residents (including veterans) get annual payment from oil revenue
- ~$1,000-$2,000/year (varies)

**Challenges:**
- High cost of living (everything shipped in)
- Harsh climate
- Remote locations
- Limited VA facilities (Anchorage VA, CBOC in Fairbanks)

**Best For:** Veterans who love outdoors, hunting, fishing, solitude

**Best Cities:**
- Anchorage (VA hospital, largest city, JBER)
- Fairbanks (Fort Wainwright, UA Fairbanks)

---

### **HAWAII**

**Property Tax:**
- **100% disabled:** Full exemption
- Surviving spouses also get exemption

**Income Tax:**
- **Military retirement fully exempt** (no state tax on pensions)
- HI tax rates: 1.4% - 11% (high for other income)

**Education:**
- Dependents of 100% disabled/deceased get tuition waiver at University of Hawaii

**Challenges:**
- EXTREME cost of living (highest in nation)
- Expensive housing ($800K+ median home price)
- Geographic isolation
- Limited VA facilities (Honolulu, Hilo)

**Benefits:**
- Year-round warm weather
- Strong military culture
- Beautiful environment

**Best For:**
- 100% disabled veterans (full property exemption helps offset costs)
- Military retirees (pension tax-free)
- Veterans with high disability compensation (helps cover high costs)

**Best Cities:**
- Honolulu (VA hospital, job market, Oahu)
- Hilo (Big Island, lower cost than Oahu)
- Kailua-Kona (retirees, tourism jobs)

**Reality Check:** Even with tax benefits, Hawaii is expensive. Only choose if you have substantial income or value lifestyle above all else.

## 13. DECISION MATRIX & ACTION PLAN

### **DECISION TREE**

**Are you 100% disabled?**
→ YES: Choose **Nevada, California (if low-income), or Washington** (best exemptions)
→ NO: Continue

**Are you a military retiree?**
→ YES: Choose **Nevada, Washington, Wyoming, Alaska** (no income tax)
→ NO: Continue

**Do you have children attending college?**
→ YES: Choose **California** (UC/CSU fee waiver) or **Arizona** (free state school)
→ NO: Continue

**What's your priority?**
→ **Lowest taxes:** NV, WA, WY, AK (no income tax)
→ **Job market:** CA, WA, CO (tech/aerospace/defense)
→ **Outdoor lifestyle:** CO, MT, UT, AK (mountains, skiing, hunting)
→ **Cost of living:** NM, ID, MT (affordable)
→ **Weather:** CA, AZ, NV, HI (warm year-round)

### **ACTION PLAN**

**STEP 1: Calculate Your Potential Savings**

Use online property tax calculators for target cities:
- Find tax rate (usually 0.7% - 1.2% of home value in West)
- Apply exemption
- Calculate annual savings

**Example (100% disabled, $350K home):**
- NV: $0 tax (save ~$3,200/year)
- WA: ~$500/year (save ~$2,700/year)
- CA: ~$1,500/year (save ~$2,500/year with exemption)

**STEP 2: Visit Top 2-3 States**

□ Spend time in target cities
□ Check job market, housing costs, VA facilities
□ Experience climate/lifestyle
□ Talk to local veteran groups

**STEP 3: Establish Residency**

□ Get driver's license (typically need proof of address)
□ Register vehicle
□ Register to vote
□ Update address with VA, DEERS

**STEP 4: File for Benefits**

□ Property tax exemption (county assessor's office)
□ Income tax exemption (state tax return)
□ Education benefits (university financial aid office)
□ Veteran license plates
