# FAA Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide to Federal Aviation Administration

If you controlled aircraft in the military, the FAA is your natural civilian destination. The Federal Aviation Administration needs your skills—and has special hiring programs specifically for military air traffic controllers.

The FAA employs over 45,000 people operating the world's largest and safest aviation system. For veterans with aviation backgrounds, particularly air traffic control, FAA offers excellent careers with competitive pay and early retirement.

## Why FAA for Veterans?

### Direct Skill Application

Military aviation experience is directly applicable:
- Air traffic control transfers directly
- Aviation maintenance experience valued
- Flight operations knowledge
- Safety culture familiarity

### Military ATC Priority

FAA actively recruits military controllers:
- Prior experience hiring pathway
- Skills transfer directly
- Training time reduced
- Premium on military ATC backgrounds

### Excellent Compensation

ATC positions among highest-paid federal jobs:
- $100,000-$200,000+ possible
- Location premium pay
- Excellent benefits
- Early retirement option

## Air Traffic Controller Path

### The Most Common Veteran Path

Military ATC → FAA ATC is well-established:

**Military ATC Experience Accepted:**
- Army (15Q)
- Navy (AC)
- Air Force (1C1X1)
- Marine Corps (7257)
- Coast Guard (similar specialty)

### ATC Hiring Tracks

**Prior Experience:**
- Military controllers qualify
- Reduced training time
- Direct path to operational positions

**CTI (Collegiate Training Initiative):**
- FAA-approved schools
- Foundational training
- Entry pathway

**Off-the-Street (OTS):**
- No experience required
- Age limit 30
- Full training required

### ATC Requirements

**Age:**
- Prior experience: Under 36 at hire
- OTS: Under 31 at hire
- Must retire at 56

**Citizenship:**
- U.S. citizen

**Security:**
- Background investigation
- Drug testing
- Security clearance

**Medical:**
- FAA Class 2 medical certificate
- Vision and hearing standards
- No disqualifying conditions

**Experience (for Prior Experience track):**
- 52 weeks of ATC experience
- Within 5 years of application
- Military ATC qualifies

### ATC Hiring Process

**Step 1: Application**
- Through USAJOBS or FAA careers
- Verify prior experience eligibility

**Step 2: Air Traffic Skills Assessment (ATSA)**
- Cognitive and aptitude testing
- May be waived for military controllers

**Step 3: Interview/Review**
- Experience verification
- Panel interview possible

**Step 4: Medical**
- FAA medical examination
- Drug testing
- Psychological evaluation

**Step 5: Security**
- Background investigation
- Clearance determination

**Step 6: Training**
- FAA Academy (Oklahoma City)
- Facility-specific training
- On-the-job training
- Certification process

### ATC Training

**FAA Academy (Oklahoma City):**
- 2-5 months depending on track
- Academics and simulation
- Prior experience track shorter

**Facility Training:**
- Facility-specific procedures
- On-position training
- 1-3 years to full certification
- Progressive certification levels

## Air Traffic Controller Salary

### Pay Structure

ATC uses unique pay system:

**Core Compensation:**
- Base pay varies by facility
- Premium pay for location/staffing
- Locality adjustments

### Pay by Facility Level

Facilities are categorized by complexity (Level 4-12):

| Level | Base Pay Range | With Premiums |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Level 5 | $60,000-$90,000 | $80,000-$120,000 |
| Level 7 | $70,000-$100,000 | $95,000-$140,000 |
| Level 9 | $80,000-$120,000 | $110,000-$160,000 |
| Level 11 | $90,000-$140,000 | $130,000-$185,000 |
| Level 12 | $100,000-$160,000 | $150,000-$200,000+ |

### Premium Pay

**Location premium:** Staffing shortages = higher pay
**Night/weekend differentials:** Standard federal differentials
**Overtime:** Frequently available

### Highest-Paid Facilities

**Major TRACONs and Centers:**
- New York TRACON
- Southern California TRACON
- Atlanta Center
- Chicago Center
- Dallas/Fort Worth TRACON

Controllers at major facilities routinely exceed $150,000.
