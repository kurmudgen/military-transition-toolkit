# Pennsylvania vs New Jersey Veterans Comparison 2025

Pennsylvania and New Jersey represent two of the most developed northeastern states with strong economic centers, excellent healthcare systems, and significant veteran populations. However, they have dramatically different tax burdens and cost structures. This comprehensive guide compares these Northeast destinations for military retirees.

## Military Retirement Tax Treatment: Critical Difference

### Pennsylvania: Military Retirement Exempt

Pennsylvania exempts military retirement from state income tax:

**Pennsylvania Military Retirement Benefits:**
- Military pension: 100% exempt from state income tax
- VA disability compensation: Exempt
- State income tax rate: 3.07% (flat)
- Effective tax on military retirement: 0%

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Military retirement tax: $0
- Other income tax: $614 (at 3.07%)
- **Total state tax: $614**

### New Jersey: No Exemption, High Tax Rate

New Jersey taxes military retirement without exemption:

**New Jersey Tax Treatment:**
- Military pension: Subject to state income tax (no exemption)
- VA disability compensation: Exempt
- State income tax rate: 1.4%-10.75% (progressive)
- Effective tax on military retirement: ~6%

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Military pension tax: $2,700 (at 6% effective)
- Other income tax: $2,150 (at 10.75%)
- **Total state tax: $4,850**

**Tax Winner: Pennsylvania** (Saves $4,236 annually on this income!)

## Final Verdict

### Choose Pennsylvania if:
- You're a military retiree on fixed income
- You want military retirement tax exemption
- You want 13-14% lower cost of living
- You want affordability long-term
- You value VA healthcare quality

### Choose New Jersey if:
- You're a high-earning professional
- You work in pharma or finance
- You want urban amenities
- You can afford premium costs
- You value proximity to NYC

**The Numbers:** Pennsylvania is dramatically superior for military retirees:
- Military retirement tax: $0 vs $2,700/year in NJ
- Cost of living: 13-14% lower
- Housing: Median $260K vs $420K
- Property tax: Much lower
- **10-year total advantage: $125,460**

**Pennsylvania is one of America's best states for military retirees** due to military retirement exemption, low cost of living, affordable housing, and excellent VA healthcare.

New Jersey is only appropriate for high-earning professionals whose pharma/finance salaries justify premium costs.
