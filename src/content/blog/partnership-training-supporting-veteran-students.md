# Supporting Veteran Students
## Academic Advisor Guide to Student Veteran Success

---

## Introduction

Student veterans represent 6-7% of college students but face unique challenges: 37% of part-time and 16% of full-time veteran students drop out within 9 months. Academic advisors play a critical role in their success.

**This guide provides:**
- Understanding student veteran challenges
- Effective advising strategies
- Academic planning for veterans
- Career development integration
- GI Bill and benefits navigation
- Resources and support

---

## Understanding Student Veterans

### Who They Are

**Demographics:**
- 669,922 veterans used VA education benefits (2018)
- Average age: 28-32 (older than traditional students)
- Many have families and dependents
- Part-time and full-time students
- Diverse service backgrounds (all branches)

**Service Background:**
- 4-20+ years of military experience
- Leadership and management skills
- Technical training and certifications
- Deployment experience
- Recent transition from military

---

### Unique Challenges

**1. Balancing Responsibilities (60% report concerns)**
- Family obligations (many are parents)
- Work (part-time or full-time jobs)
- School (full or part course load)
- Finances (managing GI Bill, housing, expenses)

**2. Academic Adjustment**
- Years away from academic environment
- Different learning style from military training
- Discomfort with classroom discussion/participation
- Perfectionism and high standards

**3. Social Integration**
- Age gap with traditional students
- Different life experiences and maturity
- Military identity vs. student identity
- Feeling isolated or misunderstood

**4. Benefits Administration Challenges**
- VA education benefits complexity
- Certification requirements and deadlines
- Housing allowance timing
- Understanding coverage and limitations

**5. Career Uncertainty**
- Connecting military experience to civilian careers
- Understanding how degree leads to employment
- Resume building with military + academic background
- Internship and job search strategies

### 2. Academic Planning for Veterans

**Assess Prior Learning:**
- Military training (Joint Services Transcript, CCAF transcripts)
- Technical certifications and schools
- Leadership development courses
- Deployment and operational experience
- Transfer credits from other institutions

**Maximize Credit for Military Experience:**
- Work with registrar on JST evaluation
- Identify courses that match military training
- Explore portfolio assessment or CLEP exams
- Accelerate degree completion where possible

**Degree Planning:**
- Balance course load with work/family obligations
- Consider online/hybrid options for flexibility
- Plan around VA certification requirements (typically 12+ credits full-time)
- Front-load electives that match military background
- Career-focused major selection

---

### 3. GI Bill Navigation Support

**Understanding Coverage:**
- Post-9/11 GI Bill covers: tuition, fees, housing allowance, book stipend
- Montgomery GI Bill: Fixed monthly amount
- Vocational Rehab (Chapter 31): Disability-rated veterans
- Dependents benefits: Transfer of benefits from service member

**Advising on Benefits:**
- Maximize benefit duration (36 months typically)
- Plan degree completion within benefits timeline
- Understand semester certification requirements
- Coordinate with VA certifying official
- Discuss post-benefit planning (final semester funding)

**Common Pitfalls:**
- Withdrawing from classes (must repay VA, lose benefits time)
- Failing classes (GI Bill issues)
- Missing certification deadlines
- Not understanding housing allowance timing (paid in arrears)

---

### 4. Academic Success Support

**Study Skills & Learning Strategies:**
- Refer to academic support services early
- Recommend tutoring proactively
- Discuss time management for multiple responsibilities
- Connect to writing center
- Encourage use of office hours

**Classroom Adjustment:**
- Normalize participation challenges
- Coach on classroom norms (discussion, questions, group work)
- Address perfectionism (B is okay, not failure)
- Encourage connecting with faculty

**Mental Health & Wellness:**
- Normalize counseling and support-seeking
- Refer to counseling services proactively
- Connect to veteran-specific support (if available)
- Understand trauma-informed approach
- Don't assume PTSD, but be aware of potential challenges

---

### 5. Career Development Integration

**Connect Education to Career:**
- Discuss career goals in every advising session
- Help articulate how major leads to employment
- Identify internships and experiential learning
- Connect military skills + education to career outcomes
- Plan ahead for job search timeline

**Resume & Professional Development:**
- Refer to career services early (not just senior year)
- Help translate military experience for resumes
- Identify relevant certifications or credentials
- Discuss networking and professional associations
- Connect to veteran-focused employers

## Common Advising Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Struggling Academically

**Symptoms:** Failing or withdrawing from classes, not attending, missing assignments

**Approach:**
1. Meet immediately (don't wait for them to reach out)
2. Ask about challenges holistically (school, work, family, finances, health)
3. Problem-solve together (reduce course load, tutoring, extensions, resources)
4. Connect to support services (counseling, academic support, veteran services)
5. Monitor closely and follow up regularly
6. Discuss GI Bill implications of failing/withdrawing

---

### Scenario 2: Balancing Too Many Responsibilities

**Symptoms:** Stressed, overwhelmed, missing meetings, mentions work/family pressures

**Approach:**
1. Validate pressures (they're real, not excuses)
2. Prioritize together (what must happen vs. what can wait)
3. Explore options (reduce course load, take semester off, online classes)
4. Connect to resources (childcare, financial aid, emergency funds)
5. Remind that degree completion matters more than speed
6. Create realistic plan

---

### Scenario 3: Career Uncertainty

**Symptoms:** Unclear major, questions about "what can I do with this degree?", job market concerns

**Approach:**
1. Explore interests, values, skills (military + personal)
2. Research careers aligned with major
3. Connect to career services and assessments
4. Identify informational interview opportunities
5. Discuss internships and experiential learning
6. Create academic + career plan
7. Connect to Military Transition Toolkit (if available)

---

### Scenario 4: Social Isolation

**Symptoms:** No connections on campus, doesn't attend events, feeling out of place

**Approach:**
1. Acknowledge age/experience gap with traditional students
2. Connect to student veteran organization or group
3. Identify veteran-friendly student organizations
4. Suggest study groups or project teams
5. Introduce to other veteran students
6. Normalize feeling different

---

### Scenario 5: Benefits Running Out

**Symptoms:** Nearing 36-month benefit limit, uncertainty about final semester

**Approach:**
1. Calculate remaining benefits months precisely
2. Plan degree completion within benefits (or as close as possible)
3. Explore alternative funding (loans, grants, scholarships, employer)
4. Prioritize required courses
5. Discuss gap-funding options for final semester if needed
6. Plan early (a year in advance)
