[INTRO]

**Cold Open:**

Petty Officer First Class David Chen spent 12 years in the Coast Guard conducting port security, boarding operations, and maritime law enforcement. He separated in 2023 and within 4 months landed a $105,000 position as Maritime Security Manager for a major port authority.

This is Jacob. Today: Coast Guard to high-paying private maritime security.

---

[MAIN CONTENT]

**Military Background:**
Maritime Enforcement Specialist (ME), conducted vessel boardings, port security post-9/11, counter-narcotics operations, held TWIC and multiple maritime certifications.

**Transition Strategy:**
Targeted port authorities, shipping companies, and private maritime security firms. Coast Guard experience highly valued in maritime industry for regulatory knowledge and operational expertise.

**Job Search:**
Used LinkedIn to connect with maritime security professionals. Attended maritime industry conference. Applied to 15 positions, received 6 interviews, got 3 offers. Chose Port of Long Beach for salary, benefits, and career growth.

**Current Role:**
Manages security operations for major port. Oversees security personnel, coordinates with federal agencies (Coast Guard, CBP, TSA), ensures MTSA compliance, conducts security assessments. Annual compensation $105K base + bonuses.

**Keys to Success:**
1. Coast Guard regulatory knowledge directly applicable
2. Federal law enforcement experience valued by ports
3. TWIC and maritime certifications gave immediate credibility
4. Understands both operational and regulatory sides

**Advice:**
Coast Guard veterans are pipeline to maritime security careers. Maintain certifications, network in maritime industry, target major ports and shipping companies, leverage federal law enforcement background.

[CONCLUSION]

David Chen transitioned from Coast Guard to $105K maritime security manager in 4 months. His regulatory knowledge, operational experience, and federal certifications made him ideal for private sector maritime security.
