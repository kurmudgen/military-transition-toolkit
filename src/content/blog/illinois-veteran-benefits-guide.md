# Illinois Veteran Benefits: Complete State Programs Guide (2025)

Illinois honors its 600,000+ veterans with substantial state benefits including property tax exemptions, significant education assistance through IVA scholarships, and employment preferences. The Illinois Department of Veterans' Affairs provides comprehensive support throughout the state.

This guide covers all Illinois veteran benefits with eligibility requirements and application procedures.

## Property Tax Exemptions

### Disabled Veterans Standard Homestead Exemption

**70%+ Service-Connected Disabled Veterans:**
- **Exemption:** $5,000 reduction in EAV (Equalized Assessed Value)
- **Tax Savings:** Approximately $500-$1,000+ annually

**50-69% Service-Connected Disabled Veterans:**
- **Exemption:** $2,500 reduction in EAV

**30-49% Service-Connected Disabled Veterans:**
- **Exemption:** Additional exemptions may apply

### Specially Adapted Housing Exemption

**100% Disabled Veterans with Specially Adapted Housing:**
- Up to $100,000 exemption
- For veterans with special housing grants from VA

### Returning Veterans Homestead Exemption

**Recently Deployed Veterans:**
- $5,000 reduction in EAV
- For two years following return from deployment
- Applies to National Guard and Reserve members

### Surviving Spouse Exemption

**Surviving Spouse of Killed in Action:**
- Complete exemption from property taxes
- Must not remarry
- Must continue occupying the home

### How to Apply

1. Obtain VA disability rating letter
2. Contact your county Assessor's office
3. Complete appropriate exemption application
4. Submit DD-214 and VA letter
5. Apply by deadline (typically early in year)

## Education Benefits

### IVA (Illinois Veteran Grant) Scholarship

**One of the Most Valuable State Education Benefits in the Country**

**Benefit:**
- Full tuition and fees at Illinois public universities
- Full tuition and fees at Illinois community colleges
- Up to 120 credit hours or 4 equivalent years

**Eligibility:**
- Illinois resident for 1 year before entering service OR
- Illinois resident for 1 year after service
- Served on active duty (any branch)
- Honorable discharge
- Not a current member of Armed Forces

**Coverage:**
- All Illinois public universities (U of I, ISU, NIU, etc.)
- All Illinois community colleges
- Does NOT cover books, housing, or fees beyond tuition

**How to Apply:**
1. Complete IVA Grant Application (online)
2. Provide DD-214
3. Provide proof of Illinois residency
4. Apply through ISAC (Illinois Student Assistance Commission)

**Note:** This is one of the best state education benefits in the nation. Combined with GI Bill housing allowance, this creates a powerful education package.

### Illinois National Guard Grant

**Benefit:**
- Tuition and mandatory fees at Illinois public colleges
- For active National Guard members

**Eligibility:**
- Active Illinois National Guard member
- Satisfactory Guard performance
- Enrolled in eligible program

### MIA/POW Scholarship

**For Dependents:**
- Full tuition and fees at Illinois public schools
- For children and spouses of POW/MIA or service members killed in action

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Illinois exempt military retirement pay from taxes?

Not fully. Illinois provides a general retirement income subtraction but does not have a specific military retirement exemption.

### What is the IVA Grant?

The Illinois Veteran Grant provides full tuition and fees at Illinois public colleges for up to 120 credit hours. It's one of the best state education benefits in the nation.

### What disability rating do I need for property tax exemption?

70%+ for the $5,000 exemption, 50-69% for $2,500 exemption. 100% disabled with specially adapted housing may receive up to $100,000 exemption.

### How do I apply for the IVA Grant?

Apply through the Illinois Student Assistance Commission (ISAC) with your DD-214 and proof of Illinois residency.

### How many state veterans homes does Illinois have?

Four: Anna, LaSalle, Manteno, and Quincy.
