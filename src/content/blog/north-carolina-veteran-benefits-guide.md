# North Carolina Veteran Benefits: Complete State Programs Guide (2025)

North Carolina, home to major military installations like Fort Liberty (formerly Fort Bragg), Camp Lejeune, and Cherry Point, offers extensive benefits to its 775,000+ veteran residents. From property tax exemptions to education assistance and employment preferences, NC provides meaningful support for those who served.

This guide covers all North Carolina veteran benefits with eligibility requirements and how to apply.

## Property Tax Exemptions

### Disabled Veteran Homestead Exclusion

**100% Permanently and Totally Disabled Veterans:**
- **Exclusion:** First $45,000 of appraised home value
- **Tax Savings:** Approximately $450-$900+ annually (varies by county)
- **Eligibility:** 100% service-connected disability rated permanent and total

**Requirements:**
- Own and occupy home as permanent residence
- Honorable discharge
- VA certification of permanent and total disability

### Surviving Spouse Exemption

**Eligibility:**
- Surviving spouse of veteran who died from service-connected causes
- Surviving spouse of veteran killed in action
- Surviving spouse of veteran who was 100% disabled at death

**Benefit:**
- Same $45,000 exclusion as disabled veteran

### How to Apply

1. Obtain VA disability rating letter (stating permanent and total)
2. Complete AV-9 form from your county tax office
3. Submit DD-214 and VA letter
4. File by June 1 for current tax year

## Education Benefits

### North Carolina National Guard Tuition Assistance Program

**Benefit:**
- Up to 100% tuition at NC public colleges and universities
- For active National Guard members

**Eligibility:**
- Active NC National Guard member
- Satisfactory Guard performance
- Enrolled in eligible program

### Scholarships for Children of Wartime Veterans

**North Carolina Scholarships for Children of War Veterans:**
- Class I-A: Children of 100% disabled veterans
- Class I-B: Children of wartime veterans receiving VA compensation
- Class II: Children of veterans who died in wartime
- Class III: Children of POW/MIA veterans
- Class IV: Children of veterans who died of wartime service-connected causes

**Benefit:**
- Free tuition at NC public colleges and universities
- Up to 4 academic years

**How to Apply:**
- Apply through NC Division of Veterans Affairs
- Deadline: On rolling basis, apply early

### In-State Tuition for Veterans

**All Veterans:**
- Qualify for in-state tuition regardless of prior residency
- Applies to GI Bill users
- Effective immediately upon enrollment
- Covers all UNC system and community colleges

## Vehicle Benefits

### Disabled Veteran License Plates

**Free Plates Available:**
- Disabled Veteran plate (100% disabled)
- Purple Heart plate
- Medal of Honor plate
- POW plate
- Combat Veteran plates (various wars)

**100% Disabled Veteran:**
- Free registration for one vehicle
- Free plate

### Property Tax Exemption on Vehicle

**Disabled Veterans:**
- One motor vehicle exempt from property tax
- 100% disabled veterans qualify

### How to Apply

1. Visit NC DMV
2. Bring DD-214 and VA disability documentation
3. Complete Form MVR-27D

## Hunting and Fishing

### Free Hunting/Fishing License

**Permanently and Totally Disabled Veterans:**
- Free Resident Sportsman License
- Valued at $45+ annually

**Requirements:**
- NC resident
- VA-rated permanent and total disability
- Apply through NC Wildlife Resources Commission

### Discounted Licenses

**50%+ Disabled Veterans:**
- Discounted hunting and fishing licenses
- Various discount levels based on disability rating

**All Veterans:**
- Veteran Fishing License: $11 (vs. $25 regular)
- Veteran Hunting License: $11 (vs. $25 regular)

### How to Apply

1. Visit NC Wildlife license agent or website
2. Provide VA disability documentation
3. Show NC driver's license

## Employment Preferences

### State Employment Preference

**Veterans' Preference in NC State Government:**
- Veterans: 5-point preference
- Disabled Veterans: 10-point preference
- Applied to passing scores on examinations

**How to Claim:**
- Indicate veteran status on state job application
- Provide DD-214
- Submit VA disability documentation if applicable

### NC4Vets (Works4NC)

**Services:**

- Resume assistance

- Career counseling
- Priority of service

**Access:**
- Visit any NCWorks Career Center
- Register at ncworks.gov

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is military retirement pay taxed in NC?

No. North Carolina completely exempts military retirement pay from state income tax.

### Do I qualify for property tax exemption if I'm not 100% disabled?

The disabled veteran homestead exclusion requires 100% permanent and total disability. However, check with your county for other relief programs.

### Can my children get free college tuition?

Yes, if you're 100% disabled, died from service-connected causes, or meet other criteria. Apply through the NC Division of Veterans Affairs.

### How do I get my free hunting/fishing license?

Visit a NC Wildlife license agent or the website with proof of 100% permanent and total disability rating.

### Are these benefits available to Guard/Reserve members?

Many benefits require qualifying service. Guard/Reserve members who served on active duty may qualify depending on the specific benefit.
