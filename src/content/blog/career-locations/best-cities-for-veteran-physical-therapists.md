# Best Cities for Veteran Physical Therapists: 2025 Guide

Military physical therapists, physical therapy technicians, and rehabilitation specialists have restored service members to duty after combat injuries, training accidents, and deployments. Your experience with musculoskeletal rehabilitation, sports medicine, and working with driven patients translates exceptionally well to civilian practice. With an aging population and increased focus on rehabilitation, physical therapists are in strong demand.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Top 3 Cities:**
1. Las Vegas, NV - $105,000 avg salary - No state tax, high demand, growing market
2. Houston, TX - $98,000 avg salary - Best purchasing power, no state tax
3. San Francisco, CA - $115,000 avg salary - Highest base pay, strong market

**Key Factors:**
- Salary range: $68,000 - $130,000 depending on setting and specialization
- Job growth: 15% projected through 2032 (much faster than average)
- Licensing: DPT degree required; state license required
- Veteran advantage: Military PT experience with orthopedic/sports injuries highly valued

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Do military PT officers transition directly?**
Yes. Military PTs have DPT degrees and clinical experience. State licensure reciprocity makes transition straightforward.

**2. Can PT technicians become PTs?**
Yes, with additional education. GI Bill covers DPT programs. Some programs give credit for military experience.

**3. What's the job market like?**
Strong. 15% growth projected. Outpatient orthopedic positions most common, home health growing rapidly.

**4. Which specialty is best for veterans?**
Orthopedic/sports medicine leverages military experience with active-duty and veteran populations.

**5. Is private practice ownership viable?**
Yes. Many PTs transition to private practice ownership after 5-10 years of experience.

**6. How is work-life balance?**
Generally good. Outpatient typically M-F, some weekends for sports settings. Home health offers flexibility.

**7. What certifications add value?**
OCS (Orthopedic), SCS (Sports), FAAOMPT (Manual Therapy) add $5,000-15,000 to salary.

**8. Can I work with veterans?**
Yes. VA, military treatment facilities, and private practices serving veterans offer opportunities.

**9. Is travel PT an option?**
Yes. Travel PT contracts pay $1,800-2,500/week for 13-week assignments.

**10. What about telehealth?**
Growing rapidly. Some states allow telehealth PT, expanding work options.
