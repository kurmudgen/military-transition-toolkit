# Best Cities for Veteran Financial Advisors: 2025 Guide

Military veterans possess the integrity, discipline, and leadership that make excellent financial advisors. Your experience building trust, understanding benefits, and helping others plan for the future translates directly to financial services. Veterans serving military clients bring unique value—understanding military pay, TSP, SBP, VA benefits, and the military financial journey.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Top 3 Cities:**
1. San Francisco, CA - $150,000+ median - High net worth clients
2. New York City, NY - $145,000+ median - Financial capital
3. Dallas-Fort Worth, TX - $120,000 median - No state tax, growing wealth

**Key Factors:**
- Income range: $50,000 - $500,000+ depending on practice and assets under management
- Job growth: 13% projected through 2032 (much faster than average)
- Requirements: Series 7/66 licenses, CFP preferred
- Veteran advantage: Trust, military network, understanding of military benefits

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Is financial advising good for veterans?**
Excellent fit. Trust, discipline, and military network provide strong foundation.

**2. How do I get started?**
Join wirehouse or insurance company for training and licensing. Build experience before independence.

**3. What licenses do I need?**
Series 7 and 66 for securities. Life/health for insurance. Firm sponsors licensing process.

**4. Should I specialize in military clients?**
Yes. Your understanding of military benefits is unique advantage. USAA, First Command, and independent RIAs serve this market.

**5. What about CFP certification?**
Highly valuable. Requires education, exam, and experience. Consider pursuing after establishing practice.

**6. Is income guaranteed?**
No. Most compensation is commission or fee-based. Wirehouse training programs offer temporary salary.

**7. How long to build successful practice?**
Typically 3-5 years to sustainable income. First two years are challenging.

**8. What about robo-advisors?**
Human advice remains valuable for complex planning. Military benefits complexity favors human advisors.

**9. Can I work with military members?**
Yes. FINRA doesn't restrict. Some firms specialize in military market (First Command, USAA).

**10. What about compliance concerns?**
Highly regulated industry. Training programs cover compliance. Integrity foundation helps.
