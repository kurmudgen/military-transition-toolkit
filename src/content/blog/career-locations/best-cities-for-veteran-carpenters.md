## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What military MOSs translate to carpentry?**
12W (Carpentry/Masonry), BU (Builder - Seabees), 3E3X1 (Structural - USAF)

**2. Do I need an apprenticeship?**
Not always, but apprenticeships (2-4 years) provide best path to journeyman status.

**3. What certifications help?**
OSHA 10/30, NCCER carpentry, scaffold certification, forklift certification.

**4. Union vs non-union?**
Unions offer higher wages, benefits, and training. Non-union offers more flexibility.

**5. What's earning potential?**
$38,000-62,000 typical. Union journeymen in high-cost areas can exceed $80,000.

**6. Is carpentry physically demanding?**
Yes. Heavy lifting, climbing, working in weather conditions are constant.

**7. What tools do I need?**
Hand tools and basic power tools ($1,000-3,000). Employers often provide specialty tools.

**8. Can I specialize?**
Yes. Finish carpentry, formwork, and millwright work command premiums.
