# Best Cities for Veteran Radiology Technicians: 2025 Guide

Military radiology technicians have operated sophisticated imaging equipment in field hospitals, aboard ships, and at military treatment facilities worldwide. Your experience with X-ray, CT, and other imaging modalities—often in challenging conditions—translates directly to civilian radiology. With medical imaging technology advancing rapidly, skilled radiology technicians remain in strong demand.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Does military radiology experience transfer?**
Yes. 68P and equivalent MOSs provide strong foundation. ARRT certification required for civilian positions.

**2. What modality pays best?**
MRI, interventional radiology, and nuclear medicine typically command highest premiums.

**3. How long for additional modality training?**
CT: 6-12 months. MRI: 12-18 months. Programs available on-the-job or formal.

**4. Is there shift work?**
Yes. Hospitals require 24/7 coverage. Night and weekend differentials typically $2-4/hour.

**5. What about travel rad tech positions?**
Travel contracts pay $1,800-2,800/week for 13-week assignments. CT/MRI travelers earn most.

**6. Can I work at the VA?**
Yes. VA hires radiology technicians with federal benefits and veteran-friendly environment.

**7. Is radiation exposure a concern?**
Proper protocols and monitoring ensure safe exposure levels. ALARA principle guides practice.

**8. What's career advancement?**
Staff tech → Lead tech → Supervisor → Manager → Director. Or clinical specialist in modality.

**9. Is cross-training common?**
Yes. Many techs are cross-trained in X-ray plus CT or other modality, increasing employability.

**10. What about imaging informatics?**
Growing field. PACS administrators and imaging informatics specialists earn $80,000-110,000.
