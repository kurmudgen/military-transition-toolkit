# Best Cities for Veteran Truck Drivers (CDL): 2025 Guide

Military veterans with driving experience—from tactical vehicles to heavy transports—find natural alignment with commercial truck driving. Your experience operating large vehicles, maintaining safety standards, and working long hours translates directly to the trucking industry. With a nationwide driver shortage and strong veteran hiring programs, CDL drivers are in exceptional demand.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Top 3 Cities:**
1. Dallas-Fort Worth, TX - $72,000 avg salary - Major hub, no state tax
2. Houston, TX - $70,000 avg salary - Port city, energy sector
3. Indianapolis, IN - $65,000 avg salary - Central location, distribution hub

**Key Factors:**
- Salary range: $48,000 - $95,000+ depending on type and experience
- Job growth: 4% projected through 2032 (average, but high turnover = opportunity)
- Requirements: CDL-A license; military CDLs often transfer
- Veteran advantage: Military driving experience highly valued; expedited CDL programs

## Military CDL Conversion

**Military CDL Skills Waiver:**
Veterans with military driving experience may waive skills test for CDL:
- Must have operated military CMV within 1 year
- Need DD-214 showing driving experience
- Must pass knowledge test and medical
- Available in all 50 states

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Does military driving experience transfer?**
Yes. Military CDL skills waiver allows expedited CDL in all states for veterans with CMV experience.

**2. How long is CDL school?**
Typically 3-6 weeks. Many company-sponsored programs are free with work commitment.

**3. Which endorsements add value?**
Tanker (N), Hazmat (H), and Doubles/Triples (T) add $0.03-0.10 per mile.

**4. What's the realistic first-year income?**
$45,000-60,000 for OTR with major carrier. Increases significantly with experience.

**5. Is home time realistic?**
Yes. Local = daily. Regional = weekly. Dedicated routes offer predictable schedules.

**6. What about owner-operator?**
Higher income potential but significant expense. Recommend 2-3 years company experience first.

**7. How physical is the job?**
Varies. Van/reefer = minimal. Flatbed = significant (tarping, strapping). LTL = dock work.

**8. What about electronic logs?**
ELD mandate requires electronic logging. 11-hour drive limit, 14-hour on-duty, 10-hour rest.

**9. Is automated trucking a concern?**
Long-term consideration. Likely hybrid roles before full automation. Local/last-mile safer.

**10. What benefits do carriers offer?**
Health insurance, 401k, paid vacation/holidays. Major carriers offer competitive packages.
