# Best Cities for Veteran Welders: 2025 Guide

Military welders have fused metal in combat zones, aboard ships, and in demanding field conditions that civilian welders rarely encounter. Your experience with structural repair, pipe welding, and fabrication under pressure translates directly to high-paying civilian welding careers. With manufacturing reshoring, infrastructure investment, and skilled welder shortages, veterans with welding certifications command premium wages.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Top 3 Cities:**
1. Houston, TX - $72,000 avg salary - Petrochemical/pipeline premium, no state tax
2. New Orleans, LA - $68,000 avg salary - Offshore/shipyard demand
3. Tulsa, OK - $58,000 avg salary - Best purchasing power, pipeline hub

**Key Factors:**
- Salary range: $40,000 - $120,000+ depending on specialization and certifications
- Job growth: 4% projected through 2032 (average)
- Certification: AWS certifications significantly impact pay
- Veteran advantage: Military welding training and discipline highly valued

## AWS Certifications and Pay Impact

AWS (American Welding Society) certifications significantly impact earning potential:

**Certification Pay Impact:**
| Certification | Pay Premium | Common Industries |
|--------------|------------|-------------------|
| 3G Plate | +$2-4/hr | Structural, general |
| 4G Plate | +$3-5/hr | Structural, heavy |
| 6G Pipe | +$5-10/hr | Pipeline, refinery |
| Combo Welder | +$8-15/hr | Industrial, specialty |
| CWI (Inspector) | +$15-25/hr | Quality/inspection |

**Military Certification Recognition:**
Most AWS testing facilities recognize military welding certifications. Many veterans can test directly for civilian certifications based on military training records.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What military MOSs translate to welding?**
91E (Allied Trades Specialist), HT (Hull Technician - Navy), 3E3X1 (Structural - Air Force), and combat engineer welding specialties.

**2. Does military welding certification transfer?**
Often yes. AWS testing facilities can evaluate military records for direct certification or accelerated testing.

**3. What certifications pay the most?**
6G pipe welding certifications command the highest premiums, followed by combination certifications.

**4. What's the earning potential?**
$40,000-70,000 typical. Specialized pipe/turnaround welders can earn $100,000-150,000+.

**5. Is welding physically demanding?**
Yes. Involves heavy materials, awkward positions, heat exposure, and PPE requirements.

**6. What specializations exist?**
Pipe, structural, TIG, MIG, stick, underwater, aerospace, and specialized alloys.

**7. Can I start my own welding business?**
Yes, with proper certifications and business licensing. Mobile welding is popular.

**8. What's travel welding?**
Working temporary assignments at industrial sites nationwide. Higher pay but away from home.

**9. How dangerous is welding?**
Requires constant safety awareness (fumes, burns, UV). Military safety training is valuable.

**10. What's the job outlook?**
Stable demand with skilled welder shortage. Infrastructure investment increasing opportunities.
