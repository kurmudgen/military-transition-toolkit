# Best Cities for Veteran Federal Law Enforcement: 2025 Guide

Military veterans have protected our nation abroad, and many continue that mission as federal law enforcement officers protecting the homeland. Your military training in investigations, security, leadership, and working under pressure translates exceptionally well to federal agencies. With mandatory veteran hiring preferences for federal positions, veterans have a significant advantage in federal law enforcement careers.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Top 3 Cities:**
1. Washington, DC - $115,000 avg salary - Headquarters, most opportunities
2. San Diego, CA - $110,000 avg salary - Border security, CBP focus
3. New York City, NY - $105,000 avg salary - Major field offices, diverse agencies

**Key Factors:**
- Salary range: $55,000 - $160,000 depending on agency, location, and grade
- Hiring: Veteran preference is law for federal positions
- Agencies: FBI, DEA, ATF, CBP, Secret Service, HSI, USMS, and more
- Veteran advantage: Veterans' preference points; military experience highly valued

## Veteran Hiring Preference

**Veterans' Preference is Law:**
- 5-point preference for honorable discharge
- 10-point preference for disabled veterans
- 30% or more disabled: direct hire authority
- VRA (Veterans Recruitment Appointment): direct hire for eligible veterans

**Military Experience Value:**
| Military Background | Federal Value |
|--------------------|---------------|
| Military Police/Security Forces | Direct translation |
| Intelligence (35 series, CTT) | FBI, DEA, HSI |
| Infantry/Special Operations | HSI, USMS, tactical units |
| Criminal Investigation (CID, NCIS, OSI) | FBI, DCIS, any agency |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How does veteran preference work?**
Veterans receive 5 or 10 points added to passing scores. Disabled veterans (30%+) can be directly hired through special authority.

**2. What education is required?**
Most 1811 (Criminal Investigator) positions require bachelor's degree. Some accept equivalent experience.

**3. Which agency is easiest to enter?**
CBP and Border Patrol typically have most positions. HSI and USMS also hire frequently.

**4. Is there an age limit?**
Most 1811 positions require hiring before age 37. Veterans may receive age waivers equal to qualifying military service.

**5. What's the training like?**
Federal Law Enforcement Training Centers (FLETC) in Georgia handles most agency basic training. FBI and DEA have their own academies.

**6. How competitive is federal law enforcement?**
Very competitive, but veteran preference provides significant advantage.

**7. Can I choose my location?**
Generally limited. Many agencies require mobility. Popular locations are highly competitive.

**8. What about federal retirement?**
Federal law enforcement officers qualify for enhanced retirement (FERS LEO) at age 50 with 20 years or any age with 25 years.

**9. Is military time creditable?**
Yes. You can buy back military time toward federal retirement.

**10. Do security clearances transfer?**
Yes. Existing clearances facilitate federal hiring process significantly.
