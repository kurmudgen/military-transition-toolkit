# Best Cities for Veteran Construction Workers: 2025 Guide

Military construction personnel—combat engineers, Seabees, and RED HORSE squadrons—have built infrastructure in the most challenging environments on Earth. Your experience with rapid construction, heavy equipment, and working under pressure translates directly to civilian construction careers. With infrastructure investment increasing and skilled labor shortages acute, veterans with construction backgrounds are highly sought after.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Top 3 Cities:**
1. Houston, TX - $52,000 avg salary - Constant growth, no state income tax
2. Dallas-Fort Worth, TX - $50,000 avg salary - Massive market, tax advantages
3. Phoenix, AZ - $48,000 avg salary - Year-round work, no military retirement tax

**Key Factors:**
- Salary range: $35,000 - $85,000 depending on specialty and experience
- Job growth: 5% projected through 2032 (average)
- Licensing: Varies by specialty (some require state licenses)
- Veteran advantage: Military construction training and work ethic highly valued

## Methodology

**How We Ranked Cities:**
- Average salary for construction workers (40% weight)
- Number of job openings (25% weight)
- Cost of living index (20% weight)
- Veteran population & resources (15% weight)

Data sources include Bureau of Labor Statistics, construction industry surveys, and regional data from 2024-2025.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What military MOSs translate to construction?**
12B (Combat Engineer), 12N (Horizontal Construction), EO/BU (Seabees), 3E2X1 (Pavement/Construction - Air Force), and related specialties.

**2. Do I need certifications?**
OSHA 10/30 are standard. Specialty certifications (crane, forklift) add value. Some roles require specific licenses.

**3. What's the earning potential?**
$35,000-52,000 typical. Foremen earn $60,000-80,000. Superintendents earn $80,000-120,000+.

**4. Is construction seasonal?**
Varies by location. Southern/Western cities have year-round work. Northern cities see winter slowdowns.

**5. What safety training helps?**
OSHA 10/30, First Aid/CPR, and equipment-specific certifications are valuable.

**6. Can I advance to management?**
Yes. Foreman → Superintendent → Project Manager is common. Military leadership helps.

**7. Union vs non-union?**
Unions offer higher wages, benefits, and training. Non-union offers more flexibility. Regional preferences vary.

**8. What physical requirements exist?**
Must lift 50+ lbs, work on feet all day, handle heights and weather conditions.

**9. How does military construction experience transfer?**
Directly. Employers value military construction discipline, safety focus, and ability to work under pressure.

**10. What equipment experience helps?**
Heavy equipment operation, carpentry tools, surveying equipment, and power tools all transfer.
