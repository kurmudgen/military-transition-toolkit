# Best Cities for Veteran Firefighters: 2025 Guide

Military veterans have demonstrated courage, physical fitness, and the ability to perform under extreme pressure—all qualities essential to firefighting. Whether you served in combat, aviation rescue, or military fire protection, your training and discipline translate directly to civilian fire service. With firefighter retirements increasing nationwide, departments actively recruit veterans who embody the service-before-self mentality.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Does military experience help with fire hiring?**
Significantly. Physical fitness, discipline, and teamwork are valued. ARFF experience translates directly.

**2. Do I need EMT certification?**
Most departments require EMT-B minimum. Many require paramedic or will train to paramedic.

**3. What's the testing process?**
Written exam, physical ability test (CPAT), interview, background check, medical exam, academy.

**4. Is firefighting competitive?**
Very. Popular departments may have 1,000+ applicants for 40 positions. Test preparation is essential.

**5. What's the typical schedule?**
24-hour shifts are common (24 on, 48 off). Some departments use 10/14 or other schedules.

**6. How is firefighter retirement?**
Most departments offer defined benefit pensions after 20-25 years, often 50-80% of final salary.

**7. What specialty assignments exist?**
Truck, rescue, hazmat, wildland, arson investigation, training, EMS—varies by department.

**8. Is there overtime?**
Yes. Overtime and off-duty employment can add 20-50% to base salary.

**9. How physically demanding is the job?**
Very. Maintaining fitness throughout career is essential. Many injuries occur over time.

**10. Can I be a firefighter with military disability rating?**
Depends on disability type and department requirements. Many veterans serve with service-connected ratings.
