# Best Cities for Veteran Respiratory Therapists: 2025 Guide

Military respiratory therapists and cardiopulmonary technicians have managed airway emergencies, ventilators, and critical care in the most demanding environments. Your experience with life-saving interventions and working under pressure translates directly to civilian respiratory care. Post-pandemic, respiratory therapists remain in critical demand across hospitals and healthcare systems.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Does military respiratory experience transfer?**
Yes. Military training provides strong clinical foundation. Additional civilian education/credentialing may be required for RRT.

**2. What's the job market like?**
Excellent. 13% growth projected. Post-pandemic demand for respiratory care remains strong.

**3. Is the RRT credential required?**
Most employers require RRT. CRT (Certified Respiratory Therapist) is entry-level but limited opportunities.

**4. What specialization pays best?**
ECMO specialist, neonatal/pediatric ICU, and flight/transport RT typically command highest premiums.

**5. Is there shift work?**
Yes. Hospitals require 24/7 coverage. Night and weekend differentials typically $2-5/hour additional.

**6. What about travel RT positions?**
Travel RT contracts pay $1,800-2,500/week for 13-week assignments. Good for exploring locations.

**7. Can I work in the VA?**
Yes. VA hires respiratory therapists with federal benefits and veteran-friendly environment.

**8. What's career advancement?**
Staff RT → Senior RT → Lead RT → Supervisor → Director. Or clinical specialist path.

**9. Is the work physically demanding?**
Moderate. Patient lifting/positioning, long shifts, and emergency responses require stamina.

**10. How is work-life balance?**
Shift work offers days off during week. However, nights/weekends/holidays are required in most positions.
