# Best Cities for Veteran Postal Workers: 2025 Guide

Military veterans find excellent opportunities with the United States Postal Service, which offers strong veteran hiring preferences, union benefits, and stable federal employment. Your military experience with logistics, attention to detail, and working in all conditions translates well to postal work. With USPS hiring nationwide and mandatory veteran preference, this is one of the most accessible federal career paths for veterans.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Top 3 Cities:**
1. San Francisco, CA - $68,000 avg salary - Highest locality pay
2. New York City, NY - $65,000 avg salary - Large system, union strong
3. San Jose, CA - $67,000 avg salary - High locality pay, good benefits

**Key Factors:**
- Salary range: $42,000 - $75,000 depending on position and locality
- Job availability: USPS hires continuously nationwide
- Requirements: Pass exam (or exempt), background check, physical requirements
- Veteran advantage: Veterans' preference REQUIRED for USPS hiring; direct hire for some

## Veteran Hiring Preference

**USPS Must Give Veterans Preference:**
- 5-point preference (honorable discharge)
- 10-point preference (disabled veterans)
- Direct hire authority (some positions)
- No exam required for many veterans

**Special Hiring Programs:**
- Veterans' preference points

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Do veterans get hiring preference?**
Yes, mandatory. USPS must give veterans preference. Disabled veterans (30%+) can be directly hired.

**2. Is there an entrance exam?**
Some positions require exam 474/475/476/477. Many veterans are exempt or get additional points.

**3. What's the career path?**
Non-career (CCA, RCA, PSE) → Career (after ~2 years) → Supervisory/Management

**4. Are benefits good?**
Yes. Career employees get full federal benefits including health insurance, retirement (FERS), and paid leave.

**5. Is there overtime?**
Yes, substantial especially during holidays. Many carriers earn 20-40% above base.

**6. Is the work physical?**
Yes. Carriers walk routes, lift packages, work in all weather. Good fitness required.

**7. What's the conversion timeline?**
Non-career to career typically 1-2 years, depending on location and hiring.

**8. Can I transfer between cities?**
Yes, career employees can request transfers. Competitive based on seniority.

**9. What about federal retirement?**
FERS (Federal Employees Retirement System) with defined benefit pension plus TSP matching.

**10. Is job security good?**
Excellent. USPS is essential service with strong union protection.
