# Best Cities for Veteran Aircraft Mechanics: 2025 Guide

Military aircraft maintainers have kept the world's most sophisticated aircraft mission-ready in demanding conditions. Whether you turned wrenches on fighters, helicopters, or transports, your experience translates directly to civilian aviation maintenance. With an aging mechanic workforce and growing aviation demand, A&P mechanics are in exceptional demand, and military experience provides a direct pathway to FAA certification.

## Military to FAA A&P Certification

**FAA AFTP (Air Force Training Program) & Similar:**
Military maintainers can use experience toward A&P:
- 18-30 months military experience qualifies
- Must pass FAA written, oral, and practical exams
- Community colleges offer military-to-A&P programs

**Military-Friendly A&P Programs:**
- Spartan College (Tulsa)
- Aviation Institute of Maintenance
- Community college programs
- GI Bill approved

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Does military aircraft maintenance experience count?**
Yes. Military experience (crew chief, maintainer) counts toward FAA A&P requirements. Must pass FAA exams.

**2. How long to get A&P certification?**
With military experience: prepare for exams (1-3 months), test, receive certificate. Traditional school: 18-24 months.

**3. What's the difference between A&P and IA?**
A&P (Airframe & Powerplant) is standard mechanic certificate. IA (Inspection Authorization) allows return-to-service authority.

**4. Is there a mechanic shortage?**
Yes, significant. Average mechanic age is 50+. Industry actively recruiting military maintainers.

**5. Airlines vs. MRO vs. corporateâ€”which is better?**
Airlines: benefits, stability, seniority. MRO: variety of aircraft. Corporate: better schedules, diverse work.

**6. What about avionics?**
Avionics technicians specialize in electrical/electronic systems. Good military MOS translation. Often higher pay.

**7. Is the work physical?**
Yes. Standing, climbing, tight spaces, weather exposure. Fitness important throughout career.

**8. What about shifts?**
Airlines and MROs often require nights/weekends. Corporate aviation may have more regular hours.

**9. Can I specialize?**
Yes. Engines, avionics, composites, specific aircraft types. Specialization can increase pay.

**10. What about manufacturing vs. maintenance?**
Manufacturing (Boeing, Textron) is production-focused. Maintenance is repair-focused. Both value military experience.
