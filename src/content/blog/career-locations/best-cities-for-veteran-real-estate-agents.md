## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Is real estate good for veterans?**
Excellent fit. Discipline, integrity, and people skills translate well. Military network provides client base.

**2. How long to become successful?**
Typically 6-18 months to consistent income. First year is challenging. Persistence essential.

**3. What does it cost to start?**
$1,500-3,500 for licensing, plus ongoing costs (MLS fees, marketing, etc.). GI Bill doesn't cover licensing.

**4. Should I specialize in military clients?**
Yes. Your experience is unique advantage. VA loan expertise is valuable specialization.

**5. What brokerage should I join?**
Consider training/support (important for new agents), commission splits, and military-friendly culture.

**6. Is income guaranteed?**
No. 100% commission-based. No sales = no income. Budget for 6+ months startup.

**7. Can I do this part-time?**
Yes, but harder to build momentum. Part-time agents average $20,000-40,000. Full-time required for serious income.

**8. What about the market downturn risk?**
Real estate is cyclical. Diversification (buyers, sellers, rentals) helps weather downturns.

**9. How do I get clients?**
Military network, sphere of influence, online presence, open houses, referrals. Building takes time.

**10. What about property management?**
Additional opportunity. Many military families become landlords. Property management adds income stream.
