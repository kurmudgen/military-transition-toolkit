## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Do I need a degree to teach?**
Most states require bachelor's degree. Alternative certification programs allow career changers to teach while completing requirements.

**2. Does military experience count toward certification?**
Yes, in many programs. Troops to Teachers and state alternative routes recognize military training and leadership.

**3. Can I teach without traditional certification?**
Some states allow provisional or emergency certification. Private schools may have different requirements.

**4. What subjects are easiest to enter?**
JROTC (direct military connection), math, science, and special education have highest demand and most pathways.

**5. Is there veteran preference in school hiring?**
Some districts have veteran preference. JROTC positions specifically prefer or require military experience.

**6. How is teacher retirement?**
Most states offer defined benefit pensions. Some allow military time purchase toward teacher retirement.

**7. What about JROTC positions?**
Requires retired military status (typically E-7+ or O-3+). Each service manages JROTC instructor certification.

**8. How is work-life balance?**
Generally good. Summers off, holidays, but significant evening/weekend work for grading and preparation.

**9. Can I teach overseas?**
Yes. DoDEA (Department of Defense schools) hires teachers for overseas military bases.

**10. What support exists for veteran teachers?**
Troops to Teachers, veteran teacher associations, and many districts have veteran support programs.
