## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Does military behavioral health experience count?**
Yes. Military experience strengthens applications and provides clinical foundation. Master's degree and licensure still required.

**2. What's the best license to pursue?**
LPC/LMHC for counseling, LCSW for broader scope. Consider state portability and your career goals.

**3. How long is the licensure process?**
Master's degree (2-3 years) + supervised hours (2-4 years) + exam. Total 4-7 years from start.

**4. Is there demand for veteran-focused counselors?**
Absolutely. VA, Vet Centers, and private practices actively recruit counselors with military understanding.

**5. Can I work while getting licensed?**
Yes. Associate-level positions allow supervised practice while accumulating hours toward licensure.

**6. What about private practice?**
Viable after licensure. Requires business skills, referral network, and typically 3-5 years experience.

**7. Is telehealth growing?**
Rapidly. Post-pandemic telehealth expanded significantly. Some platforms hire nationally-licensed counselors.

**8. How is work-life balance?**
Varies by setting. Private practice offers flexibility. Agency work may have high caseloads. Self-care is critical.

**9. What populations can I serve?**
Generalist or specialized: veterans, trauma, substance abuse, children/adolescents, couples/families, LGBTQ+, etc.

**10. Is burnout a concern?**
Yes. Mental health counseling has high burnout rates. Supervision, self-care, and reasonable caseloads essential.
