# Best Cities for Veteran HVAC Technicians: 2025 Guide

Military HVAC technicians, mechanical specialists, and utilities personnel have maintained heating and cooling systems in extreme environments worldwide. Your experience with critical climate control systems, refrigeration, and working under demanding conditions translates directly to civilian HVAC work. With climate extremes increasing and buildings becoming more sophisticated, skilled HVAC technicians are essential—and in short supply.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Top 3 Cities:**
1. Phoenix, AZ - $58,000 avg salary - Year-round AC demand, no military retirement tax
2. Houston, TX - $56,000 avg salary - Best purchasing power, constant demand
3. Las Vegas, NV - $62,000 avg salary - No state income tax, extreme climate needs

**Key Factors:**
- Salary range: $38,000 - $95,000 depending on specialization and certifications
- Job growth: 6% projected through 2032 (average)
- Licensing: EPA 608 certification required; state licenses vary
- Veteran advantage: Military HVAC training and refrigerant handling experience valued

## Methodology

**How We Ranked Cities:**
- Average salary for HVAC technicians (40% weight)
- Number of job openings (25% weight)

Data sources include Bureau of Labor Statistics, HVAC industry surveys, and regional data from 2024-2025.

## Licensing Requirements

HVAC licensing varies by state, but EPA 608 certification is universally required:

**EPA 608 Certification Types:**
- Type I: Small appliances
- Type II: High-pressure systems
- Type III: Low-pressure systems
- Universal: All types (recommended)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What military MOSs translate to HVAC?**
91C (Utilities Equipment Repairer), 3E1X1 (HVAC - Air Force), UT (Utilitiesman - Navy), and related mechanical specialties.

**2. What certifications do I need?**
EPA 608 Universal is essential. NATE certification adds value. State licenses vary.

**3. How long to become fully certified?**
2-4 years depending on path (apprenticeship, trade school, military experience credit).

**4. What's the earning potential?**
$38,000-65,000 typical, with senior/commercial techs earning $75,000-100,000+.

**5. Is HVAC work seasonal?**
Depends on location. Hot climates have year-round AC demand; temperate areas have seasonal peaks.

**6. What specializations pay best?**
Commercial refrigeration, building automation, and industrial HVAC typically pay premiums.

**7. Can I start my own HVAC business?**
Yes, with proper licensing and contractor credentials. Many veterans run successful HVAC companies.

**8. Is HVAC physically demanding?**
Yes. Involves lifting, working in tight spaces, attics, and rooftops in extreme temperatures.

**9. What tools do I need?**
Basic tools ($500-1,000) initially. Gauges, meters, and specialty tools often provided or added over time.

**10. How does heat affect outdoor work?**
Summer work in hot climates is challenging. Rooftop commercial work can be dangerous without precautions.
