# Best Cities for Veteran Linemen: 2025 Guide

Military electrical specialists and power generation personnel understand working with high-voltage systems in challenging conditions. Linework—building and maintaining electrical transmission and distribution lines—is one of the highest-paying skilled trades, and veterans with electrical backgrounds are well-positioned for this demanding career. With infrastructure aging and grid modernization ongoing, skilled linemen are in critical demand.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Top 3 Cities:**
1. Houston, TX - $88,000 avg salary - Utility demand, no state tax, storm work
2. Dallas-Fort Worth, TX - $82,000 avg salary - Growing grid, tax advantages
3. Phoenix, AZ - $75,000 avg salary - Grid expansion, year-round work

**Key Factors:**
- Salary range: $55,000 - $130,000+ (overtime, storm work can double base)
- Job growth: 9% projected through 2032 (faster than average)
- Certification: CDL, journeyman lineman certification required
- Veteran advantage: Military electrical training and discipline valued

## Storm Work Economics

Storm restoration dramatically increases lineman earnings:

| Work Type | Typical Rate | Hours | Annual Impact |
|-----------|-------------|-------|---------------|
| Base utility | $35-45/hr | 40/wk | $72,000-93,000 |
| Storm restoration | $50-75/hr | 80+/wk | +$20,000-50,000 |
| Travel lineman | $40-60/hr | 60/wk | $100,000-150,000 |

**Storm Work Reality:**
- Away from home for weeks
- 16-hour days, 7 days/week
- Dangerous conditions
- Premium pay (double time, per diem)
- Can double annual income

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What military MOSs translate to linework?**
12R (Interior Electrician), 12P (Power Generation), 3E0X1 (Electrical Systems), CE (Construction Electrician - Navy)

**2. Is linework dangerous?**
Yes. Working with high voltage at heights requires constant safety focus. Military safety discipline is valuable.

**3. What's the starting salary?**
Apprentices start $40,000-55,000. Journeyman wage is $65,000-95,000 base.

**4. How much can I really earn?**
Journeyman base is $70,000-90,000. With storm work and overtime, $120,000-180,000 is achievable.

**5. Do I need to travel for work?**
Depends on employer. Utility employees stay local. Contract/traveling linemen follow work.

**6. How long is the apprenticeship?**
3-4 years typically. Military electrical experience may reduce this.

**7. Is linework physically demanding?**
Extremely. Climbing poles, working at heights, lifting heavy equipment in all weather.

**8. What about unions?**
IBEW represents many linemen. Union offers better wages, benefits, training.

**9. Can I work my way to management?**
Yes. Foreman → Supervisor → Manager is common progression.

**10. What's the job outlook?**
Excellent. Grid modernization, renewable integration, and retirements create strong demand.
