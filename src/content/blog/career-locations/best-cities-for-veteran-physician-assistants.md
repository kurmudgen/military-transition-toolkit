# Best Cities for Veteran Physician Assistants: 2025 Guide

Military physician assistants have provided comprehensive medical care in combat zones, field hospitals, and military treatment facilities worldwide. Your experience with autonomous decision-making, trauma care, and working in resource-limited environments makes you exceptionally valuable in civilian healthcare. With PA scope of practice expanding and provider shortages nationwide, PAs are in extremely high demand.

## Military to PA Pathway

| Military Background | PA Path | Notes |
|--------------------|---------|-------|
| 65D (Physician Assistant) | Direct transition | Already PA, state license transfer |
| 68W (Combat Medic) | IPAP or civilian PA school | GI Bill, strong candidate |
| HM (Navy Corpsman) | IPAP or civilian PA school | GI Bill, strong candidate |
| 68C (LPN) | PA school | Strong healthcare foundation |
| Flight Medic | PA school | Critical care experience valued |

**Interservice Physician Assistant Program (IPAP):**
- Army program producing PAs
- 29-month program
- Service obligation upon completion
- Civilian transition after service

## Licensing Considerations

**Requirements:**
- Master's degree from ARC-PA accredited program
- PANCE (Physician Assistant National Certifying Exam)
- State license
- Continuing education and recertification every 10 years

**Military PA License:**
Military PAs have full PA licensure. State license transfer is straightforward.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Do military PAs transition easily?**
Yes. Military PAs (65D) have full credentials and translate directly. State licensure required.

**2. Can combat medics become PAs?**
Yes. 68W experience is highly valued in PA school admissions. IPAP or civilian PA school with GI Bill.

**3. What's the job market like?**
Excellent. 28% growth projected. PAs are in critical demand across all specialties.

**4. Which specialty pays best?**
Cardiothoracic surgery, dermatology, and orthopedics typically command highest salaries.

**5. Is rural practice in demand?**
Extremely. Rural areas offer signing bonuses ($20,000-50,000) and loan repayment programs.

**6. What about VA employment?**
VA actively recruits PAs. Federal benefits, veteran patient population, and competitive salary.

**7. Is private practice viable?**
Some states allow independent PA practice. Most work collaboratively with physicians.

**8. How is work-life balance?**
Varies by specialty. Primary care typically M-F. ER/surgery may have nights/weekends/call.

**9. Can I teach?**
Yes. Experienced PAs can become clinical preceptors or PA program faculty.

**10. What about locum tenens?**
Locum (temporary) PA positions pay $100-150/hour. Good for exploring locations or supplementing income.
