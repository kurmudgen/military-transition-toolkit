# Best Companies for 19D Cavalry Scouts

## BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

19D Cavalry Scouts transition to premium positions in law enforcement, federal investigations, corporate security, and intelligence agencies. Compensation ranges $50,000-$140,000+ depending on agency and role. Top employers include FBI, DEA, Secret Service, U.S. Marshals, corporate security (Google, Microsoft, Facebook), and private investigation firms. Your military reconnaissance, tactical operations, and decision-making under pressure expertise directly translates to high-value investigative and protective roles. Most 19Ds transition to $65,000-$95,000 law enforcement roles within 3-6 months with clear advancement to supervisor and management earning $120,000-$200,000+.

## Federal Law Enforcement (Highest Pay and Prestige)

### FBI (Special Agent)
- **Positions**: Special Agent, Intelligence Analyst, Investigator
- **Starting**: $70,000-$95,000 (GS-10)

- **Advantage**: Military background strongly preferred

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What's the highest-paying path for 19D scouts?**
A: Corporate security at major tech companies (Google, Meta, Microsoft) offers fastest path to $200,000-$300,000+. Federal positions reach $150,000-$200,000 at senior levels.

**Q: Should I choose federal law enforcement or corporate security?**
A: **Federal**: Prestige, mission impact, federal benefits. **Corporate**: Higher pay, faster advancement, better work-life. Choose based on mission vs. compensation priority.

**Q: How quickly can I advance to supervisor?**
A: Federal: 5-10 years. Corporate: 3-6 years. Corporate tech companies fastest.

**Q: What's the job market like?**
A: Strong demand. Law enforcement hiring shortages; Tech security roles growing rapidly. Your military background highly valued.

**Q: Can I get hired directly as federal agent without police experience?**
A: Yes. Military intelligence/reconnaissance directly translates. FBI and federal agencies specifically recruit military scouts and investigators.

**Q: Are there remote opportunities?**
A: Limited in field roles. Investigative and analytical roles increasingly hybrid. Corporate security offers more remote options.
