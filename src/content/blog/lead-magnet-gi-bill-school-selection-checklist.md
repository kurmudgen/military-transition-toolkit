---# GI Bill School Selection Checklist
## The Complete Guide to Choosing the Right School and Maximizing Your Education Benefits
**By Jacob, Navy E6 | Formation Labs**

## THE $100,000 DECISION

Choosing the wrong school can cost you $50,000-$100,000+ in wasted GI Bill benefits. This checklist ensures you select a school that provides real value, not just slick marketing.

## GI BILL COMPARISON TOOL

**Start here:** www.va.gov/gi-bill-comparison-tool

**What it shows:**
✓ Tuition costs covered by GI Bill
✓ Housing allowance (BAH rate for school location)
✓ Graduation rates
✓ Student debt levels
✓ Number of GI Bill students enrolled
✓ School complaints and warnings

## THE 7-STEP SCHOOL SELECTION PROCESS

### STEP 1: DEFINE YOUR GOALS

□ Career objective (what job do you want?)
□ Degree level needed (associate's, bachelor's, master's)
□ Timeline (how fast do you need to finish?)
□ Location preference (on-campus vs. online)
□ Budget (will GI Bill cover everything?)

### STEP 2: RESEARCH SCHOOL TYPES

**COMMUNITY COLLEGE (2 years → Associate's)**
**Pros:** Low cost, transferable credits, open admission
**Cons:** No bachelor's degree, may need to transfer
**Best for:** Saving GI Bill months, getting gen-eds done cheap
**GI Bill Strategy:** Use community college + transfer to 4-year (save 12-18 months of benefits)

**PUBLIC 4-YEAR UNIVERSITY (Bachelor's)**
**Pros:** GI Bill covers 100% tuition (in-state rate), respected degrees
**Cons:** Competitive admission, may need to relocate
**Best for:** Traditional college experience, established reputation
**Top Picks:** State flagship universities (UT Austin, UF, Ohio State, etc.)

**PRIVATE UNIVERSITY (Bachelor's/Master's)**
**Pros:** Smaller classes, more support, GI Bill + Yellow Ribbon can cover full cost
**Cons:** Expensive if school doesn't offer Yellow Ribbon, depletes benefits faster
**Best for:** Top-tier schools offering Yellow Ribbon (Stanford, Northwestern, etc.)
**Check:** Yellow Ribbon participation before applying

**ONLINE UNIVERSITY (Any level)**
**Pros:** Flexibility, work while studying, no relocation
**Cons:** Less networking, requires self-discipline, some employer skepticism
**Best for:** Working veterans, those with family obligations
**Top Picks:** Arizona State Online, Penn State World Campus, University of Maryland Global Campus

**FOR-PROFIT SCHOOLS (AVOID MOST)**
**Pros:** Easy admission, aggressive recruiting
**Cons:** Poor outcomes, high debt, low graduation rates, employer skepticism
**Red Flags:** University of Phoenix, DeVry, Ashford University (check latest accreditation)
**Exception:** Some career-focused programs (e.g., coding bootcamps) can be valuable

### STEP 3: VERIFY ACCREDITATION

□ **Regional accreditation** (best - credits transfer easily)
□ **National accreditation** (acceptable but credits may not transfer)
□ **Programmatic accreditation** (specific programs like engineering, nursing)

**Check:** www.chea.org or www.ed.gov

❌ **AVOID:** Schools without proper accreditation

### STEP 4: CALCULATE TOTAL COSTS

**Tuition:** Will GI Bill cover it?
- Public in-state: Usually 100%
- Public out-of-state: May need Yellow Ribbon
- Private: Definitely need Yellow Ribbon

**Housing Allowance (Post-9/11 GI Bill):**
- E-5 with dependents BAH rate for school zip code
- Online-only: $1,072.50/month (national rate)
- Check rate at GI Bill comparison tool

**Books/Supplies:**
- Up to $1,000/year from GI Bill

**Other Costs:**
- Parking, technology fees, health insurance usually NOT covered

### STEP 5: CHECK VETERAN SUPPORT SERVICES

□ Dedicated veterans office?
□ VA certifying official on staff?
□ Student veteran organization?
□ Veterans-only orientation?
□ Transition support services?
□ Percentage of students using GI Bill? (Higher = better support)

**Strong Veteran Programs:**
- Schools near military bases
- Schools with 500+ GI Bill students
- Schools in "Best for Vets" rankings

### STEP 6: EVALUATE OUTCOMES

**Graduation Rate:** Above 50% is acceptable, 70%+ is good
**Employment Rate:** 80%+ within 6 months of graduation
**Average Salary:** Research PayScale.com for degree programs
**Student Debt:** Lower is better (GI Bill students should have minimal debt)

**Red Flags:**
❌ Graduation rate below 30%
❌ High student loan default rates
❌ Low employment rates
❌ VA investigations or lawsuits

### STEP 7: VISIT OR ATTEND INFO SESSION

□ Campus visit (if nearby)
□ Virtual tour/info session
□ Talk to current veteran students
□ Meet with veterans office
□ Sit in on a class (if allowed)

## YELLOW RIBBON PROGRAM EXPLAINED

**What it is:** Schools contribute funds to cover costs beyond GI Bill maximums

**Who needs it:**
- Private school students (high tuition)
- Out-of-state students at public universities
- Graduate students at expensive programs

**How it works:**
- School contributes $X
- VA matches $X
- Together they cover gap between GI Bill and actual cost

**Important:** Each school sets own Yellow Ribbon amount and number of slots
- Some schools: Unlimited slots, unlimited amounts
- Other schools: 10 slots, $5,000 maximum
- Check BEFORE applying

**Find Yellow Ribbon Schools:** www.va.gov/education/yellow-ribbon-participating-schools

## GI BILL HOUSING ALLOWANCE (BAH) BY LOCATION

**High BAH areas (use to maximize benefits):**
- San Francisco, CA: ~$4,200/month
- Los Angeles, CA: ~$3,400/month
- New York City, NY: ~$4,100/month
- Washington, DC: ~$3,200/month
- San Diego, CA: ~$3,600/month

**Lower BAH areas:**
- Rural colleges: $1,000-$1,500/month
- Small cities: $1,200-$1,800/month
- Online only: $1,072.50/month

**Strategy:** If doing online degree, enroll in school with high BAH zip code but take online classes (get higher housing allowance)
**Note:** Must take at least one on-campus class per term to get location BAH

## DEGREE PROGRAM SELECTION BY CAREER

**HIGH ROI DEGREES (Good job market, good pay):**
✓ Computer Science / IT
✓ Nursing / Healthcare
✓ Engineering (all types)
✓ Accounting / Finance
✓ Cybersecurity
✓ Data Science
✓ Business (with concentration in high-demand field)

**MEDIUM ROI DEGREES:**
~ Education
~ Criminal Justice (if pursuing law enforcement)
~ Business Administration (general)
~ Psychology (if pursuing grad school)

**LOW ROI DEGREES (Tough job market):**
⚠️ Liberal Arts (unless pursuing grad school)
⚠️ General Studies
⚠️ Communications (oversaturated)
⚠️ Art/Music (passion field, low pay)

**Choose based on career goals, not just interest.**

## GI BILL MAXIMIZATION STRATEGIES

**Strategy 1: Community College First**
- 2 years community college (cheap, save GI Bill)
- Transfer to 4-year university
- Use GI Bill for last 2-3 years
- **Result:** Save 12-18 months of benefits for grad school

**Strategy 2: High BAH Location**
- Choose school in expensive city
- Live frugally, save extra BAH
- **Example:** $3,500 BAH - $1,200 actual rent = $2,300/month saved

**Strategy 3: Yellow Ribbon for Grad School**
- Use state university for undergrad (fully covered)
- Save GI Bill months
- Use remaining benefits at expensive grad school with Yellow Ribbon
- **Result:** Bachelor's + Master's with one GI Bill

**Strategy 4: Vocational Rehab (Chapter 31) Instead**
- If 20%+ VA rating, may qualify for Voc Rehab
- Voc Rehab pays tuition + provides stipend
- SAVE your GI Bill for later or family
- **Result:** Get degree free AND keep GI Bill for kids/spouse

## RED FLAGS: SCHOOLS TO AVOID

❌ **High-pressure recruiting** (constant calls, promises that sound too good)
❌ **"Start Monday!"** (Legit schools have application processes)
❌ **Guaranteed admission** (No standards = low quality)
❌ **Graduation rate under 30%**
❌ **Under investigation by VA or Dept of Education**
❌ **For-profit with poor outcomes** (check College Scorecard)
❌ **Credits don't transfer** (you'll be stuck there)
❌ **No accreditation or fake accreditation**

**Check before applying:**
- Better Business Bureau complaints
- VA warning letters (search "VA [school name] investigation")
- Student reviews on Reddit, GI Bill subreddit

## SCHOOL SELECTION CHECKLIST

□ School is regionally accredited
□ GI Bill comparison tool shows good outcomes
□ Graduation rate above 50%
□ Employment rate above 70%
□ Tuition fully covered by GI Bill (or Yellow Ribbon available)
□ Housing allowance adequate for area cost of living
□ Strong veterans office and support
□ Degree program aligns with career goals
□ Credits will transfer (if planning to transfer/continue education)
□ No major red flags or investigations
□ Visited campus or attended virtual session
□ Talked to current veteran students
□ Understand total cost and what GI Bill covers
□ Filed FAFSA (may qualify for additional grants)
□ Confirmed VA certifying official responsive

## APPLICATION TIMELINE

**12 MONTHS BEFORE START:**
□ Research schools and programs
□ Verify GI Bill eligibility
□ Request transcripts from previous schools

**6 MONTHS BEFORE:**
□ Apply to 3-5 schools
□ Complete FAFSA
□ Request GI Bill Certificate of Eligibility

**3 MONTHS BEFORE:**
□ Accept admission offer
□ Submit GI Bill paperwork to school
□ Register for classes
□ Arrange housing

**1 MONTH BEFORE:**
□ Confirm GI Bill certification with school
□ Attend veteran orientation
□ Purchase books (will be reimbursed)
□ Finalize schedule
