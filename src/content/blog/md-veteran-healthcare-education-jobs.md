# Maryland Veterans: Healthcare Facilities, Education Benefits & Job Resources

Maryland serves approximately 400,000 veterans - about 8.5% of the state's adult population. With major military installations including Fort Meade, Aberdeen Proving Ground, Andrews Air Force Base, and Naval Air Station Patuxent River, Maryland has one of the highest concentrations of veteran resources in the nation.

This guide provides actual addresses, phone numbers, and actionable information for Maryland veterans.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Healthcare**: 3 VA medical centers (Baltimore, Loch Raven, Perry Point), 5+ community-based clinics, and comprehensive specialty care

**Education**: Edward T. Conroy Scholarship (free tuition for dependents of 100% disabled veterans), strong Yellow Ribbon programs, in-state tuition for GI Bill users

**Employment**: Federal hiring preference, NSA (Fort Meade), Aberdeen Proving Ground (7,500+ civilians), Andrews AFB, defense contractors (Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman), Johns Hopkins

**Veteran Population Hotspots**: Prince George's County (47,000+), Baltimore County (45,000+), Anne Arundel County (42,000+), Montgomery County (40,000+)

- Mental health: 7-14 days for initial consultation

**Best practice**: Enroll in VA healthcare immediately upon separation to establish eligibility and priority group.

### How to Enroll in VA Healthcare

**Online**: [va.gov/health-care/apply](https://www.va.gov/health-care/apply/)
**Phone**: 1-877-222-VETS (8387)
**In person**: Visit any VA medical center or clinic with your DD-214

**Documents needed**:
- DD-214 (discharge paperwork)
- Social Security card
- Insurance information (if you have other coverage)

**Priority groups**: Service-connected disabled veterans get highest priority.

## Education Benefits for Maryland Veterans

### Edward T. Conroy Memorial Scholarship

Maryland's premier education benefit - **free tuition for dependents of 100% disabled veterans**.

**Eligibility**:
- Children and spouses of veterans rated **100% Permanent & Total**
- Children of service members killed in action or died from service-related injuries
- Children/spouses of POWs or MIA service members
- Children of state/local public safety employees killed in line of duty

**Benefit amount**:
- **Full in-state tuition** at University System of Maryland schools
- **Room and board** included
- Maximum 5 years (undergraduate) or 4 years (graduate)

**Total value**: $100,000-$150,000+ over 4-5 years

**Qualifying schools**:
- University of Maryland, College Park (flagship)
- University of Maryland, Baltimore County (UMBC)
- University of Maryland, Baltimore (UMB) - professional schools
- Towson University
- Salisbury University
- Bowie State University
- Coppin State University
- Frostburg State University
- University of Maryland Eastern Shore
- All Maryland community colleges

**Important**: This is a "last dollar" scholarship - other aid is applied first, then Conroy fills the gap up to full cost.

**How to apply**:
1. Complete FAFSA (required)
2. Apply through Maryland Higher Education Commission (MHEC)
3. Submit VA rating documentation showing 100% P&T
4. Coordinate with school financial aid office

**Contact**: MHEC at (800) 974-1024 or [mhec.maryland.gov](https://mhec.maryland.gov)

**This benefit alone can be worth more than $100,000** if you have eligible dependents.

### Veterans of Afghanistan and Iraq Conflicts (VAIC) Scholarship

**Eligibility**: Maryland resident veterans who served in Afghanistan or Iraq conflicts

**Benefit**: Covers up to **50% of tuition, fees, room and board**

**Maximum**: Actual annual costs at University System of Maryland schools

**Duration**: Up to 5 years

**Can be combined with**: Federal GI Bill for potentially 100% coverage

**How to apply**: Through MHEC at [mhec.maryland.gov](https://mhec.maryland.gov)

### Maryland National Guard Tuition Waiver

**Eligibility**: Active Maryland National Guard members

**Benefit**: 15-50% tuition reduction at Maryland state-supported colleges

**Can be stacked with**: Federal GI Bill and VAIC for up to 100% coverage

### In-State Tuition for Veterans

Under the Veterans Access, Choice, and Accountability Act (VACAA), veterans using GI Bill benefits qualify for **in-state tuition** at all Maryland public schools, regardless of residency.

**Eligibility**:
- Using Post-9/11 GI Bill or Montgomery GI Bill
- Living in Maryland while attending school
- Enrolling within 3 years of discharge

This eliminates the out-of-state tuition barrier.

## Employment Resources for Maryland Veterans

### Federal Hiring Preference

Maryland veterans benefit from federal hiring preference at major installations:

**Who qualifies**:
- Honorably discharged veterans receive 5-point preference
- Disabled veterans (10%+ rating) receive 10-point preference
- Purple Heart recipients receive 15-point preference (Maryland state jobs)

**How it works**: Points added to passing civil service exam scores; preference in selections

### Major Military Installations & Federal Employment

#### Fort Meade (Anne Arundel County)
**Employees**: 50,000+ (military and civilian) - **4th largest Army workforce in U.S.**

**Major tenants**:
- **National Security Agency (NSA)** - 30,000+ employees
- **U.S. Cyber Command** - 2,000+
- **Defense Information Systems Agency (DISA)**
- **Defense Courier Service**
- **Defense Media Activity**
- **70th Intelligence, Surveillance and Reconnaissance Wing**

**Typical jobs for veterans**:
- Intelligence analysts
- Cybersecurity specialists
- Information technology
- Signals intelligence
- Network operations
- Systems engineering
- Security specialists

**Security clearance**: Most positions require Secret or Top Secret/SCI clearance

**Pay**: Federal GS scale (GS-7 to GS-15 typical); many contractor positions paying $80K-$150K+

**How to apply**: [usajobs.gov](https://www.usajobs.gov/) (filter by Fort Meade/NSA)

**Veterans advantage**: Federal preference applies; military intel/cyber experience highly valued

#### Aberdeen Proving Ground (Harford County)
**Employees**: 7,500+ civilians, 5,000+ military

**Mission**: U.S. Army research, development, and testing

**Major commands**:
- U.S. Army Test and Evaluation Command
- U.S. Army Research Laboratory
- Army Materiel Systems Analysis Activity
- C5ISR Center

**Typical jobs**:
- Engineers (mechanical, electrical, chemical, systems)
- Scientists (chemistry, physics, materials)
- Test and evaluation specialists
- Logistics specialists
- Program managers
- Quality assurance

**Pay**: Federal GS scale; many positions GS-11 to GS-14

**How to apply**: [usajobs.gov](https://www.usajobs.gov/) (filter by Aberdeen Proving Ground)

**Veterans advantage**: Engineering and technical military experience highly valued

#### Joint Base Andrews (Prince George's County)
**Employees**: 10,000+ military and civilian

**Mission**: Presidential airlift support, homeland defense, fighter operations

**Known for**: Home of Air Force One

**Typical jobs**:
- Aircraft maintenance
- Logistics
- Security forces
- Contracting specialists
- Civil engineering

**Pay**: Federal GS scale and military pay

**How to apply**: [usajobs.gov](https://www.usajobs.gov/) (filter by Joint Base Andrews)

#### Naval Air Station Patuxent River (St. Mary's County)
**Employees**: 20,000+ (military, civilian, contractors)

**Mission**: Naval aviation test and evaluation

**Major command**: Naval Air Warfare Center Aircraft Division (NAWCAD)

**Typical jobs**:

## Key Takeaways

1. **Enroll in VA healthcare immediately** - Baltimore, Perry Point, and Loch Raven plus 5 clinics provide excellent access
2. **Use the Edward T. Conroy Scholarship** - If you're 100% disabled, your dependents get free college (worth $100K+)
3. **Target federal employment** - Fort Meade (NSA), Aberdeen, Andrews, Pax River offer 50,000+ jobs with veteran preference
4. **Leverage cybersecurity opportunities** - Maryland has the highest concentration of cyber jobs in the U.S.
5. **Connect with your county VSO** - Free help claiming all benefits

Maryland's veteran support infrastructure is among the best in the nation - comprehensive VA healthcare, exceptional education benefits for dependents, and unmatched federal employment opportunities in cybersecurity and intelligence.
