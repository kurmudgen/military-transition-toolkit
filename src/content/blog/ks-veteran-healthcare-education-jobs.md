# Kansas Veterans: Healthcare Facilities, Education Benefits & Job Resources

Kansas serves approximately 172,000 veterans - about 6.9% of the state's adult population. Whether you're transitioning to civilian life or already living in Kansas, you need to know what healthcare facilities, education benefits, and job opportunities are available.

This guide provides actual addresses, phone numbers, and actionable information for Kansas veterans.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Healthcare**: Kansas has 3 VA medical centers (Topeka, Leavenworth, Wichita), multiple community-based clinics, and 2 state veterans homes - excellent VA healthcare network for a rural/moderate-population state

**Education**: In-state tuition at all Kansas public universities and colleges for GI Bill users; Kansas State University and University of Kansas both have strong veteran support programs

**Employment**: State hiring preference, two major military installations (Fort Riley, McConnell AFB), strong defense contractor presence, veteran-friendly employers statewide

**Veteran Population Hotspots**: Sedgwick County (Wichita - 28,000+), Johnson County (Kansas City suburbs - 25,000+), Riley/Geary Counties (Fort Riley area - 15,000+)

### How to Enroll in VA Healthcare

**Online**: [va.gov/health-care/apply](https://www.va.gov/health-care/apply/)
**Phone**: 1-877-222-VETS (8387)
**In person**: Visit any VA medical center or clinic with your DD-214

**Documents needed**:
- DD-214 (discharge paperwork)
- Social Security card
- Insurance information (if you have other coverage)
- Income information (for copay determination)

**Best practice**: Enroll as soon as you separate from active duty, even if you have other insurance. This locks in your eligibility and priority group.

**VA Eastern Kansas Health Care enrollment**: (785) 350-4111

## Education Benefits for Kansas Veterans

### In-State Tuition for Veterans (Choice Act)

Under the Veterans Access, Choice, and Accountability Act (VACAA), veterans using GI Bill benefits qualify for **in-state tuition** at Kansas public schools regardless of residency duration.

**Eligibility**:
- Using Post-9/11 GI Bill or Montgomery GI Bill
- Living in Kansas while attending school
- Enrolling within 3 years of discharge (Post-9/11 GI Bill)

**Benefit**: Eliminates out-of-state tuition surcharge (typically $10,000-15,000/year)

**Applies to**: All Kansas public universities, colleges, and community colleges

**Example savings**:
- University of Kansas out-of-state tuition: ~$28,000/year
- University of Kansas in-state tuition: ~$12,000/year
- **Savings with Choice Act**: $16,000/year

### Post-9/11 GI Bill - Key Information

**Monthly BAH rates in Kansas** (2024-25):
- Manhattan/Fort Riley: $1,735
- Lawrence: $1,735
- Wichita: $1,572
- Topeka: $1,429
- Online only: $1,118 (national rate)

**What GI Bill covers at Kansas public schools**:
- 100% of in-state tuition and fees (Post-9/11 GI Bill)
- Books and supplies stipend ($1,000/year)
- Monthly housing allowance based on location
- Tutorial assistance if needed

**Example budget** (K-State or KU student):
- Tuition: $10,000-12,000 (covered by GI Bill)
- BAH: $1,735/month x 9 months = $15,615
- Books stipend: $1,000
- **Total benefits**: ~$27,000/year

This allows veterans to attend school full-time without working, or work part-time for additional income.

## Employment Resources for Kansas Veterans

### State Hiring Preference

Kansas law grants veterans significant advantages in state employment.

**What you get**:
- **Points added** to passing exam scores for qualified veterans
- **Absolute appointment preference** within a given grade
- **Higher retention standing** in case of layoffs

**Who qualifies**:
- Veterans who served at least 6 months with honorable discharge **OR**
- Discharged based on hardship or service-connected disability
- Kansas resident

**How it works**: If you meet minimum qualifications and pass the exam, your veteran status gives you priority over non-veteran applicants with similar scores.

**Where to apply**: [Kansas.gov/jobs](https://www.kansas.gov/jobs) or contact Kansas Department of Administration

**Major state employers**:
- Kansas Department of Corrections
- Kansas Department of Transportation (KDOT)
- Kansas Department of Wildlife & Parks
- Kansas Highway Patrol
- State universities and colleges

## Key Takeaways

1. **Enroll in VA healthcare immediately** - Kansas has 3 VA medical centers and multiple clinics - excellent coverage

Kansas genuinely supports its veteran population with tangible, valuable benefits - especially strong VA healthcare access and excellent employment opportunities around Fort Riley and Wichita's aviation/defense industry.
