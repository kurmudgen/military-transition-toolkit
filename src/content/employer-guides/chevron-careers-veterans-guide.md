# Chevron Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 45,000+ employees globally
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: Chevron Military Recruiting, VETNET ERG
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: Operations Tech, Engineer, Maintenance, Project Management, HSE
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $60,000 - $180,000+ depending on role
- **Energy Leader**: One of world's largest integrated energy companies
- **Technical Focus**: Engineering and operations excellence aligned with military training

Chevron is one of the world's largest energy companies, operating across exploration, production, refining, and distribution. Veterans—especially those with technical, mechanical, and engineering backgrounds—find excellent career opportunities and a culture that values military discipline and safety focus.

## Company Overview

Chevron explores for, produces, and transports crude oil and natural gas; refines petroleum; sells and distributes fuels; and is expanding into renewables and low-carbon technologies.

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 45,000+ globally
- **Annual Revenue**: $200+ billion
- **Daily Production**: 3+ million barrels oil equivalent
- **Refineries**: 4 US refineries
- **Countries**: 180+ countries of operation

### Culture for Veterans

Chevron's culture emphasizes safety, operational excellence, and "The Chevron Way" values. The company's focus on safety, reliability, and technical precision mirrors military operations. Veterans with mechanical, electrical, and process backgrounds find natural fit in refinery and upstream operations.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Find Open Positions

**Where to Look:**
- Primary: chevron.com/careers
- LinkedIn: Chevron
- Indeed for specific locations
- SkillBridge: Through installation

**Keywords to Search:**
- "Operations Technician"
- "Engineer"
- "Maintenance"
- "HSE" (Health, Safety, Environment)
- "Refinery" or "Upstream"

**Best Times to Apply:**
- Year-round hiring
- After major project approvals
- New facility startups

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What Chevron Looks For:**
- Technical/mechanical aptitude
- Safety consciousness
- Operational reliability
- Team collaboration
- Continuous learning

**Resume Format:**
- Technical experience prominent
- Safety record highlighted
- Equipment/systems experience
- Certifications listed

**Resume Keywords That Work:**
- "Process operations"
- "Mechanical maintenance"
- "Safety compliance"
- "Heavy equipment"
- "Petroleum/refinery"

**Important:**
- Emphasize safety focus
- Note technical training and certifications
- Highlight shift work availability

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Online Application
2. Phone Screen with Recruiter
3. Technical Interview
4. Panel Interview
5. Site Visit/Assessment
6. Offer

**Common Questions:**
- "Why Chevron / energy industry?"
- "Describe your technical background"
- "Tell me about safety in your work"
- "How do you handle shift work?"
- "Describe troubleshooting complex equipment"

**What They Value:**
- Safety commitment
- Technical excellence
- Operational reliability
- Teamwork
- Continuous improvement

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- Technician: 2-4 weeks
- Engineering: 3-6 weeks
- Management: 4-8 weeks

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive base salary
- Annual bonus (15-30%+)
- 401(k) with match (up to 8%)
- Pension (cash balance plan)
- Healthcare
- Stock purchase plan

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Some flexibility on base
- Bonus potential is significant
- Relocation assistance available
- Ask about sign-on bonus

## Requirements

### Education
- Technician: High school + technical training
- Engineering: Bachelor's in engineering
- Management: Bachelor's, often advanced degree

### Certifications
- Process Technology certificate
- Electrical/mechanical certifications
- OSHA/safety training
- Engineering PE (some roles)

### Physical Requirements
- Climb ladders and stairs
- Work in industrial environment
- Lift moderate weight
- Pass drug screening

### Experience
- Technician: 2-5 years technical/military
- Engineer: 0-5 years engineering
- Senior: 10+ years industry
