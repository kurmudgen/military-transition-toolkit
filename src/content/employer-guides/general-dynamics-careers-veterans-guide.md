# General Dynamics Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 106,000+ employees globally
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: Military Transition, Business Unit Programs
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: IT Specialist, Ship Builder, Engineer, Program Manager, Technician
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $55,000 - $150,000+ depending on role
- **Business Units**: GDIT, Electric Boat, Land Systems, Bath Iron Works, Mission Systems
- **Veteran Focus**: Each business unit has dedicated veteran hiring programs

General Dynamics comprises multiple business units, each with distinct veteran hiring opportunities. From IT services (GDIT) to nuclear submarines (Electric Boat) to tanks (Land Systems), GD offers diverse career paths for veterans of all backgrounds.

## Company Overview

General Dynamics is a global aerospace and defense company with five business segments: Aerospace (Gulfstream jets), Combat Systems (tanks, vehicles), Information Technology (GDIT), Marine Systems (submarines, ships), and Mission Systems (electronics).

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 106,000+ globally
- **Annual Revenue**: $42+ billion
- **Business Units**: 5 major segments
- **Defense Revenue**: $35+ billion
- **Key Products**: M1 Abrams, Stryker, Virginia-class submarines

### Culture for Veterans

General Dynamics' diverse business units each have their own culture, but all share a commitment to veterans. The defense-focused units (Combat Systems, Marine Systems) have particularly high veteran populations and deep understanding of military experience. GDIT's government IT work naturally attracts cleared veterans.

## Veteran Hiring Programs

### General Dynamics Information Technology (GDIT)

GDIT has extensive veteran programs:
- Dedicated military recruiting team
- Cleared job matching
- Transition assistance
- Military spouse programs
- SkillBridge partnerships

### Electric Boat (Submarines)

Strong veteran hiring for:
- Nuclear-trained sailors
- Shipbuilders
- Engineers
- Pipe fitters, welders, electricians
- Apprenticeship programs

### Land Systems (Combat Systems)

Natural fit for Army veterans:
- Tank and vehicle experience valued
- Lima, OH facility (M1 Abrams)
- Michigan facilities (Stryker)
- Technical and engineering roles

### Bath Iron Works (Destroyers)

Naval shipbuilding offers:
- Navy veterans especially valued
- Trades apprenticeships
- Engineering positions
- Production management

### Veteran Resource Groups

Each business unit has veteran ERGs providing:
- Mentorship
- Networking
- Career development
- Community

## Application Process

### Step 1: Find Open Positions

**Where to Look:**
- GDIT: gdit.com/careers
- Electric Boat: gdeb.com/careers
- Land Systems: gdls.com/careers
- Bath Iron Works: gdbiw.com/careers
- Corporate: gd.com/careers
- LinkedIn: General Dynamics + business unit

**Keywords to Search:**
- Business unit name
- "Military" or "Veteran"
- "Cleared" (for GDIT)
- "Apprentice" (for trades)
- Specific system names (M1, Virginia, etc.)

**Best Times to Apply:**
- After contract awards
- Government fiscal year (October)
- Continuous for cleared IT (GDIT)
- Continuous for shipbuilding

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What General Dynamics Looks For:**
- Relevant military experience for the business unit
- Security clearance (GDIT especially)
- Technical certifications
- Safety focus
- Team collaboration

**Resume Format:**
- Target resume to specific business unit
- Technical skills prominent
- Clearance featured (if applicable)
- Certifications listed

**Resume Keywords (by business unit):**

**GDIT:**
- "Security clearance"
- "IT systems"
- "Cybersecurity"
- "Federal/government"

**Combat Systems:**
- "Armored vehicles"
- "Heavy equipment"
- "Maintenance"
- "Production"

**Marine Systems:**
- "Shipbuilding"
- "Nuclear" (for submarine)
- "Trades" (welding, electrical, etc.)
- "Navy" experience

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Application Review
2. Recruiter Phone Screen
3. Hiring Manager Interview
4. Technical Assessment (some roles)
5. Background/Clearance Check
6. Offer

**Interview Format:**
- Technical questions (role-dependent)
- Behavioral questions (STAR method)
- Safety scenarios
- Team collaboration examples

**Common Questions:**
- "Why General Dynamics / [specific business unit]?"
- "Describe your experience with [relevant system]"
- "How do you prioritize safety in your work?"
- "Tell me about working under pressure"
- "How do you handle technical troubleshooting?"

**What They Value:**
- Relevant military experience
- Safety consciousness
- Technical skills
- Quality focus
- Team collaboration

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- Manufacturing/Trades: 1-2 weeks
- Cleared IT (GDIT): 2-4 weeks
- Engineering: 2-3 weeks

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive base salary
- Annual bonus (varies)
- 401(k) with match
- Pension (some business units)
- Comprehensive benefits

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Varies by business unit
- Cleared candidates have leverage at GDIT
- Ask about apprenticeship completion bonuses
- Consider total compensation

## Requirements

### Education
- Trades: High school diploma, apprenticeship
- IT: Associates or experience, certifications
- Engineering: Bachelor's in relevant field
- Management: Bachelor's typically required

### Certifications
- Security+ (GDIT cyber)
- AWS/Azure (GDIT cloud)
- Welding certifications (shipbuilding)
- PMP (program management)
- Trade certifications (varies)

### Clearance
- GDIT: Many require Secret/TS/SCI
- Mission Systems: Secret often required
- Manufacturing: May not require
- Active clearance: Accelerates GDIT hiring

### Experience
- Apprentice: 0 years (training provided)
- Technician: 2-5 years
- Engineer: 3-5+ years
- Manager: 7-15+ years
