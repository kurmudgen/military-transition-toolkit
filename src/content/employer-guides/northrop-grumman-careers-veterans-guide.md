# Northrop Grumman Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 95,000+ employees globally
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: Military Veterans Network, Operation IMPACT
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: Cybersecurity, Systems Engineer, Program Manager, Analyst, Technician
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $65,000 - $170,000+ depending on role
- **Veteran Workforce**: 30%+ are veterans
- **Clearance Focus**: Extremely high percentage of positions require TS/SCI

Northrop Grumman is the fifth-largest defense contractor and one of the most veteran-friendly employers. With 30% of their workforce being veterans, they deeply understand military experience. Their focus on classified programs makes active security clearances extremely valuable.

## Company Overview

Northrop Grumman is a leading defense and security company providing autonomous systems, cyber, C4ISR, space, and logistics services. Products include the B-21 Raider, Global Hawk, James Webb Space Telescope, and numerous classified programs.

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 95,000+ globally
- **Annual Revenue**: $39+ billion
- **Veteran Workforce**: 28,000+ veterans (30%)
- **Classified Work**: High percentage of programs
- **Space Systems**: Major satellite and space contractor

### Culture for Veterans

Northrop Grumman's culture is deeply defense-focused with emphasis on innovation, integrity, and serving the warfighter. With nearly one-third of employees being veterans, military experience is woven into the company's fabric. The classified nature of much work means veterans with clearances find immediate value and understanding.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Find Open Positions

**Where to Look:**
- Primary: northropgrumman.com/careers
- Military specific: northropgrumman.com/careers/military
- LinkedIn: Northrop Grumman + location filter
- ClearanceJobs.com (they're a major employer)

**Keywords to Search:**
- "Cleared" or "TS/SCI"
- "Cyber"
- "Space"
- "Engineer"
- "Analyst"
- "Secret" or "Top Secret"

**Best Times to Apply:**
- After major contract awards (B-21, GBSD)
- Government fiscal year (October)
- Continuous for cleared positions

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What Northrop Looks For:**
- Active security clearance (critical)
- Technical skills
- Mission focus
- Problem-solving
- Integrity and ethics

**Resume Format:**
- Clearance level first/prominent
- Technical skills detailed
- Specific systems/programs listed
- Quantified achievements

**Resume Keywords That Work:**
- "TS/SCI" or "Polygraph" (if applicable)
- "Classified programs"
- "Cyber operations"
- "Space systems"
- "Autonomous systems"
- Specific weapon systems

**Important:**
- Clearance is often THE key factor—feature it prominently
- Note polygraph type and date
- List specific classified programs (program names only if unclassified)

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Application Review (clearance verified early)
2. Recruiter Phone Screen
3. Technical/Hiring Manager Interview
4. Panel Interview (senior roles)
5. Security Verification
6. Offer

**Interview Format:**
- Technical assessment questions
- Behavioral (STAR method)
- Security awareness
- Integrity scenarios

**Common Questions:**
- "Why Northrop Grumman?"
- "Describe your experience handling classified information"
- "How do you approach complex technical problems?"
- "Tell me about leading in high-pressure situations"
- "How do you stay current with emerging threats?"

**What They Value:**
- Security consciousness
- Technical excellence
- Innovation
- Integrity
- Mission dedication

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- Active clearance: 2-3 weeks
- Clearance transfer: 3-4 weeks
- New clearance: Extended (months)

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive base salary
- Annual bonus (varies by level)
- 401(k) with match (up to 6%)
- Pension plan
- Stock purchase plan
- Comprehensive benefits

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Cleared candidates have leverage
- Ask about signing bonus
- Negotiate level/band
- Consider location cost of living

## Requirements

### Education
- Technical: Associates or relevant experience
- Engineering: Bachelor's in relevant field
- Program Management: Bachelor's, advanced often required
- Senior Technical: Advanced degree preferred

### Certifications
- Security+ (cyber roles)
- CISSP (senior cyber)
- PMP (program management)
- Engineering PE (some roles)
- AWS/Cloud certifications (IT roles)

### Clearance
- Secret: Minimum for many roles
- TS/SCI: Required for most positions
- Polygraph: Required for many programs
- Active clearance: Essential for competitive hiring

### Experience
- Technician: 2-5 years relevant
- Engineer: 3-5+ years
- Senior Engineer: 8-12+ years
- Program Manager: 10-15+ years
