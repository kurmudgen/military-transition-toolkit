# Lowe's Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 300,000+ employees
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: Track to the Trades, Lowe's Heroes
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: Department Manager, Store Manager, Pro Sales, Installation, Distribution
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $30,000 - $120,000+ depending on role
- **Trades Training**: Free skilled trades training through Track to the Trades
- **Veteran Focus**: Committed to hiring 25,000+ military spouses

Lowe's combines retail careers with a unique focus on skilled trades training through their Track to the Trades program. Veterans can pursue traditional retail careers or gain certifications in carpentry, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, and appliance repairâ€”all while earning a paycheck.

## Company Overview

Lowe's is America's second-largest home improvement retailer, serving homeowners and professionals. Beyond retail, Lowe's emphasizes installation services and skilled trades development.

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 300,000+
- **Annual Revenue**: $90+ billion
- **US Stores**: 1,700+
- **Distribution Centers**: 40+
- **Installation Projects**: Millions annually

### Culture for Veterans

Lowe's culture emphasizes serving others, teamwork, and doing the right thing. Their investment in skilled trades training recognizes that many veterans have construction/mechanical backgrounds. The company actively supports military families through hiring commitments and benefits.

## Application Process

**Where to Look:**
- Primary: jobs.lowes.com

- LinkedIn: Lowe's
- In-store applications

**Keywords to Search:**
- "Department Manager"
- "Pro Sales"
- "Store Manager"
- "Installation"
- "Trades"

**Best Times to Apply:**
- Year-round hiring
- Spring surge: February-April
- Track to the Trades: Program cycles

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What Lowe's Looks For:**
- Customer service focus
- Technical/trade skills helpful
- Leadership experience
- Reliability
- Team collaboration

**Resume Format:**
- Skills-focused format
- Technical abilities highlighted
- Customer interaction examples
- Leadership roles

**Resume Keywords That Work:**
- "Construction/trades"
- "Customer service"
- "Team leadership"
- "Pro/contractor experience"
- "Installation/repair"

**Important:**
- Note interest in Track to the Trades
- Highlight construction/mechanical MOS
- Show leadership experience

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Online Application
2. Assessment
3. In-Store Interview
4. Management Interview (senior roles)
5. Offer

**For Track to the Trades:**
1. Application
2. Skills assessment
3. Interview
4. Program acceptance
5. Training start

**Common Questions:**
- "Why Lowe's?"
- "What trade skills do you have?"
- "Tell me about helping a customer solve a problem"
- "How do you handle multiple priorities?"
- "What interests you about skilled trades?"

**What They Value:**
- Customer focus
- Trade knowledge
- Leadership
- Reliability
- Learning orientation

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- Hourly: 1-2 weeks
- Management: 2-4 weeks
- Track to the Trades: Program cycle

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive wages/salary
- Healthcare benefits
- 401(k) with match
- Employee discount
- Tuition reimbursement
- Track to the Trades (free)

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Management has some flexibility
- Specialist roles pay more
- Trade certifications increase value

## What Veterans Should Know

### Pros

1. **Track to the Trades**: Free certifications in high-demand fields
2. **Military Family Support**: Spouse hiring commitment
3. **Skills Application**: Construction MOS directly applicable
4. **Career Growth**: Retail and trades paths
5. **Benefits**: Healthcare, retirement, education
6. **Community**: Strong veteran employee network
7. **Stability**: Major retailer with job security

### Cons

1. **Retail Hours**: Weekends, some holidays
2. **Physical Work**: Standing, lifting
3. **Seasonal Peaks**: Spring rush intense
4. **Smaller Footprint**: Fewer stores than competitor
5. **Entry Pay**: Retail-level starting wages

## Requirements

### Education
- Associate: No degree required
- Management: Degree helpful
- Trades: Program training
- Corporate: Bachelor's typically required

### Certifications
- Trade licenses (Track to the Trades provides)
- Forklift certification (training provided)
- Management: Store training programs

### Physical Requirements
- Lift up to 50 lbs
- Standing extended periods
- Work in warehouse conditions

### Experience
- Associate: 0 years
- Department Manager: 1-2 years
- Store Manager: 3-5 years
- Track to the Trades: Military or spouse status
