# Leidos Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 47,000+ employees
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: Leidos Military & Veterans Program, Operation MVP
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: Cybersecurity, IT Specialist, Systems Engineer, Program Manager, Analyst
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $65,000 - $160,000+ depending on role
- **Government Focus**: Major defense, intelligence, and civil contracts
- **Veteran Workforce**: 20%+ are veterans

Leidos (formerly SAIC before 2013 split) is a major government IT and engineering services company. With strong positions in defense, intelligence, health IT, and civil agencies, Leidos offers diverse opportunities for cleared veterans looking to continue serving government missions.

## Company Overview

Leidos provides IT, engineering, and science solutions to government and commercial customers. Their portfolio includes cybersecurity, health IT, air traffic control, border security, and defense systems. They manage major programs like the NGEN Navy network and TSA airport security.

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 47,000+ globally
- **Annual Revenue**: $15+ billion
- **Government Revenue**: 85%+
- **Major Contracts**: NGEN, TSA, Social Security Admin
- **Veteran Workforce**: 20%+ (9,000+ veterans)

### Culture for Veterans

Leidos' culture emphasizes innovation, integrity, and mission focus. With one in five employees being veterans, military experience is deeply understood and valued. The company's work directly supports national security, health, and civil services—allowing veterans to continue meaningful service in civilian roles.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Find Open Positions

**Where to Look:**
- Primary: leidos.com/careers
- LinkedIn: Leidos + location/clearance filter
- ClearanceJobs.com
- Military hiring events

**Keywords to Search:**
- "Cleared" or specific clearance level
- "Cyber" or "Security"
- "Health IT"
- "Program Manager"
- "Engineer"

**Best Times to Apply:**
- After major contract awards
- Government fiscal year (October)
- Continuous for cleared positions

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What Leidos Looks For:**
- Security clearance (many roles)
- Technical skills
- Government/contractor experience
- Mission focus
- Customer service orientation

**Resume Format:**
- Clearance prominently displayed
- Technical skills detailed
- Government experience highlighted
- Certifications listed

**Resume Keywords That Work:**
- "Security clearance" with level
- "Government contractor"
- "Cybersecurity"
- "IT modernization"
- "Program support"
- Agency names

**Important:**
- Clearance is often required—feature prominently
- Note specific agencies/programs supported
- Include certifications (Security+, CISSP, etc.)

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Application Review
2. Recruiter Phone Screen
3. Technical Interview with Hiring Manager
4. Panel Interview (senior roles)
5. Security Verification
6. Offer

**Common Questions:**
- "Why Leidos?"
- "Describe your clearance and investigation status"
- "What government/military experience do you have?"
- "How do you handle customer requirements?"
- "Tell me about a technical challenge you solved"

**What They Value:**
- Active clearance
- Technical capabilities
- Customer focus
- Mission dedication
- Team collaboration

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- Active clearance: 2-3 weeks
- Clearance transfer: 3-4 weeks
- Position dependent: Varies

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive base salary
- Annual bonus (varies by level)
- 401(k) with match (up to 6%)
- ESPP
- Comprehensive benefits
- Tuition reimbursement

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Cleared candidates have leverage
- Some flexibility on base salary
- Ask about signing bonus
- Consider location cost of living

## What Veterans Should Know

### Pros

1. **Clearance Value**: Your clearance is highly valued
2. **Diverse Contracts**: Defense, intel, health, civil
3. **Veteran Culture**: 20%+ workforce are veterans
4. **Career Growth**: Large company with many opportunities
5. **Benefits**: Strong 401(k), healthcare, education
6. **Remote Options**: Many positions allow remote work
7. **Mission Variety**: Not limited to defense work

### Cons

1. **Clearance Required**: Many positions need clearances
2. **Contract Dependent**: Job tied to contract wins/losses
3. **Recompetes**: Contracts periodically rebid
4. **Location Dependent**: Best opportunities in certain areas
5. **Government Pace**: Subject to federal processes

## Requirements

### Education
- IT Support: Associates or certifications
- Analyst: Bachelor's preferred
- Engineering: Bachelor's in relevant field
- Senior/Management: Bachelor's required

### Certifications
- Security+ (baseline for many IT roles)
- CISSP (senior cyber)
- AWS/Azure (cloud roles)
- ITIL (service management)
- PMP (program management)

### Clearance
- Secret: Many positions
- TS/SCI: Required for intel and defense
- Public Trust: Some civil positions
- Active clearance: Significant advantage

### Experience
- Entry: 0-2 years (with clearance)
- Mid-level: 3-5 years
- Senior: 5-10 years
- Management: 8-15+ years
