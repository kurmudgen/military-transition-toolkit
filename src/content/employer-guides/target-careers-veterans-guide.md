# Target Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 440,000+ employees
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: Target Military Business Council, Veteran ERG
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: Store Team Lead, Executive Team Lead, Distribution, Asset Protection, Corporate
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $30,000 - $130,000+ depending on role
- **Leadership Focus**: Veterans often fast-tracked to leadership roles
- **Culture**: Guest-obsessed, team-oriented, growth-focused

Target is known for its positive culture and career development opportunities. Veterans find Target's team-oriented, leadership-focused environment familiar. The company actively recruits veterans for leadership positions and offers clear advancement paths.

## Company Overview

Target operates retail stores across the United States, complemented by a growing e-commerce business. Known for a differentiated shopping experience, Target emphasizes design, quality, and guest service.

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 440,000+
- **Annual Revenue**: $110+ billion
- **US Stores**: 1,950+
- **Distribution Centers**: 50+
- **Supply Chain Facilities**: 60+

### Culture for Veterans

Target's culture emphasizes teamwork, inclusion, and development. The company's leadership principles align with military values—ownership, teamwork, and excellence. Target actively supports military families and values veteran perspectives in leadership roles.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Find Open Positions

**Where to Look:**
- Primary: jobs.target.com
- LinkedIn: Target
- In-store applications
- Career fairs

**Keywords to Search:**
- "Executive Team Lead"
- "Team Lead"
- "Distribution"
- "Asset Protection"
- "Store Director"

**Best Times to Apply:**
- Year-round hiring
- Seasonal surge: August-October
- Post-holiday: January

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What Target Looks For:**
- Leadership experience
- Guest/customer focus
- Team collaboration
- Problem-solving ability
- Adaptability

**Resume Format:**
- Achievement-focused
- Leadership examples
- Customer service highlighted
- Team collaboration

**Resume Keywords That Work:**
- "Team leadership"
- "Customer/guest service"
- "Operations"
- "Safety and security"
- "Inventory management"

**Important:**
- Emphasize leadership at any level
- Show customer focus
- Note military team experience

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Online Application
2. Assessment
3. Phone Screen
4. In-Person Interview
5. Follow-up Interview (leadership roles)
6. Offer

**Common Questions:**
- "Why Target?"
- "Tell me about a time you led a team"
- "How do you handle a difficult guest situation?"
- "Describe a time you went above and beyond"
- "How do you prioritize competing demands?"

**What They Value:**
- Guest obsession
- Team collaboration
- Ownership mentality
- Continuous improvement
- Inclusion

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- Hourly: 1-2 weeks
- Leadership: 2-4 weeks

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive wages/salary
- Healthcare benefits
- 401(k) with match
- Team member discount (10%+20% wellness)
- Education assistance

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Leadership roles have some flexibility
- Ask about advancement timeline
- Discuss training opportunities

## Requirements

### Education
- Team Member: No degree required
- ETL: Bachelor's preferred
- Corporate: Bachelor's typically required
- Store Director: Experience-based

### Certifications
- Pharmacy Tech: Certification required
- Forklift: Certification for DC
- Leadership: Target training provided

### Physical Requirements
- Lift up to 40 lbs
- Standing for extended periods
- Mobility throughout store

### Experience
- Team Member: 0 years
- Team Lead: 1-2 years
- ETL: 2-4 years or recent grad + leadership
- Store Director: 5+ years retail/management
