# Lockheed Martin Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 116,000+ employees globally
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: Military Relations, Veterans Leadership Council
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: Systems Engineer, Program Manager, Cybersecurity, Technician, Logistics
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $60,000 - $160,000+ depending on role
- **Veteran Workforce**: 25%+ of employees are veterans
- **Clearance Essential**: Most positions require active security clearance

Lockheed Martin is the world's largest defense contractor and one of the most veteran-friendly employers in America. With one in four employees being a veteran, the company deeply understands military experience and actively recruits transitioning service members for engineering, program management, and technical roles.

## Company Overview

Lockheed Martin is a global aerospace, defense, security, and technologies company. Their products include F-35, F-22, F-16 aircraft, Black Hawk helicopter, Aegis Combat System, THAAD missile defense, and numerous space systems including GPS satellites and the Orion spacecraft.

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 116,000+ globally
- **Annual Revenue**: $67+ billion
- **Veteran Workforce**: 29,000+ veterans (25% of workforce)
- **R&D Investment**: $1.5+ billion annually
- **Prime Contracts**: Largest DoD contractor

### Culture for Veterans

Lockheed Martin's culture is mission-driven—"We never forget who we're working for." This resonates deeply with veterans who understand protecting those who serve. The company invests heavily in STEM, supports veteran causes, and maintains a culture where military experience is genuinely valued and understood.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Find Open Positions

**Where to Look:**
- Primary: lockheedmartinjobs.com
- Military specific: lockheedmartinjobs.com/military
- LinkedIn: Lockheed Martin + filter
- On-base job fairs and TAP events

**Keywords to Search:**
- "Cleared" or "Clearance Required"
- "Engineer"
- "Program Manager"
- "Analyst"
- "Technician"
- MOS code directly

**Best Times to Apply:**
- After major contract awards
- Government fiscal year start (October)
- Continuous for cleared positions

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What Lockheed Looks For:**
- Active security clearance
- Technical skills and certifications
- Mission focus and dedication
- Team collaboration
- Problem-solving abilities

**Resume Format:**
- Clear, detailed format
- Technical skills prominently displayed
- Clearance level and status featured
- Specific platforms/systems experience

**Resume Keywords That Work:**
- "Security clearance" (with level)
- "Program management"
- "Systems engineering"
- Specific weapon systems names
- "DoD" and "Government"

**Important:**
- Feature clearance prominently (top of resume)
- Note polygraph if completed
- List specific military systems worked on

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Application Review
2. Recruiter Phone Screen (30-45 minutes)
3. Technical/Hiring Manager Interview
4. Panel Interview (for senior roles)
5. Security Verification
6. Offer

**Interview Format:**
- Technical questions for engineering
- Behavioral questions (STAR method)
- Mission alignment questions
- Security awareness questions

**Common Questions:**
- "Why Lockheed Martin?"
- "Describe your experience with [specific system]"
- "How do you handle classified information?"
- "Tell me about leading a technical team"
- "How do you prioritize competing requirements?"

**What They Value:**
- Mission dedication
- Security consciousness
- Technical excellence
- Team collaboration
- Integrity and ethics

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- Cleared positions: 2-4 weeks (faster with active clearance)
- New clearance required: May take months
- Technical roles: 2-3 weeks

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive base salary
- Annual bonus (3-10% typical)
- 401(k) with generous match (up to 6%)
- Pension plan contributions
- Comprehensive benefits

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Clearance holders have leverage
- Some flexibility on base salary
- Ask about signing bonus
- Negotiate level when possible

## What Veterans Should Know

### Pros

1. **Mission Continuity**: Supporting the same warfighters you served with
2. **Clearance Value**: Your clearance is extremely valuable here
3. **Veteran Culture**: 25%+ workforce are veterans
4. **Benefits**: Pension contributions, strong 401(k) match
5. **Stability**: Long-term government contracts
6. **Career Growth**: Clear technical and management tracks
7. **Education**: Tuition assistance for continued learning

### Cons

1. **Clearance Required**: Most positions need active clearance
2. **Location Dependent**: Jobs concentrated in specific areas
3. **Government Pace**: Subject to contract and budget cycles
4. **Large Corporation**: Bureaucracy in processes
5. **Security Restrictions**: Work limitations due to classification

## Requirements

### Education
- Technician: High school diploma/GED, relevant experience
- Engineering: Bachelor's in relevant field
- Program Management: Bachelor's, often advanced degree
- Senior Technical: Advanced degree often preferred

### Certifications
- Security+ (for cyber roles)
- PMP (for program management)
- Engineering PE (for some roles)
- Lean Six Sigma (valued)
- FAA A&P (for aircraft mechanics)

### Clearance
- Secret: Minimum for most positions
- TS/SCI: Required for many roles
- Polygraph: Required for some positions
- Active clearance: Significantly speeds hiring

### Experience
- Technician: 2-5 years relevant
- Engineer: 3-5+ years
- Senior Engineer: 8-12+ years
- Program Manager: 7-15+ years
