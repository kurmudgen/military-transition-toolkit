# Boeing Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 170,000+ employees globally
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: Boeing Military Skills Translator, Veterans Outreach
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: Aircraft Mechanic, Manufacturing, Engineering, Program Management, Cybersecurity
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $55,000 - $150,000+ depending on role
- **Clearance Value**: Many positions require Secret or TS/SCI clearances
- **Veteran Workforce**: 15%+ of employees are veterans

Boeing is one of the largest employers of veterans in the United States. As the world's largest aerospace company and a major defense contractor, Boeing offers natural career paths for veterans with aviation, maintenance, engineering, and security clearance backgrounds.

## Company Overview

Boeing designs and manufactures commercial airplanes, defense products, space systems, and provides global services. Their products include the 737, 787 Dreamliner, F-15, F/A-18, Apache helicopter, KC-46 tanker, and numerous space systems.

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 170,000+ globally
- **Annual Revenue**: $75+ billion
- **Veteran Workforce**: 25,000+ veterans employed
- **Defense Revenue**: $25+ billion annually
- **Locations**: 65+ countries, major US presence

### Culture for Veterans

Boeing's culture is deeply influenced by its aerospace and defense heritage. Precision, safety, quality, and mission focus are paramount. Veterans often find Boeing's structured environment, attention to detail, and technical rigor familiar. The strong veteran population means immediate community and understanding of military experience.

## Veteran Hiring Programs

### Boeing Military Skills Translator

Online tool that converts:
- MOS/Rating/AFSC to Boeing positions
- Military experience to job requirements
- Service achievements to civilian qualifications

### Veterans Outreach Program

Boeing's dedicated veteran initiative includes:
- Military recruiting specialists
- Transition assistance partnerships
- Targeted job fairs on military installations
- Resume and interview support

### Boeing Employee Veterans Association (BEVA)

Employee resource group with chapters nationwide offering:
- Mentorship programs
- Career development
- Networking events
- Community service
- Executive sponsorship

### SkillBridge Program

Boeing participates in DoD SkillBridge:
- 12-week internships
- Manufacturing and engineering tracks
- Potential conversion to full-time
- Multiple locations participating

### Military Spouse Employment

Boeing supports military spouses with:
- Remote work options
- Flexible schedules
- Spouse hiring initiatives

## Application Process

### Step 1: Find Open Positions

**Where to Look:**
- Primary: boeing.com/careers
- Military Skills Translator: boeing.com/careers/military
- LinkedIn: Boeing + filter by location
- Job fairs on military installations

**Keywords to Search:**
- "Mechanic"
- "Manufacturing"
- "Engineer"
- "Program Manager"
- "Cleared" or "Clearance"
- MOS code directly

**Best Times to Apply:**
- After major contract awards
- Pre-production ramp-ups
- Continuous for manufacturing roles

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What Boeing Looks For:**
- Technical precision and attention to detail
- Safety consciousness
- Quality focus
- Team collaboration
- Relevant certifications

**Resume Format:**
- Clear, technical format
- Highlight aviation/aerospace experience
- Include certifications and licenses
- Note clearance level prominently

**Resume Keywords That Work:**
- "Airframe and powerplant"
- "Quality assurance"
- "Safety compliance"
- "Production/manufacturing"
- "Systems maintenance"
- "Security clearance"

**Important:**
- Note FAA certifications (A&P license if applicable)
- List clearance level and status
- Highlight specific aircraft/systems experience

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Application Review
2. Phone Screen with Recruiter
3. Technical Interview/Assessment
4. On-Site Interview (may include facility tour)
5. Background/Clearance Verification
6. Offer

**Interview Format:**
- Technical questions for technical roles
- Behavioral questions (STAR method)
- Safety scenarios
- Quality focus questions

**Common Questions:**
- "Why Boeing?"
- "Describe your experience with aircraft maintenance/systems"
- "Tell me about a time you identified and resolved a safety issue"
- "How do you ensure quality in your work?"
- "Describe working under time pressure while maintaining standards"

**What They Value:**
- Safety first mentality
- Quality obsession
- Technical excellence
- Team collaboration
- Integrity

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- Manufacturing: 1-3 weeks
- Engineering: 2-4 weeks
- Cleared positions: May take longer (verification)

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive base salary
- Annual bonus (varies by level)
- 401(k) with match
- Pension plan (one of few remaining)
- Comprehensive benefits

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Some flexibility, especially for cleared positions
- Shift differentials for manufacturing
- Ask about hiring bonus
- Negotiate level when possible

## What Veterans Should Know

### Pros

1. **Natural Fit**: Aviation and defense background valued
2. **Clearance Leverage**: Many roles require/prefer clearances
3. **Pension Plan**: One of few companies still offering defined benefit
4. **Veteran Community**: Large, active BEVA presence
5. **Career Paths**: Clear progression in manufacturing and engineering
6. **Benefits**: Comprehensive healthcare, 401(k), pension
7. **Job Security**: Major long-term government contracts

### Cons

1. **Bureaucracy**: Large defense contractor processes
2. **Location Dependent**: Jobs concentrated in specific regions
3. **Economic Cycles**: Commercial aviation affected by economy
4. **Production Pressure**: Manufacturing roles can be demanding
5. **Background Process**: Clearance verification takes time

## Requirements

### Education
- Manufacturing: High school diploma/GED
- Technician: Associates or relevant certifications
- Engineering: Bachelor's in relevant field
- Program Management: Bachelor's, often advanced degree

### Certifications
- FAA A&P license (aircraft mechanics)
- AWS welding certifications
- NDT certifications
- Security+ (for cyber)
- PMP (for program management)

### Clearance
- Many roles: Secret minimum
- Defense programs: TS/SCI often required
- Commercial: May not require clearance
- Active clearance = significant advantage

### Experience
- Mechanic: 2-5 years relevant
- Engineering: 3-5+ years
- Program Manager: 5-10+ years
- Manufacturing: 0-2 years (training provided)
