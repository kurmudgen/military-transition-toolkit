# SAIC Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 26,000+ employees
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: SAIC Military Talent, Veterans ERG
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: IT Specialist, Cybersecurity, Program Analyst, Systems Engineer, Logistics
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $60,000 - $150,000+ depending on role
- **Government Focus**: 97% of revenue from government contracts
- **Clearance Essential**: Most positions require Secret or TS/SCI

SAIC (Science Applications International Corporation) is a premier government IT and technical services company. With 97% government revenue, virtually all positions support federal missions. This makes SAIC ideal for cleared veterans who want to continue serving government missions in a civilian capacity.

## Company Overview

SAIC provides IT modernization, engineering services, and mission support to the Department of Defense, intelligence community, and civilian agencies. Services include cloud migration, cybersecurity, logistics support, training, and systems integration.

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 26,000+
- **Annual Revenue**: $7.5+ billion
- **Government Revenue**: 97%
- **Defense Clients**: All military branches
- **Intelligence Clients**: Major IC agencies

### Culture for Veterans

SAIC's culture is mission-focused with most employees working on government sites alongside federal employees and military personnel. The environment feels familiar to veterans—similar mission focus, security consciousness, and government processes. Many colleagues are fellow veterans, creating instant community.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Find Open Positions

**Where to Look:**
- Primary: saic.com/careers
- LinkedIn: SAIC + location/clearance filter
- ClearanceJobs.com
- USAJobs.gov (for contract positions on gov sites)

**Keywords to Search:**
- "Cleared" or specific clearance level
- "IT" or "Cyber"
- "Program Analyst"
- "Logistics"
- Government agency names

**Best Times to Apply:**
- After contract awards
- Government fiscal year (October)
- Continuous for cleared positions

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What SAIC Looks For:**
- Active security clearance (critical)
- Government/contractor experience
- Technical skills
- Mission understanding
- Program support experience

**Resume Format:**
- Clearance level prominently displayed
- Government experience detailed
- Technical skills listed
- Certifications highlighted

**Resume Keywords That Work:**
- "Security clearance" with level
- "Government contractor"
- "Federal experience"
- "Program support"
- Specific agency names (DoD, Army, etc.)

**Important:**
- Clearance is typically required—feature it prominently
- Note specific agencies/programs supported
- Include contract/program names if unclassified

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Application Review (clearance verified early)
2. Recruiter Phone Screen
3. Hiring Manager Interview
4. Possible Technical Assessment
5. Security Verification
6. Offer

**Common Questions:**
- "Why SAIC?"
- "Describe your government/military experience"
- "What is your clearance status?"
- "How do you handle multiple priorities?"
- "Tell me about supporting a government customer"

**What They Value:**
- Clearance status
- Government understanding
- Customer service orientation
- Technical skills
- Mission focus

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- Active clearance: 1-2 weeks
- Clearance transfer: 2-3 weeks
- New clearance: Extended timeline

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive base salary
- 401(k) with match
- Annual bonus (some roles)
- Comprehensive benefits
- Education reimbursement

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Cleared candidates have leverage
- Ask about signing bonus
- Consider location cost of living
- Review contract end dates

## What Veterans Should Know

### Pros

1. **Clearance Value**: Your clearance is essential here
2. **Government Mission**: Continue supporting federal agencies
3. **Familiar Environment**: Work alongside military/gov civilians
4. **Veteran Community**: Many colleagues are veterans
5. **Diverse Contracts**: Work across DoD, IC, civilian agencies
6. **Remote Options**: Some positions allow remote work
7. **Career Growth**: Large contractor with many contracts

### Cons

1. **Clearance Required**: Most positions need active clearance
2. **Contract Dependent**: Job tied to specific contracts
3. **Location Varies**: May need to relocate for best opportunities
4. **Government Pace**: Subject to federal processes/shutdowns
5. **Recompetes**: Contracts periodically recompeted

## Requirements

### Education
- IT Support: Associates or certifications
- Analyst: Bachelor's preferred
- Engineering: Bachelor's in relevant field
- Management: Bachelor's required

### Certifications
- Security+ (most IT roles)
- CISSP (senior cyber)
- ITIL (service management)
- PMP (program management)
- AWS/Azure (cloud roles)

### Clearance
- Secret: Minimum for most positions
- TS/SCI: Required for many roles
- Active clearance: Essential for competitive hiring

### Experience
- Entry: 0-2 years (with clearance)
- Mid-level: 3-5 years
- Senior: 5-10 years
- Management: 7-15+ years
