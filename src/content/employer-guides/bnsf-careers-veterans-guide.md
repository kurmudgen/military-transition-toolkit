# BNSF Railway Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 35,000+ employees
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: BNSF Military Veterans Program, Berkshire Hathaway backing
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: Conductor Trainee, Locomotive Engineer, Mechanical, Track Maintenance, Dispatcher
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $50,000 - $100,000+ depending on role
- **Berkshire Hathaway**: Owned by Warren Buffett's companyâ€”financially stable
- **Veteran Focus**: One of most veteran-friendly employers in America

BNSF Railway (Burlington Northern Santa Fe) is one of North America's largest freight railroads. Owned by Berkshire Hathaway, BNSF offers exceptional job security and has been recognized repeatedly as a top military-friendly employer. Veterans thrive in the safety-focused, disciplined railroad environment.

## Company Overview

BNSF operates approximately 32,500 miles of track across 28 states and 3 Canadian provinces. They move consumer goods, agricultural products, industrial products, and coal across the western two-thirds of North America.

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 35,000+
- **Annual Revenue**: $24+ billion
- **Track Miles**: 32,500+ miles
- **States/Provinces**: 28 US states + 3 Canadian provinces
- **Freight Tons**: 200+ million tons annually

### Culture for Veterans

BNSF's culture emphasizes safety, service, and respect for people. The railroad's disciplined, rules-based environment mirrors military structure. As a repeatedly recognized Military Friendly Employer, BNSF actively integrates veteran perspectives and values the leadership and reliability veterans bring.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Find Open Positions

**Where to Look:**
- Primary: jobs.bnsf.com
- LinkedIn: BNSF Railway
- Military.com job board
- On-base job fairs

**Keywords to Search:**
- "Conductor Trainee"
- "Mechanical"
- "Track Maintenance"
- "Signal"
- "Dispatcher"

**Best Times to Apply:**
- Year-round for train crew
- Spring hiring surge for track work
- Continuous recruiting nationwide

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What BNSF Looks For:**
- Safety-first mentality
- Reliability and dependability
- Schedule flexibility
- Physical capability
- Clean background

**Resume Format:**
- Safety accomplishments highlighted
- Equipment operation experience
- 24/7 operations experience
- Leadership examples

**Resume Keywords That Work:**
- "Safety record"
- "Heavy equipment"
- "Shift work/24-7"
- "Regulatory compliance"
- "Team leader"

**Important:**
- Clean driving record required
- Drug screening required
- Must accept variable schedules
- Physical ability test

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Online Application
2. Assessment Testing
3. Interview (phone or in-person)
4. Physical Abilities Test
5. Medical Examination
6. Background Check
7. Offer

**Interview Format:**
- Behavioral questions
- Safety-focused scenarios
- Schedule flexibility assessment
- Rule compliance examples

**Common Questions:**
- "Why are you interested in railroad work?"
- "Can you work nights, weekends, and holidays?"
- "Tell me about a time safety was your top priority"
- "How do you handle being away from home?"
- "Describe following strict procedures under pressure"

**What They Value:**
- Safety commitment
- Reliability (attendance is critical)
- Flexibility
- Rule compliance
- Team collaboration

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- After all assessments complete
- Background check 2-4 weeks
- Medical exam required

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive union wages
- Railroad Retirement benefits
- Comprehensive healthcare
- Paid training
- Significant overtime potential

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Union scales are fixed
- Location preferences may be negotiable
- Management roles have flexibility

## What Veterans Should Know

## Requirements

### Education
- Train Crew: High school diploma
- Mechanical: High school + technical aptitude
- Management: Bachelor's preferred
- Signal: Technical training or experience

### Certifications
- Federal Railroad Administration certifications (training provided)
- CDL helpful for some roles
- Technical certifications for mechanical positions

### Physical Requirements
- Lift 50+ lbs frequently
- Climb on/off equipment
- Work outdoors in all conditions
- Pass physical abilities test

### Experience
- Conductor Trainee: 0 years (paid training)
- Mechanical: Technical aptitude required
- Engineer: 1-2 years as Conductor
- Management: 3+ years relevant
