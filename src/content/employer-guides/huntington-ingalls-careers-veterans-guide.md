# Huntington Ingalls Industries Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 44,000+ employees
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: HII Military Talent Programs, Apprentice Schools
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: Shipbuilder, Welder, Electrician, Engineer, Nuclear Technician
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $45,000 - $130,000+ depending on role
- **Navy Focus**: Builds ALL US Navy aircraft carriers and nuclear submarines
- **Natural Fit**: Navy veterans have direct experience with HII products

Huntington Ingalls Industries (HII) is America's largest military shipbuilder—if you served on a carrier or nuclear submarine, it was built by HII. With two major shipyards and growing technical services, HII offers exceptional opportunities for Navy veterans and skilled trades workers.

## Company Overview

HII operates two major shipyards: Newport News Shipbuilding (Virginia) builds aircraft carriers and submarines; Ingalls Shipbuilding (Mississippi) builds surface combatants including destroyers and amphibious ships. HII also has a growing Technical Solutions division.

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 44,000+
- **Annual Revenue**: $11+ billion
- **Newport News**: Only builder of US Navy aircraft carriers
- **Ingalls**: Largest builder of Navy surface ships
- **Apprentice School**: 100+ year history training shipbuilders

### Culture for Veterans

HII's culture is deeply patriotic and Navy-focused. Building the ships that project American power creates meaningful work. Navy veterans especially find that HII understands military culture, terminology, and the importance of quality. The trades-focused environment values hands-on skills.

### Step 1: Find Open Positions

**Where to Look:**
- Primary: hii.com/careers
- Newport News: huntingtoningalls.com/careers
- Ingalls: ingalls.huntingtoningalls.com/careers
- Apprentice School: as.edu (Newport News)
- LinkedIn: Huntington Ingalls

**Keywords to Search:**
- "Shipbuilder"
- "Welder"
- "Electrician"
- "Nuclear"
- "Apprentice"
- Navy rate names

**Best Times to Apply:**
- Apprentice School: Annual application cycles
- General: Continuous (always building ships)
- After new ship contract awards

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What HII Looks For:**
- Hands-on trade skills
- Navy experience (especially relevant)
- Safety focus
- Quality consciousness
- Team collaboration

**Resume Format:**
- Navy rating clearly explained
- Specific ships/systems experience
- Trade certifications listed
- Safety record highlighted

**Resume Keywords That Work:**
- "Shipboard maintenance"
- "Welding certifications"
- "Nuclear experience"
- "Naval vessels"
- Specific ship classes (CVN, DDG, SSN)

**Important:**
- If you served on an HII-built ship, mention it
- Translate Navy rates to civilian terms
- Note any formal trade training

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Application Review
2. Recruiter Screen
3. Skills Assessment (trades)
4. Hiring Manager Interview
5. Background Check
6. Offer

**For Apprentice School:**
1. Application
2. Aptitude Testing
3. Interview
4. Physical Assessment
5. Selection

**Common Questions:**
- "Why shipbuilding?"
- "Describe your experience maintaining [system]"
- "How do you ensure quality in your work?"
- "Tell me about working in confined spaces/at heights"
- "How do you handle safety on the job?"

**What They Value:**
- Quality craftsmanship
- Safety consciousness
- Reliability
- Team collaboration
- Pride in work

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- Trades: 1-2 weeks
- Engineering: 2-3 weeks
- Apprentice School: Specific cycle dates

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive wages (trades)
- Salary (professional roles)
- 401(k) with match
- Pension plan
- Comprehensive benefits
- Overtime opportunities

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Trade wages based on certification level
- Engineering has some flexibility
- Apprentice School is structured program

## What Veterans Should Know

### Pros

1. **Direct Relevance**: Navy vets know the products
2. **Apprentice School**: Free education + paid training
3. **Job Security**: Long-term Navy contracts
4. **Meaningful Work**: Building America's fleet
5. **Overtime Available**: Significant earning potential
6. **Benefits**: Pension + 401(k) + healthcare
7. **Career Progression**: Trades to leadership paths

### Cons

1. **Location Limited**: Newport News or Pascagoula
2. **Physical Work**: Shipbuilding is demanding
3. **Shift Work**: 24/7 production operations
4. **Weather Exposure**: Outdoor work in shipyard
5. **Long Commutes**: Large shipyard facilities

## Requirements

### Education
- Trades: High school diploma

- Engineering: Bachelor's in relevant field
- Management: Bachelor's preferred

### Certifications
- AWS welding certifications
- Electrical certifications
- Nuclear training (for nuclear work)
- Maritime certifications

### Clearance
- Some positions require Secret
- Nuclear programs: Additional screening
- Most trades: No clearance needed

### Experience
- Apprentice: 0 years (training provided)
- Trades: Experience preferred but not required
- Engineering: 3-5+ years
- Management: 7+ years
