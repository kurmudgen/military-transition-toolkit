# Raytheon Technologies (RTX) Careers for Veterans: Complete Guide

## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Company Size**: 185,000+ employees globally
- **Veteran Hiring Program**: RTX Military & Veteran Talent Programs, VALOR ERG
- **Top 5 Roles for Veterans**: Systems Engineer, Program Manager, Cybersecurity, Technician, Supply Chain
- **Average Starting Salary Range**: $60,000 - $160,000+ depending on role
- **Veteran Workforce**: 20%+ are veterans or military family members
- **Company Structure**: RTX includes Collins Aerospace, Pratt & Whitney, and Raytheon

RTX (Raytheon Technologies) is an aerospace and defense powerhouse formed from the 2020 merger of Raytheon and United Technologies. With products ranging from jet engines to missile systems to aircraft interiors, RTX offers diverse career paths for veterans across all backgrounds.

## Company Overview

RTX operates through three main segments: Collins Aerospace (aircraft systems), Pratt & Whitney (jet engines), and Raytheon (defense systems). Products include Patriot missile systems, F135 jet engine, Tomahawk cruise missiles, aircraft avionics, and commercial aviation components.

### By the Numbers
- **Employees**: 185,000+ globally
- **Annual Revenue**: $68+ billion
- **Defense Revenue**: $30+ billion
- **Commercial Aerospace**: $38+ billion
- **R&D Investment**: $7+ billion annually

### Culture for Veterans

RTX's culture balances aerospace precision with defense mission focus. The company emphasizes innovation, safety, and quality. Veterans find familiarity in the mission-driven defense work while commercial aerospace offers different challenges. With 20%+ veteran workforce, military experience is understood and valued throughout the organization.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Find Open Positions

**Where to Look:**
- Primary: rtx.com/careers (unified portal)
- Collins Aerospace: collinsaerospace.com/careers
- Pratt & Whitney: prattwhitney.com/careers
- Raytheon: raytheon.com/careers
- LinkedIn: RTX, Collins, Pratt & Whitney, Raytheon

**Keywords to Search:**
- "Military" or "Veteran"
- "Cleared" (for defense roles)
- "Technician" or "Mechanic"
- "Engineer"
- "Program Manager"

**Best Times to Apply:**
- After contract awards
- Q1 hiring (February-April)
- Continuous for manufacturing roles

### Step 2: Resume & Application

**What RTX Looks For:**
- Technical skills and certifications
- Safety and quality focus
- Security clearance (for defense)
- Team collaboration
- Problem-solving ability

**Resume Format:**
- Clear, organized format
- Technical skills highlighted
- Clearance featured (if applicable)
- Certifications listed

**Resume Keywords That Work:**
- "Aerospace" or "Aviation"
- "Defense systems"
- "Security clearance"
- "Quality assurance"
- "Manufacturing/production"
- Specific system names (Patriot, F135, etc.)

**Important:**
- Apply to the right business unit (Collins, P&W, or Raytheon)
- Note clearance for defense positions
- Highlight specific aircraft/systems experience

### Step 3: Interview Process

**Typical Interview Stages:**
1. Application Review
2. Recruiter Phone Screen
3. Technical/Hiring Manager Interview
4. On-Site or Panel Interview
5. Background/Clearance Verification
6. Offer

**Interview Format:**
- Technical questions (for technical roles)
- Behavioral questions (STAR method)
- Safety and quality scenarios
- Teamwork examples

**Common Questions:**
- "Why RTX/Collins/Pratt & Whitney/Raytheon?"
- "Describe your technical experience with [system]"
- "Tell me about ensuring quality under pressure"
- "How do you approach complex troubleshooting?"
- "Describe a time you improved a process"

**What They Value:**
- Technical excellence
- Safety consciousness
- Quality focus
- Innovation mindset
- Team collaboration

### Step 4: Offer & Negotiation

**Typical Offer Timeline:**
- Non-cleared: 2-3 weeks
- Cleared positions: 2-4 weeks
- New clearance: Extended timeline

**Compensation Package:**
- Competitive base salary
- Annual bonus (varies by level)
- 401(k) with match (up to 6%)
- Pension plan (varies by legacy company)
- Comprehensive benefits

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Some flexibility on base salary
- Ask about signing bonus
- Negotiate level when possible
- Consider total compensation package

## Requirements

### Education
- Technician: High school + relevant experience
- Engineering: Bachelor's in relevant field
- Program Management: Bachelor's, advanced degree preferred
- Senior roles: Advanced degrees often preferred

### Certifications
- FAA A&P license (aircraft mechanics)
- Security+ (cyber roles)
- PMP (program management)
- Engineering PE (some roles)
- Lean Six Sigma (valued)

### Clearance
- Defense roles: Secret or TS/SCI typically required
- Commercial: May not require clearance
- Active clearance: Speeds hiring process

### Experience
- Technician: 2-5 years relevant
- Engineer: 3-5+ years
- Senior roles: 8-12+ years
- Management: 7-15+ years
