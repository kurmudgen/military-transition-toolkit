# Washington vs Oregon Veterans Comparison: Complete Guide 2025

Washington and Oregon represent two of America's most progressive, outdoor-focused states with strong economic growth, excellent quality of life, and growing veteran communities. However, their tax structures differ significantly for military retirees. This comprehensive guide helps veterans choose between these Pacific Northwest destinations.

## Military Retirement Tax Treatment

### Washington: No State Income Tax (All Income)

Washington offers the ultimate tax benefit with no state income tax:

**Washington Tax Benefits:**
- Military pension: 0% state income tax
- VA disability compensation: 0% tax
- All other income: 0% state income tax
- Sales tax: 10.25% average (varies by county)
- Property tax: 0.84-0.94%

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Washington state income tax: $0
- Sales tax impact: ~$650/year (on typical purchases)
- **Net state tax advantage: Significant**

### Oregon: Military Retirement NOT Exempt, But Lower Rate

Oregon taxes military retirement but at lower rates than many states:

**Oregon Tax Treatment:**
- Military pension: Subject to state income tax
- VA disability compensation: Exempt
- State income tax rate: 4.75%-9.9% (progressive)
- Sales tax: 0% (no sales tax)
- Property tax: 0.97%

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Military pension tax: $1,688 (at ~3.75% effective)
- Other income tax: $1,680 (at ~8.4% effective)
- **Total state tax: $3,368**

**Tax Winner: Washington** (Saves $3,368 annually vs Oregon on this income)

## Final Verdict

### Choose Washington if:
- You earn $100,000+ (high-earning advantage)
- You work in tech/aerospace
- You want zero state income tax
- You prefer mountain recreation
- You can afford higher cost of living

### Choose Oregon if:
- You're on military retirement income only
- You want lower overall cost of living (3-4%)
- You prefer beach and wine country recreation
- You seek progressive, arts-focused communities
- You want to maximize purchasing power

**The Numbers:** Washington's tax advantage (no income tax) is significant for higher earners but less impactful for military retirees with modest additional income. Oregon's lower cost of living (3-4%) partially offsets Washington's tax advantage.

For a military retiree with $45,000 pension + modest other income, costs are essentially equivalent. For high-earning professionals, Washington's tax-free income is decisive.

**Choose based on income level and lifestyle:** Washington for high earners and tech professionals, Oregon for budget-conscious retirees who value beach recreation and wine country.
