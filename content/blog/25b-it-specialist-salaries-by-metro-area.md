# 25B IT Specialist: Salaries by Metro Area & Geographic Analysis

## BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

IT specialist salaries vary dramatically by region and metro area. San Francisco/Bay Area IT specialists earn highest salaries ($110,000-$160,000+), but face highest cost of living (+45-65% above national average). Seattle and Boston offer strong earning potential ($100,000-$145,000) with better cost-of-living ratios. Dallas, Austin, Denver, and Atlanta provide excellent earning potential ($85,000-$125,000) with moderate cost of living. Midwest markets (Chicago, Minneapolis) offer stable $80,000-$110,000 salaries with lowest cost of living (+5-15% above national average). Strategic location selection can increase net earning potential by 30-50% when accounting for cost of living adjustments.

---

## National Salary Context

### National Average Baseline (25B IT Specialist with 1-3 years experience)

- National average: $70,000-$85,000
- With certifications (Security+, CCNA): $80,000-$100,000
- Senior IT specialist (5+ years): $95,000-$120,000
- IT manager/supervisor: $110,000-$155,000+
- National cost of living: Baseline (100%)

## Tier 1 Metro Areas (Highest Salaries)

**Salary Ranges:**
- IT specialist entry: $100,000-$120,000
- Mid-level IT specialist: $120,000-$145,000
- Senior IT specialist: $145,000-$180,000
- IT manager: $155,000-$220,000+

**Cost of Living:** +60-65% above national average
- Rent: $2,200-$3,500/month
- Home price: $1,500,000-$2,500,000+
- Groceries: +45% vs. national
- Overall: Budget $140,000+ salary needed for comfortable living

**Why High Salaries:**
- Silicon Valley tech companies (Apple, Google, Facebook, Netflix, Salesforce)
- Cloud computing expertise (AWS, Azure growth)
- Venture capital and startup ecosystem
- Intense competition for IT talent

**Pros:** Maximum earning potential, cutting-edge tech, networking, startup opportunities
**Cons:** Extremely high cost of living, crowded, traffic, expensive housing

**5-Year Earning Potential:** $610,000-$750,000
**Net After Cost of Living:** $350,000-$450,000 (significantly reduced)

## Conclusion

25B IT specialists should strategically choose location based on career goals and personal preferences. San Francisco offers highest nominal salaries but extreme costs reduce net benefit.
