## What Does a 35S Signals Collector/Analyst Do?

As a 35S, you conducted signals collection and analysis operations targeting foreign communications and non-communications emitters. You operated SIGINT collection systems including receivers, direction-finding equipment, spectrum analyzers, and technical collection platforms. You analyzed signals across the electromagnetic spectrum to identify threat emitters, determine technical characteristics, and extract intelligence. You conducted electronic warfare support by identifying and characterizing threat radar, communications, and electronic systems. You performed geolocation operations to pinpoint enemy emitter locations. You maintained electronic order of battle (EOB) databases and produced technical intelligence reports on threat capabilities.

Your responsibilities included monitoring and collecting signals according to NSA tasking procedures, conducting technical analysis of RF signals to identify emitter types and characteristics, operating direction-finding and geolocation systems, producing SIGINT reports including technical reports and time-sensitive intelligence, collaborating with SIGINT analysts and other intelligence disciplines, maintaining and troubleshooting SIGINT collection equipment, and supporting electronic warfare operations through technical intelligence.

This role required technical proficiency with RF systems and electronics, understanding of communications theory and electronic warfare principles, analytical thinking to identify patterns in complex technical data, attention to technical detail, troubleshooting and problem-solving skills, report writing abilities, and TS/SCI clearance to handle NSA systems and SIGINT operations.

## Required Certifications & Training

### High Priority (Get These)

**CompTIA Security+**

Baseline cybersecurity certification required for DoD contractor positions.

- **Cost:** $400-600 exam
- **Time:** 2-6 weeks study
- **ROI:** Required for most defense SIGINT/EW contractor roles
- **Action:** Get this 6-12 months before separation (Army COOL may fund)

**Maintain TS/SCI Clearance**

Your clearance with SIGINT technical expertise is worth $25,000-$40,000 in salary premium.

- **Cost:** $0 if you accept cleared job within 24 months
- **Value:** Required for 85% of highest-paying 35S positions
- **Action:** Accept cleared position within 24 months to keep active

**Bachelor's Degree (Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Computer Science, or related technical field)**

Required for many NSA positions and engineering roles. Preferred for contractor positions.

- **Cost:** $0 with GI Bill
- **Time:** 2-4 years
- **ROI:** Required for government engineering positions, significantly increases competitiveness
- **Action:** Enroll immediately if you don't have one, prioritize technical degree

### Medium Priority (Career Enhancers)

**CISSP (Certified Information Systems Security Professional)**

Advanced security certification, industry standard for senior positions.

- **Cost:** $749 exam + $500-3,000 study
- **Requirements:** 5 years security experience (military counts)
- **ROI:** Average CISSP salary $131,000+
- **Time:** 3-6 months study

**Network+ / CCNA**

Networking certifications demonstrating understanding of network protocols.

- **Cost:** Network+ $338 exam, CCNA $300-450
- **Value:** Foundation for technical and cybersecurity roles

**GIAC Certifications (GMON, GCIA, etc.)**

Advanced technical certifications from SANS Institute.

- **Cost:** ~$949 exam + $8,275 course = ~$9,000+ per certification
- **ROI:** Average GIAC salary ~$132,000+

**RF/Telecommunications Certifications**

Various industry certifications for RF engineering and telecommunications.

- **Value:** Demonstrates technical RF expertise for commercial positions

**Master's Degree (Electrical Engineering, Cybersecurity, Systems Engineering)**

Accelerates career to senior technical or leadership positions.

- **Cost:** $0-40,000 with GI Bill
- **ROI:** Opens senior engineering and technical leadership roles
- **Top programs:** Georgia Tech, Carnegie Mellon, Johns Hopkins, Virginia Tech

### Low Priority (Nice to Have)

**Vendor-specific certifications** (Cisco, etc.)

Useful for specific technical roles but not broadly critical.

**Cloud certifications** (AWS, Azure)

Growing importance but not immediately necessary for most 35S transitions.
