# Best Great Plains States for Veterans 2025

The Great Plains represent America's most affordable region for military retirees, combining exceptional tax benefits for military retirement, very low cost of living, agricultural heritage, and increasingly diverse job markets. This guide ranks the best Great Plains states for transitioning service members seeking maximum purchasing power.

## Top 3 Great Plains States for Veterans

### 1. TEXAS - BEST OVERALL GREAT PLAINS CHOICE

**Why Texas Dominates:**
- No state income tax: On any income source (ultimate advantage)
- Military retirement: 100% tax-free
- Cost of living: Low-moderate (97 index)
- Job market: Excellent (especially tech, energy, healthcare)
- Military presence: Numerous bases, strong culture
- VA healthcare: Extensive statewide network
- Housing: Median $315,000 (very affordable)
- Climate: Warm, minimal winter

**Financial Advantages:**
- **Zero state income tax** on all income
- Military retirement completely tax-free
- Other income completely tax-free
- Property tax: 1.8% (higher rate but no income tax offset)
- Very affordable housing throughout state
- Growing job market

**Best Cities:**
- **San Antonio:** Very affordable ($290K), military culture, excellent VA, growing tech
- **Austin:** Tech hub, higher cost ($480K), excellent job market, outdoors access
- **Dallas:** Major city, good jobs, affordable suburbs ($380K)
- **Houston:** Large metro, diverse economy, good jobs ($310K)

**Financial Profile - $50K Military Pension + $10K Other:**
- State income tax: $0
- Property tax: ~$5,400/year on $300K home
- Cost of living: 97 index
- **Annual cost: ~$38,000-$40,000**

**10-Year Cost: ~$380,000-$400,000**

**Outdoor Recreation:**
- Hunting and fishing (abundant)
- State parks (95 parks)
- Hill Country outdoor recreation
- Water recreation (lakes, rivers)
- Hiking trails throughout

**Recommendation:** Texas optimal for military retirees wanting no state income tax combined with good job opportunities and affordability.

---

### 2. KANSAS - LOWEST PROPERTY TAX + MILITARY EXEMPTION

**Why Kansas Appeals to Budget-Conscious Retirees:**
- Military retirement: 100% exempt
- Property tax: 0.71% (lowest Great Plains)
- Cost of living: 98 index (very low)
- Housing: Median $220,000 (very affordable)
- Climate: Four seasons, tornado consideration
- Job market: Moderate, growing aerospace (Wichita)

**Financial Advantages:**
- Military retirement completely tax-free
- Lowest property tax rate in region: ~$2,130/year on $300K
- Very low cost of living
- Very affordable housing
- 30-year property tax savings: $30,000+ vs national average

**Best Cities:**
- **Wichita:** Aerospace hub, growing economy ($220K)
- **Topeka:** Capital city, government jobs, affordable ($200K)
- **Smaller towns:** Extremely affordable housing ($160K)

**Financial Profile - $45K Pension Only:**
- State income tax: $0
- Property tax: ~$2,130/year
- Cost of living: 98 index
- **Annual cost: ~$30,000-$32,000 (lowest Great Plains)**

**10-Year Cost: ~$300,000-$320,000**

**Drawbacks:**
- Tornado season (more severe than Texas/Missouri)
- Smaller job market
- Less diverse recreation

**Recommendation:** Kansas best for retirees prioritizing absolute affordability over job opportunities or recreation diversity.

---

### 3. MISSOURI - BALANCED OPTION

**Why Missouri Deserves Consideration:**
- Military retirement: 100% exempt
- Cost of living: Very low (100 index)
- Job market: Better than Kansas
- Property tax: 0.97% (moderate)
- Housing: Median $235,000 (affordable)
- Climate: Moderate winters, less tornadic than Kansas

**Financial Profile:**
- Military retirement tax-free
- Property tax: ~$2,910/year
- Cost of living: Very low
- Better job market than Kansas
- Good VA healthcare network

**Best Cities:**
- **St. Louis:** Major city, diverse economy ($240K)
- **Kansas City:** Metro area, good opportunities ($260K)
- **Springfield:** Small city, affordable ($190K)

**Financial Profile - $50K Pension + $10K Other:**
- State income tax: $910
- Property tax: ~$2,910
- Cost of living: 100 index
- **Annual cost: ~$34,500**

**10-Year Cost: ~$345,000**

**Recommendation:** Missouri ideal balance between affordability, job opportunities, and lifestyle quality.

## Recommendations by Great Plains Veteran Profile

### Budget-Conscious Retirees ($45K Pension Only)
**Best: Kansas**
- Lowest annual costs (~$33K)
- Military retirement tax-free
- Lowest property taxes
- **Challenge:** Limited job market, tornadoes
- Recommendation: Wichita or small towns

### Military Retirees Seeking Balance
**Best: Missouri**
- Affordable but not absolute minimum
- Better job market than Kansas
- Better weather than Kansas
- Good VA healthcare
- Recommendation: Springfield, St. Louis suburbs

### Working Veterans or Career-Focused
**Best: Texas**
- No state income tax (major advantage)
- Excellent job market + salaries
- Strong military culture/base presence
- Extensive VA healthcare
- Best for: San Antonio, Austin (tech), Houston
- **Trade-off:** Higher property tax

### Veterans Wanting Urban Amenities
**Best: Texas (Dallas, Houston) or Missouri (St. Louis)**
- Texas: Larger metro areas, more opportunities
- Missouri: More affordable than Texas cities
- Recommendation: Dallas suburbs or St. Louis

### Disabled Veterans on Fixed Income
**Best: Kansas or Missouri**
- Military retirement tax-free
- Low cost of living
- Moderate property taxes (especially Kansas)
- Recommendation: Kansas small towns or Missouri Springfield

## Conclusion

The Great Plains represents **America's best region for budget-conscious military retirees**, combining:
- No or exempted military retirement income
- Lowest cost of living (Kansas, Missouri)
- Affordable housing
- Strong military culture (especially Texas)
- Growing job markets (especially Texas)
- Good and growing VA healthcare
- Outdoor recreation (hunting, fishing)

**Texas dominates** for working veterans wanting no income tax + job opportunities.

**Missouri offers best balance** of affordability, opportunities, and quality of life.

**Kansas best for retirees** prioritizing absolute lowest costs.

A military retiree with $55,000 annual income can achieve excellent quality of life for $33,000-$39,000 annually in the Great Plains—enabling travel, recreation, and substantial savings. The region's combination of tax benefits, affordability, military presence, and growing economies makes it increasingly attractive for next-generation military retirees.
