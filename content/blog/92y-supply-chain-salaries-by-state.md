# 92Y Supply Specialist: State-by-State Salary Analysis

## BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

92Y Supply Specialists earn highest salaries as supply chain managers in major logistics hubs and Fortune 500 headquarters. Texas (Dallas/Houston): $100,000-$140,000+, no state income tax advantage. California (SF): $110,000-$160,000+ but extreme cost-of-living. Illinois (Chicago): $105,000-$145,000 with reasonable cost-of-living. Georgia (Atlanta): $95,000-$135,000+ with affordable costs. New York: $100,000-$145,000+ with strong supply chain presence. Strategic state selection and supply chain specialization impact 20-year earnings by $600,000-$1,200,000+.

- **Supply Chain Specialist:** $85,000-$105,000
- **Supply Chain Manager:** $110,000-$155,000+
- **Director:** $150,000-$220,000+

## Strategic Recommendations

**Highest Earning:** San Francisco/California supply chain managers ($600,000-$850,000 nominal)

**Best Net Earning:** Texas supply chain (no state income tax) ($550,000-$800,000 net)

**Best Balance:** Chicago or Atlanta (good salary, reasonable costs)

**Fastest Advancement:** Growing logistics hubs (Atlanta, Austin, Dallas)

## Conclusion

92Y Supply Specialists should prioritize Texas for best net earning with no state income tax advantage. California offers highest nominal salaries but costs reduce net benefit. Chicago offers good balance of salary and costs. Strategic state selection impacts 20-year earnings by $600,000-$1,200,000+.

**Key Takeaway:** Texas: $550,000-$800,000 (net, 5-year). San Francisco: $290,000-$420,000 (net, after cost). Chicago: $500,000-$725,000 (net). Strategic state selection critical for supply chain careers.
