## How to Maximize Your Chances with These Companies

### 1. Translate Your 68W Experience Effectively

**Don't write:** "68W Combat Medic, provided medical care to soldiers"

**Do write:** "Emergency medical professional with 5,000+ patient care hours spanning trauma stabilization, primary care, pharmacology, and emergency response in high-stress environments"

### 2. Highlight Specific Clinical Skills

- Emergency medical procedures (airway management, IV therapy, trauma care)
- Patient assessment and triage
- Medication administration and pharmacology
- Documentation and medical records
- Medical equipment operation
- Sterile technique and infection control
- Numbers: "Managed care for 300+ patients," "Performed 500+ emergency medical procedures"

### 3. Get Foundational Certifications Before Applying

- **NREMT (National Registry EMT):** Required for EMS positions, 68W can challenge exam
- **BLS/ACLS:** Basic and Advanced Cardiac Life Support - $200-400 total
- **CMA (Certified Medical Assistant):** Quick certification for MA roles
- **State licenses:** Required for some positions

### 4. Use Your Network

- Connect with veteran employees at target companies (LinkedIn)
- Join company veteran employee resource groups
- Attend military hiring events and job fairs
- Use veteran recruiting services (Hiring Our Heroes, RecruitMilitary)

### 5. Apply Strategically

- Apply to 10-20 positions at once (increases odds)
- Follow up 1 week after application
- Prepare for interviews: Clinical scenarios, behavioral questions
- Emphasize adaptability, teamwork, and clinical judgment

### 6. Consider Geographic Flexibility

- Some regions pay significantly higher (California, Northeast)
- Rural and underserved areas offer loan repayment incentives
- Some companies offer relocation assistance
- Remote positions available with some companies (case management, utilization review)
