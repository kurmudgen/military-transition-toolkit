# Idaho vs Montana Veterans Comparison: Complete Guide 2025

Idaho and Montana represent two of America's most picturesque states with strong outdoor recreation, growing economies, and exceptional quality of life. Both appeal to veterans seeking outdoor living combined with affordable housing. This comprehensive guide compares these Northern Rockies destinations.

## Military Retirement Tax Treatment

### Idaho: Military Retirement Exempt

Idaho provides complete military retirement exemption:

**Idaho Military Retirement Benefits:**
- Military pension: 100% exempt from state income tax
- VA disability compensation: Exempt
- State income tax rate: 1%-5.8% (progressive)
- Effective tax on military retirement: 0%

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Military retirement tax: $0
- Other income tax: $950 (at 4.75% effective)
- **Total state tax: $950**

### Montana: Military Retirement Exempt with Extra Benefits

Montana also exempts military retirement:

**Montana Military Retirement Benefits:**
- Military pension: 100% exempt from state income tax
- VA disability compensation: Exempt
- State income tax rate: 1%-6.9% (progressive)
- Effective tax on military retirement: 0%

**Tax Example - $45,000 military pension + $20,000 other income:**
- Military retirement tax: $0
- Other income tax: $1,200 (at 6% effective)
- **Total state tax: $1,200**

**Tax Winner: Idaho** (Saves $250 annually on other income)

## Outdoor Recreation and Lifestyle

### Idaho Recreation

**Recreation Profile:**
- Mountains: Sawtooth, White Cloud mountains
- Skiing: Sun Valley, Schweitzer Mountain
- Rivers: Salmon River, Snake River
- Lakes: Payette Lake, Coeur d'Alene Lake
- Hiking/Backpacking: Excellent trails
- Fishing: World-class fly fishing
- Year-round outdoor activities

**Community:**
- Outdoor enthusiast culture
- Growing tech culture (Boise)
- Family-friendly communities
- Less crowded than Montana ski towns

### Montana Recreation

**Recreation Profile:**
- Mountains: Northern Rockies, Beartooth Range
- Skiing: Big Sky (premier resort), other mountains
- Rivers: Yellowstone, Missouri rivers
- Lakes: Flathead Lake (largest freshwater west of Mississippi)
- Hiking: Some of America's best trails
- Fishing: Excellent fly fishing
- National Parks: Glacier, Yellowstone access

**Community:**
- Outdoor culture very strong
- College towns (Missoula, Bozeman)
- Less developed, more "frontier" feel
- More crowded in popular areas (ski towns)

**Recreation Winner: Tie** (Both exceptional; Montana has national parks, Idaho has emerging tech culture)

## Final Verdict

### Choose Idaho if:
- You want better job opportunities (tech boom)
- You prefer higher salaries
- You want better VA healthcare
- You like emerging tech culture with mountains
- You seek outdoor recreation plus career growth

### Choose Montana if:
- You want 1% lower cost of living
- You prioritize outdoor recreation focus
- You value national parks proximity
- You prefer more "frontier" lifestyle
- You want less development pressure

**The Numbers:** Montana saves approximately $9,900 over 10 years due to slightly lower costs, though Idaho's job market (especially tech) may offset this for working professionals.

Idaho's rapidly growing tech sector and higher salaries make it attractive for career-focused veterans, while Montana's slightly lower costs and national park access appeal to outdoor-focused retirees.

**For working tech professionals, Idaho is superior.** For outdoor-focused retirees, Montana offers slightly better value. Both offer exceptional outdoor recreation combined with affordable living by national standards.

**Critical Consideration:** Idaho's housing market is experiencing rapid inflation due to tech boom. Prices rising faster than Montana. Long-term value may favor Montana as prices stabilize.
