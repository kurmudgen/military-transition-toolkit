# Minnesota Veterans: Healthcare Facilities, Education Benefits & Job Resources

Minnesota serves approximately 314,000 veterans - about 7.0% of the state's population. Whether you're transitioning to civilian life or already living in Minnesota, you need to know what healthcare facilities, education benefits, and job opportunities are available.

This guide provides actual addresses, phone numbers, and actionable information for Minnesota veterans.

## Bottom Line Up Front

**Healthcare**: Minnesota has 1 VA health care system with multiple campuses, 8+ community-based clinics, and excellent private healthcare options

**Education**: Free tuition at state schools for dependents of deceased/100% disabled veterans, strong GI Bill support, Yellow Ribbon programs

**Employment**: State hiring preference, Fortune 500 headquarters (Target, UnitedHealth, 3M, General Mills), medical device industry, strong economy with low unemployment

**Veteran Population Hotspots**: Hennepin County (Minneapolis - 45,000+), Ramsey County (St. Paul - 25,000+), Dakota County (25,000+)

## Education Benefits for Minnesota Veterans

### Free Tuition for Dependents of Deceased/100% Disabled Veterans

This is Minnesota's premier education benefit for veteran families.

**Minnesota State Grant for Dependents of Veterans**

**Eligibility**:
- Children (and spouses in some cases) of veterans who:
  - Died as a result of service-connected disability
  - Are rated 100% permanently and totally disabled by the VA
  - Are classified as Missing in Action or Prisoner of War
  - Died on active duty or from service-connected causes
- Must be Minnesota resident
- Must be under age 30
- Must maintain satisfactory academic progress

**Benefit amount**:
- **Free tuition** at Minnesota State system schools
- Covers undergraduate and graduate programs
- Also provides stipend for books and fees

**Qualifying schools**:
- Minnesota State University campuses (formerly MnSCU):
  - Bemidji State University
  - Minnesota State University, Mankato
  - Minnesota State University Moorhead
  - Metropolitan State University
  - St. Cloud State University
  - Southwest Minnesota State University
  - Winona State University
- All Minnesota State community and technical colleges (30 campuses)

**Duration**: Until bachelor's degree is earned or student reaches age 30

**How to apply**:
1. Apply through Minnesota Office of Higher Education
2. Provide VA rating documentation or death certificate
3. Provide proof of relationship to veteran
4. Apply annually for renewal

**This is one of the most generous dependent education benefits in the country.**

### Minnesota GI Bill State Tuition Reimbursement

**Eligibility**: Minnesota veterans who served after September 11, 2001, and are eligible for federal education benefits

**Benefit**: Reimbursement for tuition and fees not covered by federal GI Bill benefits at Minnesota public schools

**Maximum**: Up to 50% of tuition costs at Minnesota public institutions

**How to use**: Enroll in Minnesota public school, use federal GI Bill first, then apply for state reimbursement for remaining balance

### In-State Tuition for Veterans

Under the Veterans Access, Choice, and Accountability Act (VACAA), veterans using GI Bill benefits qualify for in-state tuition at Minnesota public schools, regardless of residency status.

**Eligibility**:
- Using Post-9/11 GI Bill or Montgomery GI Bill
- Living in Minnesota while attending school
- Enrolling within 3 years of discharge

## Employment Resources for Minnesota Veterans

### State Hiring Preference

Minnesota law provides hiring preference to veterans for state government positions.

**Who qualifies**:
- Honorably discharged veterans
- Disabled veterans receive additional preference (10 points)
- Non-disabled veterans receive 5-point preference

**How it works**: Veterans who meet minimum qualifications receive preference points added to their application scores.

**Where to apply**: [mn.gov/careers](https://www.mn.gov/careers) (Minnesota Management and Budget)

Minnesota has over 30,000 state employees, making this a significant opportunity.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Enroll in VA healthcare immediately** - Minneapolis VA provides excellent care with campuses throughout Minnesota
2. **Use free tuition benefit** - If you're 100% disabled or deceased, your children get free tuition at state schools
3. **Leverage state hiring preference** - Minnesota state government employs 30,000+ with veteran preference
4. **Target Fortune 500 companies** - 18 headquarters in Minnesota, most actively recruit veterans

Minnesota's veteran support infrastructure is excellent, with strong healthcare, generous education benefits, and exceptional employment opportunities in one of the nation's strongest economies.
