# Best Companies for 15W UAV Operators

## BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

15W UAV Operators transition to high-demand positions at aerospace companies, defense contractors, commercial drone operators, tech firms, and government agencies. Compensation ranges $70,000-$150,000+ depending on company and specialization. Top employers include DJI (commercial drones), Auterion, Insitu/Boeing, General Atomics, Lockheed Martin, Google Wing, Amazon Prime Air, and government contractors. Your military UAV expertise, advanced piloting skills, and operational knowledge directly translates to premium drone operations and development roles. Most 15Ws transition within 2-4 months with strong demand and certification advantages.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Which company pays most for 15W UAV operators?**
A: Amazon Prime Air leads at $95,000-$130,000 starting + $30,000-$80,000 signing bonus. Google Wing follows at $90,000-$125,000. Year 5+ earning potential: $150,000-$200,000+ at both.

**Q: What's Part 107 and do I need it?**
A: FAA Remote Pilot Certificate required for commercial drone operation. Your military experience exempts you from some testing. Can obtain in 2-3 weeks with your background.

**Q: Should I choose tech (Google/Amazon) or defense contractors?**
A: **Tech**: Higher compensation, faster growth, equity upside. **Defense**: Military background valued, clearance advantage, stable. Both excellent; depends on growth vs. stability preference.

**Q: What's the commercial drone industry outlook?**
A: Booming. Delivery, inspection, agriculture, construction creating massive demand. Fastest-growing job market in aerospace/unmanned systems. Year 10+ demand very strong.

**Q: Are there remote opportunities?**
A: No, field operations required. Some office roles (managers, planners) increasingly remote after experience.

**Q: Can I transition to drone software development?**
A: Yes, increasingly common. Operators often transition to product management or autonomy engineering at tech companies. Supplemental computer science education helps.
