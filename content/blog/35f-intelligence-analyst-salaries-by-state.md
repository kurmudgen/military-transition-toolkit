# 35F Intelligence Analyst: Salaries by State

## BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

35F Intelligence Analysts earn highest salaries in federal contractor hubs where intelligence operations concentrate. Washington DC/Maryland/Virginia metro: $100,000-$150,000+. Northern Virginia (contractor capital): $110,000-$170,000+. Colorado Springs (NORAD/cyber): $95,000-$140,000+. San Antonio/Austin (intelligence focus): $90,000-$135,000+. National salary range: $70,000-$150,000+ depending on location, clearance, and contractor. Strategic location selection impacts 20-year earnings by $800,000-$1,500,000+ accounting for cost-of-living differences.

---

## Top Paying States for 35F Intelligence

### Virginia (Federal Contractor Capital)
- **Salary Range:** $110,000-$170,000+
- **Key Hubs:** Northern Virginia (Alexandria, Falls Church, Arlington), Quantico area
- **Contractors:** BAH, SAIC, Deloitte, Raytheon, Leidos
- **Clearance Premium:** +$30,000-$50,000
- **Cost of Living:** +25-35%
- **5-Year Earning:** $575,000-$850,000 (gross)
- **Net After Cost:** $425,000-$650,000

### Maryland (Intelligence Community Nexus)
- **Salary Range:** $100,000-$150,000+
- **Key Hubs:** Fort Meade (NSA headquarters), Baltimore-Washington Corridor
- **Employers:** NSA, Contractors, Federal agencies
- **Federal Position Advantage:** Pension, benefits
- **Cost of Living:** +20-30%
- **5-Year Earning:** $525,000-$750,000
- **Net After Cost:** $415,000-$580,000

### DC Metro (Federal & Contracting Blend)
- **Salary Range:** $95,000-$140,000
- **Employers:** Federal, contractors, think tanks
- **Intelligence Community Jobs:** Dense employment market
- **Cost of Living:** +25-30%
- **Federal Jobs:** Government contractor roles
- **5-Year Earning:** $500,000-$725,000
- **Net After Cost:** $375,000-$550,000

### Colorado (Intelligence & Tech Hub)
- **Salary Range:** $90,000-$135,000
- **Key Hub:** Colorado Springs (NORAD, space force intel), Denver
- **Employers:** Military intelligence, contractors, tech companies
- **Cost of Living:** +10-20%
- **5-Year Earning:** $460,000-$685,000
- **Net After Cost:** $410,000-$600,000 (excellent ratio)

### Texas (Emerging Intelligence Hub)
- **Salary Range:** $85,000-$130,000+
- **Key Hubs:** San Antonio (military intel), Austin (intelligence community expansion)
- **No State Income Tax:** Critical advantage (+$12,000-$18,000/year)
- **Cost of Living:** +10-15%
- **5-Year Earning:** $430,000-$650,000
- **Net After Cost + Tax Savings:** $450,000-$700,000 (excellent after-tax)

## Secondary Markets

### Massachusetts (Boston Area)

- **Cost of Living:** +20-25%

## Strategic Recommendations

**Highest Earning Potential:** Northern Virginia federal contracting ($575,000-$850,000 gross over 5 years)

**Best After-Tax Value:** Austin, Texas no state income tax + moderate cost ($450,000-$700,000 net)

**Best Balance:** Colorado Springs (good salary + reasonable costs + intelligence focus)

**Fastest Advancement:** Northern Virginia contractors due to dense opportunity market

---

## Conclusion

35F Intelligence Analysts should prioritize Northern Virginia federal contracting for highest nominal earnings and career advancement. Texas offers excellent after-tax value with no income tax. Colorado Springs provides good salary/cost balance. Strategic location selection impacts lifetime earnings by $800,000-$1,500,000+.

**Key Takeaway:** Northern Virginia: $575,000-$850,000 (5-year), Austin: $450,000-$700,000 (net, after-tax). Federal contractor roles in intelligence hubs provide fastest path to six-figure compensation.
