## A/B Test Hypothesis

### What We're Testing
**Primary:** Does emphasizing risk-free trial (no credit card, guarantee, safety) convert better than feature/benefit-focused messaging?

**Secondary:** Does removing friction (no credit card) increase trial sign-ups even if conversion to paid is lower?

**Tertiary:** Does transparency about the process build trust and lead to higher quality leads?

### Expected Winner
**High confidence this will be a top-3 performer.**

**Why it should work:**
- **Removes barrier:** No credit card = lowest friction possible
- **Builds trust:** Transparency and honesty resonate with veteran audience
- **Addresses skepticism:** Many veterans are skeptical of "free trials" that auto-charge
- **Risk-reversal:** "You win either way" positioning is powerful

**Potential risks:**
- **May attract tire-kickers:** People who sign up with no intention to pay
- **Lower conversion rate:** Trial sign-ups may be higher but trial-to-paid may be lower
- **Trust vs urgency:** Low pressure may lead to procrastination vs immediate action

**Prediction:** #1-2 in trial sign-ups, #3-5 in trial-to-paid conversion. Overall strong performer, especially for skeptical audience.

### Success Metrics
- **Primary:** Free trial sign-up rate (target: 20%+ due to low friction)
- **Secondary:** Trial-to-paid conversion rate (target: 5-7% ‚Äî lower than other variants)
- **Quality:** Trial engagement (do free users actually use the platform?)
- **Long-term:** Lifetime value (hypothesis: lower initial conversion but higher retention once they pay)
- **Trust:** Return visit rate (users come back after thinking about it)

### Statistical Significance Needed
- Minimum 300 visitors per variant
- 95% confidence level
- Run for 14-21 days (longer to see trial-to-paid conversion)
- Track trial engagement metrics (% who actually use features during trial)
- Monitor long-term: trial-to-paid timeline (how many days before they pay?)
