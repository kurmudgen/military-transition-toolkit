## FAQ Section

### Question 1: Is this just another TAP resource website?
No. This is your personal transition command center. We don't duplicate TAP—we organize all the information TAP gave you, plus benefits calculators, state-specific guides, career tools, and financial planning. Think of it as your TAP binder, but actually useful.

### Question 2: What if I'm not tech-savvy?
If you can check email, you can use this. Clean interface, no complicated menus, no overwhelm. We built this for E4s and O6s alike—simple enough for anyone.

### Question 3: How is this different from bookmarking websites?
Bookmarks don't calculate your GI Bill value. Bookmarks don't tell you which state has better property tax exemptions. Bookmarks don't save your profile and personalize recommendations. This actually works.

### Question 4: What if I'm still 18 months from separation?
Perfect. The earlier you start, the better. You'll catch benefits you would've missed, avoid last-minute panic, and actually have a plan instead of scrambling at month 11.

### Question 5: Do you sell my data or spam me?
Hell no. We're veterans. We hate that crap too. Your data is yours. We don't sell anything. No spam. Just the platform and occasional useful updates (that you can turn off).

### Question 6: What if I've already started my transition and feel behind?
You're not behind. Most people don't start planning until 90 days out and end up scrambling. Wherever you are, MTT meets you there and shows you what's next. Better to start now than keep procrastinating.

### Question 7: Is this free during the government shutdown?
Yes. While .mil email verification is down, we've made the platform completely free. No credit card needed. When verification comes back, you'll get grandfathered into founder pricing.

## Design Notes

### Layout Recommendations
- **Hero:** Full-width, high-contrast background (dark blue/navy). White text. Large, bold headline. CTA button in bright orange (#FF6B35) to stand out.
- **Value Prop:** Two-column layout (desktop). Left: pain point bullets. Right: solution summary.
- **Features:** Grid layout, 2x3 on desktop, stacked on mobile. Each feature gets an icon (trash can, lightbulb, clock, etc.).
- **Social Proof:** Card-based testimonials with photos (if available) or branch insignias.
- **FAQ:** Accordion-style to save space. Expand on click.
- **Final CTA:** Full-width section, contrasting background color (green #10B981), large button.

### Color Psychology
- **Primary CTA:** Orange (#FF6B35) = urgency, action
- **Secondary CTA:** Green (#10B981) = safety, go
- **Background:** Navy/dark blue = trust, military connection
- **Text:** White on dark, dark on light (high contrast)

### Visual Hierarchy
1. Headline (largest, bold)
2. Subheadline (medium, lighter weight)
3. CTA button (bright, contrasting)
4. Supporting text (smallest, but readable)

### CTA Button Colors/Placement
- **Above-fold:** Orange button, center-aligned, 60px height
- **Mid-page:** Green buttons in feature sections
- **Final CTA:** Large orange button, 70px height, center-aligned
- Always include: "No credit card required" text near button

### Image Suggestions
- **Hero:** Blurred image of transition documents/laptop/stressed service member
- **Value Prop:** Before/after visual (messy tabs vs clean dashboard)
- **Features:** Screenshots of actual platform (dashboard, calculators, guides)
- **Social Proof:** Branch insignias or anonymous silhouettes
- **Final CTA:** Clean, minimal—focus on button

## A/B Test Hypothesis

### What We're Testing
**Primary:** Does leading with pain points (frustration, overwhelm, wasted time) convert better than leading with benefits/solutions?

**Secondary:** Does explicitly calling out "12+ websites" and "browser tab chaos" resonate more than generic "complicated transition" messaging?

### Expected Winner
**High confidence this will perform well** with service members who are mid-transition (6-12 months out) and already feeling overwhelmed. May underperform with early-stage planners (18-24 months out) who aren't feeling the pain yet.

**Prediction:** 2nd or 3rd best performer overall. Will beat feature-focused pages but may lose to founder story or benefit-focused variants.

### Success Metrics
- **Primary:** Email capture rate (target: 15%+)
- **Secondary:** Free trial sign-ups (target: 8%+)
- **Engagement:** Scroll depth to FAQ section (target: 60%+)
- **Quality:** Trial-to-paid conversion rate (target: 5%+)

### Statistical Significance Needed
- Minimum 200 visitors per variant
- 95% confidence level
- Run for at least 7 days to account for day-of-week variance
- Track by traffic source (organic, paid, referral)

## Mobile Optimization Notes

### Critical Elements
1. **Headline:** Must be readable without zooming—max 40 characters, 32px font minimum
2. **CTA Button:** Thumb-friendly size (48px height minimum), sticky bottom bar option
3. **Features:** Stack vertically, one per viewport
4. **Testimonials:** Swipe carousel instead of grid
5. **FAQ:** Accordion closed by default to reduce scroll

### Mobile-Specific Copy Adjustments
- Shorten headline if needed: "Drowning in TAP Worksheets and VA Forms?"
- Reduce subheadline: "Stop wasting hours on 12+ websites. One platform. Everything."
- CTA text: "Start Free Trial" (drop "No Credit Card Required" to button subtext)

### Performance
- Hero image: Compress to <200KB for mobile
- Lazy load testimonials and FAQ sections
- Preload CTA button interaction (no delay on tap)
