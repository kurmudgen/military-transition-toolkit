[INTRO]
Sergeant First Class Roberto Garcia, 68W Combat Medic with 12 years experience and 4 deployments, advanced to Nurse Practitioner earning $125K through strategic use of education benefits and VA career ladder programs.

[MAIN CONTENT]
**Military:** 68W Combat Medic, battalion aid station NCOIC, managed trauma care in combat, trained junior medics.
**Education Path:** ASN (VA + Pell Grants, 2 years), BSN (GI Bill, 2 years), MSN-NP (VA healthcare scholarship program, 2 years). Total: 6 years, minimal debt.
**Career:** RN at VA hospital during BSN ($65K), NP at VA after MSN ($125K). Works in primary care clinic, 4-day work week.
**Keys:** VA actively recruits veteran healthcare workers, tuition assistance programs for employees, experience credit accelerated advancement.

[CONCLUSION]
Roberto went from Combat Medic to $125K Nurse Practitioner in 6 years using stacked education benefits. VA healthcare provides career ladder for veteran medical professionals.
