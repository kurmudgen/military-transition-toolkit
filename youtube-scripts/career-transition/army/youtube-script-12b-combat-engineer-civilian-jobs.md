[MAIN CONTENT - 7-8 minutes]

**Section 1: Your Market Value (60 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Salary ranges by position]

**Entry-level (0-2 years civilian):**
- Construction laborer: $35,000-$50,000/year
- Equipment operator: $45,000-$65,000/year
- Construction technician: $40,000-$60,000/year

**Mid-level (3-5 years):**
- Foreman/supervisor: $55,000-$75,000/year
- Project coordinator: $60,000-$80,000/year
- Estimator: $60,000-$85,000/year

**Senior level (5-10 years):**
- Project manager: $75,000-$110,000/year
- Construction manager: $80,000-$120,000/year
- Superintendent: $85,000-$130,000/year

**With degree:**
- Civil engineer: $68,000-$108,000/year

**Your skills:** Heavy equipment operation, demolition knowledge, construction basics, project coordination, safety protocols. These matter.

**Section 2: Construction Management (No Degree Required) (90 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Career progression path]

**Path to $85K+ without bachelor's degree:**

**Years 0-2: Start as Equipment Operator or Laborer**
- Equipment operator: $45,000-$65,000/year
- You already know excavators, dozers, loaders
- Many companies hire 12Bs directly into operator roles

**Years 2-4: Move to Foreman**
- Foreman: $55,000-$75,000/year
- Lead small crews (3-10 workers)
- Your military leadership experience = valuable

**Years 4-7: Project Coordinator or Estimator**
- $60,000-$85,000/year
- Learn to read blueprints (you have basic skills)
- Estimate material costs and timelines

**Years 7-10: Project Manager or Superintendent**
- $75,000-$120,000/year
- Manage entire construction projects
- Coordinate multiple crews
- Your combat engineer project experience translates directly

**Companies that hire 12Bs:**
- Bechtel (major projects worldwide)
- Fluor Corporation
- KBR (defense and infrastructure)
- Turner Construction
- Kiewit Corporation
- Local construction companies (often best path)

**Section 3: Heavy Equipment Operator (Immediate Income) (90 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Equipment types and pay rates]

This is your fastest path to income. You already know this equipment.

**Equipment operator salaries:**
- Excavator operator: $45,000-$70,000/year
- Dozer operator: $50,000-$75,000/year
- Crane operator: $55,000-$85,000/year
- Grader operator: $50,000-$70,000/year

**Union operators (highest pay):**
- Operating Engineers union
- Hourly rates: $35-$55/hour
- Full benefits and pension
- Apprenticeship programs available

**Required certifications:**
- OSHA 10 or OSHA 30: $50-$150
- Equipment-specific certs: Often free through employer
- Commercial Driver's License (CDL): Helpful but not always required

**Top markets for equipment operators:**
- Alaska: $70,000-$95,000/year (high demand, harsh conditions)
- California: $60,000-$85,000/year
- New York: $55,000-$80,000/year
- Texas: $45,000-$65,000/year (lower cost of living)

**How to get hired:**
- Apply directly to construction companies
- Join Operating Engineers union (Local unions by area)
- Search "equipment operator" + your city
- Many companies train if you have military heavy equipment experience

**Section 4: Civil Engineering (The Degree Path) (90 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Education path and ROI]

If you want the professional engineering title and $100K+ ceiling, you need a degree.

**Civil Engineer salary: $68,000-$108,000/year**
- Entry-level: $60,000-$75,000/year
- Mid-career: $80,000-$100,000/year
- Senior/PE license: $95,000-$130,000/year

**Education requirement:**
- Bachelor's in Civil Engineering (4 years)
- Many credits transfer from your Army training
- GI Bill covers tuition + housing allowance

**Best programs for veterans:**
- Online civil engineering tech programs (2-year associate)
- State universities with veteran support
- Some schools give credit for 12B training

**Professional Engineer (PE) license:**
- After degree + 4 years experience, take PE exam
- PE license = $10,000-$20,000 salary increase
- Required for signing off on projects

**Is it worth it?**
- Yes if: You want $100K+ career, enjoy technical work, willing to spend 4 years in school
- No if: You want immediate income, don't want desk job, prefer hands-on work

**Alternative:** Construction management degree (often easier than engineering, still leads to high pay)

**Section 5: Demolition Specialist (Unique Path) (60 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Demolition career details]

Your demo experience is rare and valuable.

**Demolition contractor:**
- Salary: $50,000-$85,000/year
- Specialized skill set
- High demand for controlled demolition

**Explosive demolition expert:**
- Salary: $60,000-$95,000/year
- Requires blasting license
- Your Army demo experience = huge advantage

**Environmental remediation:**
- Salary: $55,000-$80,000/year
- Removing hazardous materials, asbestos
- Combines demo with safety knowledge

**Companies:**
- Controlled Demolition Inc.
- NASDM member companies
- Local demolition contractors
- Blasting companies

**Certifications needed:**
- Blaster's license (state-specific)
- OSHA hazmat certification
- Confined space entry

**Section 6: Union Apprenticeships (Free Training + Pay) (90 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Union benefits comparison]

This is the path smart 12Bs take: Get paid while learning advanced skills.

**Operating Engineers Union:**
- Heavy equipment apprenticeship
- Paid from day one: $20-$30/hour as apprentice
- Full journeyman: $40-$55/hour
- 3-4 year program
- Free training, no cost to you
- Full benefits and pension

**Ironworkers Union:**
- Structural steel, reinforcing steel
- Similar pay scale to operating engineers
- Your welding and construction knowledge helps

**Carpenters Union:**
- Formwork, framing, finish carpentry
- $25-$45/hour journeyman wage
- 4-year apprenticeship

**Electricians (IBEW):**
- Electrical construction
- $35-$50/hour journeyman
- Your technical aptitude from Army helps

**How to join:**
- Google "[your city] operating engineers local"
- Apply to apprenticeship program
- May have entrance exam and interview
- Veterans often get preference

**Why union over non-union:**
- Higher pay (20-40% more)
- Benefits: Health, pension, annuity
- Job security through union hall
- Training throughout career

**Section 7: Certifications That Pay (60 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Certification ROI list]

**High-value certifications:**

**OSHA 30-Hour Construction:** $150-$300
- Required for supervisors
- Salary impact: $5,000-$10,000

**Certified Construction Manager (CCM):** $500-$1,000
- Professional credential
- Salary impact: $10,000-$20,000
- Requires experience + exam

**NCCER Certifications:** Free through some programs
- National Center for Construction Education & Research
- Recognized across construction industry
- Hard Hat Heroes program for veterans

**Equipment-specific certifications:**
- NCCCO Crane Operator: $500-$800
- Forklift certification: $50-$150
- Rigging certification: $300-$600

**Project Management Professional (PMP):** $400-$600
- Not construction-specific but valuable
- Salary impact: $15,000-$25,000
- Free through Hiring Our Heroes

**Use DoD COOL:** Many of these are covered while still in or within 180 days.

**Section 8: Companies Actively Hiring 12Bs (60 seconds)**

[Visual suggestion: Company logos and focus areas]

**Major contractors:**
- **Bechtel:** Infrastructure, oil & gas, nuclear
- **Fluor:** Industrial construction worldwide
- **KBR:** Defense, government infrastructure
- **AECOM:** Engineering and construction
- **Jacobs Engineering:** Diverse projects

**Heavy civil contractors:**
- **Kiewit Corporation:** Highways, bridges, dams
- **Granite Construction:** Infrastructure
- **Skanska:** Large infrastructure projects

**Local and regional:**
- Often the best path for 12Bs
- Faster advancement than mega-contractors
- Google "construction companies [your city]"

**Government contractors:**
- Army Corps of Engineers (civilian)
- State DOT construction projects
- Federal facilities maintenance

**Overseas contracting:**
- Construction in Middle East, Africa
- $80,000-$150,000/year tax-free
- Away from family for 6-12 months at a time

12B Combat Engineers have multiple high-paying paths.
