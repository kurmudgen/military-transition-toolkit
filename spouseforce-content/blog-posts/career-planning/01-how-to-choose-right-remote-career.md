## The 5-Factor Framework: How to Evaluate Any Remote Career

Use this framework to assess whether a remote career is right for you.

### Factor 1: True Portability Score (0-100%)

**Question: Can this career move with me to ANY location, including OCONUS?**

**High Portability (90-100%):**
- Software Developer
- Content Writer
- Graphic Designer
- Virtual Assistant
- Social Media Manager
- Online Course Creator
- E-commerce Business Owner

**Medium Portability (60-89%):**
- Registered Nurse (travel nursing, telehealth growing)
- Real Estate Agent (must re-license in new state)
- Teacher (online schools, certification transfers)
- Accountant (remote roles growing)

**Lower Portability (30-59%):**
- Hair Stylist (physical location required)
- Personal Trainer (hybrid possible, but limited remote)
- Event Planner (local connections critical)

**Action:** Aim for careers with 85%+ portability if you PCS frequently.

---

### Factor 2: Time to Employability

**Question: How long until I can start earning income?**

**Immediate (0-3 months):**
- Virtual Assistant ($0-$500 startup, immediate income)
- Data Entry ($0 startup, start within days)
- Social Media Manager (if you have existing skills)
- Freelance Writer (portfolio in 2-4 weeks)

**Short-Term (3-6 months):**
- Graphic Designer (portfolio + tools)
- Bookkeeper (QuickBooks certification)
- Customer Service Rep (training provided)
- Project Coordinator (if transferable skills)

**Medium-Term (6-12 months):**
- UX/UI Designer (bootcamp or self-study)
- Digital Marketing Manager (certifications + portfolio)
- Software Developer (coding bootcamp)
- Data Analyst (Google/IBM certificate)

**Long-Term (12+ months):**
- Cybersecurity Analyst (degree or extensive certs)
- Registered Nurse (degree + licensing)
- Physical Therapist Assistant (associate degree)
- Teacher (certification/degree)

**Action:** Match timeline to your situation. PCSing in 6 months? Choose immediate or short-term options. Stable for 2 years? Invest in medium/long-term skills.

---

### Factor 3: Lifestyle Compatibility

**Question: Does this career fit my ACTUAL life right now?**

Consider:

**Your Current Family Situation:**
- Babies/toddlers at home ‚Üí Need extreme flexibility (freelancing, asynchronous work)
- School-age kids ‚Üí 9-5 remote job might work well
- Teenagers ‚Üí More traditional structure possible
- No kids ‚Üí More intensive careers feasible

**Upcoming Deployments:**
- Deployment coming? ‚Üí Choose careers with no real-time meetings (writing, design, development)
- Post-deployment? ‚Üí Client-facing roles might work (coaching, consulting, virtual assistant)

**PCS Frequency:**
- PCS every 1-2 years ‚Üí Must be 90%+ portable, minimal client relationship dependency
- Stable for 3+ years ‚Üí Can invest in local + remote hybrid models

**Spouse's Job Demands:**
- High-tempo military job ‚Üí You need flexibility for last-minute schedule changes
- Deployed often ‚Üí Independent work without real-time collaboration needs

**Action:** Be brutally honest about your life right now, not your ideal future life.

## Step-by-Step: Choose Your Remote Career in 7 Days

### Day 1: Self-Assessment

**Take These Free Assessments:**
1. **16Personalities** (personality type) - 16personalities.com
2. **CliftonStrengths** (top 5 strengths) - gallup.com ($19.99)
3. **O*NET Interest Profiler** (career interests) - mynnextmove.org/explore/ip

**Reflect on:**
- What did I love doing in past jobs?
- What tasks make time fly?
- What do people say I'm naturally good at?
- What work drains me?

---

### Day 2: Lifestyle Mapping

**Answer These Questions:**
- How many hours per week can I realistically work? (Be honest)
- Do I need income within 30 days, 90 days, or 6+ months?
- What's our PCS timeline? (Immediate, 6 months, 1+ years?)
- What's the minimum income I need? My target income?
- Do I want employee benefits or okay with independent contractor?

---

### Day 3: Career Research

**Browse the SpouseForce Career Guides** for careers that match your assessments.

Create a shortlist of 3-5 careers. For each, research:
- Day-to-day tasks (YouTube "day in the life" videos)
- Real salary data (Glassdoor, Payscale, Bureau of Labor Statistics)
- Training requirements (time and cost)
- Job availability (Indeed, FlexJobs, LinkedIn)

---

### Day 4: Skill Gap Analysis

For each career on your shortlist:

**List:**
- Skills I already have
- Skills I need to develop
- Time to acquire missing skills
- Cost to acquire missing skills
- MyCAA eligibility for training

**Example: UX/UI Designer**
- ‚úÖ Have: Adobe Photoshop experience, eye for design
- ‚ùå Need: Figma, user research, prototyping, portfolio
- ‚è± Time: 4-6 months (bootcamp or self-study)
- üí∞ Cost: $0-$5,000 (MyCAA covers $4,000!)
- ‚úÖ MyCAA eligible: Yes (Google UX Design Certificate)

---

### Day 5: Income Modeling

For your top 2-3 careers, create income projections:

**Year 1:** $____
**Year 3:** $____
**Year 5:** $____

Factor in:
- Entry-level salary in your chosen career
- Typical raises/progression
- Freelance vs. employee paths
- Part-time vs. full-time
- Entrepreneurship potential

---

### Day 6: Reality Check

**Talk to 2-3 people actually doing the job.**

Find them via:
- LinkedIn (search job title + "military spouse")
- Military spouse Facebook groups
- SpouseForce community
- MSEP partner companies

**Ask:**
- What's your typical day like?
- How did you get started?
- What surprised you about this career?
- Is it truly portable? Flexible?
- What do you wish you knew before starting?

---

### Day 7: Make Your Decision

**Use This Decision Matrix:**

Create a spreadsheet with your top 3 careers. Rate each (1-10) on:

1. Portability
2. Time to start earning
3. Lifestyle fit
4. Income potential (Year 1)
5. Income growth (Year 5)
6. Skill alignment
7. Interest level
8. Training accessibility (MyCAA, cost, time)

**Multiply each score by your personal weight** (how important is each factor to you?)

**The highest total score is your answer.**

## Common Mistakes Military Spouses Make (And How to Avoid Them)

### Mistake #1: Choosing Based on "Hot" Careers, Not Personal Fit

**Example:** Everyone's talking about software development, so you force yourself through a coding bootcamp‚Äîbut you hate it.

**Fix:** Choose based on YOUR assessments and interests, not trends.

---

### Mistake #2: Underestimating Training Time

**Example:** You want to start earning immediately, but choose cybersecurity (12-24 months of training).

**Fix:** Match training timeline to your financial reality and PCS schedule.

---

### Mistake #3: Ignoring Lifestyle Realities

**Example:** You choose a career requiring 9am-5pm ET meetings, but your spouse's schedule is unpredictable and you have toddlers.

**Fix:** Be brutally honest about your current life, not your ideal life.

---

### Mistake #4: Overvaluing Passion

**Example:** "I'm passionate about writing!" But writing pays $30-$50K for years, and you need $60K to meet expenses.

**Fix:** Passion + market value + lifestyle fit = sustainable career choice.

---

### Mistake #5: Not Leveraging MyCAA

**Example:** Paying $5,000 out of pocket for training when MyCAA would cover $4,000.

**Fix:** Check MyCAA eligibility FIRST before paying for training.
