# Remote Business Careers for Military Spouses - Complete Guide

## Category Overview

Remote business careers leverage organizational, communication, and analytical skills that many military spouses already possess. These roles provide stable remote work with clear career progression across all industries.

**Why Business Careers Work for Military Spouses:**
- 80-90% remote work availability
- Transferable skills across industries
- Professional development opportunities
- Stable salaries with benefits
- Clear career advancement paths

---

## 1. Virtual Assistant ($35K-$65K+)

**Remote: 95%+ | Entry: 1-3 months | No Degree Required**

**Why Ideal:**
- Easiest remote career to start immediately
- Flexible hours
- Can specialize (real estate VA, executive VA, tech VA)
- Build independent VA business

**Salary:** $15-$50/hour or $2,000-$6,000/month retainer per client

**Key Skills:**
- Email management and calendar scheduling
- Data entry and database management
- Social media management
- Customer service
- Project coordination
- Software: Google Workspace, Microsoft Office, project management tools

**Getting Started:**
1. Month 1: Learn common VA tools (Calendly, Asana, Slack)
2. Month 2: Offer services to 1-2 clients at $20-25/hour
3. Month 3+: Raise rates, specialize, scale to 3-5 clients

**Platforms:** Upwork, Belay, Time Etc, Fancy Hands, directly with small businesses

## 3. Bookkeeper / Accountant ($45K-$85K+)

**Remote: 85%+ | Entry: 6-12 months with certification | High Demand**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Bookkeeper: $40,000 - $60,000
- Staff Accountant: $55,000 - $75,000
- Senior Accountant: $75,000 - $100,000+
- CPA: $70,000 - $120,000+

**Key Certifications:**
- **QuickBooks Certified** (most important for bookkeepers)
- **Certified Bookkeeper (CB)** - AIPB or NACPB
- **CPA** (Certified Public Accountant) - Requires bachelor's + exam + experience

**Key Skills:**
- **Bookkeeping**: Accounts payable/receivable, bank reconciliation, financial reports
- **Software**: QuickBooks Online (essential), Xero, FreshBooks, Wave
- **Accounting**: General ledger, journal entries, financial statements
- **Tax**: Basic tax preparation (can specialize)
- **Payroll**: Processing payroll, tax filings

**Getting Started:**
1. **Months 1-3**: Learn QuickBooks Online (free 30-day trial, practice with sample company)
2. **Month 4-6**: Get QuickBooks Certified ($175 exam) or CB certification
3. **Month 7+**: Start with 2-3 small business clients at $300-$1,000/month each

**MyCAA Options:** Accounting certificate programs at community colleges

**Freelance:** Many bookkeepers prefer self-employment ($40-$75/hour or $500-$3,000/month per client)

## 4. HR Manager (Remote) ($60K-$110K+)

**Remote: 70%+ | Entry: Requires HR experience | Professional Growth**

**Salary Ranges:**
- HR Coordinator: $45,000 - $60,000
- HR Generalist: $55,000 - $75,000
- HR Manager: $75,000 - $100,000
- HR Director: $100,000 - $150,000+

**Key Skills:**
- Recruitment and talent acquisition
- Onboarding and offboarding
- Benefits administration
- Employee relations
- Performance management
- HRIS systems (Workday, BambooHR, Namely)
- HR compliance (FMLA, ADA, EEO)

**Certifications:**
- **SHRM-CP** (Society for Human Resource Management Certified Professional) - $300 exam
- **PHR** (Professional in Human Resources) - HRCI - $395 exam

**Getting Started:**
- Typically requires bachelor's degree OR significant administrative experience
- Start as HR Coordinator or Recruiter, advance to HR Generalist/Manager
- Remote HR roles common in tech startups and distributed companies

## 5. Recruiter / Talent Acquisition ($50K-$95K+)

**Remote: 80%+ | Entry: 3-6 months | Commission Potential**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Entry Recruiter: $45,000 - $60,000 + commission
- Mid-Level: $60,000 - $80,000 + commission
- Senior: $80,000 - $120,000+ (with commissions)
- Agency Recruiters: Often 100% commission ($60K-$150K+ potential)

**Types of Recruiting:**
- **Corporate Recruiter**: Work for one company, salary + small bonus
- **Agency Recruiter**: Work for staffing agency, commission-based (higher earning potential)
- **Executive Recruiter**: High-level positions, very high commissions
- **Technical Recruiter**: Tech roles (software developers, engineers) - high demand

**Key Skills:**
- Sourcing candidates (LinkedIn Recruiter, Boolean search, networking)
- Interviewing and screening
- ATS systems (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday)
- Negotiation
- Relationship building
- Pipeline management

**Getting Started:**
1. Start with agency recruiting (easier entry, learn quickly)
2. Specialize in industry (tech recruiting pays most)
3. Build LinkedIn network aggressively
4. Consider independent recruiting after 2-3 years experience

**Military Spouse Advantage:** Understanding military talent pool, veteran recruiting

## 6. Sales Representative (Remote) ($50K-$150K+)

**Remote: 75%+ | Entry: Varies | High Earning Potential**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Entry (SDR): $45,000 - $60,000 base + commission (OTE $60K-$80K)
- Mid (Account Executive): $60,000 - $90,000 base + commission (OTE $100K-$150K)
- Senior (Enterprise Sales): $90,000 - $120,000 base + commission (OTE $180K-$300K+)

**Types of Sales Roles:**
- **SDR/BDR** (Sales/Business Development Rep): Outbound prospecting, qualifying leads
- **Account Executive**: Closing deals, managing sales cycle
- **Account Manager**: Managing existing client relationships, upselling
- **Customer Success** (see next): Retention-focused

**Key Skills:**
- Cold calling and email outreach
- Consultative selling
- CRM software (Salesforce, HubSpot)
- Product knowledge
- Objection handling
- Negotiation
- Pipeline management

**High-Paying Industries:**
- **SaaS** (Software as a Service): Highest paying remote sales
- **Tech**: Cloud platforms, cybersecurity, developer tools
- **Healthcare**: Medical devices, pharma
- **Financial services**: Insurance, investments

**Getting Started:**
1. Start as SDR ($50K-$70K) - easiest entry
2. Prove yourself (6-12 months hitting quota)
3. Promote to Account Executive ($100K-$150K OTE)
4. Specialize in high-value industries

## 7. Customer Success Manager ($55K-$105K+)

**Remote: 85%+ | Entry: Customer service experience helpful | Growing Field**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Entry CSM: $55,000 - $70,000
- Mid CSM: $70,000 - $90,000
- Senior CSM: $90,000 - $115,000
- Director of CS: $120,000 - $160,000+

**What They Do:**
- Ensure customers successfully use product/service
- Onboarding new customers
- Training and education
- Proactive outreach to prevent churn
- Upselling and cross-selling
- Collecting feedback for product team

**Key Skills:**
- Relationship building
- Problem-solving
- Communication (written and verbal)
- Technical aptitude (understanding software)
- CRM tools (Salesforce, HubSpot, Gainsight)
- Data analysis (track customer health scores)

**Best Industries:**
- **SaaS companies** (highest demand)
- Tech platforms
- Subscription services

**Getting Started:**
- Often transition from customer service or sales roles
- Tech-savvy with excellent communication
- Start as CSM at smaller company, grow to larger SaaS company

## 9. Business Consultant ($75K-$150K+)

**Remote: 90%+ | Entry: Requires significant experience | High Earning**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Independent consultant: $100-$300/hour or $5,000-$30,000+ per project
- Consulting firm: $75,000 - $150,000+ salary

**Types of Consulting:**
- **Management Consulting**: Strategy, operations, organizational design
- **Small Business Consulting**: Helping small businesses grow
- **Niche Expertise**: Industry-specific (healthcare, manufacturing, non-profit)
- **Implementation**: Software implementation (Salesforce, SAP, etc.)
- **Change Management**: Helping organizations through transitions

**Key Skills:**
- Subject matter expertise in specific domain
- Problem-solving and analytical thinking
- Communication and presentation
- Project management
- Business acumen
- Relationship building

**Getting Started:**
- Typically requires 5-10 years business experience first
- Develop deep expertise in specific industry or function
- Build network and reputation
- Start with 1-2 freelance clients while employed
- Transition to full-time independent consulting

**Military Spouse Advantage:** Can consult remotely, travel for engagements, work project-based

## 10. Grant Writer ($50K-$85K+)

**Remote: 90%+ | Entry: 6-12 months | Writing Skills**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Entry Grant Writer: $45,000 - $60,000
- Mid-Level: $60,000 - $75,000
- Senior: $75,000 - $95,000
- Freelance: $50-$125/hour or $1,500-$10,000+ per grant

**What They Do:**
- Research grant opportunities for non-profits, schools, governments
- Write compelling grant proposals
- Manage grant application process
- Track deadlines and requirements
- Report on grant outcomes

**Key Skills:**
- **Research**: Finding appropriate grants
- **Persuasive writing**: Convincing funders to award grants
- **Attention to detail**: Following specific guidelines exactly
- **Project management**: Managing multiple grant deadlines
- **Budgeting**: Creating project budgets
- **Storytelling**: Narratives that demonstrate need and impact

**Best Sectors:**
- **Non-profits**: Education, healthcare, social services, arts
- **Higher education**: Universities and colleges
- **Government agencies**: State and local government
- **Healthcare**: Hospitals and clinics

**Getting Started:**
1. **Months 1-3**: Study successful grant proposals, take grant writing course (free: Grant Writing USA)
2. **Months 4-6**: Volunteer to write grants for military non-profit or local charity
3. **Month 7+**: Start freelancing at $40-60/hour or pursue full-time role

**Certifications:**
- **Grant Professional Certified (GPC)** - Grant Professionals Association ($250 exam, requires experience)

**MyCAA Options:** Some community colleges offer grant writing certificates
