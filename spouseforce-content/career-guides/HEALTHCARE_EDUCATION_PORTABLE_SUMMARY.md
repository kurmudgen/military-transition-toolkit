# Healthcare & Education (Portable) Careers for Military Spouses - Complete Guide

## Category Overview

Healthcare and education careers offer military spouses stable employment with portable credentials that transfer across states. Many states now have license reciprocity agreements for military spouses, making these careers increasingly viable for frequent movers.

**Why Healthcare & Education Work for Military Spouses:**
- High demand at every installation
- Portable licenses (improving with military spouse reciprocity laws)
- Meaningful work helping others
- Stable employment and benefits
- Clear career paths and certifications

---

## 1. Registered Nurse (RN) - Travel Nursing ($70K-$120K+)

**Portable: HIGH | License: State-specific with reciprocity | High Demand**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Staff RN: $65,000 - $90,000
- Travel Nurse: $80,000 - $120,000+ (with housing stipends)
- Specialties (ICU, OR, ER): $85,000 - $110,000+
- Nurse Practitioner (advanced): $100,000 - $140,000+

**Why Ideal for Military Spouses:**
- **Travel nursing** perfect for PCS moves - work 13-week contracts anywhere
- Compact Nursing License: Practice in 40+ states with one license
- High demand = always employable near any installation
- Flexible scheduling (12-hour shifts, PRN work)
- Can work part-time, full-time, or per diem

**Education Requirements:**
- **Associate's Degree in Nursing (ADN)**: 2 years, MyCAA can help fund
- **Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN)**: 4 years (GI Bill transfer)
- **Pass NCLEX-RN exam**: National licensure exam
- Many hospitals now prefer BSN but ADN still hireable

**Getting Started:**
1. Complete prerequisite courses
2. Enroll in ADN or BSN program (2-4 years)
3. Pass NCLEX-RN exam
4. Apply for Compact Nursing License (eNLC) to practice in multiple states
5. Consider travel nursing agencies (Aya, Cross Country, Travel Nurse Across America)

**Military Spouse Advantages:**
- **Compact License**: Work in 40+ states without relicensing
- **Military Spouse Licensure Portability**: Many states expedite military spouse licenses
- **TRICARE knowledge**: Understanding military healthcare is asset
- **Flexibility**: Travel nursing contracts align with PCS schedules

**Specializations:**
- Emergency, ICU, Labor & Delivery, Pediatrics, Oncology, Surgery
- Each specialty commands premium pay

## 2. Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN) ($45K-$60K)

**Portable: MODERATE | License: State-specific | Quick Entry**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Entry LPN: $40,000 - $50,000
- Experienced LPN: $50,000 - $60,000
- Specialty settings: $55,000 - $65,000

**Why Ideal:**
- Faster entry than RN (1 year vs 2-4 years)
- Work in hospitals, clinics, nursing homes, home health
- Always in demand
- Can bridge to RN later

**Education:**
- **LPN Certificate/Diploma**: 12-18 months
- **Pass NCLEX-PN exam**: National licensure
- Many community colleges offer LPN programs (MyCAA-eligible)

**Getting Started:**
1. Complete LPN program (12-18 months, ~$10,000-$25,000)
2. Pass NCLEX-PN exam ($200)
3. Apply for state license
4. Work while pursuing RN if desired (LPN-to-RN bridge programs)

**Portability:**
- Must relicense with each PCS to new state
- Many states offer expedited military spouse licensing
- Temporary licenses available while waiting for full license

## 3. Medical Coder / Biller ($40K-$70K)

**Remote: 75%+ | Portable: 100% | No Degree Required**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Entry Coder: $40,000 - $50,000
- Experienced Coder: $50,000 - $60,000
- Certified Professional Coder: $60,000 - $70,000+
- Remote positions widely available

**Why Ideal for Military Spouses:**
- **Highly remote-friendly** - work from anywhere
- **No degree required** - certification-based
- **Quick entry** - job-ready in 4-8 months
- **Stable demand** - healthcare always needs coders
- **Flexible hours** - many remote positions offer flexibility

**Key Certifications:**
- **CPC (Certified Professional Coder)** - AAPC - $399 exam (most recognized)
- **CCS (Certified Coding Specialist)** - AHIMA - $399 exam
- **COC (Certified Outpatient Coder)** - AAPC - $399 exam

**Key Skills:**
- Medical terminology
- ICD-10 diagnosis codes
- CPT procedure codes
- HCPCS codes
- Medical billing software
- Attention to detail
- Understanding of insurance and reimbursement

**Getting Started:**
1. **Months 1-4**: Complete medical coding course (AAPC, AHIMA, or community college - MyCAA-eligible)
2. **Month 5**: Study for CPC exam
3. **Month 6**: Pass CPC exam ($399)
4. **Month 7+**: Apply for remote coding positions (start at $40K-$45K, grow to $60K+)

**Training Options:**
- **AAPC CPC Certification Course**: $1,800-$2,500 (online, self-paced)
- **Community College Medical Coding Certificate**: $3,000-$6,000 (MyCAA-eligible)
- **CareerStep Medical Coding**: $3,199 (online, MyCAA-approved)

**100% Portable**: No licensing required, work remotely from any state

## 4. Pharmacy Technician ($30K-$50K)

**Portable: MODERATE | Certification: National with state requirements | Stable**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Entry Pharmacy Tech: $30,000 - $38,000
- Experienced: $38,000 - $48,000
- Certified (CPhT): $40,000 - $50,000
- Hospital pharmacy tech: $42,000 - $52,000

**Why Ideal:**
- Quick entry (6-12 months training)
- Work in retail pharmacies (CVS, Walgreens near all bases)
- Hospital and clinic opportunities
- Flexible part-time and full-time options

**Certification:**
- **PTCB (Pharmacy Technician Certification Board)** - National certification
- **Exam**: $129
- **Requirements**: High school diploma, background check
- **Recertification**: Every 2 years with 20 CE hours

**Getting Started:**
1. Complete pharmacy technician training program (6-12 months, community college or online)
2. Gain hands-on experience (internship/externship)
3. Pass PTCB exam ($129)
4. Apply for state registration (requirements vary)

**MyCAA Options**: Many pharmacy tech programs at community colleges are MyCAA-approved

**Portability**:
- National PTCB certification recognized everywhere
- State registration required (usually quick process)
- Retail chains hire pharmacy techs at all locations

## 5. Occupational Therapy Assistant (OTA) ($50K-$65K)

**Portable: MODERATE | License: State-specific with reciprocity | Rewarding**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Entry OTA: $50,000 - $58,000
- Experienced OTA: $58,000 - $68,000
- Specialized settings: $60,000 - $70,000

**Why Ideal:**
- Help people regain independence (very rewarding)
- Work in hospitals, rehab centers, schools, home health
- High demand
- Better work-life balance than many healthcare roles

**Education:**
- **Associate's Degree in Occupational Therapy**: 2 years
- **Clinical fieldwork**: Included in program
- **Pass NBCOT exam**: National certification
- **Cost**: $15,000-$30,000 for degree (MyCAA + GI Bill transfer can cover)

**Getting Started:**
1. Complete OTA associate's degree program (2 years)
2. Complete fieldwork requirements
3. Pass NBCOT certification exam ($555)
4. Apply for state license

**Portability:**
- Must be licensed in each state
- Interstate Compact being developed (not yet active for OTAs)
- Military spouse expedited licensing in many states

## 6. Teacher (Virtual/Substitute) ($40K-$70K)

**Remote: Growing | Portable: State licensure with reciprocity | Flexible**

**Salary Ranges:**
- **Substitute Teacher**: $100-$200/day ($15,000-$30,000/year part-time)
- **Virtual Teacher**: $45,000 - $65,000
- **Full-time Teacher**: $50,000 - $70,000 (varies greatly by state and district)
- **Online Private Tutor**: $25-$75/hour

**Why Ideal for Military Spouses:**
- **Substitute teaching**: Ultimate flexibility, work when you want
- **Virtual teaching**: Remote online schools (K12, Connections Academy)
- **Department of Defense Schools (DoDEA)**: Priority hiring for military spouses
- **Summers off**: Align with kids' schedules
- **Transfer teaching license** with military spouse reciprocity laws

**Education Requirements:**
- **Bachelor's Degree**: Required (any field for substitute, education for full-time)
- **Teaching License**: State-specific (requirements vary)
- **Alternative Certification**: Some states offer alternative paths

**Getting Started - Substitute Teaching (Fastest):**
1. Have bachelor's degree (any field)
2. Complete background check
3. Attend substitute orientation (1-2 days)
4. Start subbing (flexible schedule, work when you want)

**Getting Started - Virtual Teaching:**
1. Obtain teaching license in state where online school operates
2. Apply to online schools (K12, Connections Academy, many others)
3. Complete online teaching training
4. Teach from anywhere

**Military Spouse Advantages:**
- **DoDEA Schools**: Department of Defense Education Activity schools on installations worldwide - priority hiring for military spouses
- **Interstate Teacher Mobility Compact**: 13 states allow reciprocal licenses
- **Many states offer expedited military spouse teacher licensing**

**Remote Options:**
- K12.com - Virtual public schools
- Connections Academy
- VIPKid (teach English to Chinese students) - $14-$22/hour
- Online private tutoring platforms

## 7. Tutor (Online) ($25K-$75K+)

**Remote: 90%+ | Portable: 100% | Flexible**

**Salary Ranges:**
- **Part-time online tutoring**: $15,000 - $30,000 (10-15 hours/week)
- **Full-time online tutoring**: $35,000 - $50,000
- **Private tutoring business**: $50,000 - $75,000+ ($50-$100/hour)
- **Hourly rates**: $25-$100+ depending on subject and experience

**Why Ideal:**
- **100% remote** - work from anywhere
- **Flexible hours** - tutor around military family schedule
- **No formal certification required** for many platforms
- **Can build independent tutoring business**

**Subjects in High Demand:**
- **Test Prep**: SAT, ACT, GRE, GMAT ($50-$100/hour)
- **Math**: All levels, especially calculus ($30-$75/hour)
- **Science**: Chemistry, Physics, Biology ($30-$60/hour)
- **English/Writing**: Essay writing, grammar ($25-$50/hour)
- **Foreign Languages**: Spanish, French, Mandarin ($25-$60/hour)
- **College Admissions Consulting**: $75-$200/hour

**Online Tutoring Platforms:**
- **Wyzant**: Set your own rates ($25-$100/hour), large student base
- **Tutor.com**: $10-$16/hour, flexible hours, beginner-friendly
- **Chegg Tutors**: $20/hour average
- **VIPKid**: Teach English to Chinese kids ($14-$22/hour)
- **Outschool**: Teach group classes online ($15-$40/student/class)

**Getting Started:**
1. **Week 1**: Sign up for 2-3 online tutoring platforms
2. **Week 2**: Create compelling tutor profile highlighting your expertise
3. **Week 3-4**: Start with platform's rates, gain reviews
4. **Month 2+**: Raise rates, build private client base, specialize

**Building Independent Tutoring Business:**
1. Start on platforms to gain experience and testimonials
2. Create website showcasing your expertise
3. Market to military families (kids at your base, online military spouse groups)
4. Specialize in high-value subjects (test prep, college admissions)
5. Charge $50-$100/hour for private clients

**No Degree Required** for many platforms, but bachelor's degree preferred and commands higher rates

## 8. School Counselor ($50K-$75K)

**Portable: MODERATE | License: State-specific | Meaningful Work**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Entry School Counselor: $48,000 - $58,000
- Experienced: $58,000 - $70,000
- Senior/Lead Counselor: $70,000 - $80,000

**Why Ideal:**
- Help students navigate academics, college, careers
- Work in schools (including DoDEA schools on installations)
- School calendar = summers off
- Stable, meaningful work

**Education Requirements:**
- **Master's Degree in School Counseling**: Required (2 years beyond bachelor's)
- **Internship**: 600-700 hours typically required
- **State Licensure**: Required to work as school counselor
- **Cost**: $20,000-$50,000 for master's (GI Bill transfer can cover)

**Getting Started:**
1. Complete bachelor's degree (any field, though education/psychology helpful)
2. Enroll in master's in school counseling program (2 years, can be online)
3. Complete internship hours
4. Pass state licensure exam (Praxis in most states)
5. Apply for school counseling positions

**Portability:**
- Must be licensed in each state
- Many states have reciprocity agreements
- Military spouse expedited licensing available in many states

**DoDEA Advantage**: Department of Defense schools worldwide hire school counselors and give military spouse preference

## 9. Speech-Language Pathology Assistant (SLPA) ($40K-$60K)

**Portable: MODERATE | Certification: Varies by state | Growing Field**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Entry SLPA: $38,000 - $48,000
- Experienced SLPA: $48,000 - $60,000
- School-based: $40,000 - $55,000
- Clinical settings: $45,000 - $62,000

**Why Ideal:**
- Help children and adults with speech/language disorders
- Work in schools, clinics, hospitals, teletherapy
- High demand for services
- Can work part-time or full-time
- Some teletherapy opportunities (remote)

**Education Requirements:**
- **Associate's Degree or Certificate in SLPA**: 2 years or less
- **ASHA Certification** (optional but preferred): Speech-Language Pathology Assistant certification
- **State Requirements Vary**: Some states require license, others don't
- **Cost**: $8,000-$20,000 for program (MyCAA can help fund)

**Getting Started:**
1. Complete SLPA associate's degree or certificate program (18-24 months)
2. Complete clinical practicum hours
3. Pass ASHA SLPA exam if pursuing certification ($325)
4. Apply for state license if required in your state
5. Work under supervision of licensed Speech-Language Pathologist (SLP)

**Portability:**
- Requirements vary by state (some require license, others just certification)
- Research requirements for each state you PCS to
- Teletherapy opportunities growing (work remotely)

**Career Path**: Many SLPAs go on to become Speech-Language Pathologists (master's degree required, $70K-$100K salary)

## 10. Health Coach ($40K-$80K)

**Remote: 85%+ | Portable: 100% | No License Required**

**Salary Ranges:**
- Part-time Health Coach: $30,000 - $50,000
- Full-time Health Coach: $50,000 - $70,000
- Independent Practice: $60,000 - $100,000+
- Hourly: $50-$150 per session

**Why Ideal for Military Spouses:**
- **Highly remote** - coach clients via video calls
- **No state licensing** - 100% portable
- **Flexible schedule** - build around military life
- **Meaningful work** - help people improve health
- **Can build independent practice or work for companies**

**What Health Coaches Do:**
- Help clients set and achieve health and wellness goals
- Nutrition guidance (not meal plans - that requires RD credential)
- Lifestyle changes, stress management, sleep improvement
- Accountability and support
- Chronic disease management support
- Weight loss and fitness goals

**Certifications:**
- **National Board Certified Health & Wellness Coach (NBC-HWC)** - Gold standard ($495 exam, requires approved training)
- **ACE Health Coach Certification** - $599
- **AFPA Health & Wellness Coach** - $699
- **Precision Nutrition Level 1** - $999 (very popular)
- **IIN (Institute for Integrative Nutrition)** - $6,000-$7,000 (comprehensive but expensive)

**Getting Started:**
1. **Months 1-6**: Complete health coaching certification (4-12 months depending on program)
2. **Month 7**: Pass certification exam
3. **Month 8-9**: Practice with free/low-cost clients, build testimonials
4. **Month 10+**: Start charging ($50-$75/session initially, build to $100-$150)

**Work Options:**
- **Independent practice**: Build your own client base ($60-$150/hour)
- **Corporate wellness**: Work for companies' employee wellness programs ($50K-$70K)
- **Healthcare systems**: Partner with doctors for patient support ($50K-$65K)
- **Telehealth companies**: Remote health coaching roles
- **Group coaching**: Run online group programs ($200-$500 per participant)

**MyCAA**: Check if health coaching certifications qualify (some programs approved)

**100% Portable**: No state licensing, work remotely from anywhere
