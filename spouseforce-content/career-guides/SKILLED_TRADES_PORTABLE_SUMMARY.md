# Skilled Trades (Portable) Careers for Military Spouses - Complete Guide

## Category Overview

Skilled trades offer military spouses hands-on careers with portable certifications, entrepreneurial opportunities, and the ability to rebuild clientele at each new duty station. Many trades have improved license reciprocity for military spouses.

**Why Skilled Trades Work for Military Spouses:**
- Build new client base at each duty station
- Entrepreneurial opportunities (be your own boss)
- Flexible schedules
- Hands-on, rewarding work
- Strong demand everywhere

---

## 1. Real Estate Agent ($25K-$150K+)

**Portable: MODERATE | License: State-specific with military spouse reciprocity | High Earning Potential**

**Salary Ranges:**
- **Part-time/New Agent**: $25,000 - $50,000 (commission-based)
- **Full-time Established**: $60,000 - $100,000
- **Top Producers**: $100,000 - $300,000+
- **Average**: $45,000-$65,000 (varies greatly by market and effort)

**Why Ideal for Military Spouses:**
- Rebuild client base at every new installation
- Flexible schedule (work around military life)
- Military families always PCS = built-in clientele
- Understand unique needs of military home buyers
- Commission-based = unlimited earning potential

**Getting Licensed:**
1. **Pre-licensing education**: 60-120 hours (varies by state, $200-$500, online available)
2. **Pass state exam**: $100-$300
3. **Join real estate brokerage**: Work under licensed broker
4. **Total time**: 2-6 months to licensed
5. **Total cost**: $500-$2,000

**Military Spouse Portability:**
- **40+ states** offer expedited/reciprocal licenses for military spouses
- Many states waive fees or education requirements for military spouses transferring license
- Some states allow you to keep your license and get temporary license in new state

**Income Reality:**
- **Commission-only**: No base salary, paid when deals close (typically 2.5-3% of sale price)
- **Average deal**: $300,000 home = $7,500-$9,000 commission (split with brokerage, so you get $3,750-$6,000)
- **Need 10-15 closings/year** for $50,000+ income
- **First year is hardest**: Building client base takes time

**Getting Started:**
1. **Month 1-2**: Complete pre-licensing course online
2. **Month 3**: Pass state exam
3. **Month 4**: Interview and join brokerages (choose military-friendly)
4. **Month 5-6**: Build sphere of influence, market to military families
5. **Month 7+**: First closings (average 6-9 months to first commission)

**Military Spouse Niche**: Specialize in helping military families buy/sell homes (understand VA loans, PCS timelines, base proximity)

## 2. Insurance Agent ($40K-$100K+)

**Portable: MODERATE-HIGH | License: State-specific with reciprocity | Stable**

**Salary Ranges:**
- **Captive Agent** (work for one company): $40,000 - $80,000 base + commissions
- **Independent Agent** (sell multiple companies): Commission-only, $50,000 - $150,000+
- **Established Book of Business**: $75,000 - $200,000+

**Types of Insurance:**
- **Life Insurance**: Sell life, disability, long-term care policies
- **Health Insurance**: Individual and group health plans
- **Property & Casualty** (P&C): Auto, home, renters insurance
- **Commercial Insurance**: Business insurance

**Getting Licensed:**
1. **Pre-licensing course**: 20-52 hours depending on state and license type ($150-$400)
2. **Pass state exam**: Life & Health OR Property & Casualty ($50-$150 per exam)
3. **Background check and fingerprints**: $50-$100
4. **Total time**: 1-3 months
5. **Total cost**: $300-$800

**Military Spouse Portability:**
- **License reciprocity**: Most states have agreements; some require exam in new state, others just paperwork
- **Non-resident licenses**: Can sell insurance in multiple states
- **Military spouse expedited licensing** in many states

**Work Options:**
- **Captive Agent**: Work for one company (State Farm, Allstate, Farmers) - base salary + commission, training provided
- **Independent Agent**: Represent multiple companies - higher commissions, more freedom, build own agency
- **Remote Insurance Sales**: Many companies hire remote insurance agents

**Getting Started:**
1. **Month 1**: Complete pre-licensing course for Life & Health OR Property & Casualty
2. **Month 2**: Pass state exam, apply for license
3. **Month 3**: Interview with insurance companies or agencies
4. **Month 4+**: Start selling (expect 6-12 months to build consistent income)

**Best for Military Spouses:**
- **USAA**: Specifically serves military families, hires military spouses
- **Navy Federal**: Insurance division hires military spouses
- **Remote insurance companies**: Work from anywhere

**Income Timeline:**
- **Year 1**: $30,000-$50,000 (building book of business)
- **Year 2**: $50,000-$75,000 (renewals + new business)
- **Year 3+**: $75,000-$150,000+ (established book of business provides recurring commissions)

## 3. Financial Advisor ($55K-$150K+)

**Portable: HIGH | License: Series exams or CFP | Professional**

**Salary Ranges:**
- **Entry Financial Advisor**: $40,000 - $65,000
- **Established Advisor**: $70,000 - $120,000
- **Certified Financial Planner (CFP)**: $90,000 - $200,000+
- **Independent Practice**: $100,000 - $500,000+ (with established client base)

**Why Ideal:**
- Help people manage money, plan for retirement, invest
- Can build virtual practice (serve clients remotely)
- Military families great target market (understand TSP, military benefits)
- Portable credentials (federal licenses)

**Credentials/Licenses:**
- **Series 65** (Investment Advisor) OR **Series 7 & 66** - $300-$500 per exam
- **Certified Financial Planner (CFP)** - Gold standard, requires bachelor's degree + exam + experience ($825 exam)
- **ChFC (Chartered Financial Consultant)** - Alternative to CFP

**Getting Started:**
1. **Obtain bachelor's degree** (required for CFP, preferred for most positions)
2. **Study for and pass Series 65** OR **Series 7 & 66** (3-6 months study)
3. **Consider CFP certification** (6-12 months additional)
4. **Join financial planning firm** OR **start independent practice**

**Work Options:**
- **Fee-only advisor**: Charge clients flat fee or hourly ($150-$400/hour)
- **Commission-based**: Earn commissions on products sold (insurance, investments)
- **Salary + bonus**: Work for larger firm (Edward Jones, Ameriprise, etc.)

**Military Spouse Niche**: Specialize in military financial planning (TSP optimization, deployment finances, VA benefits, PCS financial management)

**100% Portable**: Federal licenses (Series exams) work in all states

## 4. Hair Stylist / Cosmetologist ($25K-$75K+)

**Portable: HIGH (with challenges) | License: State-specific with reciprocity | Flexible**

**Salary Ranges:**
- **Booth Renter/Commission**: $25,000 - $40,000 (starting)
- **Established Stylist**: $40,000 - $60,000
- **High-end Salon/Specialist**: $60,000 - $100,000+
- **Salon Owner**: $50,000 - $150,000+ (with successful business)

**Why Ideal:**
- Rebuild client base at each installation
- Flexible schedule (set your own hours if booth renting)
- Always in demand
- Can work part-time or full-time
- Entrepreneurial opportunities (mobile salon, booth rental, own salon)

**Getting Licensed:**
1. **Cosmetology School**: 1,000-2,300 hours (varies by state), typically 9-15 months
2. **Cost**: $10,000-$20,000 (financial aid available, some MyCAA-eligible programs)
3. **Pass state board exam**: Written + practical ($100-$200)
4. **Total time**: 9-18 months

**Military Spouse Portability:**
- **Cosmetology license reciprocity**: Most states recognize out-of-state licenses
- **Military spouse expedited licensing**: Many states waive fees or expedite for military spouses
- **Compact license movements**: Work underway to create nationwide compact for cosmetologists

**Work Options:**
- **Commission stylist**: Work in salon for 40-60% of service prices
- **Booth renter**: Rent chair in salon, keep all proceeds (minus rent $100-$400/week)
- **Mobile stylist**: Travel to clients' homes
- **Salon owner**: Open own salon
- **Specialized services**: Bridal, extensions, color specialist (charge premium)

**Getting Started:**
1. **Month 1**: Research cosmetology schools in area (ensure accredited and state board approved)
2. **Months 2-12**: Complete cosmetology program (9-15 months full-time, or 18-24 months part-time)
3. **Month 13**: Pass state board exam
4. **Month 14+**: Work in salon to build clientele (expect 1-2 years to fully book schedule)

**Military Installation Opportunities:**
- On-base salons (AAFES, NEX) hire stylists
- Large military spouse client base
- Can build clientele that follows you if PCSing within region

## 5. Personal Trainer ($30K-$80K+)

**Portable: HIGH | Certification: National (portable) | Flexible**

**Salary Ranges:**
- **Part-time Trainer**: $20,000 - $35,000 (10-15 hours/week)
- **Full-time Gym Trainer**: $35,000 - $55,000
- **Independent Trainer**: $50,000 - $100,000+ (with full client load)
- **Online Coaching**: $40,000 - $150,000+ (scalable)
- **Hourly**: $25-$100+ per session depending on experience and location

**Why Ideal for Military Spouses:**
- **National certifications** (work anywhere)
- **Rebuild client base** at each installation
- **Military community** = health-conscious clientele
- **Flexible schedule** - train clients early morning, evening, weekends
- **Can go fully remote** (online coaching)

**Required Certifications:**
- **NASM-CPT** (National Academy of Sports Medicine) - $699, most recognized
- **ACE Personal Trainer** - $699
- **ISSA Certified Personal Trainer** - $799
- **NSCA-CPT** (National Strength & Conditioning Association) - $475
- **Study time**: 2-6 months
- **All certifications are nationally recognized** = 100% portable

**Getting Started:**
1. **Months 1-3**: Study for certification (online courses included with exam fee)
2. **Month 4**: Pass certification exam
3. **Month 5**: Get CPR/AED certified (required, $50-$100)
4. **Month 6**: Start training clients (gym, independent, or online)

**Work Options:**
- **Commercial gym** (LA Fitness, 24 Hour Fitness, Gold's): $15-$25/hour, benefits, clients provided
- **Boutique studio**: $25-$40/hour
- **Independent trainer**: $40-$100/session, keep all revenue
- **Online coaching**: Train clients virtually ($100-$300/month per client, scalable to 20-50 clients)
- **Group fitness instructor**: Teach classes ($25-$75/class)

**Military Installation Opportunities:**
- **On-base gyms**: Need certified trainers
- **Unit PT**: Some units hire trainers to improve physical fitness scores
- **Military spouse fitness groups**: Build client base

**Online Personal Training** (Best for PCS portability):
- Train clients via video calls
- Create custom workout and nutrition plans
- Use apps (Trainerize, My PT Hub)
- Charge $100-$300/month per client
- Can keep clients through PCS moves

**100% Portable**: National certifications work everywhere, easily rebuild clientele or go online

## 6. Massage Therapist ($35K-$75K)

**Portable: MODERATE | License: State-specific with reciprocity | Flexible**

**Salary Ranges:**
- **Spa/Clinic Employee**: $30,000 - $50,000
- **Established Therapist**: $50,000 - $70,000
- **Independent Practice**: $60,000 - $90,000+ (with full client schedule)
- **Hourly**: $40-$120 per massage (before expenses)

**Why Ideal:**
- Help people reduce pain and stress
- Flexible hours
- Rebuild client base at each installation
- Can work in spas, clinics, or independently
- Part-time or full-time options

**Getting Licensed:**
1. **Massage Therapy School**: 500-1,000 hours (varies by state), typically 6-12 months
2. **Cost**: $5,000-$18,000 (some financial aid, check MyCAA eligibility)
3. **Pass MBLEx**: National massage therapy exam ($195)
4. **State license**: Apply after passing MBLEx
5. **Total time**: 6-15 months

**Military Spouse Portability:**
- **MBLEx** is national exam recognized by most states
- **License reciprocity**: Most states recognize out-of-state licenses with minimal requirements
- **Military spouse expedited licensing** in many states
- **NCBTMB certification** (optional) recognized nationwide, can help with portability

**Work Options:**
- **Spa employee**: $15-$25/hour + tips, steady work, less freedom
- **Chiropractic/physical therapy clinic**: $20-$30/hour, medical setting
- **Independent practice**: Rent space or mobile, $50-$100+/massage, keep all revenue
- **Mobile massage**: Travel to clients, charge premium ($80-$150/session)
- **On-call massage**: Events, corporate wellness, sports teams

**Getting Started:**
1. **Months 1-12**: Complete massage therapy program (6-12 months)
2. **Month 13**: Pass MBLEx exam
3. **Month 14**: Apply for state license, get liability insurance
4. **Month 15+**: Work in spa to build skills, then transition to independent practice

**Military Installation Opportunities:**
- On-base spas need massage therapists
- Provide services to military families (understand deployment stress, injuries)
- Partner with on-base physical therapy clinics

## 8. Dog Groomer / Pet Sitter ($25K-$60K)

**Portable: 100% | No License Required | Flexible**

**Salary Ranges:**
- **Pet Sitter/Dog Walker**: $15,000 - $35,000 part-time, $35,000 - $50,000 full-time
- **Dog Groomer (employee)**: $25,000 - $40,000
- **Self-Employed Groomer**: $40,000 - $75,000+
- **Per service**: Dog walking $15-$30/walk, grooming $40-$90 per dog, pet sitting $25-$75/day

**Why Ideal for Military Spouses:**
- **No licensing required** = 100% portable
- **Rebuild client base easily** at each installation (everyone has pets)
- **Flexible schedule** - work around military family needs
- **Low startup costs** for pet sitting
- **Military families need pet care** (deployments, TDY, PCS)

**Dog Grooming:**
1. **Training**: Take grooming course online or at grooming school (3-6 months, $3,000-$10,000)
   - OR apprentice at grooming salon (paid while learning)
2. **Certification**: Optional but helpful (National Dog Groomers Association of America)
3. **Start**: Work at Petco, PetSmart, or independent salon ($12-$18/hour + commission)
4. **Build to independent**: Mobile grooming van or home-based salon

**Pet Sitting/Dog Walking:**
1. **No training required** (pet first aid certification helpful, $50-$100)
2. **Sign up for platforms**: Rover, Wag, Care.com
3. **Set rates**: $20-$40/walk, $30-$75/overnight
4. **Build local client base**: Market to military families
5. **Scale**: Hire other military spouses to help, build pet care business

**Military Installation Advantages:**
- Deployments = huge need for pet sitting
- TDY and trainings = regular dog walking clients
- PCS = temporary foster care opportunities
- Military families love supporting military spouse businesses

**100% Portable**: No license, easy to restart at each installation

- Regulations vary significantly by state

## 10. Notary Public ($500-$10,000+)

**Portable: State-specific | Commission: Required | Side Income**

**Income Ranges:**
- **Part-time/Side Hustle**: $500 - $5,000/year
- **Mobile Notary Full-time**: $30,000 - $60,000+
- **Loan Signing Agent**: $50,000 - $100,000+
- **Per signature**: $5-$15 (varies by state), loan signings $75-$200

**Why Ideal:**
- **Low cost to start**: $50-$200 to become notary
- **Flexible side income**
- **Can combine with other work**
- **Loan signing agent** = substantial income
- **Always needed** especially near military installations (PCS paperwork, legal documents)

**Getting Commissioned:**
1. **Apply to state**: Submit application and fee ($25-$120)
2. **Background check**: Pass criminal background check
3. **Training/exam**: Some states require course and exam ($50-$150)
4. **Bond and insurance**: Purchase errors & omissions insurance ($25-$100/year)
5. **Supplies**: Notary stamp/seal, journal ($50-$100)
6. **Total cost**: $100-$400
7. **Commission valid**: 4 years typically

**Work Options:**
- **General notary**: Notarize documents at set locations ($5-$15 per signature, ~$500-$2,000/year)
- **Mobile notary**: Travel to clients (charge travel fee + notary fee, ~$5,000-$15,000/year part-time)
- **Loan signing agent**: Specialize in mortgage closings ($75-$200 per signing, full-time $50K-$100K possible)
- **Remote online notary (RON)**: Notarize documents via video (growing, check state laws)

**Becoming Loan Signing Agent** (Where Real Money Is):
1. Become notary public first
2. Take loan signing agent training ($200-$400, National Notary Association or Loan Signing System)
3. Pass background check and certification exam
4. Get $100,000 E&O insurance ($200-$400/year)
5. Market to title companies, signing services, real estate agents
6. Income: $75-$200 per signing, can do 2-4 per day, $50K-$100K+ per year

**Military Installation Advantages:**
- **PCS paperwork** requires notarization (wills, powers of attorney, etc.)
- **Deployment documents**: Huge demand before deployments
- **Legal documents**: Military members constantly need notary services
- **Can offer mobile service** to busy military families

**Portability:**
- **State-specific commission**: Must get new commission in each state you PCS to
- **Fast process**: Usually 2-6 weeks to get commissioned
- **Loan signing agent**: Skills transfer, just need new notary commission

**Best as Side Income**: Combine with other career for supplemental $500-$10,000/year
