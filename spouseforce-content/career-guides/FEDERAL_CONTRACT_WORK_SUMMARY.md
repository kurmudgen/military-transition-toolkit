# Federal & Contract Work for Military Spouses - Complete Guide

## Category Overview

Federal government and defense contractor careers offer military spouses unique advantages: military spouse hiring preference, security clearance benefits, and understanding of military culture. Many positions are now remote-friendly.

**Why Federal/Contract Work for Military Spouses:**
- **Military Spouse Preference**: Priority hiring on USAJOBS
- **Security Clearance Advantage**: Can often obtain clearances, significant salary boost
- **Understand Military**: Your experience is an asset
- **Stable Benefits**: Federal positions offer excellent benefits
- **Remote Options Growing**: Many agencies now offer telework

---

## 1. Federal Remote Jobs (GS Scale) ($45K-$120K+)

**Portable: HIGH | Hiring Preference: Yes | Excellent Benefits**

**Salary Ranges (GS Scale):**
- **GS-5/7** (Entry): $35,000 - $55,000
- **GS-9/11** (Mid): $55,000 - $85,000
- **GS-12/13** (Senior): $85,000 - $120,000+
- **GS-14/15** (Executive): $120,000 - $160,000+

**Military Spouse Hiring Authorities:**
- **Executive Order 13832**: Grants non-competitive hiring for military spouses
- **Applies to**: Spouses of active duty, 100% disabled veterans (under certain conditions)
- **Advantage**: Don't have to compete with general public for some positions
- **Use on USAJOBS**: Filter for "military spouse preference"

**Common Remote Federal Roles:**
- Program Analyst, Management Analyst
- IT Specialist, Cybersecurity Specialist
- Contract Specialist, Procurement Analyst
- HR Specialist
- Budget Analyst
- Public Affairs Specialist

**Getting Started:**
1. Create USAJOBS account and profile
2. Build federal resume (different format - longer, more detailed)
3. Search for remote positions with military spouse preference
4. Apply using military spouse hiring authority
5. Prepare for structured interviews

**Federal Benefits:**
- Health insurance (FEHB), retirement (FERS + TSP matching)
- Pension after 5 years vested
- 13-26 days annual leave
- 13 days sick leave
- 11 federal holidays

## 2. Defense Contractor (Remote Positions) ($60K-$150K+)

**Portable: VARIES | Clearance Often Required | High Pay**

**Salary Ranges:**
- **Entry (no clearance)**: $50,000 - $70,000
- **Mid (Secret clearance)**: $75,000 - $110,000
- **Senior (Top Secret/SCI)**: $100,000 - $180,000+

**Top Defense Contractors:**
- Booz Allen Hamilton, Leidos, SAIC, CACI, General Dynamics IT
- Northrop Grumman, Raytheon, Lockheed Martin
- Accenture Federal, Deloitte Federal, GDIT

**Common Contractor Roles:**
- Intelligence Analyst, Operations Analyst
- Cybersecurity Analyst
- Program Manager, Project Manager
- IT Support, Systems Administrator
- Training Specialist
- Logistics Analyst

**Security Clearance Benefits:**
- **Secret**: Adds $10K-$25K to base salary
- **Top Secret**: Adds $25K-$50K+ to base salary
- **SCI (Sensitive Compartmented Information)**: Highest premium

**Getting Clearance as Military Spouse:**
- Must be sponsored by employer (can't self-sponsor)
- Background investigation (credit, criminal, references)
- Process takes 3-12+ months
- Military spouse status helps (investigators understand PCS moves, time overseas)

**Getting Started:**
1. Apply to defense contractors (ClearanceJobs.com, company websites)
2. Emphasize "clearance eligible" if you are
3. Many entry-level positions sponsor clearances
4. Remote positions growing in contractor world

**Applying to Federal Jobs:**
1. **USAJOBS.gov** is primary federal job site
2. **Federal resumes** are different (longer, more detailed, include everything)
3. **Use military spouse preference** when eligible
4. **Structured interviews** - prepare using STAR method
5. **Hiring is slow** - expect 3-6+ months from application to start

**Getting Security Clearance:**
1. **Must be sponsored** by employer (apply to clearance-required jobs)
2. **Be patient** - process takes 3-12+ months
3. **Financial stability matters** - pay down debt before applying
4. **Be honest** on SF-86 form - omissions worse than disclosures
5. **Document PCS moves** clearly - investigators understand military life

**Military Spouse Advantages:**
- Understanding of military culture, missions, operations
- Eligibility for security clearances
- Non-competitive hiring authority for federal jobs
- Network within military community
- Commitment and adaptability valued

**Resources:**
- **USAJOBS.gov** - Federal positions
- **ClearanceJobs.com** - Cleared contractor positions
- **FedSmith.com** - Federal career resources
- **Military Spouse Employment Partnership (MSEP)** - Federal partners
