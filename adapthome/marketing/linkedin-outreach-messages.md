## **LinkedIn Outreach Best Practices**

### **Profile Optimization (Before You Start Outreach)**

**Your LinkedIn Profile Should Include:**
- Professional headshot
- Compelling headline: "Helping Contractors Connect with Pre-Funded VA Grant Projects | AdaptHome"
- Detailed "About" section explaining AdaptHome
- Company page for AdaptHome
- Content posts about VA grants, accessibility, veterans

### **Research Before Connecting**

**Look for these signals that a contractor is a good fit:**
- ‚úÖ Located in Southern California (San Diego, Orange, Riverside, San Bernardino)
- ‚úÖ Specializes in accessibility, remodeling, or aging-in-place
- ‚úÖ CAPS certified (Certified Aging-in-Place Specialist)
- ‚úÖ Veteran-owned business (bonus)
- ‚úÖ Active on LinkedIn (posts regularly)
- ‚úÖ Has positive reviews/testimonials on their profile

**Red flags to avoid:**
- ‚ùå New construction only (not remodeling)
- ‚ùå Commercial-only contractors
- ‚ùå Out of state
- ‚ùå No clear specialization
- ‚ùå Inactive LinkedIn profile

### **Message Timing**

**Best times to send LinkedIn messages:**
- **Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM PST** (best response rates)
- **Tuesday-Thursday, 5-7 PM PST** (after work hours)
- Avoid weekends (low activity)
- Avoid Monday mornings (inbox overload)

### **Message Length**

**Connection Request (Message 1):**
- Keep it under 300 characters
- Personalize with specific detail about their profile
- Mention mutual connection if possible

**Follow-Up Messages (Messages 2-5):**
- Aim for 300-600 characters (short and scannable)
- Use bullet points for easy reading
- Include ONE clear call-to-action
- LinkedIn allows up to 1,900 characters, but shorter is better

### **Personalization Tokens**

**Use these variables for scale:**
- `[First Name]` - Contractor's first name
- `[Company Name]` - Contractor's company
- `[County]` - Their primary service area
- `[Specialization]` - Bathroom, kitchen, ramps, etc.
- `[Mutual Connection]` - If you have one
- `[Veteran Status]` - If they're a veteran

### **Response Handling**

**If they reply with interest:**
- Respond within 1 hour
- Offer to schedule a call immediately
- Send calendar link for easy booking
- Provide one-pager or resources

**If they reply with questions:**
- Answer thoroughly but concisely
- Offer to discuss on a call if complex
- Share relevant case studies or testimonials
- Address objections directly

**If they reply "not interested":**
- Thank them for their time
- Ask if you can follow up in 6 months
- Offer to connect them with resources anyway
- Ask if they know anyone who might be interested (referrals)

### **Tracking & Metrics**

**Monitor these KPIs:**
- **Connection acceptance rate** (aim for 40%+)
- **Reply rate** (aim for 15-25%)
- **Meeting booking rate** (aim for 5-10%)
- **Sign-up conversion rate** (aim for 2-5%)

**If rates are low:**
- Test different message templates
- Improve personalization
- Adjust targeting (better prospect selection)
- Optimize profile (add social proof, testimonials)

### **Automation Tools (Use Wisely)**

**Recommended tools for scaling LinkedIn outreach:**
- **Dux-Soup** - Auto-visit profiles, send connection requests
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** - Advanced search and lead lists
- **Expandi** - Automated messaging campaigns
- **Phantombuster** - Extract emails and data

**Important:**
- Use automation carefully (LinkedIn limits daily actions)
- Always personalize messages (avoid generic templates)
- Monitor account health (avoid being flagged as spam)
- Keep daily limits conservative (20-30 connections/day max)
