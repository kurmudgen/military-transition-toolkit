## SkillBridge Success Formula

**What is SkillBridge?**
DoD program allowing service members to intern with civilian companies during last 180 days of service while still receiving military pay and benefits.

**Conversion Rate:** 70% of SkillBridge participants receive full-time job offers

**How to Maximize Success:**

**12 Months Before Separation:**
- Start researching SkillBridge companies
- Identify target industry and companies
- Begin networking with companies
- Build relevant skills/certifications

**6-9 Months Before:**
- Apply to SkillBridge programs
- Get command approval
- Finalize timeline and logistics

**180 Days Before Separation:**
- Start SkillBridge internship
- Treat it like a 6-month interview
- Build relationships and demonstrate value
- Position for full-time offer

**Top SkillBridge Companies:**
- Amazon, Microsoft, Google (Tech)
- Lockheed Martin, Raytheon (Defense)
- JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America (Finance)
- UPS, FedEx (Logistics)
- HCA Healthcare (Healthcare)

## First 90 Days Success Plan

**The first 90 days in your civilian job are critical.** Here's your roadmap:

### Days 1-30: Learn and Listen
- **Week 1:** Complete onboarding, meet team, understand company culture
- **Week 2-3:** Learn systems, processes, and expectations
- **Week 4:** Identify quick wins and ways to add value

**Key Actions:**
- Schedule 1-on-1s with key stakeholders
- Take notes on everything
- Ask questions (don't assume)
- Avoid military jargon
- Observe team dynamics before jumping in

### Days 31-60: Contribute and Build Relationships
- **Week 5-6:** Deliver on assigned projects
- **Week 7-8:** Propose improvements (carefully)

**Key Actions:**
- Achieve 1-2 quick wins
- Build relationships across departments
- Demonstrate reliability and work ethic
- Seek feedback from manager
- Join company social activities

### Days 61-90: Demonstrate Value and Plan Ahead
- **Week 9-11:** Take on more responsibility
- **Week 12:** 90-day review preparation

**Key Actions:**
- Document accomplishments (quantify impact)
- Meet or exceed all initial goals
- Discuss career path with manager
- Identify next growth opportunities
- Build mentor relationships

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I don't know what I want to do in my second career. Will this course help?**
A: Yes! Module 1 includes comprehensive assessments to identify your interests, skills, and viable career paths. We'll help you narrow down options.

**Q: Is it too late if I've already separated?**
A: No! This course helps recently separated veterans and those years into civilian careers. The strategies work at any stage.

**Q: Do I need a college degree for a good second career?**
A: Not always. Many high-paying careers (IT, sales, skilled trades) don't require degrees. Certifications often matter more. The course shows you both paths.

**Q: Can I really make six figures in my second career?**
A: Many veterans do, especially in IT/cyber, project management, sales, and management roles. With clearance and the right certifications, $100K+ is very achievable.

**Q: How important is networking really?**
A: 70% of jobs are filled through networking, not job boards. Module 2.2 teaches networking strategies even if you're introverted.

**Q: Should I use my GI Bill for a degree or certifications?**
A: Depends on your career path. The course includes a decision framework based on your target industry and time available.
